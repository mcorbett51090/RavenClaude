## 4. Enhancements — and the panel's ruling on the AMBITION question

**Ruling: YES — v5 must carry an explicit, gated Track B. v5 is correctly sized as the *default*, but it is over-corrected as the *only* path.** The chair rules decisively here because the seats are unanimous and the reasoning is airtight: the critic loop legitimately killed the *solo-builds-a-general-engine / VC-SaaS* fantasy, but it then over-rotated into "solo permanently" — a conclusion the critic was entitled to *propose* but not to *impose* over Matt's explicit "no constraints on ambition." The decisive insight, raised independently by **four of five seats**, is that **the binding constraint is delivery weeks, not demand, positioning, or liability** — and that exact constraint is the one a second human absorbs without reintroducing any killed risk.

**The reconciliation of Matt's two constraints ("no ambition ceiling" + "simple to understand"): ambition becomes a set of named, gated, opt-in levers stacked on top of a simple honest default — not baked into the base-case math.** This lets Matt say yes to ambition without re-litigating the runway honesty. The master gate for *all* of Track B is the **measured build-hour reduction** v5 already commits to instrumenting — so no new measurement apparatus is needed.

**Track B — three levers, sequenced cheapest-to-test first, each shown to survive all four prior critiques:**

- **Lever 1 (Year 1, near-zero cost — test first): an origination / referral partner who sells while Matt delivers.** Formalize a fractional-CFO firm (already named in Risk #6) as a revenue-share origination partner. Sales stops competing with Matt's scarce delivery weeks; also de-risks the single-channel sourcing concentration. No headcount on Matt's books, no liability change. *The lowest-cost ambition lever — pull it first.*
- **Lever 2 (Year 2, after build-hours proven): a second delivery person — a builder, NOT a signer.** An apprentice analyst / contract senior-controller does mapping + bespoke script-build under Matt's review. This attacks the *actual* ceiling (delivery weeks). **Liability posture untouched — the buyer's reviewer still signs; the second person attests nothing.** Doubles as the G11 continuity mitigation. Gate: ≥4 full-price closes delivered AND measured build-hour reduction AND a 6-month cash buffer. Survives all four critiques (still bespoke, not a general engine; a Year-2+ move so no steady-state-math-in-Year-1 error; doesn't resurrect the subscription; doesn't change the trigger).
- **Lever 3 (Year 2+, hardest gate — where "funding allowed if value justifies" actually applies): productize the governance IP for the *developer/compliance* buyer.** This honors the research synthesis's #1 finding (S1: the binding in-loop tribunal is genuine white space; T1 was the top territory; the recommended posture was "lead with finance, productize the governance IP as the IP the consulting sells"). v5 keeps the proving ground and quietly cancels the thing being proved — a strategic under-allocation. **The Security seat supplies the real gate: the IP track is fundable precisely when — and only when — the two unshipped assurance layers (multi-tenant isolation + tamper-evident logging) are real and pen-tested**, because those are the only things between the solo service and a defensible product, and they are what a solo founder is least equipped to harden alone. Gate Lever 3 on "≥3 recurring finance-judgment decision categories route cleanly through `thing-decide.py`" AND "a design partner's reviewer confirms the lineage-card-with-verdict is decision-useful" AND "isolation + tamper-evidence shipped." **If Matt ever takes outside money, this is the line item it funds** — capital buys speed-to-market in a genuine ~12–18-month race (synthesis C4), whereas funding the *service* just buys back Matt's calendar, which the pattern library already does more cheaply.

**Best non-ambition enhancements to adopt:**

- **Make data-handling the sharpest competitive edge against in-tool AI (Security).** Excel Copilot / accounting-suite AI run *inside* the customer's tenant on live data. A consultant who works only from scoped, deletable exports and returns a signed deletion attestation can say "your most sensitive financial data never touches a third-party AI's training-eligible context, and we prove its deletion" — a story the incumbents structurally cannot tell. Converts the G2 P0 into a differentiator.
- **Publish the time-savings claim as the lead sales asset (CFO/GTM/Delivery).** Instrument every engagement into an anonymized "before: N days; after: M hours" table. Two or three real before/after numbers beat every paragraph of positioning for a skeptical finance buyer — and directly answer the G4 "cheaper than a hire" math.
- **Build one anonymized click-through reference artifact the moment the first design partner closes (GTM).** A real lineage-carrying close package a prospect clicks through unsupervised shortens a reference-driven sale materially. Make it a Month-3–8 deliverable, not a Month-12 afterthought. (The data-platform embedded-dashboard surface already exists.)
- **Sequence design-partner #1 as a stack Matt already knows intimately (Delivery)** — treat it as the lowest-risk delivery whose job is to build the asset, not maximize revenue.
- **Define month-6 leading indicators that distinguish "runway working" from "quietly going broke" (Delivery)** — first design partner signed by Month 3, first close delivered by Month 5, build-hours trending down by engagement #2 — each paired with a documented pivot/stop action.

## 5. The 3 questions only Matt can answer

1. **Does Matt actually want Track B, or is the honest solo ceiling the life he's choosing?** The panel can prove the ceiling is *removable* without reintroducing a killed risk — but "removable" is not "should be removed." A ~$130–190k Year-2 solo lifestyle business with no team to manage, no payroll, no partner to answer to is a legitimate, deliberate endpoint. Adding a second person, an origination partner, or outside capital changes the *kind* of business and the *kind of life* it is. This is a genuine values call about how Matt wants to spend his time, not a question the math decides.

2. **What is the real Year-1 runway figure, and is Matt willing to commit it?** The panel can specify the *arithmetic* (effort-weeks × analyst-rate vs household burn → required savings draw) and the GO/NO-GO gate, but only Matt knows his actual numbers: how much partial analyst income he can hold, his household burn, his savings tolerance, and his family's appetite for an income dip during the build year (memory confirms he has a family). The plan does not start without this, and only Matt can supply it.

3. **Is Matt willing to personally underwrite the liability surfaces the ambitious path requires — the data-confidentiality/DPA exposure now, and the E&O posture if Lever 3 ever ships an attestation or a multi-tenant product holding others' financial data?** The single-signer rule keeps Matt off the *numbers* liability, but G2 (holding customer GL exports) and any Track-B productization create real legal exposure that needs a lawyer conversation and possibly E&O coverage — a cost and a risk-appetite decision the panel cannot make for him.
