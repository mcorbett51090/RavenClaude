## A. The decisive finding — viability is MARGINAL (read first)

A blended model **can** cover the site's *running + content* costs, but **only in the owner-writes-content scenario**, and the load-bearing stream is **paid directory listings, not ads**:

| Stream | Year 1 | Year 2 | Note |
|---|---|---|---|
| Display ads | $480–900 | $4k–6.3k | Below hosting in Y1; needs ~25k PV/mo for Raptive. Source: thisweekinblogging.com |
| Travel affiliates (hotel+tours) | ~$1.2k–1.5k | ~$3.5k–5k | **Booking.com "Bookinggeddon" (May 2025)** cut small affiliates → route via CJ/Awin. Viator/GYG = 8%, 30-day cookie |
| **Paid directory listings** | **$2.4k–4.8k** | **$8k–12k** | **Load-bearing** — decoupled from traffic; close 10–25 local businesses @ $30–50/mo. Requires active sales. |
| Premium relocation/concierge tier | $0 | $0–2k | Upside only; 12–24mo sales cycle; Corporate Concierge Bermuda has 15-yr head start |
| **Total (base)** | **$4k–7.5k** | **$16k–26k** | |

Costs: tech stack ~$2.5k/yr; **content is the real driver** — owner-writes = $0 cash (~10–20 hrs/wk); partial freelance ~$8k; full outsource ~$27k (not viable). **Break-even verdict: owner-writes → +$3.2k Y1; mixed → −$4.8k Y1, +$10.5k Y2; outsourced → never.** Traffic ceiling ~10–25k sessions/mo by Y2 (Google 2024 HCU punishes thin niche travel sites — a comparable blog dropped 37k→12k). **Implication: the plan must center directory-listing sales + owner-written editorial, treat ads/affiliates as supplementary, and be honest that "small ads pay for it" is false.**

## B. Audience findings (decision-grade)

**Visitors** (the volume + margin engine; ~140k air leisure spend $2,051/person, 62–72% US; 538k cruise are low-intent day-trippers — target air visitors):
- #1 need = **getting around** (tourists CANNOT rent cars — scooters/minicars/bus+ferry $62/wk pass/taxis $60–85/hr). Unique, high-friction, poorly served → sharpest SEO + differentiation hook.
- Then: beaches/attractions (Horseshoe Bay, Tobacco Bay), curated activities by interest, dining (88 OpenTable restaurants + local Table.bm), where-to-stay (inventory ~75% of 2019 → "book early").
- Competitive gap: gotobermuda.com = brochure; Nomadic Matt = budget; no **high-end opinionated logistically-complete** guide exists.
- Monetizable: Viator/GYG (8%), Booking.com via CJ/Awin (~4%), OpenTable (~$1–2/reservation), travel insurance (15–35%), direct scooter-operator referrals.

**Movers** (small volume ~hundreds–low-thousands/yr, very high value, legally sensitive):
- Hard gate = **employer-sponsored work permit** (no job offer = no move; Work From Bermuda digital-nomad program CLOSED Feb 28 2025; policy revised Oct 30 2025). Cost of living ~94% above US, rent ~186% above. Mandatory health insurance (SHB $400.31/adult/mo). Car import duty 75%/150%; **one-car-per-household "Assessment Number trap"**; private school ~$24k/yr.
- 3 unsolved content gaps (the moat): (1) the **Assessment-Number/car trap** explainer, (2) a **compensation-reality calculator** (what salary you need to break even vs US/UK), (3) an **immigration-pathway decision tree** (employer vs EIRC $2.5M vs spouse vs reside-annually). All must carry **"informational only — verify at gov.bm — not legal advice"** disclaimers.
- Monetization fit: tiered free content → optional **Concierge Relocation Pack** ($250–500), + vetted-partner referrals (relocation agents, shippers Morgan/BDA, realty JBM, insurers Argus/BF&M).

**Residents** (HARD CEILING ~50–54k addressable adults; cannot self-fund a premium product):
- Recurring needs: local news (Bernews/Royal Gazette dominate), a **resident-centric events calendar** (the only standalone one, nothingtodoinbermuda.com, got absorbed into a tourism site — gap is real), **resident-discount/deals aggregator** (genuine unmet gap), curated dining for regulars, a **settling-in layer** for the ~20k non-Bermudian residents (33% of workforce, perpetually "new here"), a better local-business **directory** (incumbent BermudaYP is aging).
- Viability: even the Royal Gazette (near-universal reach) made only **$36k H1 operating profit** — the local-media market is tiny. **The resident side is the credibility/retention anchor; the Visitor side is the margin engine that subsidizes it.** Don't build resident-only.

## C. Cross-cutting strategic implications
1. **Visitors-first economically even though we launch all 3** — visitors are 10x the audience and carry the transactional intent; residents/movers are credibility + niche-value layers the visitor revenue underwrites.
2. **Directory is the spine** — it serves all 3 audiences (visitors find services, residents find trades, movers find vetted vendors) AND is the load-bearing revenue stream. Build it as core infrastructure, not an afterthought.
3. **The "high-end / curated / well-designed" positioning is a genuine white space** — every incumbent is brochure-ware, budget-voiced, news-dominated, or ugly.
4. **Content sustainability is the existential risk** — curated editorial across 3 audiences is the differentiator AND the cost that kills the economics if outsourced. The plan needs a realistic owner-content cadence + maintenance model.
