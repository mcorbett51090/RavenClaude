# Controlled Substance Compliance Is Non-Negotiable and Audited

**Status:** Absolute rule
**Domain:** Regulatory compliance / controlled substances
**Applies to:** `veterinary-practice`

---

## Why this exists

DEA Schedule II–V controlled substances in veterinary practices are subject to federal regulatory requirements with zero tolerance for record discrepancies. A DEA audit or state board inspection that finds unreconciled controlled substance logs, missing or altered records, or a positive drug test in a diversion investigation can result in DEA registration suspension, loss of veterinary license, and criminal referral. Controlled substance diversion in veterinary practices is more common than often acknowledged, and the DEA's Veterinary Initiative enforcement has increased audits in recent years. [unverified — training knowledge] No practice revenue or operational convenience justifies non-compliance with controlled substance logs.

## How to apply

Treat controlled substance compliance as a daily operations non-negotiable with weekly reconciliation.

```
Controlled substance log requirements (minimum — confirm with DEA and state board):
Daily:
  [ ] Every dispensing or administering event logged: date, time, patient ID, drug, dose,
      route, DVM authorizing, staff administering
  [ ] Remaining drug in vial/bottle estimated and logged if vial is not emptied
  [ ] Wastage documented: amount wasted, witnessed by a second staff member, both sign

Weekly:
  [ ] Physical inventory count of all Schedule II–IV substances
  [ ] Reconcile: book balance (starting inventory + received – dispensed – wasted) = physical count
  [ ] Any discrepancy ≥ 1 unit: investigate before the next business day; document findings
  [ ] Discrepancy ≥ 5 units or pattern of discrepancies: report to DEA per applicable threshold
      and consult legal counsel

At purchase:
  [ ] DEA Form 222 (paper or electronic) for Schedule II purchases — no exceptions
  [ ] Invoices retained for 2 years minimum (verify retention requirement with state board)

Storage:
  [ ] Schedule II: securely locked cabinet, separate from other controlled substances
  [ ] Access limited to licensed/registered personnel — document who has access
```

**Do:**
- Assign a designated controlled substance compliance manager (practice manager, head technician, or DVM) who owns the log, reconciliation, and DEA reporting.
- Conduct a surprise quarterly self-audit — the DEA may also conduct unannounced inspections.
- Train every staff member with drug access on the log requirements at hire and annually.

**Don't:**
- Allow the controlled substance log to fall more than 24 hours behind — catch-up logging from memory is a compliance failure and a diversion cover.
- Use a single syringe drawer vial across multiple patients without logging each use — partial-use entries are required.
- Destroy or discard a controlled substance without the correct disposal procedure (DEA-authorized take-back, DEA Form 41, or authorized reverse distributor).

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

Non-controlled drugs (e.g., NSAIDs, antibiotics, most anesthetic induction agents that are not DEA-scheduled) are outside this rule's strict application, though state board regulations may extend similar requirements to some substances in specific states — confirm with the state board.
