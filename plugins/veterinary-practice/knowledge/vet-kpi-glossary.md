# Veterinary practice KPI glossary

The metrics a veterinary practice is judged on — formulas, the misreads, and where a benchmark needs a date.

## Revenue metrics

Production per DVM, average client transaction (ACT), visits, new-client rate, revenue per FTE. Revenue is production × ACT × visits (§3 #2).

**Cited benchmarks (`[verify-at-use]`):**

| Metric | Figure | Read |
|---|---|---|
| Gross revenue / FTE veterinarian | ~$554,982 average (2024); optimal companion-animal target often cited $700k–$850k | A low number with a full schedule points at ACT or capacity, not demand |
| Average practice gross revenue | ~$1.5M (US, 2024); companion-animal-exclusive typically highest | Frame against segment, not a single national number |
| Invoices / FTE DVM / year | ~3,000 per full-time practitioner; ~5,000 per FTE doctor at practice level | Persistently above → demand may support a hire (see capacity tree) |
| New clients / FTE DVM / month | ~25 | Low *despite* demand can be a scheduling/template problem |
| Patients / DVM / day | ~20 sustainable; >20 trends to burnout | A unit-economics + retention signal (§3 #7) |

Sources: AVMA — increasing-practice-profitability/benchmarking (revenue/FTE, staffing): https://www.avma.org/news/increasing-practice-profitability-requires-benchmarking-defining-core-values ; dvm360 — when to add an associate (invoice/new-client thresholds): https://www.dvm360.com/view/qa-when-add-associate-your-team (retrieved 2026-06-05).

## Operational metrics

Schedule utilization, doctor-to-support ratio, recommended-care compliance/acceptance, no-show rate.

**Cited benchmarks (`[verify-at-use]`):**

| Metric | Figure | Read |
|---|---|---|
| Doctor-to-support ratio | Optimal ~1 DVM : 4–5 staff (AVMA) | Below this, doctor time leaks into tech-delegable work (capacity tree) |
| Credentialed-tech revenue lift | ~+$161,000 gross revenue per added credentialed vet tech per DVM (AAHA Benchmarking) | The cheapest capacity lever before a DVM hire |
| Inventory turns | COGS ÷ average inventory on hand | Low turns = cash tied in overstock + expiry shrink risk (inventory scenario) |

Sources: AVMA (staffing ratio) — https://www.avma.org/news/increasing-practice-profitability-requires-benchmarking-defining-core-values ; AAHA + Petabyte — AAHA Benchmarking (credentialed-tech lift): https://www.aaha.org/american-animal-hospital-association-and-petabyte-technology-unveil-aaha-benchmarking/ (retrieved 2026-06-05).

## Profit metrics

Gross/net margin, staff cost % of revenue, and the consolidation-market context they read against (§3 #5).

**Cited benchmark (`[verify-at-use]`):** most small-animal practices target net-income margins of roughly **10–15%**. Source: Today's Veterinary Business / industry wellness-plan margin framing — https://todaysveterinarybusiness.com/veterinary-wellness-plans/ (retrieved 2026-06-05). Treat as a segment-dependent range, not a target for every practice.
