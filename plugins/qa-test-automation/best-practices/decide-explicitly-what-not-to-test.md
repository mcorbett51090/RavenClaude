# Decide deliberately what not to test

A test strategy is as much about what you *don't* test as what you do — every test is code with a maintenance cost, and testing everything dilutes attention away from what actually breaks. Spend the budget by risk: heavily cover the high-blast-radius, frequently-changed, hard-to-reverse paths (money, auth, data integrity), and consciously *skip* the trivial (a getter, a generated DTO, a framework you'd be re-testing on its behalf) and the genuinely low-stakes. Write the not-to-test decision down so it's a deliberate choice with a rationale, not an accidental gap nobody owns. The failure modes are symmetric: untested critical paths ship defects, but over-testing the trivial produces a slow, brittle suite that taxes every change and erodes the team's trust in testing at all.
