# Don't pay the seller for buyer-created synergies (§3 #2)

**Rule.** Value = standalone + synergies − integration cost − control premium. Pay the
standalone plus a *defensible share* of the synergies you create — not the full synergy value,
and never without netting the cost to capture it.

**Why.** Synergies your team earns are your value, not the seller's. Handing them over in the
price means you did the work and the seller kept the reward — and the deal only "works" on
paper.

**Do**
- Split synergies into what any buyer could capture (partly the seller's) vs. what only you can.
- Net out one-time integration cost and expected dis-synergies before setting a ceiling.
- Treat the control premium in precedent multiples as already-paid — don't double-count it.

**Don't**
- Underwrite to the full run-rate synergy value.
- Ignore the cost and disruption of capturing the synergy.
