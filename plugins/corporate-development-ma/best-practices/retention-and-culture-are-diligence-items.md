# Culture and key-person retention are diligence items, not soft stuff (§3 #7)

**Rule.** Most deal value that evaporates leaves through the door with key people. Retention
economics and cultural fit are underwritten before signing, not discovered after close.

**Why.** In talent- and relationship-driven businesses, the assets go home every night. A
valuation that ignores who might walk — and what walks with them — is incomplete.

**Do**
- Identify key-person concentration and single points of failure in diligence.
- Build a retention plan (economics, timing, risk) before signing; route package terms to counsel.
- Assess cultural fit as a real integration risk, with a mitigation and an owner.

**Don't**
- Treat people and culture as a post-close HR task.
- Underwrite revenue that depends on people you haven't secured.
