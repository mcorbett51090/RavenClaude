# Synergies are a plan with an owner and a date, not a line item (§3 #3)

**Rule.** Every synergy dollar has a named owner, a realization date, and a one-time cost to
achieve. An un-owned synergy is a wish, and a revenue synergy is worth less than a cost
synergy until proven.

**Why.** Synergies in a model don't materialize; synergies in a plan with accountability
sometimes do. The number in the IC memo is only as real as the owner behind it.

**Do**
- Build a synergy register: owner × date × cost-to-achieve, per item.
- Weight cost synergies above revenue synergies; phase revenue synergies later and discount them.
- Track realization against the register post-close.

**Don't**
- Present a synergy total with no owners or dates.
- Book revenue synergies at the same confidence as cost synergies.
