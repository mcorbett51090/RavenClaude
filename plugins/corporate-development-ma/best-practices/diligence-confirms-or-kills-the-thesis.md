# Diligence confirms or kills the thesis — it is not a checklist (§3 #5)

**Rule.** Every diligence workstream tests a specific thesis assumption. Findings that move
neither the thesis nor the price are noise. Diligence exists to change the decision, not to
fill a data room.

**Why.** A generic checklist produces a fat report and a thin decision. Anchoring diligence to
the thesis assumptions is what turns a data room into a go/no-go.

**Do**
- Extract the thesis assumptions and map one workstream to each, with a confirm-or-kill question.
- Rank findings by thesis impact and price impact; feed re-pricing findings to valuation.
- Drop workstreams that test no assumption.

**Don't**
- Run a boilerplate checklist disconnected from the thesis.
- Treat volume of findings as diligence quality.
