# Integration risk is priced pre-signing or paid post-close (§3 #6)

**Rule.** The cost, time, and disruption of integration are part of the valuation, not a
post-close surprise. A deal that only pencils if integration is flawless is mispriced.

**Why.** Most deals that destroy value do it in integration, not at signing. Underwriting to a
frictionless integration is underwriting to a fantasy.

**Do**
- Estimate one-time integration cost, dis-synergies, and the timeline before signing.
- Feed that estimate back into the valuation and the walk-away.
- Decide integration depth (absorb / partner / standalone) per function up front.

**Don't**
- Treat integration as an after-close operational detail.
- Assume zero attrition and zero disruption.
