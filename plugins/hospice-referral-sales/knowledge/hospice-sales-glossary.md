# Hospice referral-sales glossary

Using the right term in front of a sophisticated clinician or discharge planner is a credibility requirement. ## The benefit

- **Medicare Hospice Benefit (MHB)** — Medicare coverage for hospice care for a patient with a terminal prognosis of **six months or less** if the disease runs its normal course, who **elects** the benefit and forgoes curative treatment **for the terminal illness**.
- **Election** — the patient's choice to receive the hospice benefit (signing the election statement). **Revocation** — leaving hospice to resume curative care; the benefit is **revocable** at any time.
- **Benefit periods** — two **90-day** periods followed by an unlimited number of **60-day** periods, each requiring **recertification** of the terminal prognosis. `[example — confirm]`
- **Recertification** — the physician's re-attestation of the terminal prognosis at the start of each benefit period.
- **Face-to-face encounter (F2F)** — a required in-person encounter by a hospice physician or nurse practitioner before the **third** benefit period and each subsequent one. `[example — confirm]`
- **Certification of terminal illness (CTI)** — the attending physician's and hospice medical director's written certification of the six-month prognosis. **This is the physician's act, not the rep's.**
- **Attending physician / hospice medical director** — the clinicians who certify eligibility and oversee the plan of care.

## Levels of care

- **Routine Home Care (RHC)** — the standard level, wherever the patient lives (home or facility); the large majority of hospice days.
- **Continuous Home Care (CHC)** — short-term, mainly continuous nursing during a symptom crisis to keep the patient at home; clinically gated.
- **General Inpatient Care (GIP)** — inpatient symptom control when symptoms can't be managed at home; an OIG-scrutinized level.
- **Inpatient Respite Care (IRC)** — a short inpatient stay (up to ~5 days) to relieve the caregiver.
- **Per-diem** — Medicare pays hospice a daily rate by level of care; the rep does not quote rates and treats any figure as `[example — confirm the current CMS rate]`.

## Clinical scales & eligibility terms

- **PPS (Palliative Performance Scale)** — functional status, 0–100%; a lower, declining PPS supports a decline picture.
- **FAST (Functional Assessment Staging)** — dementia staging; stage 7 plus a complication anchors the dementia LCD.
- **NYHA (New York Heart Association) class** — heart-failure functional class; Class IV anchors the cardiac LCD.
- **LCD (Local Coverage Determination)** — a Medicare Administrative Contractor's published coverage criteria, including the hospice decline guidelines by diagnosis.
- **Non-disease-specific decline guidelines** — the cross-diagnosis decline picture (functional, nutritional, recurrent acute events) that supports eligibility.
- **Palliative care** — symptom-and-support care at **any** stage of serious illness, **alongside** curative treatment; distinct from hospice.

## Funnel & census metrics

- **Referral** — a source sending a patient for hospice consideration.
- **Conversion rate** — admissions ÷ referrals (overall or by stage).
- **Time-to-admission** — elapsed time from referral to admission; a conversion lever.
- **Same-day admit** — admission on the day of referral; an important responsiveness measure.
- **Average daily census (ADC)** — the running average number of patients on service; the program's true size.
- **Length of stay (LOS)** — days on service; a **short** average/median LOS is the signature of **late referrals**.
- **Declined referral** — a referral that did not convert, with a root cause (ineligible / family-declined / lost-to-competitor / too-slow / died-first / re-routed).

## Referral-source roles

- **Discharge planner / case manager** — the hospital staff who arrange post-acute care; a primary referral relationship.
- **Hospitalist** — a hospital-based physician; a frequent referral source for declining inpatients.
- **DON (Director of Nursing)** / **administrator** — SNF/ALF leadership; key relationships for facility referrals.
- **ACO (Accountable Care Organization)** — a value-based group rewarded for total cost of care and quality; a strategic referral partner.

## Compliance terms

- **Anti-Kickback Statute (AKS)** — the federal criminal prohibition on remuneration to induce referrals of federal-program business.
- **Stark law** — the physician self-referral prohibition; strict liability; applies in addition to AKS when a referral source is a physician.
- **Beneficiary-inducement CMP** — the civil-monetary-penalty prohibition on inducing a beneficiary's choice of provider.
- **Safe harbor** — a regulatory structure that protects an arrangement from AKS liability if **every** element is met.
- **Fair market value (FMV)** — the value standard a compliant arrangement's compensation must meet.
- **Nominal value** — the small per-item/annual limit under which infrequent, non-cash gifts may fall.
- **PHI (Protected Health Information)** — patient-identifying health data governed by HIPAA; minimum-necessary, HIPAA-safe boundary, never in an example or shared note.
- **OIG (Office of Inspector General)** — the HHS enforcement office whose hospice work-plan items and advisory opinions shape the risk areas.
