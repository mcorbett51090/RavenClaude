# In-service education is the durable referral engine; a single sales call decays

**Status:** Pattern
**Domain:** Territory / education
**Applies to:** `hospice-referral-sales`

---

## Why this exists

A pop-in visit produces, at best, one referral and is forgotten by the next week. An in-service that teaches a building's clinicians to *recognize* an eligible patient keeps producing referrals long after the liaison leaves the room — and it does so by improving patient access, not by asking for business. Because clinical staff turn over, the education has to recur. The durable territory is built on a recurring in-service program, not on relationship visits alone.

## How to apply

**Do:**
- Design recurring, audience-specific in-services (the recognition picture for that source's population).
- Give clinicians a simple recognition takeaway (never a certification checklist).
- Re-run education as staff turn over and new clinicians arrive.
- Pull the clinical content from `hospice-eligibility-educator`; clear any attached meal/sponsorship with compliance first.

**Don't:**
- Rely on relationship visits with no education to sustain referrals.
- Run a one-and-done in-service and expect durable volume.
- Attach an un-cleared meal or sponsorship to the program.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

A tiny or very low-volume source may not justify a recurring program — a periodic touch is enough. The engine is for high-density, high-volume priority sources.
