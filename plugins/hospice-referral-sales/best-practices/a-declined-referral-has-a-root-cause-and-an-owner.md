# A declined referral has a root cause and an owner — "we lost some" is not a diagnosis

**Status:** Pattern
**Domain:** Funnel / root cause
**Applies to:** `hospice-referral-sales`

---

## Why this exists

Every referral that doesn't convert is information — but only if it carries a root cause and an owner. Logged as a generic "lost," it tells you nothing and fixes nothing. Categorized — ineligible, family declined, lost to another agency, response too slow, patient died before admission, facility re-routed — the *mix* points to the fix and the owner: eligibility declines route upstream to education, family-declined to the conversation coach, lost-to-competitor to the account manager and time-to-admit, too-slow to intake. The taxonomy turns a vague conversion problem into a specific, ownable action.

## How to apply

**Do:**
- Assign every decline a root cause from the taxonomy and a named owner.
- Traverse the declined-referral root-cause decision tree.
- Read the *mix* of causes, not just the count, to decide where to invest.
- Treat a "patient died before admission" decline as the strongest late-referral teaching signal.

**Don't:**
- Log declines as undifferentiated "lost."
- Assume every decline is a pricing/competitor problem (most are eligibility or timing).
- Delete declines to clean the funnel — categorize them honestly.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

A genuinely ambiguous decline can be tagged "unknown — follow up," but that is a temporary state with a follow-up owner, not a permanent category.
