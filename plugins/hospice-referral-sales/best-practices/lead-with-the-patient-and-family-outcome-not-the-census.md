# Lead with the patient and family outcome, not the census

**Status:** Absolute rule
**Domain:** Account management / messaging
**Applies to:** `hospice-referral-sales`

---

## Why this exists

A referral partner refers because patients and families do better, not because a hospice needs admissions. A partner review or outreach message that leads with the agency's referral count has signaled that the relationship is about the seller's number, not the patient — and a clinician hears that immediately. The census is a *result* of earlier, better hospice access; it is never the pitch. Leading with patient and family outcomes (timely comfort, symptom relief, avoided crisis hospitalizations, family support) is both more honest and more effective.

## How to apply

**Do:**
- Open every review and plan with what patients and families got, tied to the partner's own pressures (readmissions avoided, length-of-stay relief, family satisfaction).
- Frame growth as serving more eligible patients earlier, not as hitting a number.
- Keep the census as an internal result metric, not an external talking point.

**Don't:**
- Open a partner review with the agency's admit count.
- Frame an outreach as "we need referrals."
- Let an internal census target leak into the patient/family conversation as urgency.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

Internally — in a funnel read with the manager — census and conversion *are* the headline; that is the admissions-conversion-coach's job. The rule governs *external, patient-facing, and partner-facing* communication.
