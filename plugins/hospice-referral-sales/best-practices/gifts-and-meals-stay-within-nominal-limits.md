# Gifts and meals to referral sources stay within documented nominal limits

**Status:** Absolute rule
**Domain:** Compliance / gifts & inducements
**Applies to:** `hospice-referral-sales`

---

## Why this exists

"Small and infrequent" is the everyday version of the Anti-Kickback line — and it is a *documented structure*, not a vibe. The beneficiary-inducement CMP and AKS both tolerate genuinely nominal, non-cash, infrequent gifts within published limits, but the protection evaporates the moment a gift is cash, is tied to referrals, exceeds the limit, or accumulates into a pattern. A liaison who brings lunch to the same building every week, or hands a discharge planner a generous holiday gift, has built exactly the pattern OIG looks for.

## How to apply

**Do:**
- Keep gifts/meals non-cash, infrequent, and within the published per-item and annual nominal-value limits — and cite the limit (treat any remembered figure as example-until-confirmed).
- Document the item, the date, the recipient, and the business purpose.
- Confirm with the compliance officer before a recurring meal/in-service program.

**Don't:**
- Give cash or cash-equivalents (including gift cards).
- Tie a gift to referral volume.
- Let nominal items accumulate into a frequent pattern.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

Genuinely educational materials with no independent value (a printed clinical reference card) are generally not "remuneration." The rule governs items of value. When unsure whether something has value, treat it as if it does and route it.
