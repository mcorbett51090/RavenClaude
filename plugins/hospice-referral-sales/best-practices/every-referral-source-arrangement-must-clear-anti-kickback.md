# Every value flowing toward a referral source clears Anti-Kickback first

**Status:** Absolute rule
**Domain:** Compliance / fraud-and-abuse
**Applies to:** `hospice-referral-sales`

---

## Why this exists

Hospice is one of the most enforced corners of healthcare. The federal Anti-Kickback Statute makes it a *criminal* offense to offer anything of value to induce referrals of federal-program business, and "anything of value" is broad — meals, gifts, free services, staffing, space, sponsorships, discounts. The statute is intent-based and one improper purpose is enough. A liaison improvising a "thank-you" to a productive referral source can create a False Claims Act exposure for the whole agency. So every value exchange is checked *before* it happens, against a nominal-value limit and a safe-harbor structure — never after, and never on a gut feel.

## How to apply

**Do:**
- Route any proposed gift, meal, sponsorship, service, staffing, or space arrangement to `hospice-sales-compliance-advisor` (and the agency compliance officer) before it happens.
- Traverse the gift/meal/arrangement anti-kickback decision tree; it ends at "route to compliance officer."
- For arrangements (staffing/space/services), require a fair-market-value, written, non-volume-based structure and counsel review.

**Don't:**
- Offer cash or cash-equivalents (gift cards included) to a referral source — ever.
- Tie anything of value to referral volume or value.
- Provide free or below-FMV services that offset a source's costs.
- Green-light a value exchange yourself; the ruling is the compliance officer's.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

A hospice's own W-2 employees (the liaison's own salary) are protected by the employee safe harbor — the rule targets value flowing to *outside* referral sources. Educational in-services with no attached item of value need no clearance; the moment a meal, gift, or sponsorship attaches, the rule applies.
