# Screen against the published criteria before recommending a hospice conversation

**Status:** Absolute rule
**Domain:** Eligibility / education
**Applies to:** `hospice-referral-sales`

---

## Why this exists

Recommending a hospice conversation for a patient who clearly does not yet show the decline picture wastes a clinician's trust and trains them to discount your judgment; failing to recognize a patient who *does* show it lets an eligible patient go unserved. Screening a de-identified picture against the published non-disease-specific decline guidelines and the diagnosis LCD — before suggesting the physician conversation — makes the recommendation credible and accurate. The screen ends at "route to physician," never at "eligible."

## How to apply

**Do:**
- Map the non-disease-specific decline picture first (PPS, weight loss, recurrent infection, hospitalizations), then the diagnosis indicators.
- Cite the LCD and date; mark every threshold as example-until-confirmed.
- State what is present, what is missing to know, and route to the physician.
- Use the patient-ready decision tree and the `eligibility-indicators` tally.

**Don't:**
- Recommend a conversation on a hunch with no decline picture.
- Convert the screen into a certification ("this patient qualifies").
- Use any patient-identifying data in the screen.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

General education on the criteria needs no patient screen. The rule applies when you are about to point a source at a *specific* patient — that pointer should be grounded in the published indicators.
