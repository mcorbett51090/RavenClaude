# LCD quick-reference — diagnosis cards (EDUCATION, not certification)

> **Last reviewed: 2026-06-05.** A teaching summary of the published hospice **Local Coverage Determination (LCD)** decline indicators by diagnosis, for a liaison preparing referral-source education. **This is education for recognizing a candidate, not a tool for certifying eligibility.** The attending physician and hospice medical director certify eligibility on clinical judgment. Every threshold here is `[example — confirm against the current LCD for your MAC]`; LCDs are issued by Medicare Administrative Contractors and revised periodically. Sources: CMS hospice LCDs / the historical NHPCO/CMS local medical review policy guidelines (retrieved 2026-06-05) — verify the current text before teaching.

Across **all** diagnoses, the **non-disease-specific decline guidelines** support eligibility: progressive PPS decline, weight loss / falling BMI / declining albumin, recurrent infections, multiple hospitalizations, and increasing dependence. Teach the trajectory first, then the diagnosis card.

## Heart disease (end-stage)

- NYHA Class IV (symptoms at rest), already **optimally treated** with diuretics/vasodilators.
- Often: ejection fraction ≤ ~20% (supportive, not required), angina at rest refractory to treatment, history of cardiac arrest, syncope, or cardiogenic embolic stroke.

## Pulmonary disease (end-stage)

- Disabling **dyspnea at rest**, poorly responsive to bronchodilators; decreasing functional capacity.
- Progression: recurrent ER/hospital visits for pulmonary infections/respiratory failure; supportive markers FEV1 < ~30%, hypoxemia/hypercapnia, cor pulmonale, resting tachycardia, unintentional weight loss.

## Dementia (Alzheimer's and related)

- **FAST stage 7** — unable to ambulate, dress, or bathe without assistance; urinary/fecal incontinence; speech limited to a few words.
- **Plus** a recent medical complication: aspiration pneumonia, pyelonephritis, sepsis, multiple stage 3–4 pressure ulcers, recurrent fever, or inability to maintain intake with weight loss.

## Renal disease (end-stage)

- Not seeking dialysis or transplant (or discontinuing dialysis); creatinine clearance < ~10 mL/min (< ~15 with comorbidity) and serum creatinine > ~8.0 (> ~6.0 for diabetics) — `[example — confirm]`.
- Signs of uremia, oliguria, intractable hyperkalemia, hepatorenal syndrome.

## Liver disease (end-stage)

- PT > ~5 sec over control or INR > ~1.5, **and** serum albumin < ~2.5 g/dL.
- Plus one of: refractory ascites, hepatic encephalopathy, spontaneous bacterial peritonitis, hepatorenal syndrome, recurrent variceal bleeding.

## Stroke / coma

- Poor functional status (PPS ≤ ~40%), inability to maintain hydration/nutrition, post-stroke dysphagia with weight loss not amenable to artificial feeding (or the patient declines it).
- Coma: persistent vegetative markers, abnormal brainstem response, absent verbal/withdrawal response, decline.

## ALS / neuromuscular

- Rapid progression with critically impaired respiratory function (declining vital capacity, dyspnea at rest, declining to ventilatory support) **or** critical nutritional impairment (declining to a feeding tube with weight loss/dehydration).

## Cancer

- Metastatic or progressive disease with declining performance status (PPS ≤ ~50–70% trending down) and/or a decision against (or failure of) further disease-directed treatment.

## HIV

- CD4 < ~25 or persistent viral load > ~100,000, plus a defining opportunistic condition and declining functional status — `[example — confirm against the current LCD]`.

## Adult failure-to-thrive

- The non-disease-specific decline picture itself: BMI < ~22, declining functional status (PPS ≤ ~40%), and inability/refusal to take adequate nutrition.

---

**Teaching reminder:** present these as "the picture that should prompt a physician conversation," never "the patient qualifies." Pair with the non-disease-specific decline guidelines, and end every use with the physician-certifies line.
