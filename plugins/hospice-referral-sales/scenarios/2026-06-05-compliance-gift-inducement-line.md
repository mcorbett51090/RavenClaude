## Problem

A well-meaning liaison, trying to deepen a relationship with a productive physician practice, proposed two things: catering a generous weekly lunch for the office, and "loaning" the practice an unpaid scheduling assistant one afternoon a week to help with their workload. Both felt like good service. Both sat squarely on — or over — the Anti-Kickback line: a frequent, non-nominal meal pattern to a referral source, and a free staffing arrangement that offset the practice's costs.

## Context

- Segment: a physician practice that was a real referrer; the liaison had no bad intent, only an instinct to be generous.
- Constraint: the Anti-Kickback Statute treats *anything of value* flowing to a referral source as a question of intent, and OIG specifically scrutinizes hospice relationships with physicians and facilities, including free services and recurring meals. "Small and infrequent" is a documented structure, not a feeling; free staffing that offsets a source's costs is a classic red flag. The advisor frames the question; the compliance officer and counsel rule. [confirm against AKS / 42 CFR 1001.952 and the current OIG hospice guidance, retrieved 2026-06-05]
- The liaison had conflated "good service" with "permissible," and was about to act without routing it.

## Attempts

- Tried: ran both ideas through the gift/meal/arrangement anti-kickback gate tree. Outcome: the weekly catered lunch failed the "infrequent / within nominal limit" test (a recurring, non-nominal pattern), and the free staffing failed outright (a free service offsetting the source's costs, tied implicitly to the referral relationship).
- Tried: reframed to compliant alternatives — genuinely educational in-services with, at most, a documented nominal and infrequent refreshment within the published limit; and, for any actual service to the practice, a fair-market-value, written, non-volume-based agreement reviewed by counsel (the personal-services safe-harbor structure). Outcome: the relationship-building moved to education and responsiveness, not value transfer.
- Tried: routed the final call to the agency compliance officer rather than the advisor green-lighting it. Outcome: the compliance officer confirmed the reframed approach and documented it.

## Resolution

Both original ideas were **inducement risks**, not good service. The fix was not "do it carefully" but "frame the question, reach for the compliant structure, and route the ruling to the compliance officer." The durable relationship lever in hospice is **education and responsiveness**, never something of value. The lesson: *every value flowing toward a referral source clears Anti-Kickback first, and the advisor never green-lights — the compliance officer rules.*

**Action for the next consultant hitting this pattern:** when an idea involves *anything of value* to a referral source — meals, gifts, free services, staffing, space — stop and run the anti-kickback gate before acting. Keep gifts/meals non-cash, infrequent, within the documented nominal limit, and not volume-based; structure any real service as FMV + written + non-volume-based with counsel; and route the ruling to the compliance officer (§3 #2). Never improvise generosity toward a referral source.
