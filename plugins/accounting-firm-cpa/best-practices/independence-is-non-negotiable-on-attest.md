# Independence Is Non-Negotiable on Attest Work

**Status:** Absolute rule
**Domain:** US public-accounting / CPA-firm operations
**Applies to:** accounting-firm-cpa

## Why this exists

The attest function — audit, review, compilation with disclaimer, agreed-upon procedures — has
value precisely because it is performed by someone independent of the entity being attested.
An auditor who is not independent has no audit product to offer. The AICPA Code of Professional
Conduct (ET §1.200 and §1.210) and the SEC (for registered firms) make independence a condition
of practice, not a preference. A firm that issues an attest report while impaired is exposed to
malpractice, regulatory sanction, and license revocation.

The impairment risk in a multi-service CPA firm is elevated because the same firm that audits
a client may also prepare its taxes, do its bookkeeping, or provide CFO advisory services. Each
non-attest service must be evaluated for the independence threat it creates before the attest
engagement is accepted or continued.

## How to apply

**Do:**
- Perform the independence analysis using the engagement-type / independence decision tree in
  `knowledge/cpa-firm-decision-trees.md` before signing the engagement letter.
- Document the analysis in the engagement file: threats identified, safeguards applied (or not
  available), and the conclusion (proceed / restructure / decline).
- Confirm independence in writing at the engagement letter date and again immediately before
  the report is issued.
- Obtain a signed representation letter from management that it has disclosed all relationships
  that could impair independence.
- When in doubt about a non-attest service, restructure (limit the service to clearly
  non-management activities) or decline the attest engagement.

**Don't:**
- Begin attest fieldwork before the independence analysis is documented.
- Accept a CAS engagement that requires the firm to prepare financial statements that the firm
  will then audit, unless the client has a competent management reviewer who independently
  reviews and approves every entry.
- Allow a firm staff member to perform management functions (signing checks, making business
  decisions, having custody of client assets) on an attest client's behalf.
- Assume that a long-standing non-attest service is automatically safe — evaluate annually.
- Treat a safeguard as available when client management lacks the competence to apply it
  meaningfully.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

- **Compilation without independence:** a CPA may issue a compilation report even if not
  independent, provided the report discloses the lack of independence (AR-C 80.A23). This is
  the only attest report that can be issued without independence.
- **Non-attest services only:** if the firm is providing tax, CAS, or advisory services only
  (no attest report), the independence requirement under AICPA ET §1.200 does not apply.
  Note: SEC independence rules may apply to non-audit services for registered companies — route
  through `regulatory-compliance` for issuers.
- **Agreed-upon procedures (AUP):** the independence requirement applies (AT-C 215), but the
  report is restricted to specified parties who agreed to the procedures — the threshold for
  management-function threats remains the same.
