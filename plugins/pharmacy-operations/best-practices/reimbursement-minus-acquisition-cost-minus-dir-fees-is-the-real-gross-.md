# Reimbursement minus acquisition cost minus DIR fees is the REAL gross margin.

**Status:** Pattern. **Constitution:** §3 #3, §4.

## Use when
Any pharmacy operations deliverable where this question is in play — read, applied, and cited whole.

## The rule
The sticker reimbursement overstates margin; the number that matters is reimbursement − acquisition cost − DIR/clawback fees per script, and a script that looks profitable at fill can go negative after DIR.

## Why it matters
This is house opinion §3 #3, distilled into a citable rule. Practitioners act on these deliverables, so a framing error here is not academic — it sends real operating decisions the wrong way. The rule is cheap to apply and expensive to skip.

## How to apply
- Apply this **before** reaching for a method — it sets the framing, not the conclusion.
- Make every number in the deliverable carry a definition, a window, and a baseline (§3 #1).
- Cite a source + date for any external benchmark, or mark it `[unverified — training knowledge]` (§3 #8).
- When this rule and another both apply, route to [`pharmacy-operations-lead`](../agents/pharmacy-operations-lead.md) to sequence them — overlapping signals usually mean two drivers at once.
- Keep patient PHI out of the deliverable; route professional/legal determinations to the qualified authority (§2).
- Close with a recommendation that has an owner, a date, and an expected metric movement.

## The anti-pattern this prevents
The §4 failure mode: acting as if "reimbursement minus acquisition cost minus DIR fees is the REAL gross margin." weren't true — the most common way an analysis quietly misleads the practitioner who acts on it. The plugin's advisory hook flags a deliverable that reads as if this rule were ignored.
