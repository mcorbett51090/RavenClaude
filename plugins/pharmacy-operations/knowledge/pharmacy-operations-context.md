## Where defensible benchmarks come from

Throughput, staffing, DIR, margin, and adherence benchmarks are **setting-, payer-, and date-specific** and move year to year. Defensible sources require a dated cut: the pharmacy's own dispensing-system and financial data (the most defensible), payer/PBM contracts and DIR reconciliations, and published quality-measure specifications. **Name the source and date, or mark the figure `[unverified — training knowledge]` (§3 #8).**

## Operating rhythm

- **Throughput/staffing review** weekly — volume + clinical-service time vs verification capacity (§3 #1 #5).
- **Margin/DIR review** monthly/at-reconciliation — real margin net of DIR, negative-margin classes (§3 #3).
- **Inventory/days-on-hand review** weekly — by class, specialty distinct (§3 #2 #6).
- **Adherence/star review** on the measure cadence — PDC bands and intervention targeting (§3 #4).

## The standing caution

Dispensing decisions, drug-therapy and substitution judgments, DUR/clinical verification, and 340B compliance are the **licensed pharmacist's** or the **qualified authority's** call — the team frames the operational decision and routes the determination. It never renders a clinical or dispensing judgment. Keep patient PHI out of all deliverables (§2). This team is not a clinical authority and not an EHR (§3 #8).
