# Price to the comps, not the seller's ego

**Status:** Absolute rule
**Domain:** Listings / pricing
**Applies to:** `residential-real-estate-brokerage`

> Advisory operations rule. A CMA is not an appraisal. Local price data is `[verify-at-use]`. No client PII.

---

## Why this exists

A listing's price is an argument you make with **comparable sales**, and the market grades it in the first two weeks. An aspirational price set to win the listing appointment doesn't fool buyers — it stales the listing, trains the market to wait for a cut, and usually ends below the price a right-first listing would have fetched. The comps, adjusted, are the only defensible price story.

## How to apply

- Build the CMA on **3-6 genuinely comparable closed sales**, adjusted explicitly toward the subject for the differences that move price.
- Produce a **supported range**, then position within it by the seller's timeline and market direction — not by the seller's target number.
- Script the seller conversation around the evidence; name the cost of overpricing (stale DOM, later drop, lower net).
- Track list-to-sale ratio and DOM across your listings — a pattern of price cuts is a pricing-to-the-seller pattern.

**Do:** let adjusted comps carry the price; position within the supported range on strategy.
**Don't:** cherry-pick high comps to win the listing, then chase the market down.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

Genuinely unique properties (few or no comps) and fast-moving markets require wider comp selection and direction-reading from pendings/actives, with lower stated confidence — the discipline is the same, the inputs are just thinner.
