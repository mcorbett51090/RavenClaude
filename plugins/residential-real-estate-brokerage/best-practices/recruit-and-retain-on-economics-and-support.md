# Recruit and retain on economics and support

**Status:** Pattern
**Domain:** Brokerage P&L / talent
**Applies to:** `residential-real-estate-brokerage`

> Advisory operations rule. Commission rates, caps, and fees are business-agreement- and market-specific — `[verify-at-use]`. Not legal or tax advice. No client PII.

---

## Why this exists

Split is what gets a producing agent in the door; **support is what keeps them.** A brokerage that recruits on a headline split its P&L can't sustain buys an agent who leaves for the next higher split — and burns company dollar doing it. The durable recruit-and-retain lever is the agent's **real take-home** (split, cap, and fees together) plus the value stack: leads, coaching, transaction coordination, marketing, and brand. Sell the whole thing.

## How to apply

- Model the agent's **take-home and the brokerage's company dollar** at the agent's expected GCI — not the headline split (see the split-vs-cap tree and skill).
- Know the **cap crossover** so you can offer the right model by production tier and keep the P&L whole (`[verify-at-use]`).
- Build a **value story per agent**: what the support stack is worth against their production, alongside the economics.
- Track **retention rate**; a producer leaving is a signal the model or the support lost to a competitor, not just a personal choice.

**Do:** sell take-home + the value stack; match the comp model to the production tier.
**Don't:** win recruits on a split the P&L can't carry, or on split alone.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

A brand-new, unproven agent may fit a simpler split or fee model until production justifies a cap tier — revisit as they grow. High producers will demand (and have earned) the cap; plan for it rather than resisting it.
