# Contract-to-close is a deadline checklist

**Status:** Absolute rule
**Domain:** Transaction coordination
**Applies to:** `residential-real-estate-brokerage`

> Advisory operations rule. Contingency periods are contract- and jurisdiction-specific — `[verify-at-use]`. No client PII.

---

## Why this exists

Once a contract is executed, the deal is a set of **dated obligations counting from the effective date** — earnest money, inspection, appraisal, financing, title, walkthrough, closing. Silence past a contingency deadline can waive a right or forfeit earnest money. A deadline you didn't calendar is a deadline you will miss, and the cost of a missed one is measured in a killed deal or a lost remedy, not an apology.

## How to apply

- The moment a contract is executed, **reconstruct the entire calendar from the effective date** — every contingency and deadline dated.
- Track each item to a **hard resolution**: satisfied, waived in writing, or its required notice delivered on time. "Probably fine" is not resolved.
- **Surface at-risk items early** — inspection report not in, appraisal not ordered, loan conditions not cleared — with the next action per party (buyer, seller, lender, title).
- Confirm exact periods against the **actual executed contract**; the norms are the shape, not the durations (`[verify-at-use]`).

**Do:** calendar every date on day one; track to written resolution; flag slips early.
**Don't:** track only the closing date; assume a contingency lapsed cleanly without the notice.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

Cash, contingency-waived, or investor deals compress or drop some milestones — but the ones that remain still run on the effective-date calendar, and the discipline is unchanged.
