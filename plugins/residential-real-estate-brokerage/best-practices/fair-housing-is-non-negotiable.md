# Fair housing is non-negotiable

**Status:** Absolute rule
**Domain:** Compliance / representation
**Applies to:** `residential-real-estate-brokerage`

> Advisory operations rule, **not legal advice.** Protected-class lists are jurisdiction-specific and change — `[verify-at-use]`. No client PII.

---

## Why this exists

Fair housing is the floor the entire brokerage stands on, and it is not a deal variable. Steering — directing buyers toward or away from areas on a protected-class basis — and protected-class characterization in advertising or conversation create exposure that no commission is large enough to justify. The rule governs what you say *and what you don't say*, in every ad, every showing, and every "what's that neighborhood like?" question.

## How to apply

- Confirm the applicable **federal + state + local protected-class list** for the market — it varies and changes (`[verify-at-use]`).
- Answer area questions with **objective facts and public data** (school district boundaries, tax rates, published crime statistics); never characterize an area by who lives there.
- Write marketing that **describes the property, not the ideal occupant** — no "family-friendly," "perfect for…," or similar.
- Show against the buyer's **objective, stated criteria**; let the buyer choose areas, never steer.
- When a client asks you to characterize an area on a protected-class basis, decline and redirect to objective sources — even (especially) when asked directly.

**Do:** answer with facts and public data; describe the property; let the buyer choose.
**Don't:** steer, characterize an area by its people, or advertise to an occupant profile.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

There is no exception. Where a client's request would require a protected-class characterization, the answer is objective data or a decline — never an accommodation.
