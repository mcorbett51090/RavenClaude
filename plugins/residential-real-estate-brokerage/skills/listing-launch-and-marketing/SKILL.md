# Listing Launch & Marketing

A listing has one **first weekend** of peak buyer attention. The launch exists to make that weekend count.

## The loop

1. **Prep before live.** Decluttering, repairs, and staging happen before photography and before MLS entry. A listing that goes live mid-prep burns its best market on a bad first impression.
2. **Photograph like it matters.** Real photography (and where it fits, video/floor plan) before go-live. Photos are the click-through; a phone snapshot suppresses showings no price can recover.
3. **Enter the MLS accurately and completely.** Every field correct — beds/baths, square footage source, features, disclosures. Missing or wrong data suppresses the listing in searches and creates disclosure risk. Accuracy is compliance, not formatting.
4. **Coordinate the push to go-live.** Syndication, open house, agent/broker outreach, and social all fire around the same live date so the first weekend concentrates attention. Keep all copy **fair-housing clean** — describe the property, never the ideal buyer (see [`../../best-practices/fair-housing-is-non-negotiable.md`](../../best-practices/fair-housing-is-non-negotiable.md)).

## Metrics

| Metric | Reads | Note |
|---|---|---|
| First-weekend showings | booked showings in days 1-3 | The launch's leading indicator |
| Listing views / saves | portal engagement | Low with good price -> photos or copy |
| DOM to first offer | live to first offer | Long -> price, prep, or launch miss |

## Anti-patterns

- Going live "to test the market" before prep and photos are done.
- Marketing copy that describes the buyer ("perfect for a young family") — a fair-housing exposure.
- Inaccurate MLS data that suppresses the listing or misstates the property.
