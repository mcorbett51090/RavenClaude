# Decompose a lift from a mix-shift before claiming a win

"ARPA went up" is ambiguous: you may have priced better, or you may have churned the
cheap customers and let the average drift. The two look identical until you decompose
them. The same applies to any monetization metric after a change — separate genuine
lift from mix-shift and cohort effects before declaring a result, and don't read a
change before a full renewal cycle has turned.

**Do:** decompose every post-change metric move; report effect size + CI; route the
significance of a controlled price test to `applied-statistics`.

**Flag:** a pricing-change "win" claimed from an average that wasn't decomposed, or read
too early.
