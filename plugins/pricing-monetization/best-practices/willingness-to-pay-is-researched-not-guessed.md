# Willingness-to-pay is researched, not guessed

A price with no WTP evidence is a guess wearing a number. When a full study isn't
possible, name the method you *would* run and mark the price **provisional** — never
present a guessed number as validated. Remember the two guards: stated WTP overstates
real WTP (discount toward behavior), and in B2B you must ask the buyer, not just the user.

**Do:** attach a WTP method + confidence bound to every price; prefer a live test over
a survey when traffic exists.

**Flag:** a price presented as validated with only internal opinion or a competitor
glance behind it.
