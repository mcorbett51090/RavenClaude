# Price on value, not cost

Cost-plus sets a *floor*, not a price. The right price tracks the value the customer
captures, which is almost always disconnected from your cost to deliver. Cost-plus is
the symptom of not having done the value work — it's safe, defensible, and usually
leaves money on the table (or, for low-cost software, prices absurdly low).

**Do:** start from customer-captured value and the next-best alternative; use cost
only as a floor and a margin check (route that math to `finance`).

**Flag:** any price justified primarily by "it costs us X to deliver" with no value
story.
