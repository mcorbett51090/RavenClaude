# Document medical necessity for medical claims

**Status:** Absolute rule
**Domain:** Billing / documentation
**Applies to:** `optometry-eyecare-practice`

> Advisory operations rule, not billing or compliance advice. Medical-necessity criteria are payor-specific and `[verify-at-use]`. No PII/PHI.

---

## Why this exists

A medical eye-exam claim must survive a denial or an audit, and the only thing that lets it is the **record**: the chief complaint, the findings, and the assessment/plan. "Document the medical necessity" is not paperwork hygiene — it is what converts a routed-to-medical decision into a defensible, payable claim.

## How to apply

- For every medical claim, confirm the record carries: the medical complaint, the relevant findings, and the plan.
- No documentation → no medical claim. Route it to the vision plan or fix the record first.
- Match the documented necessity to the payor's stated criteria (`[verify-at-use]`).

**Do:** treat documentation as a precondition of the medical route.
**Don't:** bill medical on a routine note; assume the diagnosis alone is sufficient.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

Vision-plan routine exams have their own documentation standard; this rule is specific to the *medical* claim's necessity burden.
