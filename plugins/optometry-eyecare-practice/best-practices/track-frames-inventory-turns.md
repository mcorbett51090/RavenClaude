# Track frames inventory turns

**Status:** Pattern
**Domain:** Optical / inventory
**Applies to:** `optometry-eyecare-practice`

> Advisory operations rule. Turns benchmarks are `[ESTIMATE]` / `[verify-at-use]`. No PII/PHI.

---

## Why this exists

The frame board is working capital. A "full and impressive" board carrying dead stock is **frozen cash** — frames that don't turn tie up money and shelf space that a faster-moving segment would earn from. Inventory turns is the cash-efficiency number for the optical; vendor pressure to stock more is not.

## How to apply

- Measure turns by board segment, not just in aggregate; surface dead stock.
- Stock to what sells; mark down or cut segments that don't turn.
- Reconcile open lab orders and remakes against the board — a remake is a margin event.
- Set the stocking plan on turns and the practice's own demand, not on a vendor catalog.

**Do:** run the board on turns and demand.
**Don't:** equate a full board with a healthy board; let vendor minimums set your stock.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

A deliberately curated luxury/niche board may accept lower turns for higher per-unit margin — name that as the strategy, don't drift into it by neglect.
