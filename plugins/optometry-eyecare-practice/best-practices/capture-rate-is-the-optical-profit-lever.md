# Capture rate is the optical profit lever

**Status:** Pattern
**Domain:** Optical / dispensary
**Applies to:** `optometry-eyecare-practice`

> Advisory operations rule. Benchmark ranges are `[ESTIMATE]` / `[verify-at-use]`. No PII/PHI.

---

## Why this exists

The optical is where an eye-care practice's profit actually lives, and **capture rate** — the share of examined patients who fill their Rx in your optical — is the lever you most control. A patient you examined but who buys glasses elsewhere is high-margin revenue you already paid to acquire, walking out the door. Lifting capture beats squeezing frame cost.

## How to apply

- Track the funnel: eyes examined → Rx written → Rx filled in your optical → upgrades captured.
- Find the leak in order: the exam-to-optician **handoff** first (almost always the biggest), then quote accuracy vs the plan, then board/menu fit, then lab turnaround.
- Set a target against the practice's own baseline and a current benchmark (`[ESTIMATE]`, verify-at-use), not a vendor's number.

**Do:** fix the handoff before anything else.
**Don't:** treat frame markup as the growth lever; blame price for a handoff problem.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

A practice that deliberately runs exam-only (e.g. inside certain retail or medical-only models) optimizes differently — capture isn't its lever.
