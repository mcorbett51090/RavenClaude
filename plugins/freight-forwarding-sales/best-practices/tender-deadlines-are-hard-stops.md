# Treat tender submission deadlines as hard stops — no exceptions

**Status:** Absolute rule
**Domain:** RFQ / tender response
**Applies to:** `freight-forwarding-sales`

---

## Why this exists

A tender submitted one minute after the deadline is, in virtually all procurement processes, automatically disqualified — and in many cases it disqualifies the submitter from future rounds. The qualifying and pricing work invested before the deadline is entirely wasted. Beyond the immediate loss, late submissions signal to the customer's procurement team that the forwarder cannot meet commitments under pressure — precisely the opposite of what a forwarder is selling. The discipline of reverse-planning from the deadline is not a PM formality; it is a commercial competency.

## How to apply

As soon as an RFQ or tender is accepted (i.e., the qualify decision is BID), build a reverse plan from the submission deadline.

**Minimum reverse-plan milestones:**

| Milestone | Timing before deadline | Owner |
|---|---|---|
| Bid qualification decision confirmed | At receipt | Opportunity owner |
| Lane list and scope confirmed with customer | ≥ 5 days before deadline | Opportunity owner |
| Buy rates and carrier allocations sourced | ≥ 3 days before deadline | Pricing / ops |
| Draft rate matrix complete and internally reviewed | ≥ 2 days before deadline | Opportunity owner + manager |
| Bid narrative and win story drafted | ≥ 2 days before deadline | Opportunity owner |
| Commercial terms and approval sign-off | ≥ 1 day before deadline | Sales manager |
| Final submission packaged and tested in portal | ≥ 4 hours before deadline | Opportunity owner |
| Submitted | At or before deadline — not after | Opportunity owner |

**Submission-day discipline:**
- Portal uploads: test the login and upload flow at least 24 hours before — portals time out, files fail format checks.
- Email submissions: send with a read receipt and a delivery receipt; BCC your manager.
- If a force-majeure event (system outage, etc.) prevents on-time submission, contact the procurement team **before** the deadline to ask for an extension — not after.

**Do:**
- Set a calendar block for the submission deadline the day the qualify decision is made.
- If the deadline is genuinely unworkable (received 48 hours before close), call the procurement contact and ask for an extension — or politely decline and preserve the relationship.
- Flag any risk of missing the deadline internally at least 48 hours out so management can make a resource call.

**Don't:**
- Submit an incomplete or unreviewed bid just to beat the clock — a rushed bid with wrong rates is worse than a polite decline.
- Assume the portal is working on submission day without testing it 24 hours earlier.
- Treat deadline slippage as an internal ops issue; it is a commercial event with relationship consequences.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

When a customer explicitly grants an extension in writing (email from the procurement lead, not a verbal) and confirms the new deadline, the rule resets to the new hard stop. Document the extension grant in the CRM opportunity. The rule never relaxes for an assumed or hoped-for extension.
