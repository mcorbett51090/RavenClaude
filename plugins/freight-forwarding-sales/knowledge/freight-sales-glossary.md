# Freight-sales glossary

> **Last reviewed: 2026-06-04 by `claude`.** The working vocabulary a freight-forwarding sales manager needs to write a quote, an email, or a QBR with the right terms. Using the precise word (chargeable weight vs gross, THC vs ISPS, demurrage vs detention, FCA vs FOB) signals competence to a sophisticated shipper; the wrong one erodes trust. This is standard industry terminology, not any carrier's confidential glossary.
>
> Refresh trigger: a new Incoterms revision, a new advance-filing regime, or a structural change in how a charge is levied.

## Parties
- **Shipper / consignor** — the party sending the goods (often the seller/exporter).
- **Consignee** — the party receiving the goods (often the buyer/importer).
- **Notify party** — who the carrier alerts on arrival (may differ from consignee).
- **Freight forwarder** — arranges transport + documentation on behalf of shipper/consignee; may or may not take the cargo onto its own B/L.
- **NVOCC** (Non-Vessel-Operating Common Carrier) — issues its own (house) B/L and buys space from carriers; a forwarder acting as a contractual carrier.
- **Carrier / VOCC** — the asset operator (shipping line, airline) that physically moves the cargo.
- **Customs broker** — licensed to clear goods through customs; a regulated role (flag when one is required).
- **Importer of record (IOR)** — the party legally responsible for import compliance, duty, and VAT (relevant under DDP).

## Documents
- **Bill of Lading (B/L)** — ocean transport document + (for a negotiable original) document of title. Types: **negotiable** (original, controls title — needed to release cargo), **straight/seaway** (named consignee, non-negotiable, faster release), **telex release** (origin originals surrendered so destination releases without paper).
- **House B/L (HBL) vs Master B/L (MBL)** — HBL issued by the forwarder/NVOCC to the shipper; MBL issued by the carrier to the forwarder.
- **Air Waybill (AWB)** — air transport document; **non-negotiable**, not a document of title. **HAWB** (house) vs **MAWB** (master).
- **Commercial invoice** — the sale document; drives customs valuation.
- **Packing list** — contents, weights, dims per package.
- **Certificate of Origin (CO)** — declares where goods were made; **preferential CO** (e.g., under an FTA) can reduce duty.
- **Letter of Credit (L/C)** — bank payment instrument; demands strict document compliance.
- **Dangerous Goods Declaration (DGD)** — for hazmat; IMDG (sea) / IATA DGR (air).
- **Packing/weight: VGM** — Verified Gross Mass, the SOLAS-required certified container weight.

## Units & measures
- **TEU / FEU** — Twenty-/Forty-foot Equivalent Unit; vessel/space capacity units (not the FCL billing unit, which is per box).
- **Container types** — 20'GP, 40'GP, **40'HC** (high-cube, taller), 45'HC, reefer (refrigerated), open-top, flat-rack, tank.
- **CBM** — cubic metre (L×W×H in m); the volume measure for ocean.
- **W/M (weight/measure) revenue ton** — the LCL billing unit: the **greater** of 1,000 kg or 1 CBM.
- **Chargeable weight** — the weight a shipment is billed on: for air, the **greater** of actual gross and **volumetric** weight (Σ L×W×H cm ÷ 6000 IATA, /5000 some couriers).
- **Gross / net / tare weight** — gross = goods + packaging (+ container = "gross including tare"); net = goods only; tare = the container/packaging itself.

## Charges & charge points
- **Base freight / ocean freight / air freight** — the core line-haul rate before surcharges.
- **THC** — Terminal Handling Charge; **OTHC** (origin) and **DTHC** (destination) are separate lines.
- **BAF / LSS** — fuel-related surcharges (bunker / low-sulphur). **FSC** is the air fuel surcharge.
- **CAF** — Currency Adjustment Factor (FX), usually a % of base.
- **GRI** — General Rate Increase (carrier-announced, dated). **PSS** — Peak Season Surcharge.
- **ISPS / security** — port-security charge. **SSC** — air security surcharge.
- **AMS / ISF / ENS / ICS2** — advance cargo-data filings (US AMS + ISF 10+2; EU ENS / ICS2).
- **DDC** — Destination Delivery Charge (mainly US).
- **Demurrage vs Detention** — **demurrage** = container still **inside** the terminal past free time; **detention** = container **outside** the terminal (at your premises) past free time. Most-confused pair — use the right one.
- **Drayage / haulage** — short-haul trucking to/from port (pre-carriage / on-carriage).
- **Accessorials** — variable add-ons: waiting time, gen-set (reefer), OOG (out-of-gauge), hazmat, fumigation, customs exam.
- **All-in rate** — base + BAF + CAF + LSS (and sometimes more) folded into one number; gives the customer certainty, transfers volatility risk to the quoting party.

## Incoterms 2020 (one-liners — full detail in the `incoterms-2020` skill)
- **EXW** Ex Works — buyer collects from seller's door; buyer does everything incl. export clearance.
- **FCA** Free Carrier — seller delivers (cleared for export) to the carrier at a named place; **the correct containerized term.**
- **FAS / FOB** — alongside / on board the vessel at origin (sea-only; bulk/breakbulk).
- **CFR / CIF** — seller pays freight (CIF + minimum ICC C insurance) to destination port; risk passes at origin.
- **CPT / CIP** — seller pays carriage (CIP + ICC A all-risks insurance) to a named destination; risk passes at origin.
- **DAP / DPU / DDP** — delivered at place / unloaded / duty-paid; seller bears risk to destination (DDP also pays import duty + VAT).

## Sales / process terms
- **RFQ / RFP / RFI** — Request for Quote (price-led) / Proposal (solution-led) / Information (intel).
- **Spot vs contract rate** — live market rate (short validity) vs a fixed-term agreed rate.
- **Pipeline / coverage / velocity** — open deals; weighted pipeline ÷ quota; (opps × value × win-rate) ÷ cycle length.
- **QBR** — Quarterly Business Review (customer-outcome-led account review).
- **Whitespace** — services/lanes/modes a customer buys elsewhere that you could win.
- **Give-get** — never concede price without getting something (volume, term, scope, mode shift).
