# Incoterms 2020 — full responsibility matrix

> On-demand resource for the `incoterms-2020` skill. **S** = seller's obligation/cost, **B** = buyer's. Risk-transfer point is summarized in the last column. This is the standard interpretation of the ICC Incoterms® 2020 rules; for a binding contractual question, cite the ICC text and flag legal review.

| Obligation | EXW | FCA | FAS | FOB | CPT | CFR | CIP | CIF | DAP | DPU | DDP |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Export packing & marking | S | S | S | S | S | S | S | S | S | S | S |
| Loading at origin | B | S* | S | S | S | S | S | S | S | S | S |
| Origin inland haulage | B | S | S | S | S | S | S | S | S | S | S |
| Export customs clearance | B | S | S | S | S | S | S | S | S | S | S |
| Origin terminal/port charges | B | B | S | S | S | S | S | S | S | S | S |
| Main carriage (freight) | B | B | B | B | **S** | **S** | **S** | **S** | S | S | S |
| Cargo insurance | B | B | B | B | B | B | **S (ICC A)** | **S (ICC C)** | B | B | B |
| Destination terminal charges | B | B | B | B | B | B | B | B | S | S | S |
| Destination unloading | B | B | B | B | B | B | B | B | B | **S** | B |
| Import customs clearance | B | B | B | B | B | B | B | B | B | B | **S** |
| Import duty & VAT | B | B | B | B | B | B | B | B | B | B | **S** |
| **Risk transfers at** | seller's premises | hand-over to carrier | alongside ship | on board | 1st carrier (origin) | on board (origin) | 1st carrier (origin) | on board (origin) | destination | dest, unloaded | destination |

\* FCA loading: if delivery is at the seller's premises, the seller loads; if at another named place, the seller delivers ready for unloading (does not unload).

**Reading the matrix for a quote:**
- The **freight** row tells you whether the seller (C/D-terms) or buyer (E/F-terms) is paying the main carriage + its surcharges — i.e., whose quote this is.
- The **destination terminal** row is the DTHC dispute settler.
- The **import duty & VAT** row is why DDP is heavy: only DDP puts it on the seller.
- The **insurance** row is the CIP-vs-CIF gotcha: both require seller insurance, but at different cover levels.
