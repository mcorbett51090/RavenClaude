# Restaurant KPI glossary

The metrics a four-wall P&L is judged on — formulas, the misreads, and the cited, dated benchmark bands they read against. Every benchmark below carries a source + retrieval date; treat any specific number as a **dated, segment-dependent rule of thumb** and validate against the unit's actual P&L before any deliverable (§3 #8).

## Cost metrics

| Metric | Formula | The misread it prevents |
|---|---|---|
| **Prime cost** (the master number, §3 #1) | (food cost + labor cost) ÷ revenue | Reading food or labor alone and fixing the wrong half. Prime cost is the one figure that runs the business. |
| **Food cost %** | COGS ÷ food sales | Judging it against last month instead of the recipe-derived **theoretical** (§3 #2) — the gap is waste, theft, portioning, or price. |
| **Labor cost %** | total labor ÷ sales | Reading a blended weekly % that averages away an overstaffed lunch and an understaffed dinner (§3 #4). Read it **by daypart**. |
| **Theoretical vs actual food cost** | recipe-derived ideal vs actual COGS | The variance, not the invoice total, is where the money is (§3 #2). |

### Benchmark bands (dated rules of thumb — `[verify-at-use]`)

| Metric | QSR | Full-service | Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| Prime cost | ~55–60% of sales | ~60–65% of sales | 7shifts *Prime Cost Guide* |
| Food cost (COGS) | ~28–32% | ~28–35% (industry avg ~32.4%) | VantaInsights; 7shifts |
| Labor cost | ~30–32% of sales | ~36–40% (median ~36.5%; profitable ops ~34.2%) | Toast *Payroll Percentage*; Restaurant Warehouse |

> Context: payroll now exceeds a quarter of restaurant expenses, up from ~23% of revenue in 2021 — labor pressure is structural, not a one-off (Toast, retrieved 2026-06-05). `[verify-at-use]` — re-check the band each engagement; post-2020 cost structure moved sharply (§3 #8).

## Sales & throughput metrics

| Metric | Formula | Note |
|---|---|---|
| **Covers** | guests served in a window | The volume denominator for most per-guest reads. |
| **Average check** | sales ÷ covers (or ÷ checks) | Rises via mix and upsell, not only price; pair with the menu matrix (§3 #3). |
| **Sales per labor hour (SPLH)** | sales ÷ labor hours worked | Read **per daypart**, not blended — it exposes the shift that's actually broken (§3 #4). |
| **Table turns** (turnover) | parties seated ÷ tables, per service | Throughput lever for full-service; a peak bottleneck is often one station, not total headcount. |
| **RevPASH** (revenue per available seat hour) | revenue ÷ (seats × hours open) | Blends check size **and** seat utilization into one number — a high check on empty seats and a low check on full seats both read low. |
| **Comps / voids / waste rates** | comped/voided/wasted ÷ sales | A control system, not noise (§3 #6) — track the rate **and** who authorizes it. |

### Throughput / utilization (illustrative — `[verify-at-use]`)

- **RevPASH worked example:** $20,000 day ÷ (50 seats × 10 hours) = **$40 RevPASH** (Marketing4Restaurants / TouchBistro, retrieved 2026-06-05). The *level* is segment-specific — track the trend and the daypart spread, not a single absolute.

## Profit metrics

| Metric | Formula | Note |
|---|---|---|
| **Contribution margin per item** | menu price − plate (recipe) cost | Engineer on **dollars** of CM, not food-cost % — a 38%-food-cost entrée can out-earn a 22%-food-cost side (§3 #5). |
| **Menu-mix popularity threshold** | 0.70 × (1 ÷ number of items) | The Kasavana-Smith bar: an item is "popular" if its mix share clears 70% of even share. The quadrant flips on it. |
| **Four-wall EBITDA** | unit-level earnings before corporate overhead/D&A | The number a turnaround moves; rank multi-unit stores on it normalized for format/daypart (§3 #7). |
