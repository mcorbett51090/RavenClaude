# S&OP reconciles demand and supply every single month

**Status:** Pattern
**Domain:** S&OP / IBP process
**Applies to:** `supply-chain-planning`

---

## Why this exists

The S&OP cycle is not a nice-to-have. It is the process by which functional plans (sales forecast,
production plan, procurement plan) are converted into a single agreed operating plan. Without it:

- Sales runs one number, supply runs another, finance runs a third — and no one owns the gap.
- Supply-demand imbalances are discovered at shipment time, not 4–8 weeks upstream when they
  could still be resolved.
- Firefighting replaces planning, and expediting becomes the operating model.

The value of S&OP is proportional to the discipline with which it runs. A cycle skipped in a
"busy month" is skipped at the worst possible time — volatility and urgency are precisely when
cross-functional reconciliation is most valuable.

A real S&OP has **pre-work**: a demand plan, a supply plan, a gap list, and a financial view exist
before any meeting. A standing monthly meeting with slides built in the room is a status update,
not an S&OP.

## How to apply

- Fix the five-step gate calendar and treat it as non-negotiable: product review → demand review →
  supply review → pre-S&OP → executive S&OP.
- The demand plan and supply plan are **produced before** the meeting, not in it.
- The executive S&OP closes with a **decision record**: the approved scenario, action owners, and
  the triggers that would require an out-of-cycle review.
- Track the S&OP KPIs: forecast accuracy, plan attainment, on-time meeting completion.

**Do:**

- Publish the 13-month rolling S&OP calendar at the start of the year. The dates do not move.
- Require pre-work: demand plan + supply plan + gap list before any gate meeting.
- Close every executive S&OP with a written, distributed decision record.
- Measure plan attainment (actual vs. approved S&OP plan) as the primary process health metric.

**Don't:**

- Skip the cycle in "busy" or "uncertain" months — that is when it is most needed.
- Combine the pre-S&OP and executive S&OP into a single meeting — detail that belongs in
  pre-S&OP wastes executive time.
- Run the S&OP without a decision record — a meeting without decisions is reporting, not planning.
- Declare S&OP "too mature to need a cycle" — the cycle is what keeps alignment alive.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

For very small operations (< 3 product families, < 10 SKUs, single site), a formal five-step cycle
may be over-engineered. A simplified two-step weekly demand-supply review is the minimum viable
equivalent — the principle (one number, reconciled) applies; the ceremony is proportionate to size.
