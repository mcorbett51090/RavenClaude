# Build topic clusters, not orphan posts.

**Status:** Pattern. **Constitution:** §3 #4, §3 #7, §4.

## Use when
Any marketing operations deliverable involving content architecture, internal linking, or an SEO/AI-answer content plan — read, applied, and cited whole.

## The rule
Organize content as pillar pages with cluster pages that link bidirectionally — every page reachable from, and linking to, the rest of its cluster. Author the pillar first for a high-value topic, fill cluster gaps by funnel stage, refresh decaying pages before writing net-new, and ship no orphan page. An unlinked page is invisible to crawlers and readers alike.

## Why it matters
This is house opinion §3 #7 (integrity precedes the work) and §3 #4 (outcome over activity) applied to content structure. Practitioners act on these deliverables, so a framing error here is not academic — it sends real operating decisions the wrong way. Topical clusters signal authority and keep content discoverable; orphan posts dilute it.

## How to apply
- Apply this **before** publishing — it sets the framing, not the conclusion.
- Wire bidirectional pillar ↔ cluster internal links for every page.
- Prefer refresh/consolidation of decaying pages over another orphan one-off (§3 #7).
- Cite a source + date for any external benchmark, or mark it `[unverified — training knowledge]` (§3 #8).
- When this rule and another both apply, route to [`marketing-ops-lead`](../agents/marketing-ops-lead.md) to sequence them.
- Keep customer/lead PII out of the deliverable; route professional/legal determinations to the qualified authority (§2).
- Close with a recommendation that has an owner, a date, and an expected metric movement.

## The anti-pattern this prevents
The §4 failure mode: publishing standalone posts with no pillar, no cluster, and no internal links — content that cannot be found and cannot compound. The plugin's advisory hook flags a deliverable that reads as if this rule were ignored.
