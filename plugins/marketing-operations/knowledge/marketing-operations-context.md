# Marketing Operations Benchmarks & Context (2025–2026)

> Orientation for the team. **Every figure and regulatory statement here is `[unverified — training knowledge]`** and varies by geography, segment, and date. ## Where defensible benchmarks come from

Conversion-rate, CAC, and channel-ROI benchmarks are **segment-, ACV-, motion-, and date-specific** and move year to year. Practitioners cite industry demand-gen benchmark reports and comp/CAC surveys, but the most defensible source is the org's own trailing CRM + marketing-automation history. **Name the source and date, or mark the figure `[unverified — training knowledge]` (§3 #8).**

## Directional frames (illustrative only — `[unverified — training knowledge]`)

| Area | Directional frame | Must-verify |
|---|---|---|
| LTV:CAC health | Often cited around 3:1; below ~1:1 is unsustainable | Derive from the org's own gross-margin LTV and fully-loaded CAC |
| CAC-payback | Frequently targeted under ~12 months for B2B SaaS | Compute from monthly gross-margin contribution |
| MQL→SQL rate | Diverges widely by source and scoring rigor | Segment from CRM history |
| Attribution model | No model is 'correct' — each answers a different question | State the model and reconcile across models |

## Operating rhythm

- **Funnel review** monthly: stage conversion + dwell, leaking-stage diagnosis (§3 #1).
- **Economics review** monthly/quarterly: LTV:CAC and payback by channel/segment (§3 #3).
- **Attribution reconciliation** quarterly: compare models, never report one model silently (§3 #2).
- **Data-hygiene audit** continuous: dedup + UTM integrity before any analysis (§3 #7).

## The standing caution

Privacy law (GDPR/CCPA/consent), customer-contract terms, and revenue recognition are **the qualified authority's** call — the team frames the decision and routes it. Keep customer/lead PII out of deliverables; never store named contacts or behavioral records in artifacts (§2).
