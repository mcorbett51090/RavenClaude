# Pre-wire every steering committee before the formal meeting

**Status:** Absolute rule
**Domain:** Stakeholder governance
**Applies to:** `project-management`

---

## Why this exists

A steering committee meeting that introduces a significant decision, a schedule slip, or a contentious change request cold — with no prior individual conversation — almost always fails to produce a decision. Committee members form views in real time, the sponsor is surprised, resistance is unmanaged, and the item gets "parked for further review." The pre-wire is the mechanism that turns the steering meeting from a debate forum into a decision-confirmation event. Decisions happen in the corridor; the meeting ratifies them.

## How to apply

For every item in a steering pack that requires a decision or contains a surprise (bad news, scope change, a red RAG for the first time), the PM has a 1:1 conversation with the sponsor — and with any other high-power / high-interest stakeholder who might block — at least 48 hours before the meeting.

**Pre-wire agenda (15–20 minutes per stakeholder):**

1. Walk the item briefly: "I want to share something that's going into the steering pack so it doesn't land cold."
2. State the situation and the PM's recommendation clearly and directly.
3. Listen for the reaction — surface objections now, not in the room.
4. Agree on how the item will be framed in the meeting: who speaks, what the ask is, whether the sponsor will lead or the PM will lead.
5. Confirm the decision required and the timing.

**Checklist: items that require a pre-wire:**

- [ ] First Red RAG of the project
- [ ] Any change request above a defined threshold (cost or schedule impact)
- [ ] A resource request or trade-off that affects another sponsor or workstream
- [ ] An escalation memo item (by definition — send the memo, then pre-wire)
- [ ] A scope descoping proposal
- [ ] A vendor or contract issue with commercial sensitivity

**Do:**
- Pre-wire the sponsor first; then any other high-power stakeholders if the item crosses their domain.
- Take notes on the sponsor's concerns and reflect them in the pack narrative — the sponsor should be able to read the pack and see their view represented.
- Do not pre-wire to "lobby" — the goal is to surface resistance early so it can be addressed, not to manufacture consensus by excluding dissent.

**Don't:**
- Present a decision cold in a steering meeting that the sponsor is seeing for the first time.
- Pre-wire only the sponsor and ignore the high-power / high-interest members who can derail in the room.
- Skip the pre-wire because the meeting is "just a status update" — if the status is Red, there is no such thing as a routine meeting.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

Routine Green-status updates with no decisions, no significant risks to surface, and no surprises do not require a formal pre-wire. A brief "here's the pack, any questions?" message to the sponsor 48 hours out covers it. The rule is not bureaucracy for calm periods — it is a structural safeguard for high-stakes moments.
