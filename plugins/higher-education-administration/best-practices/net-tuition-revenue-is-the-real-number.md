# Net tuition revenue is the real number

**Status:** Absolute rule.

**Rule:** Headline applications, admits, and sticker price are vanity figures. The number that funds
the institution is **net tuition revenue** — gross tuition minus institutional aid/discount. Every
enrollment and aid decision is evaluated against net tuition revenue, not the headline.

## Why

An institution can grow applications, raise sticker price, and admit a larger class while net tuition
revenue *falls* — because the discount rate rose faster than gross. Boards and press celebrate the
vanity figures; the budget runs on net revenue. Decisions optimized for the headline routinely
destroy the number that actually pays salaries.

## What it looks like in practice

- Discount-rate decisions are modeled in net-revenue terms, with the diminishing-returns point named.
- Enrollment plans state the net-tuition-revenue outcome, not just a headcount or applications target.
- A yield gain bought with discount that lowers net revenue per student is reported as the loss it is.

## Anti-pattern

Reporting a record applicant pool or a larger admitted class as success while net tuition revenue is
flat or down. The hook flags aid/discount decisions stated with no net-revenue basis.
