# Close the assessment loop, or it doesn't count

**Status:** Pattern.

**Rule:** Outcomes assessment is only real if the loop closes: define outcomes → measure → analyze →
**act on what you found** → re-measure. Assessment that measures but never changes anything is the
single most common accreditation finding — and a waste of everyone's effort.

## Why

The purpose of assessment is improvement, not documentation. An institution can collect rubric scores
and survey data every term and still fail an accreditor's expectation because it never used the
results to change a course, a curriculum, or a support service. The "act" step is where assessment
earns its cost; without it, the whole cycle is theater that consumes faculty time and produces
binders nobody reads. Reviewers look specifically for evidence that findings drove change.

## What it looks like in practice

- Each assessment cycle names a concrete change made in response to the prior cycle's findings.
- Re-measurement checks whether the change worked, closing the loop.
- The standards-to-evidence map includes the "closed-loop" artifact (what changed, and the result).

## Anti-pattern

Years of measurement with no documented action — a loop that collects but never closes. The hook flags
student-data handling without a FERPA note; this rule adds that an assessment program with no "act"
step does not satisfy the purpose of assessment, however much data it gathers.
