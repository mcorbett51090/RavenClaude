# Measure cohorts, not snapshots

**Status:** Pattern.

**Rule:** Retention, persistence, and completion are cohort phenomena. A point-in-time headcount
hides the persistence story. Always frame student-success metrics by entering cohort.

## Why

Total enrollment can hold steady while a cohort hemorrhages, because new students mask the loss of
returning ones. "Enrollment is flat" and "we're losing a third of each entering class" can both be
true at once. Only cohort framing — of the students who started together, how many are still here at
each milestone — reveals the persistence reality that drives net revenue and completion.

## What it looks like in practice

- Retention is reported as year-1→year-2 (and onward) persistence by entering cohort, not as a
  headcount delta.
- Segments (academic preparation, first-gen, need, residential vs. commuter) are tracked within
  cohorts so the highest-attrition group is visible.
- Completion is a cohort outcome (e.g., 4-/6-year graduation rate of an entering class), never a
  snapshot.

## Anti-pattern

Citing stable total enrollment as evidence of healthy retention. The hook flags retention/persistence
claims stated with no cohort or persistence basis.
