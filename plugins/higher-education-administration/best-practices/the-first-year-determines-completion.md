# The first year determines completion

**Status:** Pattern.

**Rule:** Most attrition happens between year one and year two. First-year experience, early-alert,
and advising are the highest-leverage retention investments — and the place to look first when
completion rates lag.

## Why

A student who persists into the second year is far more likely to complete; the steepest part of the
attrition curve is the year-1→year-2 transition. Interventions there protect the entire remaining
enrollment, while interventions in later years touch a smaller, already-self-selected population. The
first year is where belonging, academic momentum, and financial fit are still malleable.

## What it looks like in practice

- Retention investment is sequenced first-year-first: gateway-course support, structured advising
  touchpoints, and belonging interventions.
- Early-alert signals are tuned to catch first-year disengagement early (attendance, LMS, midterm
  grades) while recovery is still possible.
- Completion analysis starts by decomposing the year-1→year-2 drop by entering-cohort segment.

## Anti-pattern

Pouring student-success resources into junior/senior retention while the year-1→year-2 cliff goes
unaddressed — intervening after most of the loss has already happened.
