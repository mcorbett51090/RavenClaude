# Retention is an early-alert problem

**Status:** Absolute rule
**Domain:** Student success / retention & persistence
**Applies to:** `higher-education-administration`

> Advisory operations rule. Retention/persistence metrics are defined differently by IPEDS/accreditor/IR — `[verify-at-use]`. FERPA-aware: cohorts and risk segments, never a named student's record.

---

## Why this exists

Retention is a lagging number. By the time it shows up in the fall-to-fall report, the students are already gone and the intervention window closed months earlier. The students who don't come back rarely decide it at the withdrawal desk — they drift, on signals that were visible in the first weeks: missed early classes, a failing gateway course at the first gradebook, an unmet aid gap, a registration hold. The leverage is entirely on the leading edge. An operation that waits for the lagging metric is grading its own homework too late to change the score.

## How to apply

- Instrument the **leading indicators** — first-weeks attendance/engagement, first-gradebook signals, aid gaps, holds — and work them in the first weeks.
- **Triage by leverage, not by alarm:** on-the-edge-and-saveable gets the first touch; a flag with no cause gets monitored, not spent on.
- Read a recurring at-risk cluster as a **process signal** (a gateway course, an onboarding gap, an aid gap), not a run of individual bad luck.
- Attach the **definition** (cohort, term, source) to every retention/persistence number before comparing it (`[verify-at-use]`).

**Do:** work the leading indicators in the first weeks; fix the process behind a cluster.
**Don't:** intervene at the withdrawal desk; quote a retention number with no definition.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

Some attrition is healthy and unpreventable (genuine transfer-up, life circumstances). The rule is about *catching the preventable early*, not driving attrition to zero.
