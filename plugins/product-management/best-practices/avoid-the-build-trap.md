# Escape the build trap — measure value delivered, not output produced

The build trap is the organization-wide failure mode where success is defined as shipping features and being busy, so velocity goes up while the metrics that represent customer and business value flatline. It is seductive because output is visible and immediate while outcomes are lagging and uncomfortable to confront, and because roadmaps full of dated features feel like progress. The escape is structural, not just attitudinal: tie funding and review to outcomes rather than feature completion, make every initiative name the metric it must move, and hold teams accountable to that metric instead of to the ship date. A team that can't tell you what changed for the customer after a quarter of shipping is in the trap, however productive it looks.
