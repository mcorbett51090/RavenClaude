# Map the opportunity-solution tree before committing to a solution

Anchor discovery on a single desired outcome, branch it into the distinct customer opportunities (unmet needs, pains, desires surfaced in interviews) that could move it, and only then branch each opportunity into candidate solutions and the experiments that test them. The tree makes the logic from outcome to feature explicit and arguable, and it forces the question most teams skip: which opportunity are we even betting on, and is it the highest-value one? Jumping straight from outcome to a favorite solution hides the opportunity you never considered and the alternatives you never weighed. Keep the tree alive as a living artifact — discovery keeps adding opportunities and pruning solutions — so the roadmap traces back to a real customer need rather than a stakeholder's hunch.
