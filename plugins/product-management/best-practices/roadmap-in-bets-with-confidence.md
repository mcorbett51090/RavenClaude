# Frame the roadmap as bets with confidence, not dated features

A roadmap of features with quarterly dates promises a precision you don't have and quietly converts a problem-solving organization into a delivery factory grading itself on shipped-on-time. Frame it instead as bets — problems to solve and outcomes to move — each carrying an honest confidence level: near-term bets are validated and specific, later ones are directional and deliberately fuzzy. This communicates intent without manufacturing false certainty, and it leaves the solution space open so the team can find a better answer than today's guess. Dates belong to delivery, which is project-management's lane; the product roadmap's currency is what we're betting on and why, with how-sure-we-are stated rather than hidden behind a Gantt bar.
