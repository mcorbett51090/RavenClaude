# Say no, and kill features that aren't earning their keep

A product is defined as much by what it refuses to do as by what it ships, and the hardest, most valuable product work is declining good ideas because they're not the best use of the next quarter. Every yes spends a team's time and adds surface area to maintain forever, so the default answer to a feature request is 'not unless it beats what's already prioritized.' Just as important: sunset features that aren't used or aren't earning their maintenance cost — a graveyard of half-used features is complexity tax the whole team and every user pays. Saying no protects focus; killing features protects the product from the slow death of accretion. Both require the courage to disappoint a stakeholder in service of the outcome.
