## 6. Emergency Escalation Ladder

```
Step 1: Check in-territory qualified tech → available within SLA window?
        YES → dispatch; confirm ETA with customer.
        NO  ↓
Step 2: Check adjacent-zone qualified tech → can reach within window?
        YES → dispatch with overtime authorization; notify customer of tech + ETA.
        NO  ↓
Step 3: Check approved subcontractors → pre-vetted sub in range with required skill?
        YES → dispatch sub; confirm insurance/liability compliance.
        NO  ↓
Step 4: Notify customer of SLA miss; provide best available ETA.
        Escalate to service manager. Log as SLA-miss event for weekly review.
```

## 7. Pre-Dispatch Parts-Readiness Check

For jobs requiring non-universal-carry parts:

- [ ] Parts required for this job identified from job type + equipment model
- [ ] Parts confirmed on technician's truck OR at staging depot
- [ ] If parts pending: ordered from supplier with confirmed delivery before dispatch
- [ ] Dispatcher logs any job dispatched without confirmed parts as a process exception
