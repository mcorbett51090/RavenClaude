# Item 19 is a disclosure, not a projection

**Rule.** Read a Financial Performance Representation for its scope, cohort, and exclusions — never as
the return your unit will earn. It's an *optional*, *selected* slice the franchisor chose to disclose.

**Why.** Item 19 figures are frequently survivorship-biased (the cohort excludes units that closed),
report averages that a few top units inflate, and mix gross with net. A headline AUV tells you almost
nothing about *your* unit in *your* market.

**Anti-pattern it kills.** Underwriting a franchise on "the average unit does \$1.2M" without checking
that the cohort is "franchised units open ≥3 years" — i.e. the ones that survived.

**In practice.** Read the cohort definition, average-vs-median, and footnotes; if there's no Item 19,
treat the absence as a signal; always build a bottom-up model and use Item 19 only as a sanity band.
