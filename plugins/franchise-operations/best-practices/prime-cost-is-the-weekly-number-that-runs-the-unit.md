# Prime cost is the weekly number that runs the unit

**Rule.** Track prime cost — COGS % + labor % of revenue — weekly, per unit, against the concept's
benchmark. It's the controllable margin, and a portfolio monthly average hides the one unit bleeding out.

**Why.** Most unit-level margin problems are prime-cost problems, and they compound fast: a labor line
run 5 points high for a month is real money gone. Weekly, per-unit visibility catches it while it's
fixable; monthly aggregate catches it after the quarter's lost.

**Anti-pattern it kills.** "The portfolio's prime cost is fine at 60%" while unit 4 sits at 68% and is
the reason the whole group's profit is soft.

**In practice.** Weekly prime-cost report per unit vs benchmark; labor scheduled against a sales forecast,
not a hope; COGS variance investigated (waste, theft, portioning, price) — not just noted.
