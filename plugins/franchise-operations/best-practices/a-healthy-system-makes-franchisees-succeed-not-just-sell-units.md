# A healthy system makes franchisees succeed, not just sell units

**Rule.** Judge (and run) a franchise system by franchisee unit-level success, not by unit-sales pace.
The durable value is profitable units compounding into royalty; a franchisor growing on franchise fees
while units close is eating its own system.

**Why.** Royalty is a share of franchisee revenue — it only compounds if units survive and grow. Item 20
churn, franchisee validation calls, and closure rates reveal whether a system creates success or just
sells the dream.

**Anti-pattern it kills.** Being wooed by a franchisor's rapid unit growth without asking how many of
those units are still open and profitable two years later.

**In practice.** Weight validation calls with existing franchisees and Item 20 churn heavily; for a
franchisor, measure system health by franchisee profitability, not just development pipeline.
