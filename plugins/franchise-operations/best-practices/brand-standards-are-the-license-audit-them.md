# Brand standards are the license — audit them

**Rule.** Treat brand-standard compliance as a contract obligation you *inspect*, not one you assume. Run
a repeatable, weighted audit across units and coach to it; a critical miss is a license risk, escalated
immediately.

**Why.** The franchise agreement can be terminated for brand-standard failure, and cross-unit
consistency is literally what the customer is buying. Standards you don't audit drift — and drift in a
food-safety or brand-integrity item is a contract and reputation threat, not a cosmetic one.

**Anti-pattern it kills.** Assuming quality is fine because no one complained, until a franchisor
inspection or a viral incident surfaces standards that quietly eroded across locations.

**In practice.** A weighted checklist tied to the operations manual, scored the same way each period,
with a coach-and-re-audit loop and the score feeding the manager scorecard.
