# Model unit economics after royalty and ad-fund

**Rule.** Take the ongoing franchise fees — royalty, ad/brand fund, tech — off gross revenue *first*,
then layer COGS, labor, occupancy, and opex. The unit's profit is what's left after the fees, not before.

**Why.** Fees off the top are the step operators skip; a unit that pencils at a 12% pre-royalty margin
can be marginal after 8% of gross goes to the franchisor. The royalty-loaded model is the only honest one.

**Anti-pattern it kills.** A pro-forma that looks healthy because it forgot the 6% royalty and 2% ad fund
that come out before anything else.

**In practice.** Revenue → minus royalty + ad fund + recurring fees (verified from FDD Item 6) → then the
operating P&L → unit profit and break-even. Verify the exact percentages from the current FDD.
