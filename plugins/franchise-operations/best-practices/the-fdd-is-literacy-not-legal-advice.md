# The FDD is literacy, not legal advice

**Rule.** Explain what the FDD's Items mean for the *business decision* — fees, investment, territory,
churn, Item 19 scope. Do **not** opine on the enforceability of the franchise agreement, what to
negotiate, or what to sign. That is a franchise attorney's job → `legal-ops-clm`.

**Why.** The franchise agreement is a binding contract with term, termination, transfer, non-compete, and
dispute-venue clauses whose enforceability is jurisdiction-specific. Business literacy and legal advice
are different services; blurring them puts the franchisee at risk.

**Anti-pattern it kills.** Telling a prospective franchisee "that non-compete won't hold up" — an
unqualified legal opinion on a binding contract.

**In practice.** Produce the decision read + a specific list of clauses (Item 17 especially) to take to a
franchise attorney; route binding review to `legal-ops-clm`.
