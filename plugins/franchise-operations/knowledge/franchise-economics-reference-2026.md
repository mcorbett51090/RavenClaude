## Unit-economics concepts (stable)

- **Royalty-loaded unit P&L** — fees come off gross revenue *before* the operating P&L. Model after fees.
- **Prime cost** = COGS % + labor % of revenue — the controllable margin, watched weekly per unit.
- **Break-even revenue** = fixed cost ÷ contribution margin.
- **Ramp / working capital** — the months of negative cash before break-even; undercapitalization here kills units.
- **AUV (average unit volume)** — the brand's average; a band to sanity-check bottom-up revenue against, never the input.

## Multi-unit operating concepts (stable)

- **Manager scorecard** — sales, prime cost, brand-audit score, turnover, a guest metric — reviewed on a cadence. The span-of-control tool.
- **Unit-variance ranking** — same metrics, same period, ranked; manage the tails, not the average.
- **Brand-standard audit** — a contract obligation; failure is a termination risk.

## Directional benchmarks (measure your own — all `[verify-at-use]`)

- Prime-cost target varies by concept (e.g. QSR/full-service differ widely); track vs your concept's norm, not a universal number.
- Royalty rates commonly fall in a single-digit-percent-of-gross band, ad fund a low-single-digit band — **verify from the specific FDD**.
