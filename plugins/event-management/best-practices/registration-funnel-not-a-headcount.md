# Registration is a funnel, not a headcount

**Status:** Pattern
**Domain:** Event marketing / revenue
**Applies to:** `event-management`

---

## Why this exists

"We need 500 registrations" is a target, not a plan. The 500 come through stages — reach → page visit → register → confirm → attend — each with its own conversion rate. A single headcount hides *where* you're losing people, so you can't fix it. Modeling the funnel turns "we're short" into "the visit→register step is leaking."

## How to apply

```
reach → page visit → register → confirm → attend
```

- Track conversion at each stage; find the leaking one and fix that.
- Account for **attrition**: registered ≠ attended (no-show rates vary by format; free virtual is much higher).
- Work the promotion plan backward from the date to feed the top of the funnel at the rate the conversions require.

**Do:** instrument each stage (UTMs, platform analytics); optimize the weakest link.
**Don't:** chase more top-of-funnel reach when the leak is downstream.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

A tiny invite-only dinner is a list, not a funnel — track confirmations directly.
