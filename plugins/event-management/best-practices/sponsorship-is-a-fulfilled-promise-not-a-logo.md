# Sponsorship is a fulfilled promise, not a logo

**Status:** Absolute rule
**Domain:** Event marketing / revenue
**Applies to:** `event-management`

---

## Why this exists

A sponsor doesn't buy a logo placement — they buy an outcome (leads, exposure, thought-leadership). If the tier is defined as "logo size" and the deliverables are never tracked or proven, the sponsor can't see the value and doesn't renew. Sponsorship revenue is recurring only when each promise is delivered and the delivery is proven.

## How to apply

1. **Define tiers by delivered value** — leads/scans, placement surfaces, speaking slots, branded moments — not by logo size.
2. **Fulfillment checklist per sponsor** — every promised deliverable with an owner and a due date relative to the event.
3. **Capture proof** — lead CSV, booth/placement photos, recording, scan exports — the renewal conversation runs on evidence.

**Do:** sell the value, deliver the value, prove the value.
**Don't:** sell "a logo" and hope; don't let a promised deliverable go unfulfilled or unproven.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

A pure goodwill/community sponsor who explicitly wants only brand association still gets a fulfillment check that the association was actually placed.
