# Agriculture economics & precision-tech context (2025)

Dated per-acre economics and precision-tech adoption for a farm engagement. Figures carry a source + date; uncontrollable-risk framing is marked where it's training knowledge.

## Per-acre economics (2025)

University 2025 budgets put corn production cost ~$909/acre (~$4.33/bu at 210 bu/acre) and soybeans ~$662/acre (~$11.03/bu at 60 bu/acre); all-in breakevens incl. cash rent run ~$4.60–$4.66/bu corn and ~$11.01–$11.56/bu soybeans. With 2025 harvest prices, central-Illinois high-productivity returns were projected negative (~-$73/acre corn, ~-$50/acre soybeans) — manage to economic optimum, not max yield (§3 #1).

## Precision-tech ROI & adoption

The precision-farming market was ~$14.18B in 2025 (remote-sensing + variable-rate ~58% share). Soil-sensor-guided nitrogen cuts over-application ~15–25% (~$35–$75/acre); AI variable-rate seeding cuts seed cost ~8–12%. ~25% of farmers globally use precision tech; ~42% of large NA/EU farms used AI crop advisory in 2025 (up from ~18% in 2021) — read yield by zone and vary inputs (§3 #2).

## Risk

Weather and price are the dominant, largely-uncontrollable risks; the controllable share is input timing, hybrid selection, and marketing (§3 #7). Date and source any figure (§3 #8).
