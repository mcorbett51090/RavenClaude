# The assurance level shapes the work, from the start

Limited and reasonable assurance are different engagements with different evidence depth, control reliance, and conclusion form — limited returns a negative conclusion ("nothing came to our attention") weighted toward analytics and inquiry; reasonable returns a positive conclusion ("in our opinion, fairly stated") and demands substantive testing and controls reliance. Know the target level *before* drafting, because the evidence trail is built to the bar, not retrofitted to it. Building a limited-assurance trail and then being asked for reasonable means reconstructing evidence under audit pressure; building reasonable-grade evidence for a limited engagement wastes effort. Confirm the target level (and the regime's trajectory — some start at limited and escalate) at scoping, and size the controls and the evidence depth to it.
