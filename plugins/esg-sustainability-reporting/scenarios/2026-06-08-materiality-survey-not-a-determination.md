## Problem

A company entering its first CSRD reporting cycle had "done its materiality assessment" — a stakeholder survey ranking sustainability topics by perceived importance — and had already started drafting disclosures and building a GHG inventory against the top-ranked topics. Their assurance provider's readiness review flagged that the assessment wasn't a double-materiality determination: it captured perceived importance but never assessed impact materiality and financial materiality as distinct axes, had no governance sign-off, and held no evidence per topic. Worse, the inventory boundary had been set before the boundary question was actually decided, so some material value-chain emissions were out of scope.

## Constraints context

- First CSRD cycle; double materiality required by ESRS.
- A survey already run and socialized internally as "the materiality assessment."
- Drafting and inventory work already underway against the survey's ranking.

## Attempts

- Tried: keep the survey and add a financial-materiality column retrospectively. Partially helped but lacked governance and evidence, so it still read as opinion, not determination.
- Tried: re-run the determination properly — assess impact materiality (effect on people/planet) and financial materiality (effect on the company) as separate axes, route the result through a governance body for sign-off, and record the evidence behind each material topic mapped to its ESRS clause. This held up.
- Tried: re-fix the reporting boundary (consolidation approach + value-chain scope) *before* finalizing the inventory, then re-scope the GHG work to match. This pulled previously-omitted material categories back in.

## Resolution

The materiality determination was redone as a governed double-materiality assessment with per-topic evidence and clause mapping; the boundary was fixed first and the inventory re-scoped to it. The drafting that had run ahead was reconciled to the corrected material-topic list. The readiness review then passed because each material topic could be traced to a test, a governance decision, and evidence.

## Lesson

Materiality is a determination, not a vibe — and scope before you calculate. A stakeholder survey is one input, not a double-materiality assessment; name the test, govern it, evidence each topic, and fix the boundary before building the inventory or drafting. Running calculation and drafting ahead of a real determination just means redoing both.
