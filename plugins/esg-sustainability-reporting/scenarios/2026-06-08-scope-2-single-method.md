## Problem

A manufacturer reported a single Scope 2 figure that had quietly dropped year-on-year, headlined as a "30% reduction in electricity emissions." The number used a market-based method reflecting newly purchased renewable energy certificates (RECs), but the report showed only that figure — no location-based number, and no description of the instruments. When the limited-assurance provider arrived, two problems surfaced: the grid (location-based) emissions had actually risen slightly, and some of the RECs didn't meet the Scope-2 market-based quality criteria, so they couldn't be claimed against the relevant load.

## Constraints context

- Operations across three grids with very different carbon intensities.
- A mix of bundled green-tariff supply, unbundled RECs, and a small PPA — varying instrument quality.
- The prior report had set a reduction narrative leadership had already communicated to an insurer.

## Attempts

- Tried: keep the single market-based figure and add a footnote. Rejected by the assurer — the dual-reporting requirement isn't satisfied by a footnote, and the location-based trend was material to understanding the physical position.
- Tried: re-source better emission factors for the location-based calculation, with named source and vintage per grid region. This fixed the traceability gap but exposed that the headline reduction was almost entirely contractual, not physical.
- Tried: screen every market-based instrument against the Scope-2 market-based quality criteria; the instruments that failed were moved back to the grid (location-based) factor, and only the qualifying ones supported the market-based figure. This worked.

## Resolution

The report moved to true dual reporting: a location-based figure (grid-average factors, sourced and vintaged per region) and a market-based figure (only quality-screened instruments, named). The "30% reduction" claim was rewritten to distinguish the contractual market-based improvement from the flat-to-rising physical/location-based position. The assurer could then trace both numbers, and the rewritten claim survived the engagement.

## Lesson

Dual-report Scope 2 — never silently pick one method. Report both location-based and market-based, name and quality-screen the contractual instruments behind the market-based figure, and source every emission factor with its vintage. A single market-based number with a reduction headline hides the physical reality and won't survive assurance.
