## Problem

A company drafted its full sustainability statement and built the GHG inventory assuming limited assurance, weighting the evidence toward analytics and management inquiry rather than substantive document trails. Late in the cycle the audit committee, anticipating the regime's trajectory and investor pressure, asked the provider to perform reasonable assurance on the GHG figures. The drafting team discovered the evidence trail had been built to the wrong bar: many Scope 1 and Scope 3 lines had a calculation and a number but no retained source documents, no control over how activity data was extracted, and no second-person review evidencing the figure. Reasonable assurance needs substantive testing and controls reliance, and there was nothing to test or rely on.

## Constraints context

- A CSRD reporting cycle where the assurance regime was widely expected to escalate from limited toward reasonable over time.
- A target assurance level never explicitly confirmed at scoping — the team defaulted to limited because it was less work.
- A fixed external reporting deadline that left little room to reconstruct evidence under pressure.

## Attempts

- Tried: ask the provider to accept the limited-assurance trail for a reasonable engagement. Rejected — the conclusion form is different (positive vs negative), and reasonable assurance can't be given on figures with no testable evidence and no controls to rely on.
- Tried: reconstruct source documents for the largest lines retroactively, under deadline. Partially worked for a few high-materiality lines but was slow, error-prone, and couldn't manufacture the *controls* evidence (the review/approval steps had never happened, so there was no contemporaneous record to retrieve).
- Tried: agree a phased path with the audit committee and provider — confirm the target level explicitly, take reasonable assurance on the most material and best-evidenced scopes this cycle, keep the rest at limited, and design the controls so the full reasonable scope is reachable next cycle without a rebuild. This held.

## Resolution

The target assurance level was set explicitly going forward, and the evidence trail and controls were designed to that bar from the start of the next cycle: source-document retention, a controlled activity-data extraction, and an evidenced second-person review on each material line. For the current cycle, reasonable assurance was scoped to the figures whose evidence could carry it, with the remainder at limited and a documented plan to close the gap. The lesson the audit committee took: confirm the bar before drafting, not after.

## Lesson

The assurance level shapes the work, from the start. Limited and reasonable are different engagements with different evidence depth, control reliance, and conclusion form — confirm the target level (and the regime's trajectory, since some start at limited and escalate) at scoping, and build the evidence trail and controls to that bar. Retrofitting a reasonable-assurance trail under deadline pressure is slow, partial, and can't recreate the controls evidence that never happened.
