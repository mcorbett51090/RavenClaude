# Prevention beats detection beats scrap

Quality designed in (an FMEA or control plan that removes the failure mode, a poka-yoke that makes the defect impossible) beats quality detected at the source, which beats catching defects at the final-inspection dock and scrapping them. Final inspection is the weakest control: it's expensive, late, and leaky. Push quality upstream — prevent the cause, or detect it where it happens — and treat final-inspection-as-strategy as a smell, not a plan.
