# Safety stock is a decision, not a default

Safety stock sized "to feel safe" is either dead capital or a recurring stockout — usually both, on different parts. The right size is a decision against stated inputs: demand variability, lead-time variability, the replenishment cycle, and a chosen service level (the z-multiplier that buys it). A part with stable demand and a short, reliable lead time needs almost none; a volatile part behind a long, unreliable lead time needs real cover. Size it to the variability and the service-level target, state those inputs, and route the deeper lead-time/supplier-reliability question to `procurement-sourcing` and the forecasting/safety-stock math rigor to `applied-statistics`. A buffer with no stated service level is a number nobody can defend.
