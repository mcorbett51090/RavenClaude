# Plan to the constraint, not to infinite capacity

A master schedule that loads work beyond the bottleneck's finite rate, or assumes material that isn't available against an as-built BOM, is a fiction that fails on the floor. Plan to finite capacity: load the constraint to its real sustainable rate, subordinate the rest, and reconcile the demand plan against supply in S&OP before releasing the MPS. Infinite-capacity planning is the default failure mode — it produces promises the plant can't keep.
