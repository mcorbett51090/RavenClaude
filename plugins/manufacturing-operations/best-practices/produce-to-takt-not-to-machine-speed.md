# Produce to takt, not to machine speed

Takt time (available time ÷ customer demand) is the rate the line should run; cycle time is what it actually does. Building faster than takt to 'keep the machines busy' makes inventory, not money — over-production is the waste that buries every other problem in WIP. Match the line's output to takt, level the schedule to it, and treat the gap between takt and cycle time as the diagnostic signal, not the machine's top speed.
