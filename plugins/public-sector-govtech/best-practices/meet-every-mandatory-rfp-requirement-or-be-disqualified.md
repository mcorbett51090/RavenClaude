# Meet every mandatory RFP requirement — or be disqualified

**Status:** Absolute rule
**Domain:** Public procurement
**Applies to:** `public-sector-govtech`

---

## Why this exists

In government procurement, a solicitation's mandatory requirements (instructions marked "shall,"
"must," or "will" in the SOW, Section L, or Section M) are not negotiable. An evaluator who finds
a single unmet mandatory requirement is typically required to mark the proposal non-responsive and
eliminate it from further consideration — regardless of how strong the rest of the response is.
This is not a matter of poor evaluator judgment; it is a legally defensible protection against
post-award protests. The rule is enforced at the Source Selection Authority level and reviewed
by GAO and the Court of Federal Claims on protest.

No amount of technical brilliance, competitive price, or strong past performance can rescue a
proposal that does not comply with a mandatory requirement. The cost of discovering a gap at Red
Team is a missed deadline. The cost of discovering it after submission is disqualification.

## How to apply

1. **Build the compliance matrix before writing prose.** Extract every "shall/must/will/required"
   from Sections L, M, C (SOW/PWS), and H. Map each to a proposal section and a response approach.
2. **Resolve every gap before the drafting kickoff.** Options: technical solution in-house, teaming
   partner, or a documented risk-acceptance decision by capture leadership. There is no fourth option.
3. **Check every amendment.** Amendments posted to SAM.gov supersede the base solicitation.
   A mandatory requirement added in Amendment 003 that you didn't read is still disqualifying.
4. **Use the Q&A period.** If a mandatory requirement is ambiguous, submit a question during the
   Q&A window. The agency's written answer becomes part of the solicitation.

**Do:**

- Read Sections L and M first — they are the laws of the response.
- Build the compliance matrix as the first deliverable, before any prose is written.
- Confirm you have the latest amendment set on the day of submission.
- Submit a question for any ambiguous mandatory requirement during the Q&A window.

**Don't:**

- Begin drafting prose before the compliance matrix is complete.
- Assume a mandatory requirement is "standard boilerplate" without verifying it applies.
- Submit a proposal with a known gap and hope the evaluator doesn't notice.
- Treat page limits, font sizes, and margin requirements as optional — they are mandatory too.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

- **Requests for Information (RFIs):** RFIs are market research tools, not procurements. There is no
  disqualification. Respond informatively; do not treat the RFI as a binding solicitation.
- **Sources Sought notices:** same as RFIs — no award, no disqualification.
- **Oral presentation components:** some agencies allow clarifications during orals that can cure
  minor technical deficiencies — but mandatory requirement gaps are typically not curable.

## Provenance

Codifies FAR 15.305(a) (evaluation of proposals) and the well-established GAO protest standard
that a proposal failing to conform to a material solicitation requirement is properly rejected as
technically unacceptable (see, e.g., GAO B-400601, Patriot Taxiway Industries, Inc., 2008).
