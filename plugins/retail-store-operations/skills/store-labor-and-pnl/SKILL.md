# Store Labor and P&L

## Start from the store P&L
Walk the line: sales → gross margin → labor % → controllable expense → four-wall contribution. Find the line that moved and size the dollar gap. Every recommendation must land on one of these lines; a tactic with no P&L line is unmeasurable and doesn't ship.

## Labor follows traffic, not a grid
Build the schedule against the conversion-weighted traffic curve. Identify the over-staffed dead hours (labor % waste) and the under-staffed peak (lost conversion). Name which you're trading on every change. Labor % over plan is usually a scheduling-shape problem before a headcount one — re-shape before cutting heads, and never cut peak labor to make a weekly number.

## Diagnose shrink to its leak
Shrink is not a fixed cost. Split it: operational (receiving, markdown, process error), theft (internal vs. external), vendor/admin (cost/count error). Most shrink is operational — rule out the cheap causes before cameras. Prescribe the cheapest counter-measure per bucket. Surveillance / employee-PII content routes to security-reviewer.

## Conversion is the other half of sales
Sales = traffic × conversion × basket. Protect conversion at peak; labor cuts that crater it are false economy invisible on the labor report.

## SOPs are the unit of consistency
Write opening/closing/receiving/cash/markdown-execution SOPs tight, name the owner, name the failure mode each step prevents. A SOP nobody owns is a wish.

## Output
A store-P&L-and-labor plan: the P&L walk and the line that moved, the schedule-to-traffic model with labor-% impact, the shrink diagnosis with per-bucket counter-measures, the SOP/conversion levers — each tied to a P&L line and quantified. Hand the dashboard build to `data-platform`, the online channel to `ecommerce-dtc`.
