# GMROI is the capital lens — turn alone doesn't prove earning

Sell-through and weeks-of-supply read the *flow*; GMROI (gross margin $ ÷ average inventory cost) reads the *capital*. They are not interchangeable: fast-turning, low-margin inventory can turn beautifully and still fail the GMROI test, because it never earns back its carrying cost. Read both lenses before defending a position. The 1.0 line is the floor — below it, the inventory does not earn its own cost and is trapped capital regardless of turn; thin GMROI (roughly 1–2) earns its keep but has no headroom, so name whether margin or turn is the binding constraint before adding more facings or buying deeper. Always value the denominator *at cost* and over the same window as the margin numerator — mixing retail and cost, or two different windows, silently breaks the ratio.
