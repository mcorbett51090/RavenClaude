# Omnichannel shares inventory, not the playbook

The store and the online channel share one pool of physical inventory — BOPIS, ship-from-store, and buy-online-return-in-store all draw the store's on-hand to serve online demand — but they do **not** share one economic playbook. A unit promised to an online order is not available to a walk-in, so the store's weeks-of-supply, safety stock, and allocation must net out the online claim on shared stock, or the store stocks out against a number that looked healthy in aggregate. Flag the shared-inventory seam explicitly on every store-SKU plan that backs online fulfillment. But the online channel's own economics — conversion, CAC, the fulfillment promise, ship-from-store cost-to-serve — route to `ecommerce-dtc`; this plugin owns the four-wall stock position, not the website's P&L. Solving an online-conversion or online-fulfillment problem with a store playbook is the anti-pattern.
