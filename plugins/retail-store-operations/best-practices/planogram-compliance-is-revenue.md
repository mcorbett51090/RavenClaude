# Planogram compliance is revenue

**Status:** Pattern
**Domain:** Merchandising execution, planogram management
**Applies to:** `retail-store-operations`

---

## Why this exists

A planogram is a revenue plan drawn on paper. When it is not executed:

1. **High-velocity SKUs may not occupy the best positions.** Eye-level, right-hand, and
   end-cap positions convert at 2–4× the rate of floor-level or side-wall positions. If a
   fast mover is in the wrong slot, those conversions are lost.
2. **Adjacencies designed for cross-sell don't work.** The planogram places complementary
   items next to each other deliberately. A non-compliant set breaks the cross-sell logic
   and reduces UPT.
3. **Out-of-stocks hide in the wrong slot.** When a SKU is displaced to a different location,
   the system may show "on-hand" while the designated slot is empty — creating phantom OOS
   from a planogram problem, not an inventory problem.
4. **Reorder signals break.** Replenishment systems tied to planogram shelf capacity will
   misfire when the product isn't in the assigned location.

A 54% planogram compliance score is not a visual-standards failure — it is a revenue leak
and an inventory accuracy problem simultaneously.

## How to apply

**Do:**

- Treat planogram compliance as a revenue KPI, not a housekeeping score.
- Conduct compliance audits immediately after a reset and at 4-week intervals.
- Root-cause non-compliance: reset not completed, wrong SKU received, fixture incompatibility,
  facing count wrong, adjacency change missed. Each has a different fix.
- Tie compliance accountability to the store director and department manager — not to a
  visual-standards team working in isolation.

**Don't:**

- Allow "we were close enough" — a slot with the wrong SKU in it has the same revenue impact
  as an empty slot.
- Audit compliance without feeding the results back to replenishment — a compliance problem
  can look like an inventory problem in the OOS data.
- Conduct a reset without confirming that the new SKUs are on-hand or confirmed in transit
  before the reset date.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

- **Planned deviations:** a manager may deliberately substitute a local/regional SKU or a
  promotional item in a planogram slot. This is a compliance exception, not a compliance failure
  — document it and set an expiry date.
- **Fixture unavailability:** if the required fixture hasn't arrived, a planogram can't be
  fully executed. Track fixture-constrained deviations separately from execution failures.

## Provenance

The link between planogram compliance and revenue is documented in retailer case studies and
space-management industry research (Category Management Association, NRF merchandising
publications). The "eye-level is buy-level" positioning principle and the revenue impact of
non-compliance are foundational to category management practice.
