# Every metric names its formula and its window

Sell-through, GMROI, weeks-of-supply, comp / same-store sales, labor %, conversion, shrink % — each is ambiguous until you state the numerator, the denominator, and the time window. "Sell-through is 40%" means nothing until you say 40% of units received over how many weeks; "comp is up 3%" hides whether the store base excludes new and closed doors. Define the metric before you defend a decision on it, and re-verify the definition against the consumer's own reporting system before quoting it — definitions drift between retailers, and a planogram, markdown, or buy decided on a mis-read metric inherits the error. When two people argue about a number, the disagreement is almost always a hidden definition gap (different window, different denominator), not a data gap — surface the formula first.
