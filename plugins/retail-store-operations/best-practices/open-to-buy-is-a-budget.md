# Open-to-buy is a budget you don't overspend

Open-to-buy (OTB) caps forward commitment: planned sales − planned markdowns + target ending inventory − (on-hand + on-order). Buying past OTB pre-commits a markdown — it clears margin the store never earned, which makes over-buying the most expensive mistake in retail. Compute OTB before approving a buy; if it's exhausted or negative, you're over-bought, so re-plan or clear before committing more. Vendor lead time and minimum-order-quantity feed the buy but don't override the cap — route those to procurement-sourcing.
