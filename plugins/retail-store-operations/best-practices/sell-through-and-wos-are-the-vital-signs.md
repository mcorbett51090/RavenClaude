# Sell-through and weeks-of-supply are the vital signs

Judge inventory health by sell-through %, weeks-of-supply (WOS), and GMROI — never by raw on-hand units. Raw units lie: 500 units is "high" or "low" only relative to the demand rate, which is exactly what WOS normalizes. GMROI answers the capital question ("does this inventory earn its carrying cost?"). Healthy inventory is the right flow, not the most stock. Always normalize to the demand rate before calling a position "over" or "under" stocked, and state the window — sell-through with no period is ambiguous.
