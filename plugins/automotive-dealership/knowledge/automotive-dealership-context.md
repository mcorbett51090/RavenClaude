## Where defensible benchmarks come from

Days-supply targets, PVR, absorption, grosses, and labor rates are **brand-, market-, and date-specific** and move with the cycle and OEM programs. Common reference sources practitioners cite require a dated cut: dealer 20-group composites, OEM financial statements, and the store's own DMS history (the most defensible source). **Name the source and date, or mark the figure `[unverified — training knowledge]` (§3 #8).**

## Directional frames (illustrative only — `[unverified — training knowledge]`)

| Area | Directional frame | Must-verify |
|---|---|---|
| New-vehicle days-supply | Often targeted around a low double-digit count, varies by brand | Derive from the store's own daily sales rate |
| Service absorption | A healthy store often cited near or above 100% | Compute from this store's fixed-ops gross and overhead |
| F&I PVR | Varies widely by store, region, and compliance posture | Use DMS history, dated |
| Total gross/unit | Front compresses; back-end carries more of the deal | Read front + back together from the DMS |

## Operating rhythm

- **Daily inventory / floorplan review** of days-supply and aged units (§3 #2).
- **Monthly absorption review** of fixed-ops gross vs overhead (§3 #5).
- **Desk/total-gross review** reading front + back per unit (§3 #3).
- **F&I menu review** of penetration and PVR inside the compliance boundary (§3 #4 §2).

## The standing caution

F&I product disclosure, pricing law, lending (Reg Z / state finance law), and advertising compliance are **counsel's** call — the team frames the operating decision and routes it. Keep customer PII (credit applications, deal jackets, financing data) out of deliverables (§2).
