# Subconsultant Scope Gaps Are the Architect's Liability

**Status:** Absolute rule
**Domain:** Architecture & AEC — subconsultant coordination
**Applies to:** `architecture-aec`

---

## Why this exists

The architect of record holds the prime contract with the owner. When the structural, MEP, or civil subconsultant leaves a gap — an uncoordinated penetration, a missing coordination note, an unresolved interface — the owner looks to the prime for resolution, not to the subconsultant. Scope boundaries that seem clearly allocated on a coordination call collapse under RFIs and change orders. The architect controls the fee for the prime contract but absorbs coordination liability across every sub's scope. Identifying and documenting scope interfaces before CDs begin is cheaper than resolving them during construction administration.

## How to apply

**At project kickoff — define every inter-discipline interface:**

```
Interface checklist (minimum):
[ ] Structural penetrations coordinated with architectural floor/wall sections
[ ] MEP ceiling-plenum depths cleared against structural depths
[ ] Electrical panel locations shown on both architectural and electrical sheets
[ ] Civil grading ties to architectural finish-floor elevations
[ ] Fire protection head locations shown on reflected ceiling plans
[ ] ADA accessible-route confirmed across all disciplines at each level
```

**At 50% CDs — run a cross-discipline coordination check:**
1. Overlay structural and architectural plans — confirm all penetrations located.
2. Overlay MEP and structural — confirm clearances and sleeves shown.
3. Confirm spec sections reference the same products the drawings show (no orphaned spec sections).
4. Document conflicts in writing to each subconsultant with a resolution-by date.

**At 95% CDs — confirm resolution:**
- Pull the 50% conflict log; confirm each item is closed.
- No item moves to permit set with "TBD by others."

**In the prime contract with each subconsultant:**
- Attach the scope matrix as an exhibit.
- State the coordination-review milestone deliverables explicitly.
- Require subs to flag scope conflicts in writing within 5 business days of receiving other disciplines' documents.

**Do:**
- Maintain a written coordination matrix — column per discipline, row per interface.
- Treat a subconsultant's silence as a coordination gap, not as confirmation.
- Charge unresolved sub-scope coordination time as additional services if the owner's program change triggered it.

**Don't:**
- Assume the subconsultant's scope "covers it" because the contract says "full MEP design."
- Wait until the GC's RFI reveals the gap.
- Resolve a sub-coordination conflict verbally without issuing a written response.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

Does not apply to design-build delivery where the GC holds the sub contracts directly. In that model, the contractor assumes coordination responsibility — the architect's role is design-intent documentation and review, not cross-discipline coordination. Confirm delivery method before applying this checklist.
