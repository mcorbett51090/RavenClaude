# Owner Sign-Off Must Be Written at Every Phase Gate

**Status:** Absolute rule
**Domain:** Architecture/AEC
**Applies to:** `architecture-aec`

---

## Why this exists

A verbal "looks great, keep going" is not phase approval. When a client later disputes the design direction at design development or construction documents, the absence of a written SD sign-off leaves the firm carrying both the rework cost and the argument about what was approved. Written phase approval is the documentary foundation of every additional-services authorization that follows — without it, the sequence of approvals collapses and the firm absorbs the delta.

## How to apply

At the close of each phase, obtain a written sign-off before issuing the next phase notice to proceed:

```
Phase Completion Sign-Off
──────────────────────────
Project:        ________________
Phase:          [ ] SD  [ ] DD  [ ] CDs  [ ] Bid  [ ] CA
Date issued:    ________________

Owner acknowledges:
  1. The deliverables listed in the phase scope have been received and reviewed.
  2. The design direction, program, and level of completion are approved as the basis
     for the next phase.
  3. Any items listed below are outstanding and will be addressed in the next phase
     [or per the attached issue log]; all other items are resolved.
  4. The owner is aware that changes to the approved work in subsequent phases will
     be subject to additional services authorization.

Outstanding items (if any):
  - _______________________________________________

Owner signature: ________________  Date: ________
Architect:       ________________  Date: ________
```

**Do:**
- Require the sign-off before issuing the NTP for the next phase — not before starting work, but before issuing the notice that defines the next phase's scope.
- Attach the phase deliverable list to the sign-off document; the approval must reference what was approved.
- Scan and file the signed document in the project record within 24 hours.

**Don't:**
- Accept an email "sounds good" as written sign-off — it works legally in some jurisdictions but is inferior evidence; a dated form with a deliverable reference is the defensible standard.
- Begin the next phase before the sign-off is received when the owner is indecisive — this is when rework accumulates fastest.
- Let a sign-off sit unsigned for more than two weeks without escalating; an owner who won't approve is a scope-risk flag.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

Fast-track delivery (design-build, integrated project delivery) may advance phases in parallel with explicit contractual authorization. In those cases, the sign-off cadence should be stated in the project delivery agreement rather than tied to phase boundaries.
