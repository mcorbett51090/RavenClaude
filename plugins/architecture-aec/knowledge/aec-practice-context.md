# Architecture practice benchmarks & context (2025–2026)

Dated firm-economics benchmarks for an AEC engagement, plus the professional-responsibility frame. Code/design judgment routes to the professional of record.

## Net multiplier & direct labor

Industry guidance puts net multiplier in the ~2.75–3.25 range (~3.0 is the long-standing average), with direct labor ~30% of net operating revenue — the firm's master profitability lens (§3 #4).

## Utilization

Target utilization runs ~75–85% for technical staff, ~65–75% for senior architects, and ~40–50% for principals, with firm-wide ~60–65% indicating a healthy billable/non-billable balance (§3 #4).

## Overhead & cash

The Deltek Clarity A&E study reports an ~162% overhead rate; firms average ~73 days to collect (institutional clients 90–120), and AIA reported average backlog ~7.6 months in 2025 amid soft billings (§3 #1).

## Professional responsibility

Design phases run schematic design → design development → construction documents → construction administration (effort heaviest in CD); building code, egress, structural, and accessibility determinations belong to the licensed architect/engineer of record — this plugin supports the work and does not certify it (§3 #7).
