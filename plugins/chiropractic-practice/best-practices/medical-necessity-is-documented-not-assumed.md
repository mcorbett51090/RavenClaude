# Medical necessity is documented, not assumed

**Rule.** A covered chiropractic visit needs a documented reason it's needed *today*: the region(s), the
functional deficit, the treatment goal, and progress toward it. If it isn't in the note, it isn't
defensible.

**Why.** The note *is* the claim. Payers and auditors don't see the adjustment — they see the
documentation. Absent findings + goal + progress, a covered claim can't be supported and is a clawback
risk.

**Anti-pattern it kills.** A cloned daily note ("adjusted, patient tolerated well") that documents no
findings, no goal, and no progress — indefensible on audit.

**In practice.** Every active-care note carries PART findings tied to the region coded, the functional
goal, and the change since last visit.
