# Active care has an endpoint; maintenance care is cash

**Rule.** Active, medically-necessary care is the phase where the patient is *improving toward a
functional goal* — it has an endpoint. Once function plateaus, continued care is supportive/maintenance
and generally not a covered benefit → move it to the cash/wellness model with an ABN where required.

**Why.** Billing maintenance care as active care is a top chiropractic audit finding; it also misleads
the patient about coverage. Recognizing the plateau protects the practice and respects the patient.

**Anti-pattern it kills.** Keeping a plateaued patient on the "insurance" path visit after visit because
nobody re-examined and called the transition.

**In practice.** A re-exam at each phase boundary documents whether the patient is still improving; the
plateau is the trigger to issue the ABN and transition to the cash membership.
