# Retention is the care plan completed, not the visit sold

**Rule.** Measure and manage to **plan completion** and **patient visit average (PVA)** — a patient who
finishes the clinically-indicated course of care — not to new-patient count or visits booked today.

**Why.** New patients refill a leaky bucket; completed care plans are the clinical outcome *and* the
durable revenue. A practice with high new-patient volume and low PVA is working hard to stand still.

**Anti-pattern it kills.** Pouring marketing spend into new patients while half of them drop off after
the relief phase because nobody re-booked them to the plan.

**In practice.** Re-book the next visit before the patient leaves; track PVA and plan-completion rate as
the headline retention metrics; a phase boundary triggers a re-exam, not a silent drop-off.
