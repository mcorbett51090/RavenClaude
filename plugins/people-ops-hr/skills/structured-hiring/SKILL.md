# Structured Hiring

## Level the role first
Define the role's level on the job-architecture ladder before anything else — the competencies to assess and the comp range an offer draws from both flow from it. If there's no ladder, route leveling to `total-rewards-analyst`. A role with no level produces a vibes-based loop and an invented offer.

## Design the loop: one competency, one assessor
Map each competency to exactly one assessor in the loop; don't have four interviewers all probe "culture fit." Define the stages, the order, and what each assessor owns. Overlap wastes signal and amplifies bias.

## Anchored scorecards + a structured debrief
Give every competency a scorecard with anchored rating levels — define what a 1 and a 4 look like. Run a structured debrief that forces *evidence* (what the candidate said/did) before any hire/no-hire verdict. A scorecard with no anchors is a feelings form; an opinion-only debrief amplifies bias.

## Instrument the funnel
Track stage conversion, time-in-stage, source quality, and offer-accept rate. Applicant *count* is vanity; conversion and time-in-stage tell you where candidates are lost (sourcing / screen / experience / offer). Diagnose before adding spend.

## Clean offer process
Define the approval chain + SLA, draw the comp range from the leveled band (`total-rewards-analyst`), sequence verbal-then-written, and name what's negotiable. Speed is a feature — a slow chain loses finalists.

## Compliance boundary
Flag EEO, ban-the-box, and pay-transparency posting points for counsel — never opine on a specific posting or candidate. This skill structures hiring; it does not give legal advice.

## Output
A structured interview kit (leveled competencies, loop + assessor map, anchored scorecards, debrief rubric), a funnel-metrics view, and an offer-process design — with compliance points flagged for counsel.
