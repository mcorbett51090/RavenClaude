# Comp Band & Leveling

## Build the leveling ladder first
Job architecture — levels × job families with a competency definition per level — comes before bands. A band with no level under it is a range with no logic for who lands where. The ladder is also what `talent-acquisition-lead` hires against and what promotions are tied to. No band without a level under it.

## Set the band: midpoint, spread, posture
Each band needs a market-data strategy (lead/match/lag, which percentile of which market), a midpoint that reflects that posture, a spread that reflects the role's growth runway, and compa-ratio logic to read where people sit in the range. A range quoted with no market posture is arbitrary.

## Pay equity: control, then surface the residual
Run the review by controlling for the legitimate explanatory factors — level, tenure, location, performance — and surfacing the *unexplained* residual gap. A raw average difference proves nothing. Name what you controlled for; frame remediation. The legal certification that the structure is equal-pay compliant is counsel's, not yours.

## Merit / promotion cycle
Split the budget into distinct pools — merit vs. promotion vs. equity adjustment. Drive merit off a matrix (performance × position-in-range), tie promotion to the ladder's criteria, and **calibrate across managers before anything is communicated**. Conflating merit and promotion budgets under-rewards real promotions.

## Benefits-design overview
Own the *design* and trade-offs of the benefits package; route carrier selection, funding, and underwriting to `insurance-life-health-benefits`. The comp budget and payroll live in `finance`.

## Compliance boundary
Flag equal-pay law, pay-transparency posting rules, and FLSA exempt-salary thresholds for counsel. This skill designs the architecture; it does not certify compliance or give legal advice.

## Output
A job-architecture ladder + band-per-level with range mechanics, a controlled pay-equity review surfacing unexplained gaps, and a merit/promotion-cycle design — with comp-compliance points flagged for counsel.
