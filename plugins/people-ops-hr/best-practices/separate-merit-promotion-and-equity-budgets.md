# Separate merit, promotion, and equity-adjustment budgets

A compensation cycle has three distinct purposes — rewarding performance in-role (merit), moving someone up the ladder (promotion), and correcting an off-market or inequitable placement (equity adjustment) — and they must be funded from three distinct pools. Conflate them and the cycle silently under-rewards real promotions, because a promotion paid out of the merit pool competes with everyone's raise and gets shaved to fit. Drive merit off a matrix (performance × position-in-range), tie promotion increases to the leveling ladder's criteria, size the equity-adjustment pool from the controlled pay-equity residual, and **calibrate across managers before anything is communicated**. Flag any equity-adjustment that traces to a protected-class pattern for counsel before it ships.
