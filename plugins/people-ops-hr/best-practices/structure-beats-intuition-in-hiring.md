# Structure beats intuition in hiring

A defined rubric assessed the same way for every candidate predicts performance and reduces bias better than freeform 'tell me about yourself' interviews. Map one competency to one assessor (not four interviewers all probing 'culture fit'), give each competency an anchored scorecard where a 1 and a 4 are defined, and run a structured debrief that forces evidence before a hire/no-hire verdict. A scorecard with no anchored levels is a feelings form; a debrief that records opinions instead of evidence amplifies the bias the structure was meant to remove.
