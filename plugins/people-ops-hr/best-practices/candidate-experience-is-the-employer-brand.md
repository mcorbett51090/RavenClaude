# Candidate experience is the employer brand

Every candidate who touches the hiring process — hired or not — leaves with an impression they share, and the rejected majority shape the employer brand more than the few who are hired. Treat candidate experience as a measured part of the funnel, not an afterthought: acknowledge applications, communicate timelines and keep them, give a same-structured loop to every candidate, and close the loop with a prompt, respectful rejection. A fast, fair, communicative process is also a *conversion* lever — slow or silent stages are exactly where strong candidates self-select out, which the funnel metrics will show as offer-decline and late-stage drop. Keep interviewer conduct inside the structured rubric, and route any candidate-facing point that touches EEO, ban-the-box, or pay-transparency posting rules to counsel rather than improvising a reply.
