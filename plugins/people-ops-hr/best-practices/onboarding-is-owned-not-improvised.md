# Onboarding is owned, not improvised

Onboarding is the first half of the lifecycle and it sets the tone for everything after — yet, like offboarding, it decays into improvisation the moment no one owns the checklist. Give it a named owner and a repeatable list that covers the HRIS record created correctly (status, level, FLSA class, manager, start date, location), access *provisioned* to the right systems, payroll and benefits enrolment, equipment, and the first-30/60/90-day plan. The same discipline that makes offboarding safe makes onboarding fair: every new hire gets the same good start, not the start that depended on who happened to be free that week. Where enrolment or classification touches employment law (FLSA exempt status, benefits eligibility, work authorisation), flag it for counsel rather than asserting it.
