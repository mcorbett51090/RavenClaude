# Compliance is the byproduct of real security, not the goal

Design controls for the risk they mitigate, and the certificate follows. A control that exists only to satisfy an auditor is theater — it adds cost, creates evidence to maintain, and gives a false sense of assurance. A passed audit over a fake or unused control is a liability, not an asset: it tells customers something is true that isn't. Build the security posture first; let the attestation be the receipt for work that already protects the org.
