# Scope is the highest-leverage decision

What sits inside the audit boundary — which systems, locations, people, data, and third parties — determines cost, effort, and audit risk more than any individual control choice. A broad scope with gaps loses to a narrow scope that is clean, because the auditor finds the gaps in the broad one. Carve the boundary to what the org can genuinely attest this cycle, defend the carve-out against the risk register, and expand the scope next cycle once the core is proven.
