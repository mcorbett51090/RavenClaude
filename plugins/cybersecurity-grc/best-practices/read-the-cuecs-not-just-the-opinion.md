# Read the CUECs, not just the opinion page

A vendor's SOC 2 report is not a control you inherit by filing the PDF. The audit opinion is the least informative page; the assurance lives in the scope, the observation period, the exceptions, and — critically — the complementary user-entity controls (CUECs). The CUECs are the controls the report *assumes you* run on your side of the shared-responsibility boundary; if you never implemented them, the vendor's clean opinion covers a system that doesn't actually exist as described in your environment. Read the exceptions for findings in the control area you depend on (a clean opinion with an exception in your critical area is not assurance), confirm the report's period overlaps the period you need covered, and map every CUEC to a control you own and evidence. "The vendor handles security" is the boundary stated by the half who isn't running your half.
