## Occupancy metrics — physical vs economic

- **Physical / unit occupancy** = units rented ÷ total units. **Square-foot occupancy** = rented sq ft ÷ total sq ft (differs from unit occupancy when unit sizes vary). These are the *capacity* view.
- **Economic occupancy** = actual revenue ÷ revenue if every occupied unit paid current **street** rate. This is the *honest* view — it exposes discounting, stale in-place rates, and delinquents counted as "occupied."
- The **gap** between physical and economic occupancy is the primary revenue diagnostic. A 95%-physical / 78%-economic facility isn't full-and-healthy; it's leaking ~17 points to discounts and stale rates — a classic **ECRI** opportunity, not a reason to discount further.
- **Operators generally run high physical occupancy** (often 90%+ in a stabilized facility) and manage *rate*, not just fill — pushing physical occupancy to 100% usually means underpricing. _(Consensus operating practice, 2026-07-09; exact targets vary by market — re-verify.)_

## Street rate vs in-place rate — the two prices

- **Street rate** = the price a *new* customer pays today. It's the acquisition price, moves with unit-type occupancy and demand, and should be **dynamic** (floor + ceiling), not set once a year.
- **In-place rate** = what a *sitting* tenant already pays. It drifts below street over time as street rates rise and the tenant stays put.
- The gap between them, across the tenant base, is the **ECRI opportunity** — the accumulated room to raise sitting tenants toward street.

## ECRIs — existing-customer rate increases (the core profit lever)

**Why they matter most:** a tenant with a full unit has **high switching costs** (renting a truck, moving heavy boxes, the time) — so a well-sized in-place increase rarely triggers a move-out, and because the facility's fixed costs are already covered, the increase **flows almost entirely to NOI**. This is the single highest-margin move in self-storage.

**How to run them (mechanics, not legal advice):**

- **Cadence:** a common pattern is the first increase after a tenure threshold (e.g. ~6–12 months in place), then periodic increases (e.g. annually or more often in strong markets). _(Pattern, not a rule — verify against the tenant base and market, 2026-07-09.)_
- **Size:** scale by the **in-place-vs-street gap** and **tenure** — a tenant well below street and long-tenured absorbs more than a recent move-in near street.
- **Churn guardrail:** model an acceptable move-out rate; the increase is worth it when *retained-revenue lift > lost-revenue from churn*. A blanket hike with no guardrail spikes churn without lifting NOI.
- **Notice:** provide the rate-change notice the rental agreement / applicable state law requires (varies — verify). ECRIs are contractual, not a lien matter.

**Anti-pattern:** never raising in-place rates "to keep tenants happy" — it leaves the biggest lever in the asset untouched. The opposite anti-pattern: a blanket increase with no tenure/gap sizing and no churn model.

## Dynamic / automated pricing

- **What it is:** street rates that adjust by unit type against real-time unit-type occupancy and demand, with a **floor** (never below) and a **ceiling** (never above). Native in some PMS platforms; also offered by dedicated revenue-management layers. _(Feature availability varies by platform — verify, 2026-07-09.)_
- **Discipline:** automate street rate; keep a human on the floor/ceiling and on the ECRI policy. Don't let automation set rates with no guardrails.

## Unit-mix optimization

- **The problem:** a mix that doesn't match demand (too many 10×10s, no 5×5s / no drive-up / no climate) caps revenue regardless of price.
- **The levers:** rebalance by converting/repartitioning units toward the sizes and types in demand; price the scarce types up. Climate-controlled units typically command a premium where the market supports it (varies by climate/region — verify).

---

## Promotions — an acquisition cost with a payback

- **Common promos:** **$1 first month**, first-month-free, or a percentage discount to win the rental.
- **Discipline:** a promotion is an **acquisition cost**, not a giveaway — it only pencils if the in-place rate and the **ECRI cadence recover it** over the tenant's expected stay. Model the payback; don't discount to fill the last units when the ECRI is the cheaper path to revenue.

## Delinquency & the lien process — prevention, then a STATE-SPECIFIC sequence

> **Lien law varies materially by US state, the statutes change, and this is operational guidance — NOT legal advice.** Every step below carries a "verify against your state + retrieval date" caveat; route the actual legal question to counsel. The self-storage lien is what lets a facility sell an abandoned/defaulted tenant's goods to recover unpaid rent — a **statutory** process with strict notice and timing rules that differ by state.

**Prevention (cheapest control):**

- **Autopay enrolment at move-in** — the single most effective delinquency control; a card on file that runs automatically prevents most roll-to-late.
- **Late-fee discipline** — clear, agreement-stated late fees applied on schedule (amounts/caps vary by state — verify).

**The lien timeline (typical *sequence*; exact days/notices/methods are state-specific — verify):**

1. **Rent due → grace → late fee** — first late fee after the grace period per the agreement/state.
2. **Gate lockout / overlock** — deny gate access and/or place an **overlock** on the unit so the tenant can't remove goods while in default (timing varies by state).
3. **Pre-lien / default notice** — a notice of default and intent to enforce the lien, sent by the method and after the delay the state requires.
4. **Lien notice** — the formal lien notice with the required contents (amount owed, deadline, that the goods will be sold), by the required delivery method (certified mail / verified mail / email where allowed — varies).
5. **Advertising / public notice** — advertise the sale as the state requires (newspaper and/or a designated online platform).
6. **Auction / sale** — sell the contents, commonly via an online platform (**StorageTreasures**, **Lockerfox**) or a live/onsite auction. _(Platform terms volatile — verify, 2026-07-09.)_
7. **Sale of goods → apply proceeds** — apply proceeds to the unpaid rent, fees, and sale costs in the state-specified order.
8. **Surplus handling** — any **surplus** over what's owed must be handled per state law (often held for / returned to the tenant, or escheated) — mishandling surplus is a frequent source of liability. Verify the state rule.

**The point:** run this as a disciplined, state-specific sequence — a mis-stepped or mis-timed notice can **void the sale** and expose the operator. Prevention (autopay) is far cheaper than any step of the lien process.

## Tenant insurance / protection plans & ancillary revenue

- **Tenant insurance / protection plans** — coverage on the stored goods, offered (often required) at move-in. **Tenant-protection plans** (e.g. SBOA-style tenant protection) are a major ancillary-revenue and margin source: the facility earns on the plan, and covered tenants reduce the facility's own claims exposure. Attach rate is won or lost at the **move-in capture point**. _(Program terms/regulatory treatment vary by state — verify.)_
- **Other ancillary revenue:** admin fees, late fees, retail (locks, boxes, packing supplies), and where offered, truck rental / other services. Together these lift **revenue per occupied unit** beyond base rent.

## Remote / unmanned & kiosk operations

- **What it is:** running a facility with no (or minimal) on-site staff — online/kiosk rental, smart/app-based access, IP cameras, and a call center / answering service for support and sales.
- **When it fits:** lower-volume or cost-pressured sites, or as a labor-cost lever across a portfolio; requires solid access control, camera coverage, and a working inbound funnel. Market acceptance varies — verify local demand before converting a staffed site.
