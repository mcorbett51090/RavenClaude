# AI-logo copyright is not trademark — document human authorship

**Status:** Absolute rule
**Domain:** Legal / IP (not legal advice)
**Applies to:** `brand-identity-studio`

> **Not legal advice.** States a general fact as of a retrieval date; every client-facing claim routes to
> `ravenclaude-core:security-reviewer` and counsel. Legal specifics `[verify-at-use]`.

---

## Why this exists

A purely AI-generated logo is **not copyrightable** in the US (no human authorship; mere prompting is
insufficient — USCO guidance, 29 Jan 2025). It **can** still be trademarked (USPTO evaluates the
source-identifier function, not authorship). These are two different rights, and conflating them is how a
studio promises a client something it cannot deliver. The fix that preserves copyrightability is a **documented
substantial human modification/arrangement** — which is exactly why the pipeline logs one at curation. Without
that log, the resale deliverable carries no copyright to assign.

## How to apply

- At `/curate-concepts`, log the **substantial human modification/arrangement** in
  `curation-and-authorship-log.md` (redraw, recomposition, custom kerning, lockup/palette decisions).
- State the **facts** ("pure-AI logos aren't copyrightable; documenting human authorship preserves it; a mark
  can be trademarked") — never the **conclusion** ("you own this / this is trademarkable").
- **Recommend a TM clearance search** (USPTO) before promising trademarkability, and an **IP-assignment
  clause** to the client.
- Route every one of those to `security-reviewer` / counsel before the brand book or a proposal asserts it.

**Do:** document human authorship; state facts; route claims to counsel.
**Don't:** assert copyright ownership, trademarkability, or IP transfer from the plugin.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

Jurisdiction matters — this is US-centric (USCO/USPTO). For other jurisdictions, the facts differ; route to
counsel and do not generalize.
