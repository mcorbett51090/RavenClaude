# Channel mix is a margin decision, never zero-OTA

Drive direct-booking share to cut distribution cost — but the right answer is a deliberate *mix*, never zero OTA. The OTA is a paid acquisition channel with a known cost that reaches demand direct cannot, so the question is always "what is the right share," weighed on net-ADR contribution per channel, not "how do we eliminate the OTA." Shifting a booking from a 20%-commission OTA to direct only wins if direct can actually capture that demand and the direct value prop (parity, loyalty, perk) makes the guest prefer it; a parity-only direct channel moves no share. Protect the residual OTA volume that reaches demand you can't otherwise win — stranding it to chase a vanity direct-share number is a RevPAR loss dressed as a margin win.
