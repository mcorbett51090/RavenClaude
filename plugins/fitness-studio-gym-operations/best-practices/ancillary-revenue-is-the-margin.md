# Ancillary revenue is the margin

**Status:** Pattern
**Domain:** Revenue mix / P&L
**Applies to:** `fitness-studio-gym-operations`

> Advisory operations rule. Attach and margin benchmarks are `[ESTIMATE]` / `[verify-at-use]`. No member PII.

---

## Why this exists

Membership dues pay for the room; the higher-margin dollars come from **ancillary** — personal training and semi-private, retail, café/juice bar, and programmed workshops. The right measure is revenue and contribution margin **per member**, and a modest lift in PT or retail attach often beats a risky dues increase that raises churn. But ancillary rides on retention: you cannot sell PT to a member who already quit, which is why it sequences *after* a retained base, not instead of one.

## How to apply

- Measure **ancillary revenue per member** and **attach rate**, by line, at contribution margin (a busy café at thin margin can lose money).
- Model ancillary headroom **before** touching dues pricing.
- Sequence it: fix retention first, then build the onboarding-assessment → PT path, then add the highest-margin line that fits your space and base.
- Use the adjacent plugins for the mechanics: retail assortment/attach → `retail-store-operations`; café P&L → `restaurant-operations`.

**Do:** grow the margin dues can't reach on top of a retained base.
**Don't:** chase a dues increase while PT/retail attach sits untapped, or add a café before the base can buy from it.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

A minimalist, low-overhead studio may deliberately run dues-only to keep operations simple — a valid model, but then dues pricing and retention carry the whole P&L.
