# The first 30 days decide the member

**Status:** Absolute rule
**Domain:** Onboarding / retention
**Applies to:** `fitness-studio-gym-operations`

> Advisory operations rule. The attendance habit threshold is `[verify-at-use]` against your own cohort data. No member PII.

---

## Why this exists

Early attendance frequency is the single best predictor of whether a new member stays. A member who builds a visit habit in the first weeks becomes durable; one who signs up, comes twice, and drifts is already gone — they just haven't filled out the cancel form yet. That makes onboarding a **retention investment**, not a welcome courtesy: the cheapest save is the churn you prevent by getting a member to the habit threshold early.

## How to apply

- Book the **first visit at signup**, not "whenever they're ready."
- Set a goal and a baseline, and drive toward the **early-frequency habit threshold** (`[verify-at-use]` against your cohort).
- Run **scheduled check-ins** through the honeymoon window — a missed re-book after onboarding is an early-cliff signal.
- Measure **30/60/90-day retention by cohort** as the onboarding scoreboard.

**Do:** design onboarding as a frequency-building program with milestones.
**Don't:** treat onboarding as a welcome email and wait for the cancel form.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

A returning/win-back member with an established prior habit may need re-activation rather than full onboarding. A pure class-pack buyer has a different (transactional) lifecycle.
