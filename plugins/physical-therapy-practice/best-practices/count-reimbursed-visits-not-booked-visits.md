# Count reimbursed visits, not booked visits

**Status:** Absolute rule.

**Rule:** The clinic's revenue unit is the **reimbursed** visit — delivered, documented, coded, and
collected. Booked visits, the gross fee schedule, and even delivered visits overstate it. Build the
P&L and every productivity decision on reimbursed-visit economics.

## Why

A full appointment book is not full revenue. Between booking and collection, visits leak to
cancellations and no-shows, claims leak to denials, and gross charges leak to contractual adjustments
and patient non-payment. A clinic that manages to "booked visits" or "gross charges" is steering by a
number disconnected from what actually funds it — and is routinely surprised when a busy month
produces thin margin. Net collection per delivered visit, multiplied by reimbursed visits, is the real
top line.

## What it looks like in practice

- The P&L starts from delivered-and-reimbursed visits, not the schedule.
- Net collection per visit (after denials and adjustments) is a headline metric.
- Payer mix is evaluated by net collection per visit, not gross fee schedule.

## Anti-pattern

Reporting a "record month" by booked appointments while denials and cancellations quietly hollow out
the net. The hook and the constitution both push the same discipline: reimbursed visits, not booked.
