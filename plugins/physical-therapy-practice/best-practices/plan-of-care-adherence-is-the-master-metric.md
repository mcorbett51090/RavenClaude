# Plan-of-care adherence is the master metric

**Status:** Absolute rule.

**Rule:** Plan-of-care adherence — the patient completing the prescribed episode of care — is
simultaneously the clinical outcome and the financial outcome. Scheduling, documentation, and billing
all exist to keep episodes complete.

## Why

A physical therapy episode is multi-visit by design. The clinical result depends on the patient
completing the prescribed course; the clinic's revenue is the sum of those delivered, reimbursed
visits. The two are the same number viewed twice. A patient who drops out at visit 4 of 12 gets a
worse outcome *and* takes two-thirds of the episode's revenue with them. Optimizing anything else
while adherence leaks is moving deck chairs.

## What it looks like in practice

- Adherence (visits delivered ÷ visits prescribed across the episode) is tracked as a headline
  clinic metric, not buried.
- Cancellation interventions, schedule templates, and patient communication are evaluated by their
  effect on episode completion.
- P&L models rest on adherence assumptions made explicit, not on booked-visit counts.

## Anti-pattern

Treating visit volume or schedule fill as the goal while episodes silently fragment. A booked
schedule that doesn't complete episodes is neither good care nor good economics.
