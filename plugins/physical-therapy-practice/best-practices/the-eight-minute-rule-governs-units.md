# The 8-minute rule governs the units

**Status:** Absolute rule.

**Rule:** Billable units for timed CPT codes follow total timed treatment minutes (the Medicare
8-minute rule and its payer variants). A unit claimed without the documented minute basis is a coding
error, a denial, and an audit exposure.

> **Compliance note:** the rule's structure is below for orientation; thresholds and payer variants
> change. `[verify against current CMS/payer policy and a certified coder]`.

## Why

Timed therapeutic codes are not "one per service" — they convert documented minutes into units on a
fixed schedule. Billing more units than the timed minutes support is overbilling; billing fewer is
leaving earned revenue uncollected. Either way, the minutes recorded in the documentation are the
single source of truth, and a claim that can't point back to them does not survive review.

## What it looks like in practice

- Total timed minutes are recorded at the point of care and converted to units via the rule
  (8–22 min = 1 unit, 23–37 = 2, 38–52 = 3, 53–67 = 4, +15/unit thereafter).
- Untimed (service-based) codes are billed once per service, not by time, and are kept distinct from
  timed codes in the count.
- Commercial-payer variants (rule-of-eights / AMA) are verified per payer rather than assumed equal
  to Medicare.

## Anti-pattern

Assigning units by habit or by what "feels right" for a visit, disconnected from the documented
minutes. The hook flags timed CPT units mentioned with no 8-minute-rule/minute basis.
