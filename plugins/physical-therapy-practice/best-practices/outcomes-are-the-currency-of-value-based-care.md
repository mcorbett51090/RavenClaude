# Outcomes are the currency of value-based care

**Status:** Pattern.

**Rule:** Functional outcomes — change measured against MCID and the baseline — are the currency that
proves care worked and justifies the care that remains. Visit counts are an activity measure;
functional progress is the outcome.

## Why

As reimbursement shifts toward value, "the patient was seen 10 times" carries less and less weight;
"the patient regained 14 points on a validated functional measure, past the MCID, toward their goal"
carries more. Outcomes are also the bridge between clinical and financial: documented functional
progress toward a goal is exactly the medical-necessity evidence that defends continued, reimbursable
visits. A clinic that doesn't measure outcomes can neither prove its value to payers nor defend its
episodes of care with the strongest available evidence.

## What it looks like in practice

- Standardized, region-appropriate measures captured at intake, interval, and discharge.
- Change judged against MCID, not reported as a raw delta.
- Outcome data captured at the point of care for value-based/quality reporting, not reconstructed.

## Anti-pattern

Reporting visit volume as if it were outcome, with no functional measurement — leaving the clinic
unable to demonstrate value or defend continued care with anything but "we kept treating." Treat
external benchmark figures as `[unverified]` until validated against the clinic's own data.
