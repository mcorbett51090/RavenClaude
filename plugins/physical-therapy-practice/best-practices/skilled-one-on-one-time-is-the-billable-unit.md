# Skilled one-on-one time is the billable unit

**Status:** Pattern.

**Rule:** Billable skilled time — the licensed therapist's one-on-one clinical work — is the unit of
both reimbursement and outcomes. Staffing and productivity decisions that dilute it below what the
codes and the care require erode the claim and the clinical result at once.

## Why

Timed therapeutic codes bill skilled, one-on-one therapist time. When a staffing model stretches a
therapist across too many simultaneous patients, or pushes skilled work onto aides, two things break:
the time that justifies the units isn't truly one-on-one skilled time (a billing and compliance
exposure), and the clinical attention that drives functional outcomes thins out (a quality and
outcomes problem). The same minute is the currency of the bill and the care — degrade it and both
suffer.

## What it looks like in practice

- Productivity targets are set against documentable, genuinely skilled one-on-one time, not raw
  throughput.
- The PT/PTA/tech/aide skill mix maps each task to who may perform and supervise it, per payer and
  state rules.
- Group vs. one-on-one time is coded honestly and distinctly.

## Anti-pattern

A productivity target met by overlapping patients or aide-delivered "skilled" work — billable on paper,
indefensible under audit, and worse for outcomes. Flag supervision/scope rules for verification against
current payer and state policy.
