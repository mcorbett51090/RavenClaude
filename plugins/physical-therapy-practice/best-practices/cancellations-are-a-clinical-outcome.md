# Cancellations are a clinical outcome, not just a revenue leak

**Status:** Pattern.

**Rule:** A missed visit breaks the prescribed episode of care — it harms the clinical result before
it harms the schedule. Treat no-shows and cancellations as an adherence and outcomes problem first,
and the revenue recovery follows.

## Why

Because a PT episode is multi-visit, each missed visit interrupts the progression the plan of care
depends on: strength, mobility, and pain goals slip, and the patient who misses is markedly more
likely to drop out of the episode entirely. The lost revenue from one slot is the small part; the
large part is the degraded outcome and the abandoned episode behind it. Interventions framed only as
"protect the schedule" miss the lever that actually matters — keeping the patient progressing toward
their functional goal.

## What it looks like in practice

- Cancellations are segmented by reason and episode position; "felt better" mid-episode is treated as
  a clinical-engagement gap, not a reminder gap.
- Interventions tie the remaining visits to the patient's functional goal, so finishing the episode
  feels purposeful rather than optional.
- Schedule templates absorb expected no-show behavior, but the primary goal is fewer broken episodes,
  not just filled holes.

## Anti-pattern

Treating no-shows purely as lost revenue and responding only with cancellation fees and overbooking.
The hook flags cancellation/no-show discussion framed solely in revenue terms with no
adherence/episode/outcome framing.
