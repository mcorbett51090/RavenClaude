# Verify authorization before the visit

**Status:** Pattern.

**Rule:** The cheapest place to prevent an authorization or coverage denial is at intake, before the
patient is treated. Verify eligibility and benefits, obtain and track authorizations and visit limits,
and track the therapy threshold — at the front end, not the back end.

## Why

An authorization or coverage denial is almost always a front-office failure surfaced weeks later as a
billing problem. By the time the claim denies, the care has been delivered, the cost incurred, and the
remedy is an appeal or a write-off. Catching it at intake — confirming active coverage, the PT
benefit, the visit limit, and any required authorization — converts a likely write-off into a clean
claim, at a fraction of the cost and effort of an appeal.

## What it looks like in practice

- Eligibility and benefits are verified before the first visit, not after.
- Authorizations are obtained and the authorized visit count/date range is tracked against visits used.
- Cumulative therapy is tracked so the threshold/KX attestation is applied on time.
- Point-of-service cost expectations are set with the patient up front.

## Anti-pattern

Treating patients first and discovering the coverage/auth problem when the claim denies. The
denial-root-cause map traces most auth denials to intake; fix them there. Verify specifics against
current payer policy.
