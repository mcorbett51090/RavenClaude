# The referral relationship is the pipeline

**Status:** Pattern.

**Rule:** Referral sources are the clinic's pipeline and are managed like one — volume and trend
tracked by source, cold sources flagged as alerts, and the loop closed with outcome communication. A
single physician source going quiet can outweigh any marketing campaign.

## Why

Most outpatient PT volume arrives through a relatively small set of referral relationships. Because the
volume is concentrated, a single high-volume source drifting away — a physician who retired, switched
groups, or quietly started referring elsewhere — can do more damage than any marketing effort can
repair, and it happens invisibly unless source-level trend is watched. Treating referrals as a
managed pipeline (not a passive inflow) is what catches the drift early and compounds the relationships
that drive the practice.

## What it looks like in practice

- Referral volume and trend are tracked by source, and a declining source triggers outreach.
- Referral → evaluation conversion is measured; speed-to-contact is treated as the dominant lever.
- The loop is closed with referrers via outcome/status updates — the relationship runs on credibility.

## Anti-pattern

Treating referrals as a passive inflow and noticing a major source has gone cold only when the
schedule thins out a quarter later. By then the relationship — and the volume — is hard to recover.
