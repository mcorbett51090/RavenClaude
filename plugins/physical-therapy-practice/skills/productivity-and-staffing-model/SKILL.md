# Productivity & Staffing Model

**Purpose:** staff the clinic and set productivity targets that deliver good care and clean
reimbursement — never the raw-unit quota that pushes utilization past what's medically necessary and
documentable.

---

## Steps

### 1. Define productivity against documentable necessity

Set visit/unit targets that the clinician can deliver with defensible documentation and genuine
skilled care. A target that can only be met by billing units the care doesn't justify is an audit
liability disguised as productivity (constitution: code what was documented, not the reverse).

### 2. Protect skilled one-on-one time

Billable skilled time is the unit of both reimbursement and outcomes. A staffing model that dilutes
one-on-one therapist time below what the codes and the care require erodes both the claim and the
clinical result (see [`skilled-one-on-one-time-is-the-billable-unit`](../../best-practices/skilled-one-on-one-time-is-the-billable-unit.md)).

### 3. Model the skill mix and supervision

Map the PT / PTA / tech / aide mix to the work that each may perform and supervise, per payer and
state rules. The mix is a cost and compliance decision at once — an aide performing skilled work is
both a quality and a billing problem. Flag supervision rules for verification.

### 4. Build capacity to demand and adherence

Size staffing to expected demand and the plan-of-care adherence the schedule must support — not to a
flat ratio. Under-staffing breaks episodes (cancellations the clinic can't reschedule); over-staffing
is idle labor cost. Use [`../../scripts/pt_calc.py`](../../scripts/pt_calc.py) `visit_utilization`.

### 5. Tie to the P&L

Labor is the largest cost line; the productivity and skill-mix decisions are where it's set. Connect
the model to net collection per visit and contribution margin (the P&L skill consumes this).

---

## Output

A productivity-target framework (against documentable necessity), a skill-mix/supervision model, and a
capacity plan tied to demand and adherence. Frames into the clinic P&L and the practice-lead's
operating model.
