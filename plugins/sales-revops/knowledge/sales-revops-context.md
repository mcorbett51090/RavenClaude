## Where defensible benchmarks come from

Win-rate, sales-cycle, and OTE benchmarks are **segment-, ACV-, and motion-specific** and move year to year. Common reference sources practitioners cite require a dated cut: industry GTM benchmark reports, comp surveys, and the org's own trailing CRM history (the most defensible source). **Name the source and date, or mark the figure `[unverified — training knowledge]` (§3 #8).**

## Directional frames (illustrative only — `[unverified — training knowledge]`)

| Area | Directional frame | Must-verify |
|---|---|---|
| Pipeline coverage target | Often cited around 3–4x for enterprise; varies with win-rate | Derive from the org's own win-rate, not a rule of thumb |
| Win-rate | Diverges widely by inbound vs outbound and by ACV | Segment from CRM history |
| Sales-cycle | Days (SMB) to many months (enterprise) | Median by segment from CRM |
| Quota attainment | A healthy plan has a median near 100% with a spread | Read the distribution, not the average |

## Operating rhythm

- **Forecast cadence** weekly with a monthly/quarterly commit; calibrate stage win-rates from trailing closed deals.
- **Pipeline council** to age deals and challenge slip risk (§3 #6).
- **Quota/territory design** annually, tied to capacity and TAM (§3 #4 #7), pre-comp-cycle.

## The standing caution

Revenue recognition (GAAP/ASC 606), comp-plan legal/tax, and customer-contract terms are **the qualified authority's** call — the team frames the decision and routes it. Keep customer/rep PII and confidential terms out of deliverables (§2).
