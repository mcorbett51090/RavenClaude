# Revenue-cycle economics

How the cash cycle works — prevention, the cash metrics, denial root cause, and front-end leverage.

## Prevention vs appeal

The cheapest denial never happens; work root cause and fix upstream (§3 #1, #6).

## The cash metrics

Net collection rate and days-in-A/R are the cash headlines (§3 #3, #4). NCR is collected-against-*allowed*, and allowed is set by the fee schedule — so when the Medicare conversion factor moves, the denominator moves with it.

## CY2026 Medicare conversion factor — two CFs (added 2026-06-25)

Effective 2026-01-01, the CMS CY2026 Physician Fee Schedule final rule (**CMS-1832-F**, finalized 2025-10-31) replaced the single Medicare conversion factor with **two**: **$33.57** for QPs (qualifying APM participants) and **$33.40** for non-QPs. Any allowed-amount or expected-pay math must use the QP/non-QP CF, not a single legacy CF. See [`rcm-benchmarks-2026.md` § CY2026 regulatory facts](rcm-benchmarks-2026.md) for the cited detail (incl. the CPT 2026 code-set change). Source: [CMS — CY2026 PFS Final Rule (CMS-1832-F)](https://www.cms.gov/newsroom/fact-sheets/calendar-year-cy-2026-medicare-physician-fee-schedule-final-rule-cms-1832-f) — retrieved 2026-06-25.
