# RCM benchmarks (2025–2026)

Dated benchmarks for a cash-cycle read. Denial behavior shifted — every figure carries a source and a retrieval date.

## Denials

Initial denials rose to ~11.8% in 2024 and are projected ~12–15% in 2025; target <5%, world-class <3%. Payer audits and denial amounts rose again in 2025 (§3 #1).

## Resolution & clean claims

First-pass resolution benchmark ~90%; 2025 clean-claim targets 95%+, many push 98% (§3 #2).

## Cash cycle

High performers run days-in-A/R <30 (31–40 acceptable per MGMA/AAFP/HFMA); A/R over 90 days target <10%; net collection rate target 96–98% (large groups 98–100%, smaller ~94%) (§3 #3, #4). Cost-to-collect for efficient operations runs <3% [verify-at-use].

## Recovery economics (added 2026-06-05)

50–65% of denied claims are **never reworked** (MGMA); roughly **two-thirds are recoverable**. Recovery decays with age: ~70–80% when worked within 60 days vs ~40–60% after 90 days — speed compounds value (§3 #3). Appeal overturn rates run ~40–60% for commercial denials and >80% for appealed Medicare Advantage denials [verify-at-use]. The cheapest denial is still the one prevented upstream (§3 #1, #6).

> Caveat: the "denials rose from 30% (2022) to 41% (2025)" figure in some vendor blogs is **[unverified]** and conflicts with AHA's 11.8% (2024) initial-denial-rate; do not cite without a primary source.
