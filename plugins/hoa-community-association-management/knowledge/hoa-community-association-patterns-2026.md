## The governing-document hierarchy — the association's constitution

Community associations are governed by a **layered set of documents**; when they conflict, the higher layer generally controls (verify per the state act):

| Layer | What it is | Governs |
|---|---|---|
| **State condo / HOA / POA act** | The controlling statute | Overrides documents where mandatory; sets floors (notice, reserves, records, collections) |
| **Declaration / CC&Rs** | Covenants, Conditions & Restrictions — recorded against the land | Use restrictions, assessment obligation, lien right, maintenance responsibility, architectural control |
| **Articles of incorporation** | Creates the association entity (usually a nonprofit) | Corporate existence, purpose |
| **Bylaws** | The operating manual | Board structure, elections, meetings, quorum, officer duties |
| **Rules & regulations / design guidelines** | Board-adopted detail | Day-to-day conduct, ARC standards, amenity rules |

**Read them before you act.** The board's authority — to assess, to fine, to lien, to approve a change — is granted and bounded here. An action the documents (and the statute) don't authorize is exposure, not governance.

## Association types

| Type | What it is | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **HOA** (homeowners' association) | Single-family / townhome community; owners own their lots + a share of common areas | Assessments fund common-area upkeep & amenities |
| **COA** (condominium association) | Owners own the unit interior + an undivided interest in common elements | The association typically insures & maintains the building envelope/common elements — bigger reserve components |
| **POA** (property-owners' association) | Broader/mixed-use owners' association | May span residential + commercial; check the declaration |

The **community-association manager (CAM)** / management company advises and executes; the **volunteer board** governs. Keeping that line clean is the job.

## Budgeting — operating + reserves

- **Operating budget** — the recurring cost of running the community: management fee, master-policy insurance, common-area utilities, landscaping/grounds, routine repairs & maintenance, trash, amenity operations, administration, taxes/audit, and a **contingency**. Build from prior-year actuals + known changes, not last year's budget echoed forward.
- **Reserve contribution** — the annual funding of the eventual replacement of major components (see the reserve study). Set it from the study, not the board's appetite for low dues.
- **Assessment (dues)** — `(operating + reserve contribution) ÷ the ownership-allocation formula` (equal per unit, by size / percentage interest, or per the declaration). Covers **both** — an operating-only assessment that starves reserves is a deferred special assessment.
- **Assessment increase caps** — many declarations/statutes cap a **board-only** annual increase; above it needs a member vote. Check before adopting.

## Reserve studies & reserve funding

- **Reserve study** — a **component inventory** (roofs, roads/asphalt, elevators, exterior painting, pool, fencing, decks, HVAC, etc.) with each component's **replacement cost** and **remaining useful life**, plus a **funding plan**. Often refreshed on a cycle (with a periodic on-site update) — a stale study is a weak basis for a funding decision.
- **Percent funded** — `reserve balance ÷ fully-funded balance`. A rough health signal: higher percent-funded ⇒ lower special-assessment risk. (The often-cited "30% / 70%" bands are heuristics, not universal thresholds — verify against the study and any statute.)
- **Funding methods** — **full funding** (~100% — lowest special-assessment risk), **baseline** (balance stays above zero across the horizon), **threshold** (hold a chosen floor), **statutory minimum** (the legal floor — often underfunds). Pick deliberately.
- **The failure mode** — deferring reserve funding to hold dues down. The members pay either way; deferral just makes it a **special assessment** later, larger and more disruptive.

## Assessments, delinquency & the lien (concept-level — legal steps route to counsel)

- **Assessment obligation** — the declaration obligates each owner to pay assessments; unpaid assessments typically become a **lien** on the unit/lot.
- **Collections sequence** (business process): payment reminder → **late fee** (per the schedule) → **demand / intent-to-lien notice** (a **statutory pre-lien notice** is often required) → the **lien-referral / escalation-to-counsel gate**.
- **Payment application** — apply payments in the order the governing documents / statute require (often oldest-first; some jurisdictions mandate an order that can't be overridden by "disputed" designations) — **verify**.
- **The legal line** — **recording the lien, foreclosing, and complying with FDCPA / fair-housing / state debt-collection rules** are **counsel's** terrain. Treasury/management runs the reminders and notices **evenly**; the lien/foreclosure decision is the board's and its mechanics are counsel's. **Never advise a lien or foreclosure step as legal advice.**
- **Lien priority** — condo/HOA assessment liens can have a limited **super-priority** over a first mortgage in some states — highly jurisdiction-specific; a foreclosure/priority question is a counsel question.

## Covenant enforcement & architectural review

- **Even-handed, documented due process** — enforce every covenant the **same way** against **every** owner. Selective enforcement can **waive** the covenant and lose in court. The sequence: **written notice** (cite the covenant + the specific violation + a **cure period**) → **hearing** (the owner's opportunity to be heard, often required before a fine) → **fine per the schedule / self-help-abatement where authorized / referral to counsel** for serious or continuing violations. Document each step with evidence.
- **Architectural review (ARC)** — owner exterior changes go through the ARC against the **published design guidelines**. Decide **within any required timeframe** (an un-acted request can be **deemed approved** in some regimes — watch the clock), decide **consistently** with prior decisions, and **record the decision and rationale**. Arbitrary or undocumented denials are a lawsuit magnet.
- **Fair housing** — enforcement and ARC decisions must not discriminate against protected classes, and **reasonable-accommodation / modification** requests (e.g., assistance animals, accessibility) have their own rules — a fair-housing question routes to **counsel**.

## Meetings & official records

- **Meeting types** — **board** meetings, the **annual / member** meeting (elections), and **special** meetings — each with its own **notice** and **quorum** requirements per the bylaws/statute. Open-meeting rules may require member access to board meetings.
- **Notice** — timing window + delivery method (and agenda for member meetings); a defectively-noticed meeting can **void** its actions.
- **Quorum & proxies** — confirm the quorum (a frequent annual-meeting failure point) and handle **proxies / absentee ballots / electronic voting** per the documents & statute.
- **Minutes** — the legal record of **what the board decided**: attendance, quorum, motions, seconds, votes, decisions, and assigned actions — not a discussion transcript. Draft → circulate → adopt at the next meeting → file.
- **Official records & inspection** — maintain governing documents, minutes, financials, contracts, the reserve study, membership roster, insurance, and violation/ARC files for the **statutory retention period**, and honor **owner inspection requests** within the timeframe and limits (some records are protected — personnel, attorney-client, specific-owner data). _(Retention & inspection rules are jurisdiction-specific — retrieved 2026-07-17.)_

## Vendors & common-area maintenance

- **Vendor coordination** — **scope** the work, get **competitive bids** (an RFP for anything material), and **verify insurance certificates and licenses** before award (an uninsured vendor's common-area injury becomes the association's liability). Respect the **board-approval threshold** for contract value and the **approved budget** — an over-threshold or off-budget award goes to the **board**.
- **Preventive maintenance** — a **maintenance calendar** for the common elements (landscaping, pool open/close, HVAC/elevator service, roof/gutter, painting cycle, pressure-washing, pest, life-safety) run on **work orders** with an **inspection loop** before payment. Deferred maintenance is deferred cost plus liability.
- **Emergency response** — a documented after-hours / emergency protocol (water intrusion, life-safety) with pre-vetted vendors.

## Insurance & risk (route the design to a broker)

- **Master property policy** — insures the common elements/buildings per the declaration's **coverage form** (**bare-walls**, **all-in / single-entity**, or **original-specifications** — the form must match what the CC&Rs assign to the association vs the unit owner, or a **gap** opens between the master policy and the owners' HO-6 policies).
- **General liability** — the association's exposure to common-area injury (pool, playground, walkways) — adequate limits vs the amenity risk.
- **Directors & officers (D&O)** — protects volunteer board members against governance/decision claims (including some enforcement/discrimination exposure — check the form).
- **Fidelity / crime** — protects association funds against theft by the manager/board; the statute or documents may mandate a **minimum limit**.
- Insurance design (coverage form, limits, deductibles) is a **broker** function — flag the gaps, don't design the program in-plugin. _(Coverage-form norms are volatile — retrieved 2026-07-17.)_

## Developer-to-homeowner transition

- **Transition** — control of the board passes from the **developer/declarant** to the **homeowners** at a trigger (a sale percentage or a date in the declaration/statute).
- **What to do at transition** — obtain the **records, financials, and reserve study** from the developer; commission a **transition study / reserve study** and an **engineering review** to assess whether reserves were adequately funded and the common elements were built to spec; watch the **construction-defect statute-of-limitations / repose window** (a defect claim has a deadline — a legal/engineering matter); and review the **insurance** and vendor contracts inherited from the developer.
- Transition is heavy on **legal and engineering** seams — route the defect claim, the document review, and the reserve/engineering study to the right professionals.

## 2026 standards & regulatory map (dated — volatile, re-verify before quoting)

- **Governing statutes:** each state has its own **condominium act** and/or **HOA/POA act** (many derived from the Uniform Common Interest Ownership Act, UCIOA, but with wide variation). Mandatory provisions override the documents. **Not legal advice — confirm with counsel.** _(Retrieved 2026-07-17.)_
- **Reserve requirements:** several states now **mandate reserve studies and/or reserve funding** (some tightened after high-profile structural failures — e.g., milestone/structural-integrity inspection and reserve mandates for certain condos). Highly jurisdiction-specific. _(Retrieved 2026-07-17; verify the current requirement.)_
- **Collections / debt collection:** federal **FDCPA** and state debt-collection statutes constrain assessment collection (especially by third-party collectors/attorneys); many states impose a **pre-lien notice** and specific procedures. **Route to counsel.** _(Retrieved 2026-07-17.)_
- **Fair housing:** the federal **Fair Housing Act** (and state equivalents) governs enforcement, ARC, and **reasonable-accommodation/modification** requests. _(Retrieved 2026-07-17.)_
- **Records/meetings:** notice periods, quorum, open-meeting, electronic-voting, and records-retention/owner-inspection rules are set by the state act + bylaws — **verify the current rule**. _(Retrieved 2026-07-17.)_
- **Taxes:** community associations commonly file **Form 1120-H** (or 1120) federally — an **accounting-bookkeeping** function. _(Retrieved 2026-07-17.)_
