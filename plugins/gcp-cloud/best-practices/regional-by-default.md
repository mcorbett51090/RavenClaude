# Choose regional resources by default; multi-region by requirement

Prefer regional resources — regional GKE clusters, regional managed instance groups, multi-zone deployments — so a single-zone disruption isn't a full outage. A zone is GCP's cheapest resilience boundary; spanning zones within a region buys high availability for little beyond cross-zone data-transfer cost. Don't reach reflexively for multi-region or a globally-replicated service: it multiplies cost, complicates data consistency, and is only warranted by a stated availability or latency requirement that regional redundancy can't meet (Spanner and multi-region GCS exist for exactly those cases — pay for them when the requirement is real). Pick the region close to users and within your data-residency constraints, go regional within it by default, and treat global as a designed, justified posture rather than a default.
