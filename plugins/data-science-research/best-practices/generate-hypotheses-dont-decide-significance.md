# Generate hypotheses — don't decide significance

Exploratory data science produces the descriptive picture and the candidate effect; whether that effect is *statistically real* — p-values, confidence intervals, power, multiple-comparison correction — is the `applied-statistics` call. Don't p-hack an exploratory pattern into a confirmatory claim by testing until something passes. Frame the finding as a hypothesis with its uncertainty and route the significance question across the seam. Feature importance explains association, not causation; route causal claims to `applied-statistics` too.
