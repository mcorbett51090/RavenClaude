# Plot the distribution; summary statistics lie

A mean, a standard deviation, and a correlation can describe wildly different data — Anscombe's quartet and the Datasaurus dozen share summary statistics while looking nothing alike. Plot the distribution before trusting any single number: the mean of a bimodal column describes nobody, an outlier can manufacture or erase a correlation, and a Simpson's-paradox confounder can flip a relationship's sign when you disaggregate by group. Read every plot adversarially — look for multimodality, skew, fat tails, clipping, suspicious spikes (a default-value sentinel masquerading as data), and the subgroup that reverses the headline. The visual is what makes the downstream score interpretable and the finding's caveats honest; a summary statistic without the picture behind it is a claim you haven't actually checked.
