# Every finding carries its uncertainty

A reported number with no sample size, no confounder caveat, and no "this could be wrong if…" is a liability, not a finding. Communicate the range and the assumption that breaks it, not just the headline. State the sample size, name the confounders, and flag the cleaning/modeling decisions that, if wrong, would change the conclusion. An exploratory finding is a *candidate* effect — describe it as such and route the "is it statistically real" question to `applied-statistics` rather than overclaiming.
