# Senior-care benchmarks & regulatory context (2025–2026)

Dated occupancy, labor, and length-of-stay benchmarks for a senior-care engagement, plus the regulatory frame. Clinical/survey determinations route to the qualified authority.

## Occupancy & demand

Senior-housing occupancy rose through 2025 (18 consecutive quarters of gains), ending the year ~89.1% overall and ~87.7% for assisted living in Q4 2025, with inventory growth at record lows — a demand-driven census tailwind (§3 #1).

## Length of stay

Average assisted-living length of stay is ~22 months, with ~60% of residents moving out to a higher level of care; rising move-in acuity shortens stays and raises the census-replacement burden (§3 #5).

## Labor

Labor (care-staff wages/benefits) is the largest expense by far; ~63% of communities report a staff shortage and ~87% report hiring difficulty, with assisted-living turnover ~41% (PCAs ~49%) — turnover is unit economics, not HR overhead (§3 #6).

## Rates & regulation

Average assisted-living cost reached ~$5,676/month in 2025 (rents climbing ~3–5%/yr); licensing, staffing rules, and survey processes vary by state, and survey/clinical determinations route to the state agency and licensed clinicians (§3 #2, #4).
