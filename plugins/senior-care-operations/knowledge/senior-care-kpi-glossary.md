# Senior-care KPI glossary

**Last reviewed:** 2026-06-05 · benchmark figures web-verified this date (NIC / Aline / USR Engage / zumBrunnen / CMS / trade sources, cited below). Benchmarks are segment-, state-, and year-dependent and carry `[verify-at-use]` markers — calibrate to the community's segment and validate against its actual data before a deliverable (§3 #8).

The metrics a senior-care operation is judged on — formulas, the misreads, and where a figure needs a `[verify-at-use]` or `[unverified]` mark.

## Census metrics

Occupancy, move-in/move-out rate, length of stay, inquiry-to-move-in conversion, time-to-move-in. **Census is a flow** (§3 #1, #7): occupancy(t) = occupancy(t-1) + move-ins − move-outs — never read the point number without the flow.

**Cited benchmarks (`[verify-at-use]`):**

| Metric | Figure | Read |
|---|---|---|
| Senior-housing occupancy (overall) | ~89.1% Q4 2025; ~87.7% for assisted living; 18 consecutive quarters of gains | A single building sliding *against* a rising market is an internal signal, not demand |
| Assisted-living length of stay | ~22 months avg; ~60% move out to a higher level of care | Rising move-in acuity shortens stays → higher census-replacement burden (§3 #5) |
| Inquiry → tour conversion | ~29% | The single most leak-prone stage; lead-response speed is the dominant driver |
| Tour → move-in conversion | ~29–34% (down from ~34% in 2024; top performers 35–42%) | If tours are fine but move-ins lag, the leak is upstream (inquiry→tour), not the close |
| Overall inquiry → move-in | ~12–15% | Multiply the two stages; a lead-buy into a leaking funnel just raises cost-per-move-in |

Sources: NIC — occupancy rose in 2025: https://www.nic.org/news-press/occupancy-rate-for-senior-living-communities-increased-in-2025-as-construction-stalled/ ; Aline — tour-to-move-in conversion: https://alineops.com/blog/lead-scoring-improve-conversions/ ; USR Engage — 2026 marketing benchmarks: https://usrengage.com/senior-living-marketing-benchmarks-2026/ ; The Senior List — assisted-living statistics (LOS): https://www.theseniorlist.com/assisted-living/statistics/ (retrieved 2026-06-05).

## Sales / acquisition-cost metrics

Cost per lead (CPL), cost per move-in (CPMI), lead-response time, channel mix.

**Cited benchmarks (`[verify-at-use]`):**

| Metric | Figure | Read |
|---|---|---|
| Cost per lead (all channels) | ~$431 average; organic/SEO far lower (~$25–$60) | The blended number hides a huge channel spread — manage the mix |
| Cost per move-in (owned channels) | ~$1,200–$2,800 | Referral-agency CPMI is far higher (memory-care fees ~$7,000–$12,000) |
| First-touch tour-booking rate | only ~18–27% book a tour on first touch | Without follow-up/retargeting, 70%+ of the best prospects are lost |

Source: USR Engage — Senior Living Marketing Benchmarks 2026 (CPL, CPMI, conversion by care type): https://usrengage.com/senior-living-marketing-benchmarks-2026/ (retrieved 2026-06-05).

## Labor metrics

Acuity-based hours-per-resident-day (PPD), labor % of revenue, agency-labor share, caregiver turnover (§3 #3, #6). **Staff to acuity-weighted PPD, not a fixed ratio.**

**Cited benchmarks (`[verify-at-use]`):**

| Metric | Figure | Read |
|---|---|---|
| Labor cost % of revenue | ~41% (the largest single expense) | A staffing model that mis-allocates labor is a margin *and* a quality problem |
| Average operating profit | ~15% | Frames how little room a labor mis-allocation leaves |
| Agency-labor cost trend | down from the 2022 peak, but ~200 bps above the 2013–2021 average in 2024 | Agency is a lagging quality indicator + margin leak — bridge only, with an exit date |
| Assisted-living turnover | ~41% overall (~49% for PCAs); ~63% report a staff shortage, ~87% hiring difficulty | Turnover is unit economics, not HR overhead (§3 #6) |
| Acuity-based PPD | community-specific (care-minutes/day per acuity tier ÷ census) | No federal AL ratio; the SNF 3.48-HPRD federal rule is **rescinded** — see below |

Sources: zumBrunnen — 2025 performance trends (labor %, operating profit, agency trend): https://zumbrunnen.com/financial-performance-trends-in-senior-living-a-balancing-act/ ; The Senior List — turnover/shortage statistics: https://www.theseniorlist.com/assisted-living/statistics/ (retrieved 2026-06-05).

## Finance / payer-mix metrics

Average rate, RevPAR/RevPOR, **payer mix** (Medicaid / Medicare FFS / Medicare Advantage / private pay), margin per payer-day.

**Cited benchmarks (`[verify-at-use]`):**

| Metric | Figure | Read |
|---|---|---|
| Average assisted-living cost | ~$5,676/month (2025); rents climbing ~3–5%/yr | A market reference, not the community's own rate |
| SNF Medicare Part A update | +4.2% net (~$1.4B) FY2025; annual PPS updates continuing FY2026 | The Medicare side has a dated rate trajectory; Medicaid is state-set and lower |
| SNF payer-mix economics | high-acuity short-stay Medicare/private cross-subsidizes long-stay Medicaid | A census number with no payer-mix split is uninterpretable at an SNF |
| Medicare Advantage erosion | MA growing, pays variably (often less) than FFS | Model payer-SPECIFIC rates — never the FFS rate on a population drifting to MA |

Sources: NIC MAP / The Senior List (avg AL cost): https://www.nicmap.com/blog/senior-housing-occupancy-rises-in-2q-2025-inventory-growth-at-record-lows/ ; CMS — FY2025 SNF PPS Final Rule: https://www.cms.gov/newsroom/fact-sheets/fiscal-year-2025-skilled-nursing-facility-prospective-payment-system-final-rule-cms-1802-f ; Simon Lee & Associates — SNF payer-mix analysis: https://snf-research.com/ ; Definitive Healthcare — SNF payor mix: https://www.definitivehc.com/resources/healthcare-insights/snf-payor-mix (retrieved 2026-06-05).

## Quality / compliance metrics

Survey deficiency count + scope/severity, incident/fall rate, elopement events, quality measures, acuity mix (§3 #4). Quality is existential — a deficiency closes a building and ends referrals.

## Regulatory status note (volatile — `[verify-at-use]`)

The **federal nursing-home minimum-staffing rule** (3.48 total HPRD / 0.55 RN / 2.45 NA / 24-7 RN, finalized 2024) is **rescinded**: Public Law 119-21 §71111 bars CMS enforcement through **Sept 30, 2034**, and the interim final rule rescinding it is effective **Feb 2, 2026**. **Assisted living** has **no** federal staffing ratio — it is state-regulated, with a growing set of states (e.g. Oregon's Acuity-Based Staffing Tool) tying staffing to acuity. Confirm the resident state's current rule and the live federal status before any deliverable. Sources: AHA — CMS repeal: https://www.aha.org/news/headline/2025-12-02-cms-repeals-minimum-staffing-requirements-skilled-nursing-long-term-care-facilities ; Federal Register — repeal interim final rule: https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2025/12/03/2025-21792/medicare-and-medicaid-programs-repeal-of-minimum-staffing-standards-for-long-term-care-facilities (retrieved 2026-06-05).
