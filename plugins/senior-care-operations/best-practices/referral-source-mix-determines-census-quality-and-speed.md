# Referral Source Mix Determines Census Quality and Speed

**Status:** Pattern
**Domain:** Census acquisition / referral management
**Applies to:** `senior-care-operations`

---

## Why this exists

Not all inquiry sources produce the same move-in probability, acuity mix, or speed. Hospital discharge planner referrals typically have high urgency and move-in speed but sometimes lower ability to pay or higher acuity than the community is equipped for. Family direct inquiries (web, word-of-mouth) tend to be slower to move in but have better financial qualification and lower acuity match risk. Placement agency referrals come with a fee (typically 50–100% of one month's rent) [unverified — training knowledge] and introduce a middleman in the family relationship. A community that relies entirely on hospital discharge planners for census growth is paying in acuity risk and margin compression; one that relies entirely on organic web inquiries may be leaving urgency-driven placements to competitors.

## How to apply

Track referral source mix monthly and manage it as a deliberate strategy.

```
Referral source tracking:
Track for every inquiry: source category, tour completion, move-in, days to move-in
Sources (minimum categories):
  - Hospital discharge planning / SNF social workers
  - Physician / geriatrician referral
  - Placement agency (named)
  - Existing resident / family referral (word-of-mouth)
  - Web / SEO / digital marketing
  - Paid ads (Google, Facebook, etc.)
  - Community events / outreach

Monthly metrics per source:
  - Inquiry volume
  - Tour conversion rate
  - Move-in conversion rate (from inquiry)
  - Average days from inquiry to move-in
  - Average acuity score at move-in (if using an acuity assessment tool)
  - Average monthly revenue per resident (to evaluate payer and acuity quality)

Strategic mix targets [unverified — training knowledge; set to community context]:
  - No single source should account for >40% of total move-ins (concentration risk)
  - Word-of-mouth and physician referral: cultivate — these produce the highest-quality leads
  - Hospital discharge: manage with a dedicated liaison and clear acuity-acceptance criteria
  - Placement agency: use selectively; track net revenue after fee
```

**Do:**
- Assign relationship management to the top 3–5 referral sources by volume — a weekly in-person or phone touchpoint with the key hospital discharge planner is a census investment.
- Build a placement agency policy: which agencies, what fee structure, and when to accept vs. pass on a placement (acuity threshold, payment verification).
- Present referral source mix in the monthly census/sales meeting — it shapes the sales and outreach strategy.

**Don't:**
- Treat all inquiries as equivalent; a placement agency referral that costs one month's rent for a 6-month length-of-stay is a poor business decision even if it fills a unit.
- Chase hospital discharge placements without confirming financial qualification — an acute-placement resident who cannot pay the monthly rate is an immediate census and collections problem.
- Allow any single referral relationship to hold more than 40% of move-in volume without a diversity plan — the relationship's disruption (staff turnover, new competitor relationship) can crater census.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

New communities in the first 6–12 months of operation often rely more heavily on placement agencies and hospital referrals to build initial census — this is acceptable as a ramp strategy but should shift toward a more diversified mix as the community matures.
