# Tour Conversion Rate Is the Primary Sales Efficiency Metric

**Status:** Primary diagnostic
**Domain:** Sales / census acquisition
**Applies to:** `senior-care-operations`

---

## Why this exists

Getting a prospective family to tour the community is the hardest step in the census sales funnel — it requires awareness, trust, and emotional readiness that marketing cannot fully manufacture. Once a family has toured, they are in the highest-probability window of the sales cycle: they are emotionally engaged, they have seen the community, and they have invested time. A community that converts fewer than 20% of tours to move-ins is losing value it has already earned. [unverified — training knowledge] A low tour conversion rate is almost always a follow-up process and relationship management problem, not a product problem — the community that loses a tour lead to a competitor after 10 days of silence is not losing on quality, it is losing on speed.

## How to apply

Track tour conversion rate as a primary sales KPI and build a structured follow-up protocol.

```
Tour conversion funnel:
Metric: Tours completed → move-ins within 90 days
Target: ≥25–35% tour-to-move-in conversion [unverified — training knowledge; benchmark varies by segment]

Follow-up protocol:
  Day 0 (tour day):
    - Email or card with a personalized note from the guide within 24 hours
    - Summarize: what they said was most important, what you heard their concerns were
    - Attach: the pricing proposal customized to their situation (not a generic brochure)

  Day 2–3:
    - Personal phone call: "I was thinking about what you shared about [specific concern]..."
    - Ask: "What would help you feel more confident about the decision?"
    - Offer: second tour, meeting with clinical staff, reference call with a current resident family

  Day 7:
    - If no engagement: reach out through the family member who was NOT the primary contact
    - Often the decision involves multiple family members; one path closing doesn't mean the door is closed

  Day 14:
    - Final outreach: "We have a unit that would be a great match for [resident name]..."
    - Introduce urgency where genuine: unit availability, rate lock, assessment slot

  Day 21+:
    - Move to long-term nurture: monthly check-in, community event invitation, newsletter
    - Some tours convert at 60–90 days; do not abandon leads after 30 days

Tracking:
  - Inquiry source for each tour lead (referral, web, hospital discharge planner, etc.)
  - Days to move-in from tour date
  - Reason tour did not convert (when known)
```

**Do:**
- Train the sales counselor to listen during the tour and reflect back what the family said — the follow-up that quotes the family's own concerns converts better than a generic pitch.
- Respond to inquiry-to-tour scheduling within 2 hours during business hours — tour scheduling delay is one of the strongest predictors of conversion failure. [unverified — training knowledge]
- Review tour-to-move-in conversion rate per sales counselor monthly; variation > 15 percentage points between counselors requires coaching review.

**Don't:**
- Send the same generic follow-up email to every tour lead — a family that said "Mom is afraid of losing her independence" needs a different message than one that said "We need immediate placement."
- Drop a lead from active follow-up after 14 days without moving it explicitly to a long-term nurture track.
- Treat tour conversion as a marketing metric; it is a sales execution metric owned by the sales counselor and the Executive Director.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

Memory care communities with limited availability and waitlists may have a naturally higher conversion rate because scarcity creates urgency. Track conversion and time-to-decision carefully to manage the waitlist efficiently.
