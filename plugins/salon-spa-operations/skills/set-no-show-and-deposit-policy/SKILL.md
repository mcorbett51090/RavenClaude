# Set No-Show & Deposit Policy

A no-show policy without a deposit (or a card on file) is a wish. Build the mechanism, not just the rule.

## The sequence

1. **Set the cancellation window.** The notice you need to resell the slot (commonly 24-48h; longer for high-cost/long services like color or a spa package).
2. **Capture the security.** A deposit at booking, or a card-on-file the system can charge. Online booking that can't hold a card leaks money.
3. **Set the fee.** What a no-show or inside-window cancel costs — a flat fee, a % of service, or forfeiture of the deposit. Size it to the chair-time you can't resell.
4. **Build the reminder cadence.** Confirmations and reminders (e.g. at booking, 48h, 24h) so honest clients can keep or move — reminders reduce mistakes; the deposit covers the deliberate no-show.
5. **Write the client-facing wording.** Clear, friendly, and shown *before* booking — not sprung at checkout.

## What "done" looks like
- The window, the deposit/card mechanism, and the fee are all named.
- The system actually enforces the card-on-file, not just the policy text.
- The wording is visible at booking and consistently applied.

## Anti-patterns
- A policy with reminders but no deposit/card behind it.
- A fee no one ever charges (policy theater).
- High-cost services (color, packages) with the same tiny deposit as a quick trim.
