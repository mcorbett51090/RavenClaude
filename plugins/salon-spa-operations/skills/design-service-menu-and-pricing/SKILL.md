# Design Service Menu & Pricing

Structure the menu so clients can trade up, and price every service to the margin you actually need.

## The sequence

1. **Tier it good-better-best.** For each service family, offer a base, a signature, and a premium option (e.g. single-process / dimensional / corrective color). The middle tier is usually the volume driver; the top tier raises the anchor.
2. **Add the add-ons.** Gloss, bond-builder, deep treatment, scalp massage, beard detail — pure ticket lift, little extra chair time.
3. **Cost each service.** Stylist time × labor cost + product/back-bar + chair overhead. This is the floor.
4. **Price to a target margin** above that floor — not to the shop down the street, whose cost structure you don't share.
5. **Map the service mix to margin per chair-hour.** Know which services and add-ons carry the shop; shape the menu and stylist focus toward them without gutting the loss-leaders that bring clients in.

## What "done" looks like
- Every service has a tier structure and at least one add-on path.
- Every price has a known cost behind it and clears the target margin.
- The menu names the high-margin-per-chair-hour services explicitly.

## Anti-patterns
- One flat price per service (no trade-up path).
- Pricing by matching competitors instead of to your cost + margin.
- A menu with no add-ons (the easiest revenue, left on the floor).
