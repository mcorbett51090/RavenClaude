# Improve Rebooking Rate

Rebooking rate — the % of clients who book their next visit before leaving the chair — is the core KPI of the whole business. The cheapest client is the one already in the chair.

## The sequence

1. **Measure the baseline.** Pull rebooking % from the POS/booking system (clients who left with a next appointment ÷ clients seen). Segment by stylist and by new-vs-returning.
2. **Set a target.** A concrete number to manage to; compare to the dated benchmark in the reference map (re-verify before quoting).
3. **Install the at-the-chair rebook.** The strongest lever: the stylist/front desk books the next visit *before checkout*, framed around the service interval ("your color will want a refresh in 6 weeks — let's hold that slot").
4. **Build the retention loop on top.** Confirmations, reminders, and a lapsed-client win-back for the ones who slipped — but this is the band-aid; the at-the-chair rebook is the cure.
5. **Coach and measure per stylist.** Rebooking is a trainable behavior; surface the rate by stylist and coach the laggards.

## What "done" looks like
- A measured baseline + a named target, segmented by stylist.
- The at-the-chair rebook is the default behavior, scripted at checkout.
- A lapsed-client loop catches the ones who didn't rebook.

## Anti-patterns
- Treating a reactivation email as the primary lever (it's the band-aid for a missed at-the-chair rebook).
- Measuring visits without measuring rebooking %.
- No per-stylist visibility (so no one is coached).

The retention loop's *campaigns* (paid/social/email demand) are a `marketing-operations` seam; the at-the-chair operational rebook is yours.
