# Retail Attach & Service Mix

Two levers move margin without adding a single appointment: what the client buys on the way out, and which services fill the hours. This skill works both.

## The loop

1. **Read revenue AND margin per service hour.** A full book of the lowest-margin service is a busy business losing ground. Rank services by contribution per hour (price minus product/back-bar and time cost), not by popularity.
2. **Tilt the mix deliberately.** Promote, schedule, and price toward the higher-contribution services and add-ons — an add-on service is incremental margin on an hour you already sold. Coordinate menu pricing with the pricing tree in [`../../knowledge/salon-spa-decision-trees.md`](../../knowledge/salon-spa-decision-trees.md).
3. **Attach retail at the chair.** Product attach rides on the provider's earned trust — the cheapest incremental margin in the building. Build the recommendation into the service (during, not at the register) and measure attach per provider. Treat benchmark attach rates as `[verify-at-use]`.
4. **Wire it to checkout.** Retail and the rebook happen at the same moment — coordinate with [`../booking-and-no-show-control/SKILL.md`](../booking-and-no-show-control/SKILL.md) so the checkout captures both.

## Metrics

| Metric | Reads | Note |
|---|---|---|
| Retail attach rate | tickets with retail / total tickets | The margin lever the owner controls; `[verify-at-use]` on norms |
| Retail-to-service ratio | retail $ / service $ | The classic salon health ratio |
| Contribution per service hour | (price - product - time cost) / hr | Reveals the low-margin full book |
| Add-on / upgrade rate | add-on tickets / total | Incremental margin on sold hours |

## Anti-patterns

- Chasing traffic when the margin lever is attach at the existing chair.
- Ranking services by count instead of contribution per hour.
- Retail "at the register" instead of recommended during the service.
- Discounting the high-margin add-on that clients would pay full price for.
