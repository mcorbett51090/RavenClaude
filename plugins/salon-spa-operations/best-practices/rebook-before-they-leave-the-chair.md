# Rebook before they leave the chair

**Status:** Absolute rule
**Domain:** Front desk / retention
**Applies to:** `salon-spa-operations`

> Operations rule. No client PII. Benchmark rebooking rates are `[verify-at-use]`.

---

## Why this exists

The single highest-yield act at the front desk is booking the **next** appointment before the client leaves. A client on the calendar is retained; a "I'll call to book" is a hope you'll pay marketing money to convert again. Rebooking rate is the leading indicator of a full future book — it predicts next quarter's calendar better than today's foot traffic.

## How to apply

- The provider recommends the next-visit interval **at the chair** — it is part of the service, not an upsell.
- The front desk offers a **specific slot**, not "call us to book."
- For the hesitant client, offer a deposit-to-hold or a tentative booking plus a reminder.
- Track rebooking rate **per provider** and coach to it.

**Do:** book the next visit at checkout; measure the rate per provider.
**Don't:** end a visit with "call us"; treat rebooking as optional.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

A one-time service with no natural cadence (a special-event style) genuinely has nothing to rebook — capture the client for retail and referral instead.
