# A no-show policy needs a deposit behind it

**Status:** Absolute rule
**Domain:** Booking / policy
**Applies to:** `salon-spa-operations`

---

## Why this exists

A no-show or late cancel is unresellable chair-time — pure lost revenue, worst for long high-cost services like color. A policy that is only words ("please give 24 hours' notice") changes nothing for the client who never intended to show. The deposit or card-on-file is the *mechanism* that makes the chair-time recoverable; the sentence without it is a wish.

## How to apply

- **Capture security:** a deposit at booking, or a card-on-file the system can actually charge. Online booking that can't hold a card leaks money.
- **Set a window** sized to the time you need to resell the slot (commonly 24-48h; longer for color/packages).
- **Set a fee** equal to the unresellable chair-time (flat, % of service, or deposit forfeiture).
- **Pair with a reminder cadence** so honest clients can keep or move — reminders cut mistakes, the deposit covers the deliberate no-show.
- **Show the wording before booking**, never at checkout.

**Do:** make the booking system enforce the card/deposit, not just display the policy.
**Don't:** rely on reminders alone, or set a fee no one ever charges (policy theater).

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

A long-standing, reliable regular may be exempted by discretion — but the policy still exists as the default; the exception is named, not the rule.
