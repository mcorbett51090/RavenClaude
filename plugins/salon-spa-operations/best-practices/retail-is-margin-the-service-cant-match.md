# Retail is margin the service can't match

**Status:** Pattern
**Domain:** Margin / the chair
**Applies to:** `salon-spa-operations`

> Operations rule. Benchmark attach rates are `[verify-at-use]`. No client PII.

---

## Why this exists

Retail product attach is the cheapest incremental margin in the building. It doesn't need more traffic, a bigger space, or another chair — it rides on the trust the provider already earned during the service. A shop that captures the service but not the product the client will buy *somewhere* is leaving margin on the table that no amount of new bookings replaces as efficiently.

## How to apply

- Recommend product **during** the service, where the trust is, not at the register as an afterthought.
- Measure retail attach rate and retail-to-service ratio **per provider**; coach the laggards.
- Stock to what the services actually use and clients actually reorder — dead retail stock is frozen cash, same as a dead frame board.
- Read contribution per service hour so you promote the mix that carries the best margin, then attach on top.

**Do:** build the recommendation into the service; measure attach per provider.
**Don't:** treat retail as a register add-on; chase traffic when the lever is attach.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

A pure-service concept that has deliberately chosen not to carry retail (space, brand, or licensing reasons) should lean harder on service mix and add-ons instead — the margin principle holds even where the retail lever is closed.
