# A no-show is inventory you can't resell

**Status:** Absolute rule
**Domain:** Front desk / calendar
**Applies to:** `salon-spa-operations`

> Operations rule. Deposit / card-on-file rules carry payment-processor and consumer-protection implications — `[verify-at-use]` and route to a professional. No client PII.

---

## Why this exists

A chair-hour or room-hour that no-shows is gone forever — unlike a product on a shelf, you cannot resell tomorrow the slot you lost today. That is why a no-show policy is worth the friction: the loss is total and unrecoverable. But the policy's job is to **change behavior, not collect fees** — the fee is a consolation, the filled chair is the win.

## How to apply

- **Prevent first:** a confirming reminder cadence with one-tap reschedule stops more no-shows than any fee recovers.
- **Then enforce:** a notice window, a deposit or card-on-file sized to the actual no-show rate, a fair fee, and consistent enforcement with an exception path.
- **Backfill:** every late cancel triggers a waitlist offer / auto-fill so a lost slot becomes a sale.
- Size the policy to the **measured** no-show rate — a low rate needs a lighter touch than a chronic one.

**Do:** prevent with reminders; enforce consistently; backfill from the waitlist.
**Don't:** state a policy you never enforce; punish loyal clients with no reschedule path.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

A brand-new client with no history for a short, low-value service may not warrant a deposit — reserve the heaviest friction for new clients on long/high-value slots and chronic offenders.
