# Rebooking rate is the core KPI

**Status:** Absolute rule
**Domain:** Booking / retention
**Applies to:** `salon-spa-operations`

---

## Why this exists

A salon, spa, or barbershop lives on repeat visits. The cheapest, most profitable client is the one already in the chair — acquiring a new one costs far more than rebooking an existing one. The rebooking rate (% of clients who book their next visit before they leave) is the leading indicator of revenue stability; everything else (marketing, discounts, promotions) is more expensive compensation for a low one.

## How to apply

- **Measure it** from the POS/booking system: clients who left with a next appointment ÷ clients seen. Segment by stylist and by new-vs-returning.
- **Set a target** and manage to it (see the dated benchmark range — re-verify before quoting).
- **Install the at-the-chair rebook** as the default: the next visit is booked before checkout, framed around the service interval.
- **Coach per stylist** — rebooking is a trainable behavior; surface the rate and coach the laggards.

**Do:** treat the at-the-chair rebook as the primary retention lever.
**Don't:** lean on reactivation emails as the main tool — they're the band-aid for a missed rebook.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

A one-off / event service (bridal, a single tourist booking) won't rebook — exclude it from the denominator rather than letting it depress the number.
