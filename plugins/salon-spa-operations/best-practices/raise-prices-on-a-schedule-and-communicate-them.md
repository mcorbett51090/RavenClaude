# Raise prices on a schedule and communicate them

**Status:** Absolute rule
**Domain:** Pricing
**Applies to:** `salon-spa-operations`

---

## Why this exists

Most salon owners are *underpriced* and *afraid* to raise prices — so they don't, until margin forces a sudden jump that lands as a checkout surprise and burns the trust rebooking depends on. The damage is almost never the price itself; it's the surprise and the sense of being nickeled. A planned, communicated, segmented increase raises margin while protecting the relationship.

## How to apply

- **Price to a target margin**, not to the shop down the street whose cost structure you don't share.
- **Raise on a schedule** (e.g. annually), so it's expected, not a shock.
- **Lead with value** — what the client gets, not "costs went up."
- **Segment / grandfather** where retention is fragile: by service, by stylist level, or with notice to the existing book.
- **Tell the existing book first**, before they discover it at checkout.

**Do:** communicate the increase to current clients ahead of the change.
**Don't:** spring a higher number at the register, or hold prices below cost out of fear ("a loved salon that loses money closes").

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

A brand-new menu or a new stylist tier sets fresh prices rather than "raising" — there's no existing book to grandfather, but the value framing still applies.
