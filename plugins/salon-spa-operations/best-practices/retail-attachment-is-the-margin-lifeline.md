# Retail attachment is the margin lifeline

**Status:** Absolute rule
**Domain:** Pricing / retail
**Applies to:** `salon-spa-operations`

---

## Why this exists

Service revenue is capped by chair-hours and stylist labor — there is a hard ceiling on how much one chair can earn in a day. Retail (product sold at the chair) has no such ceiling and the highest margin in the shop, and it doubles as the cheapest retention hook: a client using your products comes back to replenish. A salon that ignores retail leaves its most profitable, most defensible revenue line on the shelf.

## How to apply

- **Set a retail-to-service ratio target** and track it (see the dated benchmark range — re-verify before quoting).
- **Stock what stylists use in service**, so the recommend is the product the client just saw working — not a random shelf item.
- **Coach the in-service recommend**, not a checkout hard-sell: the stylist prescribes what they used; the front desk closes it at checkout.
- **Measure attachment per stylist** and coach — like rebooking, it's a trainable behavior.

**Do:** treat the recommend as part of the service (an authentic prescription).
**Don't:** stock a shelf of SKUs nobody uses in-service, or turn checkout into a pressured upsell.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

A pure booth-rental shop where renters own their own retail — there the attachment goal lives with each renter, not the owner; the owner's lever is the lease terms, not a house ratio.
