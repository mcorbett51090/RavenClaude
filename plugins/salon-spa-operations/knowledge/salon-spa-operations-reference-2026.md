## Core KPIs — typical ranges (rule-of-thumb)

| KPI | Common rule-of-thumb range | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Rebooking rate** (booked next visit before leaving) | ~30% baseline → 50-70%+ for strong, coached salons | The single most-protected KPI; new-client rebooking is usually far lower than returning. |
| **New-client retention** (returns within ~90 days) | often only ~25-40% on first visit | The first-visit rebook is the highest-leverage moment. |
| **Retail-to-service ratio** | ~5-10% common; ~15-25% for retail-strong shops | Highest-margin line; coached, not pitched. |
| **Chair / room utilization** | target ~70-85% of available hours booked | Below this, mid-week gaps and inter-appointment gaps dominate. |
| **No-show + late-cancel rate** | ~5-15% without a deposit; deposits cut it sharply | Higher for new clients and free/low-cost bookings. |
| **Average ticket** | service + add-on + retail combined | Add-ons and retail are the cheapest lift (no new acquisition cost). |

## Compensation models — the shape of the trade (NOT legal advice)

| Model | Typical structure (rule-of-thumb) | Owner controls | Owner carries |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Commission** | ~35-60% to stylist (often tiered up with tenure/sales); sometimes salary-plus-commission or hourly-plus-commission | Brand, schedule, pricing, products, client book | Payroll taxes, benefits, supplies, employer obligations |
| **Booth / chair rental** | fixed weekly/monthly rent per chair; renter keeps service + retail revenue | Little — renter sets price, schedule, products | A space + utilities; near-zero per-stylist overhead |
| **Hybrid** | rental+service-fee, commission+product-charge, or "salon suite" lease | Varies by design | Varies by design |

- **Commission % and rent figures vary widely** by market, segment, and stylist tenure — treat the above as starting points only.
- **Worker classification (employee vs independent contractor) is a legal test**, not a consequence of the label you use — it turns on control, tools, and schedule, and the test differs by jurisdiction. **Never represent a model as "compliant" or "legal."** → `people-operations-hr` for the verdict; → `accounting-bookkeeping` for payroll/tax/sales-tax mechanics.

## No-show / deposit policy — common shapes

- **Cancellation window:** commonly 24-48h; 48-72h for long/high-cost services (color, packages, spa days).
- **Security:** card-on-file (system-charged) for routine services; a deposit at booking for high-cost/long services and new clients.
- **Fee:** flat fee, a % of service, or deposit forfeiture — sized to the **unresellable chair-time**.
- **Effect:** a captured card or deposit reliably reduces no-shows vs reminders alone; reminders cover honest mistakes, the deposit covers deliberate ones.

---

## Booking / scheduling notes

- **Color processing-time overlap is capacity, not a double-book** — a colorist can start a second client during the first's development time, but it must be *encoded* (processing-time blocks, overlapping appointment types) in the system, not held in memory.
- **Online booking that can't hold a card or deposit leaks money** — pick a system that enforces the policy, not just states it.
- **Off-peak (mid-week / early-day) gaps** respond to pricing and service-mix levers; chronic emptiness across all slots is a *demand* problem (a `marketing-operations` seam).
