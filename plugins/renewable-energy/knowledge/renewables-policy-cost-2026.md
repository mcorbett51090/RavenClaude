# Solar cost & incentive landscape (2025–2026)

Dated cost and policy context. The ITC landscape shifted in 2025 — every figure carries a source and a retrieval date.

## Cost per watt

Average residential cost-per-watt ~$2.56 in 2025 (range ~$2.10/W in AZ/TX to ~$3.65/W in TN). A typical ~8.5 kW residential system cost ~$24,000–$32,000 before incentives; net cost after all incentives ranged ~$1.20/W to ~$2.60/W (§3 #4).

## ITC shift

The residential 25D credit ended Dec 31, 2025 — the biggest U.S. clean-energy incentive shift in two decades. The remaining homeowner pathway is third-party ownership (leases/PPAs/prepaid) under the 48E business ITC through end of 2027 (§3 #3).

## Qualifying costs

The ITC covers panels, inverters, installation labor, permitting, and utility interconnection fees — design the structure to the live pathway (§3 #3).
