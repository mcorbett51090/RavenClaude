# IP Accountability Is Non-Negotiable at Every Site

**Status:** Absolute rule
**Domain:** Investigational product / site operations
**Applies to:** `clinical-trials`

---

## Why this exists

Investigational product (IP) accountability — the complete record of product received, dispensed, returned, and destroyed at each site — is a GCP and GMP requirement with zero tolerance for unexplained discrepancies. An unreconciled IP discrepancy at an FDA or EMA inspection is an automatic critical finding. It suggests potential IP diversion, unblinding, or fabricated dosing data, regardless of the actual cause. IP accountability failures have resulted in clinical holds, Complete Response Letters, and in rare cases criminal referrals. No enrollment benefit at a site justifies skipping or delaying IP reconciliation.

## How to apply

IP accountability must be current at all times, not only at study closure.

```
IP accountability non-negotiables:
1. At receipt:
   - Log each shipment: date received, quantity, lot number, expiry date, condition on receipt
   - Temperature excursion assessed and sponsor/QA notified same day if observed
   - Discrepancy between packing list and received quantity → reported within 24 hours

2. At dispensing:
   - Log: subject ID, kit/bottle number, dispensing date, dose dispensed, dispensing staff ID
   - Randomization record linked to the dispensing log (for randomized studies)
   - IVRS/IWRS confirmation number recorded if applicable

3. At return/destruction:
   - Returned product logged with quantity, lot, condition
   - Reconciliation equation: received = dispensed + returned + destroyed + any unexplained
   - Unexplained discrepancy > 0: escalate to sponsor within 5 business days; never close without resolution

4. At study close:
   - Final IP reconciliation signed by PI and witnessed before site closure visit ends
   - All reconciliation documents retained in the Investigator Site File for the required retention period
```

**Do:**
- Verify IP reconciliation completeness at every monitoring visit, not only at close-out.
- Train site pharmacists and coordinators on the reconciliation equation and escalation threshold.
- Include IP discrepancy rate in the site performance tier metrics.

**Don't:**
- Allow the explanation "we think it was dispensed but can't find the record" to close an IP discrepancy.
- Permit the study drug to be stored outside the specified conditions without an immediate sponsor deviation report.
- Accept a site's verbal reconciliation — the written reconciliation log is the record of truth.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

Open-label studies with commercially available comparators that are not investigational sometimes have reduced IP accountability requirements — confirm the regulatory guidance for each jurisdiction and document the basis for any reduced accountability requirement in the protocol.
