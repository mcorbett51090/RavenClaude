# Recruitment is a costed pipeline, not a hope

**Status:** Absolute rule. **Constitution:** §3, §4.

## Use when
Any clinical trials deliverable where this question is in play.

## The rule
It costs ~$6,533 to recruit a patient and ~$19,533 to replace one lost to non-compliance; recruitment and retention consume ~20–30% of trial budget. Plan it as a funnel with a cost per stage.

## Why
This is house opinion §3 #2. Getting it wrong is the most common way a clinical trials analysis misleads the operator who acts on it.

## How to apply
- Apply it before reaching for a method.
- Make the relevant number carry a definition, window, and baseline (§3 #1).
- Cite the source + date for any external figure, or mark it `[unverified — training knowledge]`.
