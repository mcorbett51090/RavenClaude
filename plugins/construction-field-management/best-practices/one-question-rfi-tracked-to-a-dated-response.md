# An RFI asks one answerable question, tracked to a dated response

A good RFI states the conflict with drawing/spec references, asks exactly one specific question, proposes a resolution, flags the cost/schedule impact, and gives a needed-by date. "Please advise" or five bundled questions wastes a review cycle the schedule can't afford. Log every RFI with date sent, ball-in-court, and date returned — the log is the record and, in a dispute, the evidence.
