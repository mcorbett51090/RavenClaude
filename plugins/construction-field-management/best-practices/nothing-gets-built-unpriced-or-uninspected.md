# Nothing gets built unpriced or uninspected

Scope-bearing RFI answers and field directives become priced, time-impacted change orders *before* they're built; quality is verified at a hold point *before* the work is covered up. Building first and pricing or inspecting later is how margin and quality disappear — a verbal "just do it" with no written authorization is a claim risk, and a defect locked under finishes is expensive to find. Price it, authorize it, inspect it, then build.
