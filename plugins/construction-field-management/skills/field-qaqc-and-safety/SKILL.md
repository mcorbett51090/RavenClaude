# Field QA/QC & Safety

## Inspection-and-test plan (ITP)
Key the ITP to the spec. Define **hold points** (work cannot proceed past until inspected and signed off — e.g. rebar before the pour) and **witness points** (inspected during, work continues). Add special inspections (IBC Ch. 17), mockups, and first-work-installed checks. A hold point with no teeth is a checkbox — tie sign-off to the next activity, and inspect before cover-up. Log non-conformances and disposition them (rework / repair / use-as-is / reject); a sub's defect is a backcharge — flag it to `cost-and-change-controls-lead`.

## Punch list (to zero)
Deduplicate, then assign each item a responsible trade, a location, and a date. Substantial completion is not final completion. Track to zero — a 400-line list with no owners and no dates never closes.

## Safety (JHA + toolbox talk)
A JHA breaks the task into steps and names the hazard and the specific control for each (fall protection at height, exclusion zones, rigging, LOTO, shoring). The toolbox talk matches the day's actual high-risk work, with a sign-in. Cite the applicable OSHA 29 CFR 1926 requirement as the floor; the control is the one this task needs.

## Closeout
Substantial vs. final completion; O&M manuals, as-builts/record drawings, warranties, attic stock, final inspections, CO. An incomplete package holds the owner's retainage.

## Output
An ITP with hold points, a punch-to-zero plan, a JHA + toolbox talk, or a closeout package. Route the acceptance spec to `architecture-aec`, rework cost/retainage to `cost-and-change-controls-lead`, the schedule impact to `project-management`.
