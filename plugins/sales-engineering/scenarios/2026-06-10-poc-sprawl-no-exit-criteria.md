## Situation

A mid-market prospect asked for "a quick POC" of a data-integration product. The SE, eager to keep momentum, spun up a sandbox the same week with no written success criteria — "let them try it on their data and see."

## Constraints

- A friendly technical contact, but no confirmed champion with internal power.
- No agreed decision criteria — the buying team had not said what a "yes" looked like.
- The prospect kept adding "can it also…?" use cases as the POC ran.

## What we tried

1. First two weeks: the contact tested ad-hoc, hit a couple of edge cases, and asked for two new connectors "to really evaluate it."
2. The SE built them (unpaid implementation creep). The POC slid to six weeks.
3. At the end there was no scorecard — just a vague "it mostly worked, we'll discuss internally." The deal then went quiet.

## Resolution

The team restarted the engagement under the discipline: gated the POC (the four preconditions surfaced that there was **no champion** — the contact couldn't drive a decision), paused, and ran a focused executive discovery to find the economic buyer and a real champion. They then re-scoped a **2-week, signed** POC with three measurable criteria and a kill rule. With criteria the champion owned, the second POC produced a clean technical-win scorecard and the deal closed.

## Lesson

The original POC failed before it started — no champion, no decision criteria, no exit criteria. A POC you can't fail is one you can't win, and a POC with no champion has no one to carry the result. Gate the POC (tree #3), get measurable criteria **signed before kickoff**, and time-box hard. The cheaper alternative (executive discovery first) was the actual next move, not a sandbox.
