# Discovery before demo

**Status:** Absolute rule.

**Use when:** you're about to demo, or someone hands you "just give them a quick demo."

## The rule

**A demo with no discovered, quantified pain is a feature tour — and a feature tour loses.** Run `technical-discovery` first; name the critical business issue and its business impact before you build a single demo beat.

## Why

A demo is persuasive only when it shows the buyer *their* pain being resolved. Without discovery you don't know which pain to show, so you default to touring features — which educates instead of selling, bores the buyer, and surfaces capabilities they didn't ask about (inviting objections you didn't need). The buyer buys the resolution of a critical business issue; you can't resolve a pain you never uncovered.

## How

1. Confirm pain is **named and quantified** (the MEDDPICC "Metric"). If not, the next move is discovery, not a demo.
2. Build the demo storyline as a `beat → pain → impact` table; cut any beat with an empty pain column.
3. Lead with the compelling result (Great Demo! "do-it-first"); peel back only the requested layers.

## Exceptions

- **A pre-sales "overview" demo for a cold audience** (e.g., a webinar, a first-touch) where discovery hasn't happened yet — acceptable, but label it as such and keep it short; it is awareness, not an evaluation demo. The moment it's a named opportunity, discovery precedes the tailored demo.
