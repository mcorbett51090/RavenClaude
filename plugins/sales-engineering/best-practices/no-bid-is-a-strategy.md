# No-bid is a strategy, not a failure

**Status:** Pattern (strong default; deviate only with a written reason).

**Use when:** an RFP/RFI lands and the reflex is "respond to everything."

## The rule

**Qualify every RFP before writing a word, and decline the ones you can't win.** A graceful no-bid frees the team to win a winnable one; the bids you decline fund the ones you take.

## Why

Reflexively responding to every RFP burns the most expensive resource you have — SE and proposal time — on cold, low-probability, or incumbent-wired bids. RFPs are frequently issued to satisfy a procurement requirement when the buyer has already chosen a vendor (the requirements are over-specific and match one competitor's feature list). Spending a week to come second in a wired bid is worse than spending zero and putting that week into a deal where you have a champion and a fighting chance.

## How

Traverse the go/no-go tree (relationship/champion? fit to strengths? wired for an incumbent? effort justified by win-prob × deal size?). On a no-bid, decline gracefully — you want to be invited to the next one.

## Deviate when

- **Strategic logo / market entry** — a marquee account or a new segment can justify bidding a low-probability RFP for the relationship and learning, even knowing the odds. Write down that reason; don't let "strategic" become an excuse to bid everything.
