# Discovery & demo playbook

> _Last reviewed: 2026-06-10._ The durable craft behind value-based discovery and pain-mapped demos. Frameworks named here (MEDDPICC, Command of the Message, Great Demo!) are widely-published, evergreen sales-engineering method — they don't carry version/price volatility.

---

## MEDDPICC — the qualification spine

A checklist for what you must know to forecast and win a complex B2B deal. Treat each letter as a *gap to close in discovery*, not a box to tick once.

| Letter | What it means | The discovery question | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|---|
| **M** — Metrics | The quantified business impact of solving the pain | "What does this problem cost you in time / money / risk today?" | The number the business case and ROI hang on |
| **E** — Economic buyer | The person who can release the budget | "Who ultimately signs off on a purchase like this?" | A technical win that never reaches the EB stalls |
| **D** — Decision criteria | The (often unwritten) yardsticks they'll judge on | "How will you decide between options?" | You either shape these or lose to someone who did |
| **D** — Decision process | The steps + dates from eval to signature | "Walk me through how a decision like this gets made here" | No process map → no forecast, surprise stalls |
| **P** — Paper process | Procurement, legal, security review | "What does procurement/security need from us?" | Where deals die quietly after the technical win |
| **I** — Identify pain | The compelling reason to change *now* | "What happens if you do nothing?" | No pain → no urgency → "no decision" wins |
| **C** — Champion | Someone with power + personal pain who sells for you | "Who internally most wants this to happen?" | Distinguish champion vs coach vs blocker |
| **C** — Competition | The alternatives, including status quo | "What else are you considering?" | Status quo is the most common competitor |

**The Metric is the keystone.** A pain you can't quantify ("it's a bit slow") doesn't sell. Push every pain to its business impact and who feels it.

## Command of the Message — frame the pain, not the feature

- **Lead with the buyer's required capabilities and the pain they resolve**, not your feature list. The buyer buys the resolution of a critical business issue.
- **Differentiate on value, not specs.** "We do X" is weak; "you can't do <required capability> today, which costs you <metric>, and here's how that's resolved" is strong.
- **Make it about them.** Every claim ties back to a pain *they* named in discovery.

## Great Demo! — illustrate, don't educate

The single most useful demo discipline. Two rules:

1. **Do-It-first.** Open with the **compelling result** — the "after" state that resolves the discovered pain — *before* any setup, login, or navigation. Show the destination, then (only if asked) the journey.
2. **Peel back the layers.** After the result lands, reveal *how* — but only the depth the buyer asks for. Stop when the question is answered. The opposite — the "menu tour" where you click every tab — educates instead of selling and dilutes the result.

**Map the storyline before you build it:** a table of `demo beat → discovered pain it resolves → business impact`. A beat with an empty pain column is cut. (See [`se-engagement-decision-trees.md`](se-engagement-decision-trees.md) tree #4.)

**A demo is a conversation.** Insert check-in questions ("does that match how you'd use it?"). If you're talking more than ~half the time, you've slid into educating.

## The honesty boundary (the SE's cardinal rule)

Pre-decide, before the demo, where you will say:

- **"That's on the roadmap"** (and roughly when) — never dressed up as shipped.
- **"We don't do that — here's the workaround / here's how customers handle it."**
- **"Let's prove that in a POC"** — when it's a real, reachable gap.

A fabricated "yes, we do that" wins the moment and loses the deal — it surfaces in the POC or in production, and it's the fastest way to destroy the trust that is the SE's entire value. **An honest "we don't do that well" builds more trust than three smooth yeses.**
