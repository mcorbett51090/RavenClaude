# License renewal timelines are operational risk — track them like cash

**Status:** Absolute rule
**Domain:** Cannabis operations / licensing / business continuity
**Applies to:** `cannabis-operations`

---

## Why this exists

A cannabis license is not a credential that renews automatically on good standing — most state regimes require affirmative renewal applications with documentation, fees, and regulatory review, often 60-120 days before expiration. Operators who treat renewal as an administrative calendar item rather than an operational risk routinely miss the filing window during a busy period, discover a new requirement (a background check, an updated floor plan, a revised financial disclosure) they are not prepared to produce, or find that a key principal's state agent card has lapsed. An expired license suspends all operations — sales, transfers, manufacturing, cultivation — until reinstatement, and reinstatement timelines are measured in weeks to months, not days.

## How to apply

Maintain a license-renewal tracker as a standing operational document:

```
License renewal tracker — [entity] — updated [date]

License type:                    ____
License number:                  ____
Issuing authority:               ____
Expiration date:                 ____
Renewal application window opens:____  (typically 90-120 days before expiry)
Renewal application deadline:    ____  (typically 60-90 days before expiry)
Fee amount (cite source + date): $____ [state fee schedule — URL — date]

Required renewal documents (check against current state requirements):
  [ ] Updated premises diagram
  [ ] Financial disclosure (updated)
  [ ] Principal officer background checks (current within ___ months)
  [ ] Agent ID card renewals (individual licenses for key staff)
  [ ] Certificate of good standing (state business entity)
  [ ] Other: ____

Owner/point of contact for renewal:  ____
90-day alert scheduled:              Y/N — date: ____
60-day alert scheduled:              Y/N — date: ____
30-day escalation owner:             ____
```

Set calendar alerts 90, 60, and 30 days before the renewal filing deadline (not the expiration date).

**Do:**
- Treat individual agent ID card renewals as part of the license-renewal horizon — key personnel with lapsed agent IDs can individually block operations in some states.
- Pre-file the background check requests 120 days before expiry — third-party background check services introduce their own lead time.
- Confirm the current fee schedule at the time of filing; fee increases between renewal cycles are common and the check must clear to complete the renewal.

**Don't:**
- Treat the expiration date as the deadline — it is the consequence of missing the filing deadline.
- Allow a change in beneficial ownership without confirming whether it triggers a license transfer or a new application rather than a standard renewal.
- Assume renewal requirements are stable from the prior cycle; regulators add documentation requirements between cycles.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

- Provisional and conditional licenses (pre-build, pre-inspection) have conversion timelines, not renewal timelines — track them separately with the same urgency.
- Some states offer a statutory holdover period (operations may continue during a pending renewal if filed on time) — confirm whether the holdover applies and whether it covers all license types or only certain tiers.
