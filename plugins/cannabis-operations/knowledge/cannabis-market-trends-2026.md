# Cannabis market & rules (2025–2026)

Dated market and regulatory context. The market and rules move constantly — every figure carries a source and a retrieval date.

> ## ⚠️ UPDATE — partial federal rescheduling (2026-04-23, effective 2026-04-28)
>
> On **2026-04-23** the DOJ/DEA issued a final order (**effective 2026-04-28**) downscheduling **FDA-approved marijuana drugs AND state-licensed MEDICAL marijuana** from Schedule I to **Schedule III**. Consequences for affected operators:
>
> - **IRC §280E no longer applies** to those operators — a major margin/tax relief (Schedule III is not a §280E-disallowed substance).
> - A **DEA-registration pathway** is created for the rescheduled activity.
> - **RECREATIONAL / adult-use marijuana remains Schedule I and is unaffected** — §280E still applies to it.
> - A **broader-rescheduling administrative hearing runs 2026-06-29 to 2026-07-15** (could extend Schedule III to adult-use; outcome pending — do not assume).
>
> This supersedes the older "moving to Schedule III *would* remove 280E" framing below for **medical / FDA-approved** operators specifically; for adult-use the older framing still describes a possible future, not current law. State the operator's segment (medical vs adult-use) before applying.
>
> Source: [Federal Register — Schedules of Controlled Substances: Rescheduling of Marijuana (2026-08177)](https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2026/04/28/2026-08177/schedules-of-controlled-substances-rescheduling-of-marijuana) — retrieved 2026-06-25.

## Market size

The U.S. cannabis market was projected at ~$45.35B in 2025, growing ~11.5% annually through 2030; the U.S. accounts for ~54% of worldwide legal sales (§3 #8).

## 280E & scheduling

Section 280E disallows ordinary deductions, making COGS allocation essential; moving to Schedule III removes 280E and is estimated to save a typical dispensary ~$268k/yr (industry-wide ~$1.6B–$2.2B in incremental after-tax cash) (§3 #2). **As of the 2026-04-28 partial rescheduling (see the update banner above), that 280E relief is now actual law for FDA-approved and state-licensed MEDICAL operators; adult-use operators remain on Schedule I and still bear 280E.**

## Track-and-trace

Seed-to-sale tracking is mandatory but varies by state — Metrc, BioTrack, or LeafData — each with specific requirements; the Metrc rollout and tag costs have been a recurring operator pain point (§3 #1, #3).
