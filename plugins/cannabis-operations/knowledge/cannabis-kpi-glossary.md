# Cannabis operations KPI glossary

**Last reviewed:** 2026-06-05 · benchmark figures web-verified this date (trade + regulator + analyst sources, cited below). Cannabis benchmarks are **state-, segment-, and year-dependent** and carry `[verify-at-use]` markers — calibrate to the operator's segment and state and validate against its actual POS / track-and-trace / financial data before a deliverable (§3 #3, #8). Nothing here is tax, legal, or licensing advice; 280E positions are CPA decision-support (§2, §3 #2).

The metrics a cannabis operation is judged on — formulas, the misreads, and where a benchmark needs a **state and a date**.

## Compliance metrics

Track-and-trace reconciliation rate, inventory discrepancy rate, test-fail rate (by cause), destruction/waste volume, audit findings. Reconciliation **is** the license (§3 #1); a physical-vs-system discrepancy is a compliance event, not a bookkeeping error.

**Cited / framed benchmarks (`[verify-at-use]`):**

| Metric | Figure / definition | Read |
|---|---|---|
| Track-and-trace reconciliation cadence | **Daily** on high-velocity SKUs (best practice); the integration is not self-verifying | Monthly-only reconciliation lets small drifts compound past the point they can be explained (see discrepancy tree) |
| Required system of record | Metrc operates in ~30 regulated markets; others run BioTrack / state-built / unspecified-vendor | State-specific — confirm which governs *this* license before quoting any procedure |
| Test-fail rate | Operator's own % of batches failing the state panel, **tagged by cause** | Microbial often remediable; pesticide/heavy-metal usually destruction (see remediation tree). Industry-wide rates are not reliably published — use your own, `[ESTIMATE]` |

Sources: Metrc/BioTrack partnership (footprint) — https://www.metrc.com/news/metrc-and-biotrack-announce-strategic-partnership/ ; Medicinal Genomics — microbial testing regulations by state — https://medicinalgenomics.com/resource/cannabis-microbial-testing-regulations-by-state/ (retrieved 2026-06-05).

## Retail metrics

Gross margin by category, basket/average-order-value, units-per-transaction (UPT), inventory turns / days-on-hand, budtender productivity. The store runs on **margin × basket × UPT**, not raw traffic (§3 #4, #5).

**Cited benchmarks (`[verify-at-use]`):**

| Metric | Figure | Read |
|---|---|---|
| Blended gross margin | Dispensaries often target **≥50%**; vertically-integrated **~60–70%** | Below target with full traffic → discounting or category mix, not demand |
| Average basket (walk-in) | **~2.7 items**, down ~7% over ~24 months (basket compression) | A behavioral shift to smaller/more-frequent trips, not a demand collapse |
| Online vs walk-in basket | Digital carts **~44% larger (~3.9 items)**; online AOV **~$68** vs walk-in **~$50** (~35% higher) | Steer toward the higher-AOV online/pickup channel |
| Inventory days-on-hand | **Optimal ~20–35 days** depending on model | >35–70 days = trapped cash + expiry/traceability risk on perishable flower (§3 #5) |
| Days Inventory Outstanding (DIO) | **365 ÷ inventory turnover** | Lower = faster sell-through; the cash + compliance metric |
| Review cadence | **Monthly minimum; weekly for cash & inventory** | Cannabis cash/inventory move too fast for quarterly review |

Sources: Cova — dispensary KPIs (margin-by-category, basket, UPT, cadence) — https://www.covasoftware.com/cova-insights/essential-dispensary-kpis-cannabis-retail-metrics-that-matter ; Flowhub — Cannabis Retail Trends 2026 (basket compression, online vs walk-in AOV) — https://www.flowhub.com/learn/cannabis-retail-trends ; Prelude — inventory turnover / days-on-hand for cannabis — https://www.prelude.pro/resources/what-is-inventory-turnover-for-cannabis (retrieved 2026-06-05).

## Financial metrics

After-280E effective tax rate, COGS-allocation ratio, contribution margin by category. Every 280E position is **CPA decision-support** (§2, §3 #2).

**Cited benchmarks (`[verify-at-use]`):**

| Metric | Figure | Read |
|---|---|---|
| 280E effective federal rate | Routinely **>70%** on a dispensary fact pattern (vs ~21% for a non-cannabis business with identical financials) | The single biggest non-operational drag on a licensed operator's economics |
| Dispensary COGS share | Typically only **~35–50%** of total expenses qualifies as COGS | Why resale dispensaries carry the *highest* effective rates — most store cost is non-deductible selling expense |
| §471 cost-study uplift | A documented study can widen allowable COGS by **~25–40%** on the **production** side | The largest 280E lever lives at cultivation/manufacturing, not pure resale |
| §471(c) small-business threshold | Avg. gross receipts **≤ ~$32M** over prior 3 years (inflation-indexed) | Unlocks a simpler inventory method for smaller operators `[verify-at-use]` |

Sources: Northstar — 280E tax guide (effective rate, COGS share) — https://nstarfinance.com/resources/280e-tax-guide-cannabis-businesses ; LegalClarity — 280E / COGS / §471 — https://legalclarity.org/understanding-cannabis-tax-law-from-280e-to-cogs/ ; Congress.gov CRS R46709 — §280E applied to marijuana — https://www.congress.gov/crs-product/R46709 (retrieved 2026-06-05).

## Rescheduling context (volatile — read before quoting 280E relief)

In **April 2026** the DOJ/DEA moved **FDA-approved** marijuana products and **state-licensed *medical*** marijuana to **Schedule III**; **recreational/adult-use, unlicensed activity, and synthetically-derived THC remained Schedule I** (a broader rescheduling hearing was set for **June 29, 2026**). Practical consequence for these KPIs: **280E still applies to state-licensed adult-use operators** at the time of writing — do **not** model 280E relief for an adult-use store on the basis of the 2026 partial rescheduling. `[verify-at-use]` against the operator's exact segment, license type, and the current federal posture, with the operator's CPA (§2, §3 #3).

Sources: DOJ press release (April 2026) — https://www.justice.gov/opa/pr/justice-department-places-fda-approved-marijuana-products-and-products-containing-marijuana ; Federal Register 2026-04-28 — https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2026/04/28/2026-08177/schedules-of-controlled-substances-rescheduling-of-marijuana (retrieved 2026-06-05).

## Market context

US legal cannabis retail sales reached **~$33.8B in 2025**, projected toward **~$60B by 2030 (~12% CAGR)** [verify-at-use — analyst estimates vary]. Frame any operator against its **state** market, not the national aggregate (§3 #3).

Source: Cannabis Promotions — US Cannabis Market Size 2025 — https://cannabispromotions.com/stats/market-size (retrieved 2026-06-05). Analyst estimates of total market size diverge widely; treat any single figure as `[verify-at-use]`.
