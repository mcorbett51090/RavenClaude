# Itinerary Design & Quoting

An itinerary is a **structured, priced, documented commitment** — not a wish list. Build the structure, then price it honestly, then record it.

## The loop

1. **Structure first.** Fix routing, sequencing, pace, and must-haves before quoting. Sanity-check feasibility (connection times, check-in/out, minimum stays) — see the group-vs-FIT structuring tree in [`../../knowledge/travel-agency-decision-trees.md`](../../knowledge/travel-agency-decision-trees.md).
2. **Quote transparently.** Itemize: net/commissionable supplier cost, your **service/planning fee** (when the work exceeds what commission covers — ask [`travel-agency-operations-lead`](../../agents/travel-agency-operations-lead.md)), taxes/fees, and travel insurance offered. No hidden markups surfacing at final payment.
3. **Attach the penalty schedule.** For every supplier, record the deposit, final-payment date, and cancellation-penalty schedule. Treat each as `[verify-at-use]` against the live supplier at booking.
4. **Document the record.** Confirmation numbers, inclusions/exclusions, and every subsequent change go into the itinerary artifact — see [`../service-recovery-and-disruption/SKILL.md`](../service-recovery-and-disruption/SKILL.md) for change handling.

## Metrics

| Metric | Reads | Note |
|---|---|---|
| Quote-to-book conversion | booked / quoted | Low -> structure or price/trust problem |
| Average booking value | revenue per booking | Feeds the fee-vs-commission call |
| Service-fee capture | % of complex trips carrying a fee | Uncharged expertise is lost margin |
| Documentation completeness | % of elements with confirmations logged | The E&O shield |

## Anti-patterns

- Pricing before the structure is agreed.
- Burying a markup instead of naming a service fee.
- Booking without recording the cancellation-penalty schedule.
