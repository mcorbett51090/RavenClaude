# Document the itinerary and every change

**Status:** Absolute rule
**Domain:** Documentation / E&O risk
**Applies to:** `travel-agency-tour-operations`

> Advisory operations rule, not legal advice. E&O exposure and seller-of-travel obligations are jurisdiction-specific — `[verify-at-use]`. No traveler PII in artifacts — use placeholders.

---

## Why this exists

The itinerary and its change log are the artifact the entire relationship rides on. When a supplier fails, a traveler disputes what was promised, or an E&O claim lands, the question is always *what was confirmed and when*. An undocumented change — a verbal upgrade, an unrecorded penalty, a modification nobody logged — is the gap that turns a service problem into a liability. Documentation is the cheapest insurance an agency has.

## How to apply

- Record for every element: **confirmation number, inclusions/exclusions, deposit, final-payment date, and the cancellation-penalty schedule** (`[verify-at-use]`).
- Log **every modification** — rebookings, credits, upgrades, cancellations — with date and who authorized it.
- Quote transparently and document the quote itemization (net/commissionable cost, service fee, taxes) — see [`../skills/itinerary-design-and-quoting/SKILL.md`](../skills/itinerary-design-and-quoting/SKILL.md).
- During a disruption, document the recovery as it happens — see [`../skills/service-recovery-and-disruption/SKILL.md`](../skills/service-recovery-and-disruption/SKILL.md).
- Keep traveler PII out of shared artifacts; work in placeholders and structure.

**Do:** treat the change log as a deliverable; capture penalties and confirmations at booking.
**Don't:** rely on memory or verbal confirmations; let a change go unrecorded.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

None material — documentation discipline applies to every booking. The only judgment is *where* the record lives (agency system vs artifact), and PII always stays out of shared artifacts.
