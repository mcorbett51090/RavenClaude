# Service recovery is the repeat booking

**Status:** Pattern
**Domain:** Service recovery / retention
**Applies to:** `travel-agency-tour-operations`

> Advisory operations rule. Supplier remedies, insurance coverage, and card protections are policy-specific — `[verify-at-use]`. No traveler PII.

---

## Why this exists

Travel breaks: flights cancel (IROPS), hotels overbook, suppliers default. In those moments the traveler is not judging the itinerary — they're judging **who answered the phone and how fast the problem got fixed**. A well-run recovery is one of the strongest loyalty events an agency has; a fumbled one erases years of good trips. Service recovery is therefore a **sales activity**, and it's why travelers pay an advisor instead of booking online.

## How to apply

- **Rebook first, litigate fault second** — re-accommodate the stranded traveler before working out who owes the remedy. Traverse the disruption/recovery tree in [`../knowledge/travel-agency-decision-trees.md`](../knowledge/travel-agency-decision-trees.md).
- **Route the remedy** to whoever owes it — supplier rebooking desk, travel insurance, or card protection (`[verify-at-use]`) — don't eat costs someone else owes.
- **Decide goodwill deliberately** when no one strictly owes a remedy; frame the margin trade-off with [`../agents/travel-agency-operations-lead.md`](../agents/travel-agency-operations-lead.md).
- **Document every change** in the itinerary log — see [`../best-practices/document-the-itinerary-and-every-change.md`](../best-practices/document-the-itinerary-and-every-change.md).

**Do:** answer fast, rebook fast, route the remedy, document.
**Don't:** argue fault while the traveler is stranded; absorb costs an insurer/supplier owes.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

Fast recovery is always right, but goodwill spending is a judgment call — a chronically abusive or fraud-pattern client is not owed indefinite gestures. Recover the trip; size the goodwill to the relationship's real value.
