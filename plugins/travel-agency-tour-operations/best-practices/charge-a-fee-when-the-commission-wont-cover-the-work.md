# Charge a fee when the commission won't cover the work

**Status:** Absolute rule
**Domain:** Revenue model / pricing
**Applies to:** `travel-agency-tour-operations`

> Advisory operations rule. Commission rates and fee legality/disclosure vary by supplier and jurisdiction — `[verify-at-use]`. No traveler PII.

---

## Why this exists

Commission is a **supplier subsidy of your time**, not a wage. On a complex FIT itinerary, a heavy-research trip, or a near-uncommissionable all-air booking, the commission can be a fraction of the hours the trip actually takes. An agency that lives on commission alone quietly does its most skilled work for free and mistakes volume for profit. A service/planning fee prices the expertise directly, and it also filters for clients who value the advice.

## How to apply

- Before booking, ask: **does the commission cover the planning effort?** If not, quote a service/planning fee — traverse the revenue-model tree in [`../knowledge/travel-agency-decision-trees.md`](../knowledge/travel-agency-decision-trees.md).
- For net-rate product, earn margin via a **markup on net** instead of, or alongside, a fee.
- **Disclose fees** up front and confirm they're permitted under your host agreement and jurisdiction (`[verify-at-use]`).
- Track service-fee capture as a first-class metric, not an afterthought.

**Do:** price the labor when commission is thin; disclose the fee before work begins.
**Don't:** do hours of expert planning on a booking whose commission never covered it; bury a markup you're unwilling to name.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

A richly commissionable booking (e.g. a preferred-supplier cruise) that took little planning may not warrant an added fee — commission already pays for the work. The rule is about matching price to effort, not adding fees reflexively.
