# Source on total cost of ownership, not unit price

**Status:** Absolute rule. **Constitution:** §3, §4.

## Use when
Any procurement & sourcing deliverable where this question is in play.

## The rule
Freight, quality, switching, inventory, and lifecycle cost often dwarf the unit price; a 'savings' on price that raises TCO is a loss in disguise.

## Why
This is house opinion §3 #2. Getting it wrong is the most common way a procurement & sourcing analysis misleads the operator who acts on it.

## How to apply
- Apply it before reaching for a method.
- Make the relevant number carry a definition, window, and baseline (§3 #1).
- Cite the source + date for any external figure, or mark it `[unverified — training knowledge]`.
