# Go/no-go is a real decision, not a formality

Before writing a word, name the cost-to-apply (and the opportunity cost of the writing team's time), the probability of award, the strings attached (match/cost-share, restrictions, reporting burden), and the sustainability tail past the period of performance. A disciplined "no" — to a low-probability, high-effort, poorly-fitting, or unsustainable opportunity — is one of the most valuable outputs the team produces. Committing the writers before deciding whether to apply is how scarce capacity is wasted.
