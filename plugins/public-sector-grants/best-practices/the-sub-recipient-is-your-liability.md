# The sub-recipient is your liability

A pass-through entity is on the hook for the federal compliance of every dollar it passes down. Classify each outside relationship at the sub-award stage by *substance*, not the agreement's label: a partner that carries out part of the program and makes programmatic decisions is a **sub-recipient** (you owe risk assessment → flow-down sub-award terms → ongoing monitoring → single-audit follow-up); a vendor selling goods/services in a competitive market to many buyers is a **contractor** (you owe procurement compliance, not monitoring). A sub-recipient's finding becomes *your* finding, and monitoring reconstructed at audit time isn't contemporaneous — so build the chain from day one and document the classification against the current 2 CFR 200 criteria.
