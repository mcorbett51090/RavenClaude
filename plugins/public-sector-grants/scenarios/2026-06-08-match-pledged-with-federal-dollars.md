## Problem

A program required a 25% non-federal match. The grants team treated the match as a number to satisfy on paper, not as real money to source — so the proposal pledged the match without naming where each dollar would come from. When the award landed and the match had to be drawn and documented alongside the federal share, two of the sources fell apart: one "in-kind" volunteer-time line had no valuation method behind it, and another match line turned out to be funds from a *different federal* award. At the Single Audit the match was short, the federal-funds-as-match line was disallowed outright, and the org had to backfill the shortfall from unrestricted dollars it had not budgeted for.

## Constraints context

- The match was required by the program, not merely encouraged — so falling short was a compliance gap, not a missed nice-to-have.
- Nobody had checked, at the proposal stage, whether each match source was allowable, non-federal, and verifiably valued per 2 CFR — the match was assembled to hit the percentage, not built from sourced money.
- The in-kind volunteer hours were real, but with no documented valuation method (hours × a defensible rate) they could not be counted.

## Attempts

- Tried: counting the volunteer hours at audit with a rate picked after the fact. Failed — an in-kind value with no contemporaneous, documented method isn't a countable match contribution.
- Tried: arguing the second federal award's funds were "the org's money too." Failed — federal funds cannot match federal funds; the line was disallowed regardless of intent.
- Tried (the fix): treating the match as a go/no-go input — sourcing every match dollar from allowable non-federal cash or properly-valued third-party in-kind *before* pledging it, running each item through allowable/allocable/reasonable, and tracking the match drawdown alongside the federal drawdown from day one.

## Resolution

The match became a sourced, documented commitment made at the proposal stage, not a percentage scrambled for each quarter. A required match that can't be sourced is now surfaced at go/no-go as a potential no-go, not handed to the writers as a problem to paper over. Each match item carries its source, its non-federal status, and its valuation method; in-kind is hours × a defensible rate, documented. The next audit's match held.

## Lesson

Compliance starts at the proposal, and the match is real money. Source every match dollar before pledging it, never match federal funds with federal funds, value third-party in-kind with a documented method, and surface an unfundable match at go/no-go — it's a fit input, not a writing problem. A match assembled to hit a percentage instead of sourced from allowable funds is a shortfall waiting for the audit to find it.
