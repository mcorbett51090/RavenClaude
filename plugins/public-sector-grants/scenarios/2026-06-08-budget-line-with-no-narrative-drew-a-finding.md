## Problem

A grantee submitted a clean-looking budget: salaries, fringe, travel, supplies, a contractual line, and indirect. It was funded. The trouble was the budget *narrative* — half the lines had a number but no justification of the math. The travel line was a round $40,000 with no trip-by-trip basis; the indirect was applied at a rate nobody had tied to a negotiated agreement or the de-minimis option; the contractual line didn't say what the work was. At the Single Audit the lines with no narrative basis couldn't be defended cost-by-cost, and several were questioned costs — the org had to repay a portion and absorb the rest.

## Constraints context

- The budget was assembled by program staff against the dollar ceiling, working backward from "what's left," not built up from a justified, allowable basis per line.
- The indirect rate had been picked to maximize the recovery, not cited to a negotiated indirect-cost-rate agreement or the de-minimis rate — so it had no authority behind it.
- The travel and contractual numbers were placeholders that were never converted into a documented basis before submission.

## Attempts

- Tried: reconstructing the travel basis at audit time from staff calendars. Failed — a basis built after the fact isn't contemporaneous justification; the questioned-cost determination stood.
- Tried: defending the indirect rate as "industry standard." Failed — indirect recovery traces to a negotiated rate or the de-minimis election, not to a norm; with neither on file the recovery was disallowed.
- Tried (the fix): rebuilding every budget bottom-up, each line carrying a narrative that shows the math (unit × rate × quantity), names the allowable basis, ties to a logic-model activity, and cites the indirect authority — before the proposal goes out.

## Resolution

The org made the budget narrative a build-time artifact, not a post-hoc cover sheet. Every line now states its basis and traces to a logic-model activity; indirect is the negotiated or de-minimis rate, cited. The compliance analyst reviews the budget at the proposal stage, so allowability is decided when the line is created. The next audit had no questioned costs on this basis.

## Lesson

The budget is a narrative in numbers, and compliance starts at the proposal — not the award. A line with a number and no justification is a finding waiting to happen: every line should show its math, name its allowable basis, trace to the logic model, and cite its authority (especially the indirect rate) at build time. Reconstructing the basis at audit is already too late.
