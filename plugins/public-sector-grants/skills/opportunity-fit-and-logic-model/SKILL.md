# Opportunity Fit & Logic Model

## Screen the opportunity first
Before any writing, screen for **fit**: are we eligible (entity type, geography, population), is it on-mission, do we have the capacity to deliver *and* comply? An off-mission win is a liability — it commits the org to deliver the wrong thing under audit. If the screen fails, the answer is no-go regardless of the dollar amount.

## Research the funder and its rubric
The NOFO/RFP review and scoring criteria are how the application is judged. Research the funder's stated priorities, recent awards, and the program officer's signals. Work from what the funder actually funds and weights — not what the org wishes they funded. Capture the rubric so the proposal can be mapped to it section-by-section.

## Build the logic model / theory of change
Inputs → activities → outputs → outcomes → impact, with the causal narrative connecting them. No orphan activities (unfunded) and no outcomes nothing produces. The model is the spine: the objectives and the budget are written *from* it. The theory of change is the story of why these activities cause that long-term impact.

## Make a disciplined go/no-go
Name the **cost-to-apply** (and opportunity cost of the writing team), the **probability of award** (new applicant vs. incumbent, the funder's award rate, the fit — as a range if needed), the **strings** (match/cost-share, restrictions, reporting burden), and the **sustainability tail** past the period of performance. A disciplined "no" is a valid, valuable output.

## Output
A go/no-go recommendation (fit screen + funder research + cost/probability/strings/sustainability) and, on a go, a logic model + theory-of-change narrative ready for `proposal-writer`. Flag match and any allowability concerns for `grants-compliance-analyst`. Hand donor-funded match/sustainability to `nonprofit-fundraising`.
