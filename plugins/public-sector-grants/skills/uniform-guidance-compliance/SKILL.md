# Uniform Guidance Compliance (2 CFR 200)

## Run the three-part cost test
Every federal dollar is **allowable** (permitted by the 2 CFR 200 cost principles and the award terms, not a prohibited item), **allocable** (benefits the award in proportion to the amount charged), and **reasonable** (a prudent person would incur it). Fail any one and the cost is unallowable — "mission-critical" is not an allowability category. Decide allowability at the budget stage, not after the award.

## Indirect costs and match
Advise on the indirect-cost rate: a negotiated rate (NICRA) if the org has one, otherwise the de-minimis option — check the org's status, don't assume. Identify what counts as match/cost-share and how it must be documented. Match is real money the org must secure; treat it as a go/no-go input, not a footnote.

## Sub-recipient monitoring
Classify the relationship first — **sub-recipient** (carries out the program, makes programmatic decisions, subject to federal requirements) vs. **contractor/vendor** (goods/services in a competitive market). For sub-recipients the pass-through entity owes monitoring: pre-award risk assessment → sub-award terms → ongoing monitoring → single-audit follow-up. A sub-recipient's finding becomes yours.

## Drawdowns and federal financial reporting
Draw funds **to need** — drawing ahead is a cash-management finding. File the Federal Financial Report (FFR) and program performance reports on cadence; a missed deadline is a finding. Costs are allowable only within the period of performance; pre-award/post-period costs need explicit authority.

## Single-audit readiness
Check expenditures against the current single-audit threshold. Prepare the SEFA, the major-program determination, and the internal-control/compliance requirements; pre-empt common findings (time-and-effort, procurement files, match documentation). Build documentation contemporaneously, not at audit time.

## Output
A cited determination or plan grounded in 2 CFR 200 and the award terms — marked **advisory** for the org's authorized official, finance office, and auditor to confirm. Hand GL/drawdown mechanics to `finance`; federal-data security controls to `cybersecurity-grc`. Verify every threshold/deadline against current 2 CFR before quoting.
