## Where defensible benchmarks come from

Pull-through, cycle-time, and cost-to-originate benchmarks are **channel-, product-, and date-specific** and swing with the rate cycle. Defensible sources require a dated cut: the shop's own LOS history (the most defensible), industry performance reports (e.g. cost-per-loan studies), and investor/agency data. **Name the source and date, or mark the figure `[unverified — training knowledge]` (§3 #8).**

## Directional frames (illustrative only — `[unverified — training knowledge]`)

| Area | Directional frame | Must-verify |

## Operating rhythm

- **Pipeline/pull-through review** weekly — fallout by stage and the worst leak (§3 #1).
- **Cycle/capacity review** weekly/monthly — bottleneck dwell and loans-per-processor at cycle (§3 #2 #4).
- **Cost-to-originate / breakeven review** monthly — stressed against the rate-swing volume (§3 #5 #7).
- **Compliance/QC review** on a fixed cadence — operational defect signal; regulatory determinations to counsel (§3 #6).

## The standing caution

TRID/ECOA/HMDA/fair-lending/UDAAP compliance, underwriting/credit decisions, and hedge/secondary-marketing risk are the **qualified authority's** call — counsel, the compliance authority, the licensed underwriter, and the risk desk. The team frames the operational decision and routes the determination; it never renders one. Keep borrower PII / NPI out of all deliverables (§2). This team is not a compliance, legal, or underwriting authority (§3 #6 #8).
