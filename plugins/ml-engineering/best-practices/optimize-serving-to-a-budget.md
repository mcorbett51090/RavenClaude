# Optimize inference against a stated latency and cost budget

Set the latency and cost budget before you optimize, then measure against it — don't assume a bigger model is fine or reach for a GPU by reflex. Request batching, quantization or distillation, the right hardware, and caching are the levers, and each trades something: batching adds latency to gain throughput, quantization trades a sliver of accuracy for speed and cost, a larger instance trades money for headroom. Without a budget you can't tell which lever you actually need, so you either over-provision and burn money or ship something too slow and discover it under load. Profile to find where the time and cost actually go — often it's feature fetching or serialization, not the model forward pass — and optimize the real bottleneck. "Fast enough and cheap enough" is a number you committed to in advance, not a feeling you have after deploying.
