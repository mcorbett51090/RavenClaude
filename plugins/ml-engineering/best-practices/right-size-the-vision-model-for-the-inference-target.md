# Right-size the vision model for the inference target

Choose the vision model for *where it has to run*, not just for the best offline number. CV inference is heavy — images and video are large and the models are GPU-hungry — so the inference target (cloud batch, cloud real-time endpoint, on-device/edge, or an edge-filter->cloud hybrid) sets the real constraints on size, latency, power, bandwidth, and privacy. A model that tops the leaderboard but can't fit the edge device, or that round-trips full images to the cloud when raw imagery must stay local, is the wrong model regardless of its accuracy. When you compress to fit the target (quantize, prune, distill, then compile to TensorRT/ONNX/CoreML/TFLite), **re-measure accuracy on the held-out set after every optimization** — quantization moves the metric and can shift per-class behavior. And keep the **preprocessing identical in training and at the inference target** (resize, normalize, color space), or you have reintroduced training-serving skew in image form. Pick the placement deliberately, size the model to it, and re-validate after every change that the budget forced.
