# Gate every promotion on a metric you defined in advance

Decide the promotion criterion — the metric, the threshold, and the population it's measured on — before the candidate model exists, so promotion is a rule the model passes, not a judgment call made by whoever is impressed by the demo. A criterion chosen after seeing the results is a criterion chosen to justify the decision you already wanted, and it bakes confirmation bias into your release process. The gate should be enforced by the pipeline at the registry boundary: a model that doesn't clear the threshold doesn't advance, full stop. And whether the candidate's lift over the incumbent is real rather than noise is the statistician's call — wire that check into the gate rather than eyeballing a single number. A defined, pre-committed promotion gate is the difference between shipping improvements and shipping whatever looked good on the day.
