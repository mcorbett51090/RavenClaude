# Treat the feature store as the train/serve consistency contract

A feature store earns its operational weight only when it does the one job that matters: serve the same feature value, computed by the same logic, to both training and inference — with point-in-time-correct historical lookups offline and low-latency lookups online. Its purpose is not to be a fashionable component; it is to make training-serving skew structurally impossible by removing the second implementation of a feature that always drifts from the first. If your features are simple and computed by a shared transformation library both paths import, you may not need a store at all — name that honestly rather than adopting one for the architecture diagram. But the moment features are reused across models or require historical aggregation, the store's point-in-time correctness is the thing standing between you and a model that looks great offline and quietly leaks future information.
