## Market-data sources (where defensible comp comes from)

A comp band is only as defensible as its survey. Common sources practitioners cite — **each requires a current subscription/date stamp, none is quotable from memory**:

- Published salary surveys (e.g. Radford, Mercer, Willis Towers Watson, Pave, Carta-style equity benchmarks) — subscription, dated cuts.
- Government wage data (e.g. US BLS Occupational Employment and Wage Statistics) — public but lagged.
- Levels.fyi / Glassdoor-style crowdsourced data — directional, self-reported, **not** survey-grade; use as a sanity check, not a band source.

**Rule:** name the survey and the data-effective date in the deliverable, or mark the band `[unverified — training knowledge]` (§3 #8).

## Pay-transparency & pay-equity law (fast-moving — verify jurisdiction)

The regulatory surface has expanded materially and **differs by jurisdiction**:

- **Pay-range-in-posting laws** exist in a growing number of US states/cities and the **EU Pay Transparency Directive** (phasing toward member-state transposition) — they affect job postings, offer process, and reporting.
- **Pay-equity / reporting obligations** (e.g. EU gender pay-gap reporting thresholds, various US state equal-pay acts) carry filing and remediation duties.

**This is exactly the §2 boundary:** the team can *flag* that a posting/comp practice may trigger an obligation, but the determination — does this law apply, what does it require, are we compliant — is **qualified counsel's**, and any figure here must be re-verified against the current statute. Do not assert a specific threshold, deadline, or requirement without a dated source.

## 2026 IRS contribution limits & FLSA thresholds (dated — verify before a deliverable)

Unlike the directional benchmarks below, these are **specific published figures with a dated source** — quotable in a deliverable *with* the source URL + retrieval date, but still re-verify against the current statute/notice and route any compliance determination to qualified counsel (§2). Figures effective for the 2026 tax/plan year.

**IRS retirement & health-account limits (Notice 2025-67):**

- **401(k) / 403(b) / 457 elective deferral** — $24,500 (up from $23,500 in 2025).
- **IRA contribution** — $7,500 (up from $7,000), plus a $1,100 catch-up for age 50+.
- **HSA** — $4,400 self-only / $8,750 family (plus the $1,000 age-55+ catch-up).
- **Healthcare FSA** — $3,400 salary-reduction limit.

**SECURE 2.0 mandatory-Roth catch-up:** effective **Jan 1, 2026**, catch-up contributions for employees whose prior-year FICA wages exceeded **$150,000** must be made on a **Roth (after-tax)** basis — a plan-design and payroll change People-Ops/Total-Rewards should confirm is implemented before the 2026 plan year. Routes to plan counsel/recordkeeper for the determination (§2).

**FLSA white-collar exempt salary threshold (federal):** a **May 14, 2026** DOL technical amendment formally **restored the 2019 threshold** — **$684/week ($35,568/yr)**; the highly-compensated-employee threshold is **$107,432/yr** — after the 2024 rule (which would have raised these) was vacated. **State thresholds are higher and govern where applicable** (e.g. CA ~$1,352/wk, CO $1,111.23/wk, CT $871.16/wk for 2026) — flag the higher of federal vs. state and route the exempt/non-exempt classification to counsel (§2).

## Benchmark ranges (directional only — `[unverified — training knowledge]`)

These are remembered orders of magnitude to *frame* a conversation, **not** numbers to quote in a deliverable. Replace each with a dated source before it ships:

## Survey cadence & operating rhythm

- **Census engagement survey** annually, with lighter **pulse** checks between, is a common rhythm; over-surveying causes fatigue and depresses response rates.
- **Comp cycle** typically annual, with off-cycle adjustments governed by the band (not the counteroffer, §3 #2).
- **Pay-equity audit** on a defined cadence (commonly annual), pre-comp-cycle, with privileged legal review (§2, §3 #5).

## The standing caution

People data is **PII-dense and legally sensitive**. No named-person comp, no protected-class attribution to an individual, no termination recommendation in a deliverable (§2). When a question turns on what the law *requires*, that is counsel's call — the team frames the decision and routes it.
