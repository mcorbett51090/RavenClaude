You are running `/people-operations-hr:design-interview-loop`. Use the
`talent-acquisition-strategist` discipline and the `structured-hiring` skill.

## Steps

1. **Confirm role context.** Extract from the argument (or ask): role title, level/seniority,
   team, key deliverables in the first 6 months, and any known sourcing constraints.

2. **Define 4–7 competencies.** Separate must-haves from nice-to-haves. Audit for "culture fit"
   as a vague gestalt — replace with specific, observable behaviors or remove.

3. **Traverse the structured-hiring skill.** Read
   [`../skills/structured-hiring/SKILL.md`](../skills/structured-hiring/SKILL.md) and apply the
   loop-design and scorecard-authoring sections.

4. **Design the loop.** Map each competency to exactly one stage and one primary interviewer.
   Aim for ≤5 live stages, ≤8 interviewers. Produce a stage table:
   Stage | Owner | Competency | Format | Duration.

5. **Write scorecards.** For each stage, produce: competency definition, 1–4 rubric anchors
   with behavioral examples, 2–3 STAR questions with probes, and red flags.
   Use [`../templates/interview-scorecard.md`](../templates/interview-scorecard.md).

6. **Bias audit.** Check the JD (if provided) for gendered language, credential proxies, and
   coded exclusionary terms (ninja/rockstar/guru, "young", "culture fit" as sole criterion).
   Report findings; suggest rewrites.

7. **Write interviewer briefing notes.** One paragraph per interviewer: their competency,
   why it matters for this role, and a reminder to complete the scorecard before the debrief.

8. **Emit the Structured Output block.** Include: competencies defined, stages in the loop,
   scorecards produced, bias flags found, and handoff recommendations
   (performance-and-comp-analyst for the offer comp framework; people-ops-lead for post-hire
   onboarding).
