# Leveling Matrix — [Company Name] | [Job Family]

> **Instructions:** This is the canonical rubric for leveling decisions in the [Job Family]
> job family. Each level is defined by scope, impact, autonomy, and collaboration. Use this
> rubric in leveling discussions, promotion reviews, and offer-level decisions. Comp bands
> for each level are maintained separately in the comp framework.
>
> **Principle:** Level describes what someone *consistently does and delivers* at the role,
> not what they have done once or what their title was at a prior company. Leveling is based
> on observable work, not credentials or tenure.

---

## Individual Contributor Track

### IC1 — Associate / Entry

| Dimension | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Scope** | Works on well-defined, bounded tasks within a team. Rarely operates across team boundaries. |
| **Impact** | Task-level. Contributions ship as part of a larger team effort. |
| **Autonomy** | Requires close direction and regular check-ins. Asks before proceeding when uncertain. |
| **Collaboration** | Primary collaborator is immediate team and manager. |
| **Craft** | Produces quality work within established patterns and with guidance on edge cases. |
| **Typical experience** | 0–2 years in the field. Recent graduate or career-changer with foundational skills. |

---

### IC2 — [e.g., Software Engineer / Analyst / Designer]

| Dimension | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Scope** | Owns individual features or well-scoped projects. Some ambiguity is handled independently. |
| **Impact** | Feature-level. Contributions are visible within the team; occasionally cross-team. |
| **Autonomy** | Works from well-defined requirements with moderate check-ins. Identifies and resolves common blockers independently. |
| **Collaboration** | Collaborates effectively within team; beginning to engage adjacent functions. |
| **Craft** | Consistently produces quality work; beginning to improve team practices in small ways. |
| **Typical experience** | 2–5 years. Has shipped production work in a team setting. |

---

### IC3 — Senior [e.g., Senior Software Engineer / Senior Analyst]

| Dimension | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Scope** | Owns a significant component, feature set, or project. Comfortable with ambiguous requirements. |
| **Impact** | Team-level. Work has material effect on team outcomes; beginning to influence adjacent teams. |
| **Autonomy** | Works from goals, not prescriptive requirements. Designs own approach; escalates when uncertain on strategy, not execution. |
| **Collaboration** | Regular cross-functional collaborator. Facilitates alignment, not just participation. |
| **Craft** | Extends existing patterns; improves team practices; contributes to peer growth. |
| **Typical experience** | 5–8 years. Has owned a significant workstream end-to-end. |

---

### IC4 — Staff [e.g., Staff Engineer / Staff Product Manager]

| Dimension | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Scope** | Owns a subsystem, product area, or cross-team initiative. Sets scope for others. |
| **Impact** | Department or multi-team. Decisions measurably change team/org direction. |
| **Autonomy** | Operates from strategic direction; defines their own roadmap. Surfaces trade-offs to leadership. |
| **Collaboration** | Drives alignment across functions. Manages up and out; enables other ICs. |
| **Craft** | Defines team and org standards; mentors ICs 1–3; raises the overall bar. |
| **Typical experience** | 8–12+ years. Has delivered org-level impact with measurable outcomes. |

---

### IC5 — Principal [e.g., Principal Engineer / Principal PM]

| Dimension | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Scope** | Company-wide domain. Owns a multi-year technical or product direction. |
| **Impact** | Company-level. Shapes strategy across functions; external credibility. |
| **Autonomy** | Sets own agenda aligned to company strategy. Operates with minimal supervision. |
| **Collaboration** | Executive-level partner. Aligns org leadership; engages board/investors on technical/product matters. |
| **Craft** | Industry-level thought leader; defines best practice; recruits for excellence. |
| **Typical experience** | 12–20 years. Distinguished record of company-level impact. |

## Management Track

### M1 — Team Lead / Manager

| Dimension | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Scope** | Manages a team of 4–8 ICs. Also maintains IC contribution. |
| **Impact** | Team-level throughput and health. First lever for hiring, retention, and development. |
| **Autonomy** | Operates from team goals. Escalates staffing/direction decisions to senior management. |
| **People** | Runs 1:1s, gives feedback, conducts reviews, participates in calibration. |
| **Typical span** | 4–8 direct reports. |

---

### M2 — Senior Manager / Manager of Managers

| Dimension | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Scope** | Manages a team or a small group of teams (typically 1–2 managers + some ICs). |
| **Impact** | Sub-org level. Shapes org design, headcount plan, and team development strategy. |
| **Autonomy** | Defines own team roadmap aligned to department goals. Manages budget and headcount. |
| **People** | Develops managers; owns a development culture; drives retention for the sub-org. |
| **Typical span** | 10–25 total in org. |

---

### M3 — Director

| Dimension | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Scope** | Manages a department or a major functional area (e.g., Engineering Platform, Sales West). |
| **Impact** | Department-level strategy and execution. Cross-functional partner at VP level. |
| **Autonomy** | Sets department roadmap and strategy. Manages large budget; partners with Finance/HR. |
| **People** | Builds a leadership team; owns culture and performance for 25–75 people. |
| **Typical span** | 25–75 total in org. |

---

### M4 — VP

| Dimension | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Scope** | Owns a major function (e.g., Engineering, Product, Sales, People). |
| **Impact** | Company-level. Strategy partner to CEO and board. Represents function externally. |
| **Autonomy** | Full ownership of function strategy and budget. Reports to C-suite. |
| **People** | Builds executive team; sets the cultural tone for the function. |
| **Typical span** | 75–300+ total in org. |

## Promotion criteria

A promotion to the next level requires:

1. **Sustained performance** at the next level's scope and impact for ≥3 months — not just
   one project.
2. **Documented evidence** from the current review cycle (or a mid-cycle check-in) showing
   consistent operation at the next level.
3. **Calibration** — the promotion is discussed and endorsed in calibration, not decided
   unilaterally by the manager.
4. **Comp adjustment** — the promotion triggers a comp adjustment to the new band. A promotion
   without a pay change is not a promotion; it is a title change.
