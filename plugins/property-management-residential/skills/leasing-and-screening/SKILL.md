# Leasing & Screening

## Fair housing is a flag, never an opinion
Protected-class exposure — in an ad, a screening rule, a denial, a steering pattern, or an accommodation request — is **surfaced and routed to counsel**, never adjudicated. The federal classes plus state/local additions (e.g. source of income) are a prompt to escalate, not a checklist you clear.

## One written standard, applied identically
Document the screening criteria — income multiple, credit/eviction history, occupancy standard — **before** screening anyone, and apply the same standard to every applicant. Record which criterion each denial rests on. A denial you can't tie to a uniform written criterion is a fair-housing risk regardless of intent. (Criminal-history and source-of-income use is legally constrained — flag, don't freelance.)

## The leasing funnel
Lead source → tour → application → lease. Find the bottleneck (lead-to-tour, tour-to-application, application-to-lease) and the turn time. Vacancy is the most expensive thing: the turn clock starts at notice, and every day vacant is unrecoverable revenue — tie the fix to the vacancy-loss number.

## Renew, raise, or non-renew
A renewal is a math problem framed by law: market vs. in-place rent and turn/vacancy cost set the number; the *legality* of the increase or non-renewal (notice, caps, rent control) is flagged to counsel. Retaining a good paying tenant usually beats turn + vacancy loss.

## Move-in / move-out & deposits
A documented move-in condition record, an itemized condition-comparison deduction at move-out (never a round number), and deposit handling within the required timeline (flag the legal timeline to counsel). Tenant PII (screening reports, SSNs, bank data) is minimized and never pasted into outputs.

## Output
A funnel diagnosis, a documented screening standard, a renewal decision, or a move process — with every fair-housing / legal question flagged and routed to counsel, never resolved. Hand the commercial deal to `commercial-real-estate`, the books to `finance`, the turn to `maintenance-coordinator`.
