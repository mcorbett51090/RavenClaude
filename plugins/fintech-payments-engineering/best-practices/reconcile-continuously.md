# Reconcile the ledger to the processor continuously

Reconciliation is not a month-end accounting chore — it's a continuous correctness check that catches bugs, dropped webhooks, partial failures, and fraud while they're still small. Pull the PSP's settlement and balance reports on a schedule, match every line to a ledger entry, and alert on anything that doesn't tie out: a charge the PSP recorded but your ledger missed (a lost webhook), a ledger entry with no PSP counterpart (a phantom credit), a fee or FX difference you didn't model. The longer a discrepancy sits, the harder it is to explain and the more entries pile on top of the error. Treat a non-zero reconciliation difference as a defect with an owner, not a rounding curiosity to write off — mystery money is always a bug or a breach until proven otherwise.
