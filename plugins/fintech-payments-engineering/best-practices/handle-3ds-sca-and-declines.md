# Treat 3DS/SCA challenges and declines as normal flow, not errors

In SCA jurisdictions a charge entering a `requires_action` state to run a 3DS challenge is the expected happy path, not a failure — design the client flow to surface the challenge, await its completion, and only then confirm. Treating the authentication step as an error strands the customer mid-checkout on a payment that would have succeeded. Likewise, model declines as first-class outcomes carrying the issuer's reason: a clear, actionable message ('your bank declined this card — try another') recovers revenue that a generic 'payment failed' throws away. The decline reason also drives what you do next — retry, prompt for a new method, or stop — so capture and route it. The authorization result is a rich signal; flatten it to success/error and you lose both conversions and the information to recover them.
