# Retry soft declines; never retry hard declines

A decline is not one thing — soft declines (insufficient funds, issuer timeout, velocity limit, do-not-honor) are transient and worth a retry on a sensible schedule, while hard declines (stolen/lost card, invalid number, closed account, revoked authorization) are permanent and retrying them is pointless and dangerous. Retrying a hard decline burns into the issuer's fraud and retry-abuse signals, raises your decline rate, and can get a merchant account flagged by the card networks. Branch on the decline category, not a blanket retry: schedule soft-decline retries with backoff and reasonable caps, and on a hard decline stop immediately and route the customer to provide a new payment method. Map every issuer/network reason code to soft-or-hard up front — guessing turns a recoverable failure into a relationship with the card networks you don't want.
