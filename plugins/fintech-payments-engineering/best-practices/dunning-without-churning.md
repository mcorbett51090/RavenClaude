# Dun to recover revenue without manufacturing churn

A large share of subscription cancellations are involuntary — a card expired or a charge soft-declined — so a thoughtful dunning flow recovers real revenue from customers who never meant to leave. But dunning done crudely churns the very customers it's meant to keep: hammering a hard-declined card, emailing daily, or cutting off access on the first failure burns goodwill for revenue that wasn't coming back anyway. Do it well: retry only retryable (soft) declines on a smart schedule timed to paydays and bank behavior, stop immediately on hard declines and ask for a new method, communicate with a clear fix-it path rather than a threat, and offer a grace period before downgrading access. The goal is recovering the involuntary loss while never making a satisfied customer feel dunned.
