# Never log the PAN or CVV — anywhere, ever

The full card number and the CVV must never appear in a log line, an error trace, an analytics event, a crash report, a database column, or a support transcript — the CVV may not be stored after authorization under any circumstances, and a PAN in your logs drags your entire logging pipeline into PCI scope. Log what lets you operate without the secret: the token or last four, the card brand, the PSP charge id, the money operation and its outcome. Treat verbose request/response logging as a live hazard around payment calls — the easiest way a PAN leaks is an SDK or framework dumping a full request body on error. Redact at the boundary, assert in tests that card-shaped data can't reach a sink, and remember the safest card data is the data you tokenized away and never held.
