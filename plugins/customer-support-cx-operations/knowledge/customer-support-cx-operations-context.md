## Where defensible benchmarks come from

AHT, occupancy, FCR, and CSAT benchmarks are **channel-, complexity-, and date-specific** and move year to year. Practitioners cite contact-center benchmark reports and CX surveys, but the most defensible source is the org's own ticketing/telephony history. **Name the source and date, or mark the figure `[unverified — training knowledge]` (§3 #8).**

## Directional frames (illustrative only — `[unverified — training knowledge]`)

| Area | Directional frame | Must-verify |
|---|---|---|
| Target occupancy | Often cited around 80–85% for voice; lower for complex async | Derive from your own AHT + burnout/AHT-creep data |
| FCR | Diverges widely by issue complexity | Segment from ticketing history |
| Deflection rate | Highly dependent on KB quality and contact mix | Measure against self-service attempts, not assume |
| CSAT | Channel- and issue-specific; never a single org number | Segment by channel/tier/issue (§3 #3) |

## Operating rhythm

- **Forecast + staffing review** weekly: volume forecast → workload → agents at occupancy (§3 #2).
- **Backlog/SLA flow review** daily during a backlog event: arrivals vs capacity (§3 #5).
- **CSAT/quality review** monthly: segmented CSAT + FCR + QA sample (§3 #3 #4 #6).
- **Deflection review** quarterly: contact drivers + KB/self-service investment (§3 #1).

## The standing caution

Refund, warranty, and contract determinations, and privacy law (data-subject requests, retention), are **the qualified authority's** call — the team frames the decision and routes it. Keep customer PII and ticket contents out of deliverables (§2).
