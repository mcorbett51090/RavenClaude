# The 8-minute rule governs timed units

**Status:** Absolute rule
**Domain:** Billing / units
**Applies to:** `physical-therapy-rehab-clinic`

> Advisory only — not billing/coding advice. The rule variant and brackets are `[verify-at-use]` per payor.

---

## Why this exists

Timed (time-based) CPT codes bill in 15-minute units under the 8-minute rule; untimed (service-based) codes bill one unit per session. Counting units by gut, or by treating untimed codes as timed, produces over- or under-billing — both are denial and audit risks.

## How to apply

1. Separate **timed** from **untimed** codes first.
2. A single timed service needs **≥ 8 minutes** for its first unit.
3. Total the timed minutes and map cumulative minutes to units (8–22=1, 23–37=2, 38–52=3, 53–67=4, +15=+1).
4. Mixed timed codes: total → units → allocate by largest remaining minutes.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

**The payor's variant decides edges** — CMS uses the cumulative total; some commercial payors use a per-service "rule of eights." `[verify-at-use]`.
