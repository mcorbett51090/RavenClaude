Producing statements from a ledger is a commodity — every accounting system does it. The value is the governed cycle, the enforced controls, and the reusable per-entity mapping — installed in minutes.

What it is not

The honest edges.

A controller signs their name to these numbers, so here is exactly where the line is.

  • It doesn't replace the judgment that produces the trial balance.

    Accruals, cutoff, estimates, and the reconciliations that turn a raw GL into a reconciled TB stay yours.

  • The live tier ships as tested reference implementations.

    Multi-currency is fully-tested logic. The connectors, the warehouse dashboard, and the IdP sign-off are tested against mocks and a containerized Postgres — the live wiring (your OAuth tenants, your Cube instance, your identity provider) is your first-light step, with a checklist for each.

  • Local-tier sign-off is tamper-evident, not tamper-preventing.

    Identity is config-asserted until wired to your IdP; auditor-grade segregation needs your identity provider and an immutable store.

  • Nothing posts to your ledger or moves money.

    Review-and-approve by design. It proposes; you dispose.
