## The case-flow pipeline (first call → aftercare)

Every case moves through these stages. The **constraint stage** — the one that gates throughput and family satisfaction — is rarely where the complaint lands.

| Stage | What happens | Common constraint / failure |
|---|---|---|
| **First call** | The initial notification of a death; intake of decedent + informant data; the removal is dispatched | An after-hours call mishandled sets the whole relationship; slow answer = the family calls the next home |
| **Removal / transfer** | The decedent is brought into care from home/hospital/hospice/ME | Under-staffed on-call → the removal lags, backing up everything downstream |
| **Arrangement conference** | The family selects services, merchandise, and disposition; the Funeral Rule disclosures are given (→ compliance specialist) | Booked too far out because removal/prep is backed up; feels rushed or upsold |
| **Preparation** | Embalming (where chosen/required), refrigeration, dressing, casketing, restorative work | Prep-room / embalmer capacity is the classic hidden bottleneck |
| **Services** | Visitation, funeral, memorial, graveside, or celebrant-led service; livestream if offered | Facility/schedule conflicts; staffing a weekend cluster of services |
| **Billing / collections** | The itemized statement; cash-advance items; pre-need/insurance assignment; collection | Slow collection on at-need; cash-advance items mishandled |
| **Aftercare** | Grief-support referral, follow-up cadence, the review/referral loop | Skipped entirely → the retention/reputation loop never closes |

**Read the pipeline as a system.** A "slow arrangement" complaint often traces upstream to an under-staffed removal or a prep-room backlog. Find the stage that *gates* throughput, not the stage that *voices* the pain.

## Disposition mix & the cremation-rate shift

- The U.S. cremation rate has been **majority and rising** — well over half of cases and climbing, with burial declining — for years. _(Directional consensus; exact rate retrieved 2026-07-09 — re-verify the current NFDA figure before quoting a number.)_
- **This is a margin fact, not just a preference.** Direct cremation typically carries a lower average revenue per call than a traditional burial-with-service. A practice built on a burial-era cost structure in a cremation-majority market is under margin pressure by default.
- **The re-fit, not the retreat:** meet the shift with **memorialization** (celebrant-led services, memorial services with cremation, urn and keepsake merchandise, life-tribute/video, scattering coordination) rather than defending declining burial volume. A cremation family still wants a meaningful service — sell the service, not just the disposition.
- **Green/natural burial and alkaline hydrolysis (aquamation)** are growing niches — small but real demand, each with its own merchandise (shrouds, biodegradable caskets, no vault) and, for aquamation, **state-by-state legality that must be verified.**

## Staffing & on-call

- **Licensed funeral directors** run arrangements and services; **embalmers** (often dual-licensed) run preparation. Licensing requirements are **state-specific and volatile — verify.**
- **On-call / removal coverage** is the round-the-clock backbone — deaths don't keep business hours. The frequency of after-hours first calls and removals drives the coverage model.
- **Build-vs-contract** is the recurring fork: **removals** and, sometimes, **embalming/preparation** can be contracted to a trade service below a volume threshold, then brought in-house as volume justifies the fixed cost. Name the volume threshold that flips it rather than defaulting either way.
- **Burnout is a real operational risk.** On-call load on a thin roster degrades the family experience and drives turnover; capacity planning is a family-experience issue, not only a cost one.

## Pre-need as a business line

- **Funding:** a **pre-need trust** (funds trusted, regulated) vs **pre-need insurance** (a policy assigned to the home). The trusting percentage, disclosures, and portability are **state-regulated and volatile — route to compliance + counsel.**
- **The backlog is an asset and a liability.** Pre-need contracts are future revenue *and* future obligations the practice must fund and staff at need. A large pre-need book sold on unfunded price guarantees is a slow-motion solvency risk (CLAUDE.md §3 #8).
- **Run it as a program:** counselor staffing, honest conversion targets, and a stance on price-guarantee vs growth-funded — sold honestly or not pushed at all.

## Aftercare — retention & reputation

- Aftercare (grief-support referral, follow-up at intervals, anniversary contact, the review/referral ask) is **the cheapest marketing a funeral home has** — the served family is the referral you didn't pay for.
- **Refer clinical grief, don't treat it** → `behavioral-health-practice`. Aftercare is a *referral and follow-up* loop, not therapy.
- Online reviews and community reputation compound: a well-run aftercare loop fills next year's calendar.

## Family-experience patterns (grief-aware operations)

- **Grief-aware beats efficient-only.** A cost-saving that makes a bereaved family feel processed is a false economy — it costs the reputation that fills the calendar.
- **Compliance *is* compassion here.** Honest, itemized disclosure (the Funeral Rule) is what a well-served family experiences as respect. Never frame disclosure as friction.
- **First call and aftercare are the emotional bookends** — the two stages a family remembers most; under-resourcing either is disproportionately costly.
- **Personalization is the modern expectation** — celebrant services, life tributes, and meaningful memorialization (especially for cremation families) are how a service feels like *this* person's, not a template.

## Technology (dated — verify current tools before recommending)

- **Case-management systems** — the operational spine: case intake, task/stage tracking, forms/permits, the itemized statement, and pre-need records. _(Category-level; specific vendors and features are volatile — verify.)_
- **Online obituaries** — the practice site + syndication; the tribute wall and condolence/flower functions.
- **Livestreaming / recording** of services — now a standard family expectation, especially for distant relatives; a fulfillment and staffing consideration.
- **Payment / e-signature / online arrangement** intake — reduces friction at the billing and pre-need stages.
