# Enablement precedes expectation (§3 #6)

**Rule.** A partner who has not been onboarded, certified, and given a repeatable play
will not produce. Expecting pipeline from an unenabled partner is a forecasting error,
not a partner failure.

**Why.** Time-to-first-deal is dominated by enablement quality. Signing partners faster
than you can enable them produces a large roster with a low active rate.

**Do**
- Sequence onboarding → certification → a repeatable play before forecasting a partner.
- Track time-to-first-deal and certified-partner coverage as leading indicators.
- Match signing pace to enablement capacity.

**Don't**
- Put an unenabled partner in the pipeline forecast.
- Treat a low active-partner rate as the partners' fault rather than an enablement gap.
