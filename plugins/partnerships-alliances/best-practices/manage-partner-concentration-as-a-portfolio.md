# Manage partner concentration as a portfolio (§3 #7)

**Rule.** A program where one or two partners drive most of the sourced revenue carries
the same single-source risk as any supply base. Know your concentration and cultivate
the middle tier deliberately.

**Why.** A dominant partner has pricing and roadmap leverage over you, and a single
defection can crater the program's number. Concentration is invisible until you split
the headline metrics by partner.

**Do**
- Split partner-sourced ARR by partner and watch the top-1 and top-3 share.
- Invest in the middle tier — the partners who could become material with enablement.
- Treat a rising concentration ratio as a risk finding, with a mitigation and an owner.

**Don't**
- Report only the aggregate program number and miss the concentration underneath it.
- Let a single partner become un-substitutable without a plan.
