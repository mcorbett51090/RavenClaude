# MDF is an investment with a return, not a rebate (§3 #5)

**Rule.** Market-development funds are tied to an approved plan, require proof of
performance, and are measured on sourced-pipeline ROI with a floor below which funds
are redirected.

**Why.** Unmeasured MDF becomes a channel entitlement that funds activity nobody tracks.
The ROI floor is what makes it an investment rather than a rebate.

**Do**
- Require a plan (objective, segment, expected sourced pipeline) before disbursing.
- Pre-approve spend, require proof-of-performance, and enforce a claim window.
- Measure MDF ROI and redirect funds below the floor; report ROI on the QBR cadence.

**Don't**
- Accrue MDF as an automatic percentage with no plan.
- Reimburse without evidence.

**Note.** MDF tax treatment varies by jurisdiction — flag it for counsel; cite any market
MDF-rate norm with a source + date.
