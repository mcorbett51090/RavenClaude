# The joint value proposition is the product, not the logos (§3 #4)

**Rule.** An alliance needs a specific joint outcome the customer cannot get from
either party alone. Two brands on a slide is not a reason to buy.

**Why.** Customers buy outcomes, not co-branding. Alliances without a real joint value
proposition generate marketing activity and no pipeline, and the integration rots
because no one owns a reason to maintain it.

**Do**
- State the joint value proposition as one measurable customer outcome.
- Tie it to a real product integration or combined delivery, with an owner.
- Kill or pause alliances that can't articulate a JVP.

**Don't**
- Justify an alliance with logo exposure or "strategic relationship" language.
- Ship a joint solution nobody owns end to end.
