# A partner tier is a set of obligations, not a logo wall (§3 #2)

**Rule.** Every tier must trade concrete partner obligations (certifications, minimum
sourced pipeline, joint capacity, references) for concrete benefits (margin, MDF,
leads, support). A benefit with no matching obligation is a discount with a badge.

**Why.** Tiers exist to change partner behavior. Benefits granted for a logo produce
a roster of inactive partners collecting margin and no sourced revenue.

**Do**
- List the obligation side of each tier first, then attach benefits to it.
- Make thresholds measurable and time-bound, with promotion **and** demotion rules.
- Re-qualify tiers on a cadence (typically annual) with a named owner.

**Don't**
- Grant a benefit that no obligation earns.
- Build a tier you can never be demoted from — that's an entitlement.
