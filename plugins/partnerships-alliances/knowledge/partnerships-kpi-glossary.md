# Partnerships & channel KPI glossary

Definitions the team holds constant so a metric always ships with a definition, window,
and baseline. Where a benchmark is implied it is **directional** — cite a dated source
before using it (§3 #8).

| Metric | Definition | Notes / trap |
|---|---|---|
| **Partner-sourced pipeline** | Open pipeline on opportunities the partner *originated* (registered before direct engagement). | The number finance trusts. Ambiguous deals are **not** sourced (§3 #1). |
| **Partner-influenced pipeline** | Open pipeline on deals direct already had, that a partner touched. | Report **separately** from sourced; never sum the two. |
| **Partner-sourced ARR / revenue** | Closed-won ARR from sourced opportunities, over a stated window vs a baseline. | The headline program result. Reconcile against direct quota to avoid double-count. |
| **Deal-registration conversion** | Registered deals → closed-won, over a window. | Low conversion = registration theater or weak enablement (§3 #6). |
| **Partner-sourced % of new business** | Sourced ARR ÷ total new ARR. | Also the **concentration** lens — split it by partner (§3 #7). |
| **PRR (Partner Return Rate) / partner ROI** | Partner-sourced gross margin ÷ fully-loaded cost-to-serve partners. | The "is the program worth it" number. |
| **MDF ROI** | Sourced pipeline from an activity ÷ MDF spent on it. | Needs a floor; require proof-of-performance (§3 #5). |
| **Time-to-first-deal** | Onboarding start → a partner's first sourced closed-won. | Long times signal an enablement gap, not a partner failure (§3 #6). |
| **Certified-partner coverage** | Partners meeting the certification obligation ÷ active partners. | The obligation side of the tier model (§3 #2). |
| **Active-partner rate** | Partners with a sourced deal in the window ÷ total signed partners. | A big signed-partner count with a low active rate is a vanity roster. |
| **Partner NPS / satisfaction** | Partner-reported satisfaction with the program. | Leading indicator of future sourced revenue. |

## Reporting rules (team standing)
- **Sourced and influenced are two lines, never one** (§3 #1).
- Every metric carries a **definition, window, and baseline**.
- Every external benchmark (margin, MDF rate, marketplace fee) carries a **source + date** or a `[verify-at-use]` mark (§3 #8).
- Split the headline numbers by partner to expose **concentration** (§3 #7).
