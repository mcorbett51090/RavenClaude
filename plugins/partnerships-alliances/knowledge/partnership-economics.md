# Partner-motion economics

The economic reference the agents use to choose a motion and price a program. All
external norms below are **directional and volatile** — treat any specific rate as
`[unverified — training knowledge]` and re-verify with a dated source before it enters
a deliverable (§3 #8).

---

## The four motions, by who owns the transaction and the margin

| Motion | Partner owns | Typical vendor cost | Where value comes from | Watch for |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Reseller / VAR** | Transaction + margin | Partner margin/discount off list | Reach into a buyer you can't serve direct | Channel conflict with direct; margin stacking |
| **SI / services** | Delivery + often transaction | Margin + enablement + MDF | Implementation capacity you don't have | Under-enabled partners; quality variance |
| **ISV / tech alliance** | A product integration | Rev-share and/or marketplace fee, co-sell effort | A joint outcome from the integration | Integration rots without ownership; no JVP |
| **Referral** | The introduction only | Referral fee on closed revenue | Low-friction lead flow | Paying for influenced deals as sourced |

**Rule (§3):** don't grant resale margin to a partner who only refers, and don't expect
a referral partner to carry delivery. The motion sets the cost structure.

## Cost-to-serve a partner

A partner is not free pipeline. The fully-loaded cost includes partner margin/discount,
MDF, partner-team headcount, enablement/certification delivery, PRM tooling, and co-marketing.
A program is worth it when **partner-sourced gross margin** exceeds this cost-to-serve — the
same portfolio discipline as any channel (§3 #7). Concentration matters: if one or two
partners drive most sourced revenue, the program carries single-source risk.

## MDF ROI

MDF is measured, not entitled (§3 #5):

```
MDF ROI = (partner-sourced pipeline attributable to the activity × win rate × ACV)
          ÷ MDF spent on the activity
```

Set an **ROI floor** (e.g. redirect funds below a defined multiple of MDF spent in sourced
pipeline). The floor is what converts MDF from a rebate into an investment. Require
proof-of-performance before reimbursement.

## Marketplace / rev-share reality

Cloud and app marketplaces typically take a **listing/transaction fee** off the deal, often
reduced for private-offer or co-sell-eligible transactions, and the economics change with
program status. These fees and thresholds move frequently — always pull the current schedule
from the marketplace's own documentation with a retrieval date; never quote a marketplace fee
from memory in a customer- or finance-facing model.

## Sourced vs influenced — the economic reason it matters

Reporting influenced pipeline as sourced overstates program ROI and, worse, can double-count
revenue already credited to a direct rep's quota. Every program economics claim reconciles
against the direct number (seam: `sales-revops`). One defined attribution rule, applied
consistently, beats a generous rule applied optimistically.
