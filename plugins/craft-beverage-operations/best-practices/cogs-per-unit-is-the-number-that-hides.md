# COGS per unit is the number that hides

**Status:** Absolute rule
**Domain:** Production / cost
**Applies to:** `craft-beverage-operations`

> Operations rule. No PII. COGS ranges are `[verify-at-use]` — model on the producer's own numbers.

---

## Why this exists

You can't price what you can't cost, and in craft beverage the true unit cost hides — in yield loss, in packaging that's a larger share than owners expect, and in overhead absorbed across too few units. Every pricing and channel decision downstream is built on COGS per unit; if it's wrong, the channel-mix and pricing calls are wrong too.

## How to apply

- Decompose COGS per finished unit: raw material, **yield loss** (a cost line, not an afterthought), packaging (glass/can/keg + label + closure + case), and overhead absorption.
- Model on the producer's **own** process and volume — low volume inflates overhead per unit.
- Nail it before any pricing or DTC-vs-wholesale decision.
- Hand the true unit cost to the channel-mix analysis.

**Do:** decompose and defend COGS per unit before pricing.
**Don't:** treat yield loss as free; compare channel margins on gross price.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

For a loss-leader or brand-building SKU, you may knowingly sell below full-absorption cost — but you must still know the cost to make that choice deliberately.
