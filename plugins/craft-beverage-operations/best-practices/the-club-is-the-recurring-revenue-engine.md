# The club is the recurring-revenue engine

**Status:** Absolute rule
**Domain:** DTC / recurring revenue
**Applies to:** `craft-beverage-operations`

> Operations rule. No PII. Club norms are `[verify-at-use]`.

---

## Why this exists

A club/membership member is predictable, high-margin depletion and a brand advocate — worth far more than a single case sold. The club is the craft producer's recurring-revenue engine. But a club with strong sign-ups and quiet churn is a leaky bucket that feels healthy while it drains; the discipline is to design tiers on lifetime value and manage churn as hard as acquisition.

## How to apply

- Design tiers on **member lifetime value** — shipment value × frequency × tenure — not on one shipment's margin.
- Read churn by **cohort** (after shipment 1? after a price change? seasonal?) to find the driver, usually value/frequency fit rather than price.
- Treat each shipment as a **retention moment** — curation, communication, flexibility (skip/swap).
- Coordinate club allocation with production (`craft-beverage-operations-lead`); don't over-commit past supply.

**Do:** price tiers on LTV; read churn by cohort; treat shipments as retention.
**Don't:** celebrate sign-ups while ignoring churn; over-commit allocation.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

A tiny allocation-only club for scarce releases may optimize for brand/scarcity over LTV volume — but still manage churn and allocation deliberately.
