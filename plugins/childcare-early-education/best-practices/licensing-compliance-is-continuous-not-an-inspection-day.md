# Licensing compliance is continuous, not an inspection day

**Status:** Absolute rule
**Domain:** Compliance
**Applies to:** `childcare-early-education`

> Advisory compliance rule. Licensing requirements are **state-specific** — `[verify-at-use, state-specific]`. Not legal or licensing advice; not a substitute for your licensor's determination. No child PII.

---

## Why this exists

A center that "gets ready" for a licensing visit is, by definition, a center that was **out of compliance between visits** — and between visits is where children actually spend their days. Ratios, staff files, health-and-safety checks, and records are either maintained as **daily systems** or discovered as citations. Compliance-as-a-scramble also fails on its own terms: many domains (ratio held every moment, contemporaneous incident documentation, current immunization records) cannot be reconstructed the night before an inspection. Making "in compliance" the resting state is both the safe posture for children and the only reliable way to pass an inspection.

## How to apply

- Treat each **licensing domain** as a daily-maintained system: ratios & group size, staff qualifications & files, health & safety, records & documentation, physical environment.
- Run **self-audits** on the domains on a cadence, triaged by risk — **ratio/group-size first** (fastest, most serious citations), then staff files (which can void ratio-countable headcount), then health-safety and records.
- Keep documentation **contemporaneous** — incidents, medication, drills, and health-and-safety checks recorded when they happen, with signatures and retention.
- Verify every requirement against your **current state regulation** `[verify-at-use, state-specific]`.

**Do:** audit domains routinely; fix the ratio and staff-file gaps first.
**Don't:** treat a visit as the deadline; reconstruct records after the fact.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

Specific requirements, inspection cadence, and domain structure vary by state and license type. The *continuous* principle does not vary — only which rules you are continuously meeting.
