# Enroll the waitlist before you discount

**Status:** Pattern
**Domain:** Enrollment / revenue
**Applies to:** `childcare-early-education`

> Advisory operations rule. No child/family PII — work in cohorts and funnel stages.

---

## Why this exists

Discounting tuition to fill a seat that a family was already waiting for is **margin given away for nothing**. A center with a waitlist for an age band and a soft schedule usually has a **conversion problem, not a price problem** — the demand exists; it is leaking somewhere in the funnel (inquiry, tour, follow-up, or start). Cutting price papers over the leak, trains the market to expect discounts, and lowers the revenue on seats you could have filled at full tuition. The discipline is to exhaust the waitlist and fix the funnel leak first, and reserve price moves for when demand is genuinely absent.

## How to apply

- Segment the **waitlist by age band and desired start date** — availability opens by room, so a single list hides real, convertible demand.
- Diagnose the funnel by **stage** (inquiry -> tour -> application -> start) and fix the leaking stage; it is most often **tour follow-up**, not the tour.
- Attach a **follow-up cadence** to every tour (same-day, decision-window, waitlist-or-start ask).
- Only consider a discount once you have confirmed **no waitlist exists** for that age band and the funnel is converting.

**Do:** work the waitlist and the follow-up cadence first; treat a discount as a last lever.
**Don't:** discount into a live waitlist; run tours with no follow-up.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

A genuinely new center with no base and no waitlist is acquisition-led and may use introductory pricing to seed enrollment — a deliberate market-entry choice, not a reflex to a soft week. Sibling-discount and staff-tuition policies are relationship investments, not funnel discounts, and are governed separately.
