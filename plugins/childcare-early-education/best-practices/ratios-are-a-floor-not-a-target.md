# Ratios are a floor, not a target

**Status:** Absolute rule
**Domain:** Compliance / operations
**Applies to:** `childcare-early-education`

> Advisory compliance rule. Child:staff ratios and group-size caps are **state-specific** — `[verify-at-use, state-specific]`. This is not legal or licensing advice. No child PII.

---

## Why this exists

The required child:staff ratio and group-size cap by age are the **minimum legal staffing and the maximum children** a room may hold — set by state licensing regulation to keep children safe. They are a **hard floor the center may never drop below**, not a payroll number to aim at. Treating a ratio as a target invites two failures at once: staffing so lean that a single call-out or a break puts the room out of compliance, and enrolling to a headcount the room may not legally hold. A room out of ratio for even a moment is out of compliance, regardless of the daily average.

## How to apply

- Read **ratio and group size as two separate limits** — a room can satisfy one and violate the other. Check both, by age, for every configuration.
- Count only **ratio-countable** (qualified, background-checked, trained) adults toward the ratio `[verify-at-use, state-specific]`.
- Hold ratio **every moment** — open, close, breaks, nap transitions — not on average.
- For mixed-age rooms, staff to the **youngest child present** unless the state rule says otherwise `[verify-at-use, state-specific]`.

**Do:** treat the ratio as an inviolable floor; schedule float coverage so a break never drops the room below it.
**Don't:** enroll or staff *to* the ratio as if it were the plan; average ratio across the day.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

The ratio itself never bends. What varies is the *number* — it is state-specific and age-specific, and some states apply different rules for licensed-exempt or family-childcare settings. Confirm the applicable rule; the floor principle holds regardless.
