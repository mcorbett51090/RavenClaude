# Minimum necessary is the disclosure default

A valid consent on file authorizes a disclosure — it does not authorize sending *everything*. Disclose only the information the recipient actually needs for the stated purpose: the specific records, date range, and content the ROI names, not the whole chart by reflex. A benefits/auth request needs the diagnosis and medical-necessity support, not the full session-by-session narrative; a referral needs the clinically relevant history the receiving provider asked for, not unrelated content. Minimum-necessary is the default scope, tightened further for 42 CFR Part 2 SUD content (only the specifically consented content, with the redisclosure-prohibition notice). When unsure how much the purpose requires, send less and offer more on a follow-up request. Over-disclosure on a valid consent is still a privacy failure — and route any question about what a record's clinical content *means* to a clinician.
