# The referral loop closes, or it leaks

A referral is not done when it's sent — it's done when the loop is closed: the client reached the destination (an internal clinician panel, a higher level of care, an outside specialist) and that's recorded, or the gap is surfaced and worked. Inbound referrals that sit unacknowledged and outbound referrals with no confirmed landing are where clients fall through — between the people who care about them. Track every referral to a terminal state (scheduled / declined / lost-to-contact), put a name and a date on each open one, and escalate the stalls instead of letting them age silently in a queue. A referral with no closing step is a client quietly dropped. Any decision about *which* level of care or *which* clinician is clinically appropriate routes to a licensed clinician; the loop-tracking is operational.
