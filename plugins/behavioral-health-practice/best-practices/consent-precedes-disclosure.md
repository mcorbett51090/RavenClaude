# Consent precedes disclosure

No record leaves the practice — to a payer, a referral, a family member, anyone — without the right consent on file. The release of information (ROI) is the gate: verify it exists, covers this recipient and purpose, and is current *before* any disclosure, every time. Disclose only what the ROI authorizes (minimum necessary). For substance-use content, a general HIPAA authorization is not enough — the specific 42 CFR Part 2 consent must be on file. A disclosure with no verified ROI is the default failure mode and is flagged on sight.
