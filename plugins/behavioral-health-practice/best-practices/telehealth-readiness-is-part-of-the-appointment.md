# Telehealth readiness is part of the appointment

A telehealth visit fails differently than an in-person one: the client forgets the appointment *and* can't find the link, the camera doesn't work, or they're in a location that breaks privacy or coverage. Treat readiness as a step of the appointment, not an afterthought — send the join link plus a brief tech check ahead of time, confirm the client's location (for licensure, payer, and emergency-response reasons), and confirm consent to the modality. A "couldn't connect" no-show is an operational failure to prevent, not a client failure. The clinical-appropriateness of telehealth for a given client is the clinician's call; the readiness logistics are this plugin's. Confirm the place-of-service and telehealth modifier policy as `[verify-at-build]` against the current payer policy before billing the visit.
