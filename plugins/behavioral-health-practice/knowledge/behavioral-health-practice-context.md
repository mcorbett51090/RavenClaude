## Where defensible benchmarks come from

No-show rates, access-time, reimbursement, and utilization benchmarks are **setting-, payer-, and date-specific** and move year to year. Defensible sources require a dated cut: the practice's own EHR/PM-system history (the most defensible), payer contracts, and published behavioral-health operations reports. **Name the source and date, or mark the figure `[unverified — training knowledge]` (§3 #8).**

## Operating rhythm

- **Schedule/no-show review** weekly — slots lost, recovery, waitlist backfill (§3 #1).
- **Documentation/compliance check** on a fixed cadence — note timeliness and medical-necessity completeness (§3 #3); clinical review by the clinician.
- **Caseload/demand review** monthly — utilization vs the no-show-adjusted fill rate (§3 #4).
- **Payer-mix/margin review** quarterly — blended and per-payer margin, parity flags to counsel (§3 #5).

## The standing caution

Diagnosis, treatment planning, medical-necessity, licensing, mental-health parity, and any compliance/audit determination are the **licensed clinician's, the board's, or counsel's** call — the team frames the operational decision and routes the determination. Keep patient PHI out of all deliverables (§2). This team is not an EHR and not a clinical or legal authority (§3 #8).
