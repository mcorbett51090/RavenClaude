# Client Review and Suitability

> **Not legal advice.** This skill produces an educational compliance framework and checklist, not a legal conclusion or a personalized recommendation. Firm-specific obligations, registration status, and anything legally consequential route to the firm's compliance counsel.

## Suitability is gathered, then refreshed
Capture the client's objectives, risk tolerance *and capacity*, time horizon, liquidity needs, financial situation, and tax status before a recommendation — and re-confirm them at every review. Risk capacity (can they afford the loss) matters as much as tolerance (comfort with it). Document the basis for the recommendation.

## Fiduciary duty vs Reg BI
An RIA owes a fiduciary duty under the Advisers Act — duty of care + loyalty, the client's interest first, conflicts disclosed and managed. Reg BI is a *different* broker-dealer best-interest standard. Name which applies; never conflate them; route the firm-specific determination to counsel.

## Form ADV touchpoints
Know what each is for: Part 1 (registration), Part 2A (the brochure), Part 2B (brochure supplement), Form CRS (the relationship summary). The actual filing routes to counsel.

## The periodic review is a control
Each review re-confirms suitability, checks the IPS still fits, and documents the meeting — not just a market update. Set the cadence and a standard agenda; record what was re-confirmed.

## Books-and-records & the marketing rule
Retain the basis for advice, the reviews, the IPS, and the disclosures (the audit-trail mindset — if it isn't documented, it didn't happen). Apply the marketing rule to testimonials/endorsements and performance claims: substantiation and the required disclosures, not adjectives.

## Output
A suitability/KYC checklist with gaps flagged, the fiduciary-vs-Reg-BI framing, the review agenda, and the records implications — with the not-legal-advice disclaimer and the items to confirm with counsel. The plan comes from `goal-based-financial-plan`; the IPS from `portfolio-construction-and-ips`.
