# Portfolio Construction and the IPS

> **Not investment advice.** This skill produces an educational portfolio-design framework, not a personalized recommendation to buy or sell any security. The licensed adviser applies it after confirming suitability; tax specifics route to a CPA.

## The IPS is the governing document
Draft the Investment Policy Statement first: objectives, constraints (liquidity, horizon, taxes, legal, unique circumstances), the target allocation with ranges, the rebalancing rule, and review triggers. Allocation and trades flow from the IPS or they're ad-hoc. Record it in books-and-records.

## Asset allocation dominates security selection
Set the across- and within-asset-class split for the stated risk profile and horizon, always with ranges — a target with no band is unenforceable. Diversification is the one free lunch; broad, low-cost building blocks beat concentration for almost every individual client. Match the allocation to the drawdown the client can actually hold.

## Rebalancing is a written rule
Pick calendar (on a schedule) or threshold/bands (on drift) and write it into the IPS. Bands trade less and only when drift is real — pair them with a max-time backstop. In taxable accounts, rebalance tax-aware (new contributions and harvested losses first).

## Tax-aware implementation
Asset location: tax-inefficient assets in tax-deferred, tax-efficient in taxable, growth in Roth — often worth more than a cheaper fund. Tax-loss harvesting captures losses to offset gains, but mind the wash-sale rule (substantially-identical repurchase within the window disallows the loss). Route the bracket-specific call to a CPA.

## Output
A draft IPS: objectives, constraints, target allocation + ranges, the rebalancing rule, review triggers, and the tax-aware implementation notes — with the not-investment-advice disclaimer and the facts to confirm. Hand the suitability gate to `client-review-and-suitability`; the underlying goals come from `goal-based-financial-plan`.
