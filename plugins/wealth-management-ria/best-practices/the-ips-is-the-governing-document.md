# The Investment Policy Statement is the governing document

Asset allocation, rebalancing, and every later portfolio decision flow from a written Investment Policy Statement — objectives, constraints (liquidity, horizon, taxes, legal, unique), target ranges, the rebalancing rule, and review triggers. No IPS means no discipline, just ad-hoc trades with no policy to check them against. Draft the IPS first; record it in books-and-records so the basis for every position is documented.
