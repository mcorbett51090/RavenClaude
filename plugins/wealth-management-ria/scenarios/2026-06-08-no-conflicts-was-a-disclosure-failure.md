## Problem

An RIA's client-facing materials and Form ADV brochure stated, in good faith, "we have no conflicts of interest" — the team genuinely believed a flat advisory fee removed them. But the practice still earned more when assets stayed under management (a disincentive to recommend paying off a mortgage or funding a pension buyout), used a custodian whose platform credits offset the firm's costs, and had an adviser who also sold insurance through a separate entity. The "no conflicts" line wasn't a clean record — under the RIA's fiduciary duty it was a *disclosure failure*, because a fiduciary discloses and manages conflicts rather than asserting none exist. Separately, staff had started describing the firm's standard as "best interest, same as Reg BI," conflating the Advisers Act fiduciary duty with the broker-dealer rule.

## Constraints context

- The team conflated "we don't take commissions" with "we have no conflicts" — AUM-based incentives and custodian economics were invisible to them.
- "Fiduciary" and "Reg BI" were being used interchangeably in client conversations and training.
- The insurance side-business sat in a separate entity, so nobody mapped it as a conflict in the advisory file.

## Attempts

- Tried: just deleting the "no conflicts" sentence. Helped the inaccuracy but left the underlying conflicts undisclosed and unmanaged — silence isn't disclosure under a fiduciary standard.
- Tried: a generic boilerplate "conflicts may exist" paragraph. Failed — a vague catch-all doesn't identify the *specific* conflicts or how they're managed, which is what the fiduciary duty (and the ADV) actually requires.
- Tried: enumerating each real conflict (AUM incentive, custodian economics, outside insurance business), describing how each is managed (e.g., advice documented against the client's goals regardless of AUM impact; the insurance entity disclosed and the client free to decline), correcting the ADV brochure, and retraining staff that the RIA fiduciary standard is *not* Reg BI. This worked.

## Resolution

Naming and managing each conflict — instead of denying all of them — put the firm back on the right side of its fiduciary duty, and the ADV brochure matched reality. The "should they pay down the mortgage" conversation could now happen honestly, with the AUM incentive on the table rather than hidden. Re-drawing the line between the Advisers Act fiduciary duty and Reg BI stopped staff from describing a weaker standard as their own. The defensible posture turned out to be the honest one: a disclosed, managed conflict is fine; an asserted absence of conflicts is the violation.

## Lesson

A fiduciary discloses and manages conflicts — "we have none" is itself a disclosure failure — and the RIA fiduciary duty is not Reg BI. Enumerate each real conflict (AUM incentives, custodian economics, outside business), say how each is managed, keep Form ADV matching reality, and never let staff conflate the Advisers Act standard with the broker-dealer best-interest rule. This is an educational/operational framework, not legal advice — the deep securities-law read routes to a compliance attorney.
