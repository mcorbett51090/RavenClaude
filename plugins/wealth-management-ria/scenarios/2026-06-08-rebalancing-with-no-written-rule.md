## Problem

An advisory practice managed dozens of client portfolios with target allocations but no written Investment Policy Statement and no documented rebalancing rule. Rebalancing happened "when someone noticed," which in practice meant rarely and emotionally — portfolios drifted far from target in a bull run (nobody wanted to sell winners) and got rebalanced in a panic during a drop. In taxable accounts the ad-hoc trades also triggered avoidable short-term gains. When a client asked "why did you sell this and when do you do it," there was no policy to point to.

## Constraints context

- Target allocations existed in a planning tool but with no ranges/bands and no governing document.
- A mix of taxable and tax-advantaged accounts per client; no asset-location discipline.
- Rebalancing decisions were discretionary and undocumented — a books-and-records gap as much as a portfolio one.

## Attempts

- Tried: a strict quarterly calendar rebalance for everyone. Helped consistency but over-traded the taxable accounts (rebalancing on the calendar even when drift was tiny) and created unnecessary tax drag.
- Tried: telling advisers to "rebalance when it feels off." Failed — that's the original discretionary problem with a new name; no two advisers did it the same way and nothing was documented.
- Tried: writing an IPS per client with a target allocation *plus ranges*, a threshold (band) rebalancing rule with an annual max-time backstop, and a tax-aware instruction to use new contributions and harvested losses first in taxable accounts — then recording the IPS in books-and-records. This worked.

## Resolution

The bands meant portfolios only traded when drift was real, which cut both the trade count and the tax drag, and the annual backstop kept anything from drifting forever. Because the rule was written into the IPS, every rebalance had a policy to point to and a record behind it — the "why did you sell this" question now had a documented answer. Asset-location discipline (tax-inefficient assets into tax-deferred) layered on top added after-tax return with no extra risk. The discipline came from the written rule, not from any adviser's in-the-moment judgment.

## Lesson

The IPS is the governing document and rebalancing is a written rule, not a feeling. Give the target allocation ranges, choose threshold/bands (with a max-time backstop) over a blind calendar in taxable accounts, rebalance tax-aware, and record the IPS in books-and-records so every trade has a documented basis. A target with no band and no written rule is just discretionary trading waiting to drift.
