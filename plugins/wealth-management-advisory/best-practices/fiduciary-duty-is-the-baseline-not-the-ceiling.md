# Fiduciary duty is the baseline, not the ceiling

**Status:** Pattern
**Domain:** Advisory practice, RIA culture
**Applies to:** `wealth-management-advisory`

---

## Why this exists

RIAs registered under the Investment Advisers Act owe a fiduciary duty to their clients — the duty
of care (make suitable recommendations based on reasonable investigation) and the duty of loyalty
(put the client's interests ahead of the advisor's own). This is the legal minimum.

The pattern this rule codifies is a cultural upgrade: **fiduciary is where you start, not where
you stop**. An advisor who asks only "does this clear Reg BI?" or "am I technically within my
fiduciary duty?" is optimizing for the minimum. The advisor who asks "is this the best option
I can honestly recommend for this client's situation, knowing what I know?" is operating at the
professional standard that builds a durable practice.

The difference matters in practice: two investments might both be technically suitable, but one
genuinely serves the client's interests better over time. Choosing the one that pays the advisor
more while meeting the technical suitability bar may be legally defensible but is a fiduciary
character failure.

## How to apply

**Do:**

- Ask two questions before every recommendation: (1) Does this clear Reg BI / suitability? (2) Is
  this the best option I would honestly choose for this client if my compensation were identical
  across all alternatives?
- Use the suitability clearance tree as a compliance floor, then apply professional judgment above
  it.
- When two alternatives are similarly suitable, default to the lower-cost option unless there is a
  documented, substantive reason the higher-cost option is in the client's best interest.
- Disclose all conflicts proactively, not reactively — the client should hear about a conflict from
  you, not discover it later.
- Treat the fiduciary standard as the lens for every advice interaction, not just formal
  recommendations.

**Don't:**

- Choose a product because it passes the compliance checklist when a clearly superior alternative
  exists.
- Frame a recommendation as "compliant" when you wouldn't choose it for a family member in the
  same situation.
- Treat conflict disclosure as a substitute for conflict management — "I disclosed it" is not
  a fiduciary defense if the conflict materially influenced the recommendation.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

- In a broker-dealer context (not an RIA relationship), the applicable standard is Reg BI (best
  interest), not the Advisers Act fiduciary duty — though the practical content of a well-applied
  Reg BI analysis is functionally similar for most retail recommendations.
- Execution-only or order-taking relationships (where the client directs the trade and the advisor
  takes no advisory role) do not trigger the recommendation-level fiduciary analysis — though the
  broader fiduciary duty continues for the RIA relationship.
- This rule applies to investment and planning advice; it does not govern legal or tax-return
  preparation advice (which has its own professional standards).

## Provenance

Codifies the duty of care and duty of loyalty under the Investment Advisers Act of 1940, as
interpreted in SEC guidance (e.g., the 2019 Fiduciary Interpretation Release, IA-5248). The
cultural framing (baseline not ceiling) is a practice-management best practice, not a specific
regulatory requirement. Consult the firm's compliance officer for firm-specific policies. Not
legal advice.
