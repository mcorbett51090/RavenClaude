# Suitability and Reg BI clearance before any recommendation

**Status:** Absolute rule
**Domain:** Advisory compliance, RIA practice
**Applies to:** `wealth-management-advisory`

---

## Why this exists

Regulation Best Interest (Reg BI), effective June 2020, requires broker-dealers making
recommendations to retail customers to act in the customer's best interest at the time of the
recommendation. RIAs operating under the Advisers Act owe a fiduciary duty — the highest standard
in the advisory relationship. Both frameworks require that a recommendation be grounded in the
client's specific investment profile (objectives, time horizon, risk tolerance, financial situation,
liquidity needs, tax status, and investment experience) before it reaches the client.

A recommendation without a documented suitability basis is both a regulatory exposure (Reg BI
violation, fiduciary breach) and a practice risk (client harm, client complaint, FINRA/SEC
examination finding). The documentation is the proof that the analysis happened.

## How to apply

Before any product or strategy recommendation leaves a planning workflow or a proposal:

1. Confirm a current (within 12 months, or after any material life event) written client investment
   profile exists on file.
2. Confirm the recommendation aligns with the documented objectives, risk tolerance, and time
   horizon — and state why.
3. Identify and document all material conflicts of interest (compensation, proprietary products,
   revenue sharing) and disclose them in writing before or at the time of recommendation.
4. Document the reasonably available alternatives considered and why the recommended option is
   in the client's best interest over those alternatives.
5. Route the completed documentation through `advisory-compliance-advisor` before delivery.

**Do:**

- Treat the four Reg BI obligations (care, disclosure, conflict-of-interest, compliance) as a
  checklist, not a formality — each one has substance.
- Update the client profile whenever a material change in circumstances occurs (retirement date
  shift, health change, inheritance, divorce).
- Give heightened scrutiny to complex and expensive products (variable annuities, non-traded REITs,
  leveraged/inverse ETFs) — the suitability bar is higher.

**Don't:**

- Recommend a product because it was suitable for a similar client — suitability is specific to
  this client's documented profile.
- Treat disclosure of a conflict as sufficient — Reg BI requires mitigation, not just disclosure.
- Sign off on a recommendation without a written rationale, even if the recommendation seems
  obviously appropriate.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

There is no exception to this rule for product recommendations. However:
- General educational content (explaining how a Roth IRA works, describing what a bond is) does
  not trigger the suitability obligation because it is not a recommendation.
- Market commentary that does not include a specific call to action for a specific client is
  educational, not a recommendation — though it must still comply with the Marketing Rule.
- Suitability analysis applies to recommendations; the advisor's portfolio management discretion
  (for a discretionary account) is governed by the fiduciary duty and the IPS, not the per-
  recommendation analysis.
