## A) Money Service Business (MSB)

| Instrument | Year | Role | URL |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Money Service Business Act 2016** (2016:36) | in force **31 Jan 2017** `[commencement-notice unverified]` | Primary licensing statute | https://www.bma.bm/documents-centre/documents-legislation |
| **Guidance Notes — Money Service Business Act 2016** | 2018 | Minimum criteria + duties/principles | https://www.bma.bm/viewPDF/documents/2018-12-29-05-36-47-Guidance-Notes---Money-Service-Business-Act-2016.pdf |
| **(Proposed) Payment Services Act** | consultation Apr 2025 — **not enacted** | Would replace the MSB Act 2016 with a risk-based PSP framework | https://www.bma.bm/viewPDF/documents/2025-04-15-15-55-32-Discussion-Paper---Money-Service-Business---Payment-Services-Provider-Regulatory-Framework.pdf |

- **Definition (s.2(2)):** "the business of providing any or all of the following services to the general public" — **money transmission**; operating a **bureau de change** (cash-for-cash currency exchange); **cheque cashing**; **issuing/selling/redeeming drafts, money orders or traveller's cheques for cash**; and **payment services business**.
- **Who must be licensed:** no person may carry on money service business in or from within Bermuda (or as agent for such a person) without a **BMA licence**. **Exemptions:** institutions licensed under the **Banks and Deposit Companies Act 1999** are exempt; a listed service provided **ancillary** without a separate charge is "not likely" to be treated as in scope.
- **Licensing/supervision:** minimum criteria cover fitness & propriety of controllers/management, prudent conduct, and ongoing financial viability; supervision via regular senior-management meetings, scrutiny of financial/statistical information, and periodic compliance visits.
- **Code/Statement of Principles:** the duties-and-principles content is carried in the **Guidance Notes**; a discrete separately-titled "MSB Code of Conduct" with an issuance date was **not isolated** in primary text `[unverified]`.
- **Net assets / safeguarding / fees:** `[unverified]` — **do not** apply the US$100,000 digital-asset figure to MSBs (a frequent conflation); pin to the Act's schedules / MSB (fees) regulations.

## B) Digital Asset Business (DAB)

| Instrument | Year | Role | URL |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Digital Asset Business Act 2018 (DABA)** (2018:28) | in force **10 Sep 2018** | Primary licensing statute | https://www.bma.bm/viewPDF/documents/2023-11-14-11-11-48-Digital-Asset-Business-Act-2018.pdf |
| **Digital Asset Business Amendment Act 2023** (2023:19) | 2023 | Amendments | https://www.commonlii.org/bm/legis/num_act/dabaa2023315.pdf |
| **Digital Asset Issuance Act 2020 (DAIA)** | 2020 | ICO/token-offering regime (second of Bermuda's two digital-asset regimes) | — |
| **Digital Asset Business (Cyber Risk) Rules 2023** | eff. **1 Jan 2024** | Replaced the Cybersecurity Rules 2018 | https://www.bma.bm/digital-assets-supervision-regulation |
| **Operational Cyber Risk Management Code of Practice** | 2025 | Cyber code | https://www.bma.bm/viewPDF/documents/2025-03-26-10-03-57-Digital-Asset-Business---Operational-Cyber-Risk-Management-Code-of-Practice.pdf |
| **Digital Asset Business (Custody of Client Assets) Rules 2025** | 2025 | Custody/segregation of client digital assets | https://www.bma.bm/digital-assets-supervision-regulation |

- **Definition / scope:** DABA licenses **issuing/selling/redeeming** digital assets; operating as a **payment service provider** using digital assets; operating a **digital asset exchange** (incl. facilitating ICOs); providing **custodial wallet services**; and operating as a **digital asset services vendor** (Class F also lists digital-asset **trust services** and **derivative exchange**). Benchmark administration sits within the broader services-vendor/derivative scope `[discrete statutory enumeration unverified]`.
- **Licence classes:**
  - **Class T (Test)** — any DAB activity for a **defined period**, for pilot/beta testing (time-boxed proof-of-concept).
  - **Class M (Modified)** — any/all DAB activities for a **defined period** with conditions (early/limited operation while building full-compliance capacity).
  - **Class F (Full)** — any/all DAB activities, highest authorisation, no transitional limitations.
- **Issuance (DAIA 2020):** regulates initial coin/token offerings; token/ICO issuance approval runs through this regime (Digital Asset Issuance Rules 2020).
- **Capital / custody:** **net assets ≥ US$100,000** for Class M and Class F (also cited for Class T) `[verified via secondary; statutory pin = Digital Asset Business (Prudential Standards) (Annual Return) Rules 2018, unverified]`. Client-asset custody under the **Custody of Client Assets Rules 2025**.
- **Codes:** (1) **Cyber Risk Rules 2023** (eff. 1 Jan 2024) + Operational Cyber Risk Management Code of Practice; (2) a DAB **Statement of Principles** and (3) a **sector-specific AML/ATF code** exist `[exact titles/dates unverified]`.
- **Supervision & AML:** licensed DAB undertakings are **AML/ATF-regulated financial institutions** and must comply with POCA/AMLR; the BMA applies a proportionality-based supervisory approach. Travel-rule application is consistent with VASP treatment `[specific provision/threshold unverified]`.

## How the agent uses this file

`bma-financial-institutions-specialist` reads this when an entity does **money services** or **digital assets** (the sector/licence tree in [`decision-trees.md`](decision-trees.md) routes here). **Do not apply the US$100k digital-asset net-asset minimum to an MSB.** Both sectors are **AML/ATF-regulated** — the AML overlay in [`overview.md`](overview.md) applies in full.
