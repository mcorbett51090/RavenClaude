## Scope — who is an AML/ATF Regulated Financial Institution (RFI)

RFIs are designated through **POCA s.42A** (e.g. **s.42A(1)(fa)** designates CSP business). The **BMA-supervised** RFI population spans: **banks/deposit-takers; trust companies; corporate service providers; investment business and fund administration; persons processing subscriptions/redemptions for collective investment schemes; trading members of the BSX; money service businesses; digital asset businesses; and long-term (life) insurers, insurance managers and insurance brokers** — **but not reinsurance**.

**Supervisory split (verified):** the **BMA** supervises the RFIs above + financial groups; the **Registrar of Companies** supervises dealers in high-value goods; the **Superintendent of Real Estate** supervises real-estate brokers/agents; the **Barristers & Accountants AML/ATF Board** supervises lawyers/accountants; the **Casino Gaming Commission** supervises casinos. *(This file addresses the BMA-supervised RFI track; route the others per [`overview.md`](overview.md).)*

## CDD / EDD / SDD (AMLR 2008)

**CDD trigger events:** an RFI applies CDD when it (i) establishes a business relationship; (ii) carries out an **occasional transaction**; (iii) suspects ML/TF; or (iv) doubts previously obtained ID data. CDD = identify + verify the customer; identify + verify the beneficial owner; obtain the purpose/intended nature of the relationship.

**Regulation cluster `[unverified — indicative numbering]`:** CDD measures/timing **Reg 5–7 area**; SDD **Reg 10 area**; EDD **Reg 11** (verbatim); record-keeping **Reg 14–17 area**; internal reporting / Reporting Officer **Reg 17–19 area**. Treat the bracketed numbers as indicative; only Reg 11 / Reg 11(1)(aa) are confirmed verbatim.

**Timing:** verification must, as a rule, be completed **before** the relationship is established / the occasional transaction carried out, with limited risk-based exceptions to complete during establishment.

**Enhanced due diligence (Reg 11 — verified):** mandatory on a risk-sensitive basis for **(a)** non-face-to-face customers; **(b)** **correspondent banking** with respondents outside Bermuda (understand the respondent's business, reputation, supervision quality, AML controls); **(c)** **PEPs**; and **(d)** — **Reg 11(1)(aa)** — any person/transaction **from or in a FATF/CFATF higher-risk country**. Reinforced by **AML/ATF Ministerial Advisory 2/2025** on higher-risk jurisdictions.

**Simplified due diligence (Reg 10 area):** permitted only in defined lower-risk cases meeting specified conditions `[unverified eligible categories]`.

**Ongoing monitoring / reliance / records:** RFIs must conduct **ongoing monitoring** (scrutinise transactions vs the known profile; keep CDD current); **reliance on third parties** is permitted but the relying RFI keeps liability; **records retained ≥ 5 years**, sufficient to reconstruct individual transactions.

## PEPs

The Regulations define **PEPs** (+ family members and close associates) and require EDD. **Foreign PEPs** attract full EDD — senior-management approval, source of wealth + source of funds, enhanced ongoing monitoring. **Domestic PEPs** must first be **risk-assessed**; where assessed higher-risk, the **full foreign-PEP EDD applies** (a risk-based domestic-PEP model, not blanket EDD).

## Compliance function

- **Reporting Officer (MLRO equivalent):** appointed to receive internal disclosures and onward-report to the FIA; need not be senior management but must be a qualified staff member; the **Compliance Officer and Reporting Officer may be the same person**.
- **Compliance Officer:** a member of management with sufficient authority/seniority and resource/information access.
- **Fit and proper:** the Reporting Officer is subject to the **fit-and-proper test under SEA Act s.11A**.
- **Risk-based approach:** risk-sensitive policies/controls/procedures; the Guidance Notes require a documented **business-wide (enterprise) risk assessment** + **customer-level risk assessments** `[exact regulation pin unverified]`.

## Reporting & the FIA

- **FIU:** the **Financial Intelligence Agency** (FIA Act 2007) receives/analyses/disseminates **SARs**.
- **Duty to report (POCA s.46):** an offence to **fail to report** to the FIA where, in the course of business, a person knows or suspects another is engaged in ML, as soon as reasonably practicable (legal-professional-privilege exception, not extending to furthering a criminal purpose).
- **Tipping-off (POCA s.47):** an offence to disclose information likely to prejudice an investigation where a report has been / may be made.
- **Penalties:** failure-to-report / tipping-off — summary **up to 3 yrs and/or $15,000**; on indictment **up to 10 yrs and/or unlimited fine**.

---

## BMA supervision & penalties

- **Risk-based supervision** under the **SEA Act 2008** — off-site review + on-site inspection on a risk-prioritised basis.
- **Guidance Notes:** General Guidance Notes for RFIs + **sector-specific annexes** (banking, insurance, trust, investment business, CSPs, digital assets, real estate…).
- **Civil penalty ceiling raised from $500,000 to $10,000,000** for BMA-supervised persons; the BMA has imposed real penalties (e.g. the Crown Global Life Insurance AML action). Powers also include public censure, directions/restrictions, and prohibition of individuals.

---

## National coordination

- **NAMLC (POCA s.49):** the National Anti-Money Laundering Committee sets the national plan and advises on AML/ATF legislation.
- **National Risk Assessment:** Bermuda ran NRAs in **2013, 2016, 2017, 2020**.
- **CFATF/FATF:** the **CFATF 4th-round MER** (plenary Nov 2019, published 2020) rated Bermuda **39/40 Recommendations Compliant/Largely Compliant** — among the strongest technical-compliance results globally. Bermuda is preparing for the **5th round**.
