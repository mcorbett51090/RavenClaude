## 1. The BMA itself

- **Establishing statute:** **Bermuda Monetary Authority Act 1969** (1969:57). The Authority began operations **20 Feb 1969**.
- **Currency power:** authority to issue Bermuda's national currency derives from **§9 of the BMA Act 1969**.
- **Integrated/consolidated supervisor:** the BMA is Bermuda's **single financial-services regulator** across **banks, trust companies, investment business, investment funds, fund administrators, digital-asset businesses, money-service businesses, corporate service providers (CSPs), and insurance**. Each sector has its own supervisory Act (Banks and Deposit Companies Act 1999; Insurance Act 1978; Investment Business Act 2003; Trusts (Regulation of Trust Business) Act 2001; Investment Funds Act 2006; Corporate Service Provider Business Act 2012; Money Service Business Act 2016; Digital Asset Business Act 2018).
- **Supervisory philosophy:** explicitly **risk-based and proportionate**.
- **Governance:** the **Board of Directors** (one executive + 11 non-executive directors, appointed by the Minister of Finance, terms 3–5 years) sets policy/strategy. "The Authority" is the statutory body; the Board is its governing organ.
- **Fees:** the BMA Act 1969 carries fee schedules; a **Jul 2025 consultation** proposed amendments to general powers and fee provisions `[pending — not yet enacted]`.

## 2. AML/ATF regime (cross-sectoral — binds every sector file)

| Instrument | In force | Role |
|---|---|---|
| **Proceeds of Crime Act 1997 (POCA)** | 19 Jan 1998 | Primary ML offence + confiscation statute; **§49** establishes **NAMLC** (National Anti-Money Laundering Committee) |
| **Proceeds of Crime (AML/ATF) Regulations 2008 (AMLR 2008)** | 1 Jan 2009 | Operative CDD/EDD/record-keeping rules |
| **Proceeds of Crime (AML/ATF Supervision and Enforcement) Act 2008 ("SE Act")** | 1 Jan 2009 | **Designates the BMA** as AML/ATF supervisor; compel-compliance + civil-penalty powers |
| **Anti-Terrorism (Financial and Other Measures) Act 2004** | 2004 | Terrorist-financing statute |
| **Financial Intelligence Agency Act 2007** | operable 15 Nov 2008 | Establishes the **FIA** as Bermuda's FIU / SAR-STR recipient |

- **BMA as AML/ATF supervisor:** the **SE Act 2008** designates the BMA, with a duty to monitor on a risk-sensitive basis and powers to cancel registration of non-licensed AML/ATF-regulated institutions and impose civil penalties.
- **Enhanced CDD:** **Reg 11(1)(aa)/(ab) AMLR 2008** require enhanced CDD on a risk-sensitive basis for FATF/CFATF higher-risk-country or sanctions exposure.
- **FIA / FIU:** the **Financial Intelligence Agency Act 2007** establishes the FIA as the independent agency that receives/analyses/disseminates **SARs/STRs** and provides quarterly SAR statistics to the Bermuda Police Service, the BMA and NAMLC. *(Exact establishment/function section numbers `[unverified — PDF 403'd]`.)*
- **The AML occasional-transaction monetary threshold for Bermuda's AMLR 2008 is `[unverified]`** — do not assume the FATF-common €/US$15,000 figure; confirm against the Regulations.
- **CFATF:** Bermuda is a member of the **Caribbean Financial Action Task Force**. 3rd-round MER adopted May 2011, removed from follow-up Nov 2013; **4th-round MER published 2020** (current).

## 3. Sanctions

- **International Sanctions Act 2003** empowers the **Minister** to make regulations giving effect in Bermuda to the UK's international-sanctions obligations.
- **International Sanctions Regulations 2013 (BR 14/2013):** Schedule 1 lists the sanctions-related **Overseas Territories Orders** in force in Bermuda (updated on an ongoing basis). UN/UK measures reach Bermuda via these Orders in Council; the Regulations are the domestic enforcement vehicle.
- **Governor / Minister roles:** the **Governor of Bermuda** holds the core powers (designate persons, publish the consolidated list, gather information, issue/revoke licences). Effective **25 Sep 2018**, the Governor delegated certain functions to the Minister (International Sanctions (Delegation of Governor's Functions) Notice 2018).
- **Reporting:** relevant persons must report knowledge/suspicion of dealings with designated persons / frozen assets to the competent authority. Operational sanctions screening for licensed banks runs through the **Financial Sanctions Implementation Unit (FSIU)**.

## 4. Beneficial ownership

- In-scope companies under the **Companies Act 1981** must identify beneficial owners and maintain a BO register, updated **within 14 days** of a notified change.
- **BO definition:** an individual owning/controlling **>25%** of shares/voting/interests (direct or indirect); failing that, control by other means; failing that, the senior manager.
- **Register holder changed in 2025:** historically the central register was **held by the BMA** (Integra portal). The **Beneficial Ownership Act 2025** (in force 3 Nov 2025; ROC enforcement from 1 Jun 2026) **transfers the central register from the BMA to the Registrar of Companies (RoC)**. *(The BMA-held register is now legacy; current authority is the RoC.)* CSPs are the front-line collectors — see [`corporate-services.md`](corporate-services.md) for the 10%-gatekeeper vs 25%-statutory distinction.

## 5. Digital Asset Business

- **Digital Asset Business Act 2018 (DABA)** (2018:28) — one of the world's first comprehensive DAB regimes; the BMA licenses and supervises digital-asset businesses.
- **Licence classes:** **Class T (Test)** — restricted proof-of-concept; **Class M (Modified)** — full activities for a limited period with conditions; **Class F (Full)** — unrestricted DAB activities.
- **Capital:** Class M and Class F licensees must maintain **net assets ≥ US$100,000** `[verified via secondary; confirm vs DABA text]`.
- Places digital-asset issuance/custody/payments inside the same integrated, risk-based BMA perimeter — applicants must evidence AML/ATF compliance.

## 6. Enforcement

- **Powers:** civil penalties, public censure, restrictions/conditions on licences, **revocation** of licence/registration, action against controllers and directors, and **prohibition orders** against individuals deemed not fit and proper.
- **Statement of Principles:** the SE/Supervision Act **requires the BMA to publish a "Statement of Principles on the Use of Enforcement Powers."** Sector enforcement guides also exist (e.g. Exchange Control Regulations Enforcement Guide).
- **Appeal route (in transition):** enforcement actions historically carried a right of appeal to a **Tribunal** under sector-specific tribunal regulations. Government has moved to **remove the Appeal Tribunal** and have BMA decisions reviewed by the **Supreme Court** instead. **Verify the current forum at time of use — this is in transition `[unverified — confirm]`.**

## How the agent uses this file
