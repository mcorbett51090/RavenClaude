# BMA edge — Bermuda economic substance & tax transparency (RoC / OTC / CIT Agency)

> **Last reviewed:** 2026-06-04. The tax-governance obligations every in-scope Bermuda entity (including BMA-licensed banks, trust companies, CSPs, funds, etc.) faces **alongside** — and administratively **separate from** — BMA prudential supervision. **This is "edge" content: it is NOT BMA-administered.** Economic substance → **Registrar of Companies (RoC)**; CRS/FATCA/CbCR → **Office of the Tax Commissioner (OTC)**; corporate income tax → the new **Corporate Income Tax Agency**. A BMA licensee answers to the BMA for prudential matters **and independently** to these bodies. **Time-sensitive:** CIT (FY from 1 Jan 2025), the annual CRS/CbCR deadlines, and the EU-list status all move — re-verify. **Refresh when** any deadline, threshold, or EU-list status changes, or a `[unverified]` pin is resolved.

## Governing instruments

| Instrument | Year | Administered by | Role |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Economic Substance Act 2018** (2018:185) + **Economic Substance Regulations 2018** | in force **1 Jan 2019** | **Registrar of Companies (RoC)** | Substance requirements for in-scope entities |

## 1. Economic Substance (ES Act 2018 + Regulations 2018)

- **In force 1 Jan 2019**; built under **EU Code of Conduct Group** pressure + the **OECD FHTP** substance standard.
- **Administered by the RoC (NOT the BMA)** — receives the annual declaration, risk-assesses, can request information / inspect, and issues compliance/penalty notices.
- **Nine "relevant activities":** banking, insurance, fund management, financing & leasing, headquarters, shipping, distribution & service centre, intellectual property, holding entity. **No de minimis** — any gross revenue from a relevant activity brings the entity in scope.
- **The economic-substance test** — an in-scope entity must (1) be **directed and managed** in Bermuda; (2) conduct its **core income-generating activities (CIGA)** in Bermuda (outsourced CIGA disclosed + Bermuda-based + monitored); (3) maintain **adequate** people, premises, and expenditure in Bermuda.
- **Reduced test — pure equity holding entity:** only adequate people to hold/manage equity + adequate premises + corporate-law filing compliance.
- **Heightened test — high-risk IP:** rebuttable presumption of non-compliance + **spontaneous exchange** of filed information with owner/BO jurisdictions, whether or not deemed compliant.
- **Annual Economic Substance Declaration (ESD):** to the RoC via the ESD portal, **within 6 months of financial year-end** (e.g. FY-end 31 Dec 2024 → by 30 Jun 2025).
- **Penalties (tiered civil-penalty regime, s.12 `[section pin triangulated, unverified]`):** 1st notice **$7,500–$50,000**; 2nd **$25,000–$100,000**; 3rd **$50,000–$250,000**; ultimate sanction: court restriction or **strike-off**. Warning Notice + ≥28-day representation window precedes penalty.

## 2. Country-by-Country Reporting (CbCR)

- Implemented by **amendment to the TIEA Act 2005** (Feb 2017); CbC MCAA signed Apr 2016.
- **Threshold: MNE groups with ≥ €750m consolidated group revenue** (prior fiscal year); in effect for FYs beginning on/after 1 Jan 2016.
- Filed with the **OTC** via the Tax Information Reporting Portal: **CbC report due 12 months after FY-end**; notification (where applicable) by the **last day of the reporting FY**.

---

## 3. CRS & FATCA

- **CRS:** under the TIEA Act + CRS Regulations; accounts from **1 Jan 2016**; Reporting FIs self-classify (Depository / Custodial / Investment Entity / Specified Insurance Company). Portal enrolment by the first **30 April** after becoming reportable; **CRS Compliance Form by 30 September**; 2025 returns due **31 May 2026** `[current-cycle deadlines set annually by MoF notice]`.
- **FATCA:** Bermuda operates a **US Model 2 IGA** — FFIs report **directly to the IRS** (not via the Bermuda portal). The historic UK-FATCA IGA has been subsumed into CRS.
- **Portal** launched **16 June 2017** — single channel for CRS, CbC and UK-FATCA (US-FATCA direct to IRS).

---

## 4. Corporate Income Tax (CITA 2023)

- **Statute became law 27 Dec 2023**; **15% CIT** on **Bermuda Constituent Entities** of an **In-Scope MNE Group** (≥ €750m consolidated revenue in **≥ 2 of the preceding 4 FYs**). **Effective FY beginning on/after 1 Jan 2025.**
- Explicitly an **OECD Pillar Two (GloBE) response**, framed as a domestic CIT.
- **Administered by the Corporate Income Tax Agency** (Agency Act 2024); registration via the Bermuda Tax Portal (issues a TIN). Per the final May 2025 regulations: the **Filing Bermuda Constituent Entity (FBCE)** registers **≥ 10 days before** any instalment payment; other BCEs **≥ 90 days before** the first CIT return's original due date.
- **Interaction with the historic zero-tax model:** CITA overrides the Exempted Undertakings Tax Protection Act assurances **for in-scope MNE groups only**; sub-€750m and purely domestic entities remain zero-CIT; payroll tax/customs duties unaffected `[override mechanics unverified vs statute]`.

## How the agent uses this file

`bma-financial-institutions-specialist` uses this to flag the **parallel, non-BMA** obligations a BMA licensee carries — and to **route them to the right body**: economic substance → RoC; CRS/FATCA/CbCR → OTC; CIT → the CIT Agency. **Do not advise that the BMA administers any of these.** The **€750m** threshold recurs but the consolidation test differs by regime. CIT and the EU-list status are time-sensitive — re-verify before relying. For the entity-classification questions that feed CRS/FATCA (FI vs NFE), see the plugin-wide [`../compliance-decision-trees.md`](../compliance-decision-trees.md) which-return tree and the `regulatory-reporting-analyst`.
