## 1. Risk-based supervisory framework

The BMA calibrates supervisory intensity to risk — "the greater the level of risk detected, the more supervisory review." It runs risk-based models for banking, trust, insurance and investment that analyse the impact and probability of firm failures to focus resources. Two arms:

- **Off-site monitoring** — review of statutory/prudential returns + audited financials, prudential-information acquisition, market/industry analysis, and **regular prudential meetings** with senior management.
- **On-site inspection / compliance visits** — periodic structured visits: management/staff interviews + assessment of internal controls, staffing, and procedures. For MSBs, "regular compliance visits to the premises" + statutory-certification review.
- **Thematic reviews** — control/oversight assessment over emerging and high-risk matters (cyber, expanded returns, sector surveys).

## 2. Licensing & change-of-control (cross-sector)

- **Minimum criteria (Schedule 1 of each Act):** controllers and officers must be **fit and proper**, and the group position must not obstruct effective consolidated supervision. The **Statement of Principles** governs the BMA's interpretation of minimum criteria, grant/restrict powers, and revocation grounds.
- **Fit-and-proper vetting:** controller/officer questionnaires (e.g. CSP Form 2 for shareholder controllers, Form 3 for senior executives/controllers/directors).
- **Change of controller / shareholder-controller:** a **shareholder controller** generally holds **10%+ of voting shares/power** (or significant management influence); the **10 / 20 / 33 / 50%** bands trigger prior-approval or notification. Where shares trade on a recognised exchange, prior consent isn't required but the BMA must be notified **within 45 days** of completion *(45-day figure firmly verified for insurance; cross-sector identity `[unverified]` — treat as strong inference)*.
- **New directors / senior executives:** subject to BMA vetting/notification under each Act's officer-approval regime *(pre-approval-vs-notification mechanics per Act `[unverified]`)*.

## 4. Fees

- The **2025 Bermuda Monetary Authority Fees** schedule sets per-sector, **size-scaled annual fees** + distinct application fees, keyed to each Act (BDCA, CSP, FNDADM, TBA, IBA…); **revised annually**.
- **Annual fee due on or before 31 March** (explicit for IBA; treated as the general deadline).
- **Banking/trust band (search snippet, `[unverified]`):** assets **$25–50bn → $500,000 + 0.001%** above $25bn; **>$50bn → $1,000,000 + 0.0015%** above $50bn. Top-tier bank fee elsewhere cited at **US$1,987,330**; deposit company **US$20,000** (see [`banking.md`](banking.md)).

## 5. Enforcement

- **Toolkit:** directions; licence restrictions/conditions; **civil penalties**; injunctions; **public censure**; objection to controllers; **prohibition orders** against individual directors/officers; **licence revocation**; winding-up; police referral. Governed by the **Statement of Principles on the Use of Enforcement Powers**.
- **Appeal route — in transition:** historically appealable to an **Appeal Tribunal**; the reform **removes the Tribunal and sends BMA decisions to the Supreme Court** (accept the Decision Notice or appeal direct to the Supreme Court, which assesses reasonableness + factual support). **Commencement date of the switch `[unverified]` — confirm at time of use.**
- **Recent actions:** **Meritus Trust Company Ltd — $600,000 civil penalty (announced 30 Dec 2024)** for AML/ATF + sanctions failings under AMLR 2008, the Trusts Act and the CSPB Act (on-site began Jun 2022; remediation by Jul 2023); **Estera Services (Bermuda) Ltd — $500,000** for AML/CTF failures. Public list: bma.bm/enforcement-action.

## 6. Cross-sector codes & guidance

- **Conduct of business:** since **Aug 2022** the BMA's mandate expressly covers customer protection / conduct, delivered via **per-sector codes of conduct** requiring "fair outcomes for customers".
- **Corporate governance:** board-led governance embedded in the sector codes of practice.
- **Operational Resilience & Outsourcing Code:** **banks/deposit companies comply by 1 Jan 2027; other registrants by 31 Mar 2028.** Scope: banks/deposit companies, insurers, investment businesses, digital-asset businesses; the **board is ultimately responsible** (Part VIII governs outsourcing agreements).
- **Operational Cyber Risk Management Code:** applies across banks, deposit companies, CSPs, trust companies, MSBs, investment businesses and fund administrators.
