## Definition & the First Schedule

"Investment business" = carrying on a scheduled **investment activity** in respect of a scheduled **investment**.

- **First Schedule, Part 2 — six activities:** (1) **dealing in investments** (principal/agent), (2) **arranging deals in investments**, (3) **managing investments**, (4) **investment advice**, (5) **safeguarding and administration** of investments, (6) **promoting investments** to the public. ## Who must be registered (post-2022)

A person carrying on (or purporting to carry on) investment business in/from Bermuda must be one of:

- **Licensed** — full licensee.
- **Class A Registered Person** — **no place of business in Bermuda** and already licensed/authorised by a "recognised regulator" elsewhere; must appoint a BMA-approved **Senior Representative** in Bermuda.
- **Class B Registered Person** — maintains a **principal place of business in Bermuda**; lighter-touch than a full licensee.
- **Non-registrable person** (Order 2022) — e.g. an **investment fund** and a **Digital Asset Business Act 2018** licensee doing ancillary investment business; the old 2004-Order exemptees (funds, qualifying insurers/insurance managers, brokers, government) were folded into NRP status.

A **12-month transition** ran from the 27 Jul 2022 effective date for existing businesses to register/license.

## Minimum licensing criteria (Second Schedule)

Before granting a licence/registration the BMA must be satisfied the **Second Schedule** criteria are met on a continuing basis — **fit and proper** controllers/officers, adequate **capital / net-asset** resources, sound business conduct, and **client-asset segregation**.

- Net-asset figures cited in search results: **US$250,000 (principal activities) / US$100,000 (agent activities)** [verified via excerpt — **confirm against the current Second Schedule as amended 2022**].

## Code of Conduct (s.10 IBA)

The current **Code of General Business Conduct and Practice** (Dec 2022) covers prudent conduct, **suitability**, **best execution**, **client money/asset protection**, conflicts management, and **complaints handling** (efficient, impartial, disclosed, recorded, timely remediation). Non-compliance is weighed in assessing whether a provider conducts business prudently.

---

## Conduct of Business regime (status)

The BMA's **15 Dec 2020 Consultation Paper "Proposal for a Conduct of Business Regulatory Regime"** proposed a cross-sector conduct framework. In **Aug 2022** the BMA's statutory mandate was formally expanded to **customer protection / conduct of business**, implemented by embedding conduct-of-business principles into existing/new **codes of conduct** (banking, domestic insurance, digital asset, and the 2022 Investment Business Code) rather than via a single standalone "Conduct of Business Rules" instrument. **Whether discrete "Conduct of Business Rules" have been *made* as a distinct statutory instrument is `[unverified]`** — current evidence shows the regime delivered through **sector codes**. Do not assert a standalone instrument exists without confirming.

## Supervision & powers

Risk-based supervision; the BMA can impose conditions, require reporting/returns, issue directions, and vary/cancel licences or registrations; statements of principles under the IBA govern the exercise of these powers.
