## 1. The Act's architecture & key sections

The IBA 2003 (**2003:20**) replaced the Investment Business Act 1998.

- **Part I — Preliminary / Interpretation.** **s.3** is the definitional engine: *"investment business"* = engaging in **investment activity** by way of business; *"investment"* = an asset/right/interest in **Part 1 of the First Schedule**; an *"investment activity"* = an activity in **Part 2** of the First Schedule **not excluded by Part 3**. **The matrix rule:** caught only if you carry on a *scheduled activity* in respect of a *scheduled investment*, by way of business, in or from Bermuda.
- **Part II — The Authority.** **s.8** functions; **s.9** statement of principles; **s.10** **codes of conduct** (the power under which the Dec 2022 Code is issued); **s.11** Minister's directions.
- **Part III — Regulation of Investment Providers.** Restriction on carrying on investment business without authorisation; licensing/registration applications; the Second-Schedule minimum-criteria gate; supervision powers (impose conditions/restrictions, restrict/revoke, issue directions to safeguard clients, require information/reports). `[exact revoke/direct section numbers unverified]`
- **Part IV — Regulation of Investment Exchanges and Clearing Houses.**
- **Later Parts** — **offences** (unauthorised investment business; false statements; non-compliance), fines and/or imprisonment. `[exact section numbers/penalties unverified]`

**s.10 Code-of-conduct power (load-bearing):** the BMA may issue codes "as to the duties, requirements and standards to be complied with and the procedures and sound principles to be observed." Breach is **not a direct offence** but is **"taken into account... in determining whether an investment provider is meeting its obligation to conduct its business in a prudent manner"** (feeds the Second-Schedule continuing test).

## 2. The First Schedule — in depth

### Part 1 — Investments (scheduled assets/rights/interests)
1. **Shares** / stock in a company's share capital.
2. **Instruments creating/acknowledging indebtedness** — debentures, debenture/loan stock, bonds, notes, other debt instruments.
3. **Government and public securities** `[unverified — standard item]`.
4. **Warrants** / instruments entitling to subscribe.
5. **Certificates representing securities** (depositary receipts) `[unverified]`.
6. **Units in a collective investment scheme.**
7. **Options** to acquire/dispose of any investment (or currency, gold/precious metals).
8. **Futures** — contracts for future sale at an agreed price.
9. **Contracts for differences** (and contracts to secure a profit/avoid a loss by reference to price/value/index).
10. **Long-term insurance contracts** (investment-linked).
11. **Rights to and interests in** any of the above (sweep-up).

### Part 2 — Investment activities (the six heads)
1. **Dealing in investments** — buying/selling/subscribing/underwriting, or offering/agreeing to, **as principal or agent** (this single head covers both).
2. **Arranging deals in investments** — making arrangements with a view to another buying/selling/subscribing/underwriting.
3. **Managing investments** — managing (or offering/agreeing to manage) another's assets that include investments.
4. **Investment advice** — advice to clients/potential clients on the merits of purchasing/selling/subscribing/underwriting an investment or exercising a right.
5. **Safeguarding and administering investments** (custody).
6. **Promoting / inducing investment activity** ("promoting to the public") `[exact wording of head 6 unverified]`.

### Part 3 — Excluded activities (carve-outs that stop a Part 2 activity being an "investment activity")
Standard UK-pattern exclusions: dealing as principal for own account (limited); **trustees/personal representatives** acting as such; **employee share schemes**; **sale of a body corporate** (controlling-shareholding sale between commercial counterparties); **groups and joint enterprises** (intra-group); activity in the course of a **profession or non-investment business** that is a necessary part of other services; **media** advice; **provision of information** without advice; dealings through **authorised/exempt persons**. `[precise sub-paragraphs unverified]`

## 3. Who must be registered/licensed (post-27 Jul 2022) — the four statuses

| Status | Who | Footprint | Key obligations |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Licensed** (full) | Default full-substance regime for Bermuda providers not eligible for a lighter category | Carries on IB in/from Bermuda | Full Second-Schedule criteria; Net Assets/Capital/Liquidity Rules 2022; annual Certificate of Compliance + Statutory Return; appoint auditor |
| **Class A Registered Person** | Already **licensed/authorised by a "recognised regulator"** overseas **and does NOT maintain a place of business in Bermuda** | **No Bermuda place of business** | Appoint a BMA-approved **Senior Representative**; **annual + quarterly returns**; appoint auditor |
| **Class B Registered Person** | Lighter-touch domestic register; absorbs prior exemption-order categories — funds, services to HNW/high-income/sophisticated investors, or to **≤20 persons** without public solicitation, entities with **≥US$5,000,000** assets | **Bermuda principal place of business** | **Annual returns**; appoint auditor; lighter prudential profile |
| **Non-Registrable Person** | Designated by the NRP Order 2022 | n/a | No registration/application |

**⚠ Footprint test is the inverse pair:** Class A = **overseas-regulated, NO Bermuda office**; Class B = **Bermuda principal place of business**. (A garbled snippet describing Class A as "Bermuda-formed with a place of business" is **incorrect** — Class A is the no-Bermuda-office, recognised-regulator route.) **"Recognised regulator"** = an authority the BMA recognises as imposing **equivalent standards** to the IBA.

**Non-Registrable Persons (Designation) Order 2022** (in operation 27 Jul 2022): **investment funds**; **DABA 2018 licensees** doing IB **ancillary** to digital-asset business (ancillary = IB gross revenues **≤ 25%** of overall gross revenues); **qualifying insurers**; **qualifying insurance managers**; **brokers**; the **BMA**; a **public authority**; the **Government of Bermuda**.

**Transition:** 12 months from 27 Jul 2022 — existing providers had to license or register (Class A/B) **by 26 Jul 2023**.

## 4. Second Schedule — minimum criteria (gate + continuing test)

1. **Fit and proper** controllers/officers/senior executives (probity, competence, soundness of judgment, diligence).
2. **Adequate capital / net assets** — per the **Investment Business (Net Assets, Capital and Liquidity) Rules 2022**: **US$250,000** carrying on activities **as principal**; **US$100,000** **as agent**.
3. **Business conducted in a prudent manner** — adequate accounting/records, systems of control, adequate liquidity.
4. **Sound corporate governance** — proportionate to nature/scale/complexity.
5. **Client-asset protection / segregation** — client assets segregated from the provider's own; reviewed on-site.
6. **Integrity, skill, care and diligence**; conduct consistent with the s.10 Code.
7. **Direction by ≥2 individuals** + a real Bermuda nexus for full licensees `[standard four-eyes criterion, not individually quoted]`.

## 5. Code of General Business Conduct and Practice (23 Dec 2022, s.10)

Issued **23 Dec 2022** under **s.10**, replacing the June 2010 General Business Conduct and Practice Code + the 2010 Advertising Code. Non-compliance feeds the "prudent manner" test. Topics (exact paragraph numbering `[unverified]`):
- **Prudent conduct & integrity** — integrity, fair dealing, high standards of market conduct.
- **Skill, care and diligence** — competent personnel, adequate supervision/resourcing.
- **Suitability** — know the client; establish **client risk profiles**; recommendations suit the client's circumstances; client agreement / informed investment decisions.
- **Best execution** — best available terms; **fairness in allocation** among clients; Investment Policy Statement (IPS) / asset-allocation references.
- **Client money / client-asset protection** — segregation, reconciliation, safekeeping.
- **Conflicts of interest** — identify, mitigate, manage; disclose where management is insufficient.
- **Complaints handling** — efficient, impartial, disclosed to clients, recorded, timely remediation.
- **Communications & disclosure** — responsible communications; controls on **unsolicited calls**; accurate advertising/promotion.

## 6. The Senior Representative (Class A Registered Persons)

A Class A Registered Person must appoint a **BMA-approved Senior Representative** — the entity's **point of contact in Bermuda**. Duties: notify the BMA of (a) likely insolvency / failure to meet obligations, (b) various **compliance failures**, and (c) **criminal proceedings**; and serve as the conduit between the overseas-regulated Class A person and the BMA. The Senior Representative is the substance hook that lets a no-Bermuda-office, overseas-regulated entity use a register rather than a full licence.

## 7. Conduct of Business regime — status & uncertainty

- **15 Dec 2020:** BMA Consultation "Proposal for a Conduct of Business Regulatory Regime" — cross-sectoral principles (integrity & skill; deal fairly; appropriate disclosures; mitigate conflicts; complaints-handling systems), proportionate, protective of **vulnerable customers**; stakeholder feedback letter 23 Apr 2021.
- **Aug 2022:** BMA Act 1969 mandate expanded to **conduct-of-business oversight / fair customer outcomes** — the predicate for sector conduct codes.
- **Delivery via sector codes:** evidence points to conduct rules delivered **through sector codes** (the Dec 2022 Investment Business Code; a parallel insurer conduct code) **rather than a single standalone "Conduct of Business Rules" instrument.** **Whether a unified standalone instrument has been made is `[unverified]` — state this uncertainty when relied on.**

## 8. Supervision, reporting, fees, enforcement

- **Supervisory model:** risk-based — a risk-evaluation model sets supervision level/frequency; off-site monitoring + on-site visits, blended by licence/registration class.
- **Reporting:** **Annual Certificate of Compliance** (certify minimum criteria met / disclose any failure + any licence limitations); **Annual Statutory Return** (Schedule 1 of the Investment Business (Prudential Standards) (Statutory Returns) Rules 2022); **quarterly net-assets/liquidity** reporting (Capital/Net Assets/Liquidity Rules 2022) for licensees + Class A; auditor appointment required for licensees and Class A/B.
- **Fees:** the 2025 Fees schedule sets application + annual fees per status — **specific figures `[unverified]` (not captured from snippets); retrieve from the 2025 Fees PDF.**
- **Enforcement:** impose conditions, restrict/revoke, issue directions (incl. to safeguard clients), require information/reports, pursue offence penalties; subject of a 2020 "Enhancement of the Enforcement Process" consultation.

## How the agent uses this file

Read this for any Bermuda investment-business question. **Run the s.3 matrix first** (scheduled activity × scheduled investment × by way of business), then **fix the status** (Licensed / Class A / Class B / NRP) — the footprint test (Class A no-Bermuda-office vs Class B Bermuda-office) and the net-asset floor (principal $250k / agent $100k) follow. A **fund or a DABA licensee (ancillary IB ≤25%) is a Non-Registrable Person** (see [`edge-cases.md`](edge-cases.md) A2/A3). **Don't assert a standalone "Conduct of Business Rules" instrument** — the live conduct rule is the s.10 Code. Never quote the 2025 fee figures or an `[unverified]` section number as settled.
