## Licensing — who must be licensed

- Any person carrying on **trust business "in or from within Bermuda"** must hold a licence unless exempt. Licences are issued under **s.11** of the Act (the Code "applies to all holders of trust business licences issued pursuant to section 11").
- The **Statement of Principles** is issued under **s.6**; the **Code of Practice** under **s.7**.

### Licence types

- **Unlimited trust licence** — authorises carrying on trust business and soliciting from the public generally. Public trust companies hold this.
- **Limited trust licence** — authorises holding trust assets to an aggregate value of **thirty million dollars** (or such higher value as permitted by **subsections (2) and (3)**) and **does not authorise the licensee to act as sole trustee** to any trust it administers. [verified via statutory-text search snippet 2026-06-04]

### Minimum licensing criteria (First Schedule)

- Controllers and officers **fit and proper**; business **directed by at least two individuals** unless the BMA approves otherwise; appropriate board composition; **prudent conduct**; adequate insurance, records, systems and controls.
- **Minimum net assets: US$250,000 (corporate trustee) / US$25,000 (any other case)** [verified via statutory-text search snippet 2026-06-04 quoting the Act directly — "minimum net assets of $250,000 in the case of a corporate trustee or $25,000 in any other case"].

## Trust Business Code of Practice (s.7)

- "Provides guidance on the duties, requirements, procedures, standards and sound principles to be observed by persons carrying on trust business." Read with the Statement of Principles and Enforcement Guide.
- Governance content includes: the **board must be solely comprised of individuals — corporate directors are not appropriate (para 27 `[unverified ¶#]`)**; directors must understand the board's collective duty to maintain robust regulatory-compliance arrangements (**para 28 `[unverified ¶#]`**); record-protection and client-asset-handling policies.
- Current Code published **18 Dec 2019**, aligned with the Amendment Act 2019.

---

## Exemptions (Exemption Order 2002)

- The Order exempts specified persons from the licence requirement.
- **Private Trust Company (PTC) exemption — para 3 of the Order:** a PTC is exempt where it provides trustee services only to specified trusts. To qualify it must (a) be authorised in its memorandum (or, for an overseas company, by permit) to act as trustee of identified trusts plus others approved by the Minister of Finance; and (b) certify this to the BMA with particulars via a directors' letter.
- **Important AML overlay:** since 2018, **exempt PTCs are nonetheless captured as AML/ATF-regulated financial institutions** under the Proceeds of Crime (AML/ATF) Regulations 2008. A licensing exemption is **not** an AML exemption.

## Supervision, reporting & BMA powers

- The BMA **vets and continually monitors** licensees (controllers fit-and-proper, net assets, insurance, systems/controls).
- Powers per the Act + Statement of Principles + Enforcement Guide: information gathering, objection to/removal of controllers and officers, restriction and **revocation** of licences, enforcement. Precise section numbers for investigation/revocation `[unverified]`.

## How the agent uses this file

`bma-financial-institutions-specialist` reads this before any trust-business answer. **Licence type drives obligation** — a limited licensee cannot act as sole trustee and is asset-capped; an unlimited licensee solicits the public. A licensing exemption (PTC) does **not** exempt from AML/ATF — flag this every time. Underlying trust-law questions (reserved powers, Hastings-Bass, firewall) are Trustee Act 1975 matters and route to counsel, not this agent. AML/sanctions frame: [`overview.md`](overview.md).
