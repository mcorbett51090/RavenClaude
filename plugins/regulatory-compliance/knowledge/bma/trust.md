## 1. The Act — structure & key sections

In force **25 January 2002**; creates a licensing regime for any person carrying on **trust business** in/from within Bermuda unless exempted. The **BMA** is the regulator.

- **Definition (s.9(3)):** "trust business" = *"the provision of the services of a trustee as a business, trade, profession or vocation."* An **unremunerated** trustee is understood not to be carrying on trust business `[unverified — secondary]`. **s.9** is also the restriction provision (no person carries on trust business without a licence). [definition verbatim verified; "9(3)" pinpoint consistently restated]
- **s.6 — Statement of Principles:** the BMA must publish how it interprets the First-Schedule **minimum criteria**, the **grounds for revocation**, and how it exercises its grant/revoke/restrict, emergency-intervention, and information-gathering powers. [verified]
- **s.7 — Code of Practice:** power to issue a Code on the duties/standards/procedures a licensee must observe. Breach of the Code is **not itself an offence** but is admissible and weighed in assessing the minimum criteria `[unverified — secondary]`.
- **s.11 — Grant of licence:** the BMA grants licences ("the Code applies to all holders of trust business licences issued pursuant to **section 11**"). Won't grant unless satisfied the applicant meets and can continue to meet the First Schedule, and **retains discretion to refuse even when satisfied**. [verified]
- **Restriction / revocation (≈ s.16 `[unverified — secondary pinpoint]`):** a trustee may **irrevocably surrender** its licence by written notice; the BMA may **petition the Supreme Court to transfer a trust to new trustees** in the interest of settlor/beneficiaries; a **2019 amendment** lets the BMA **restrict surrender** where appropriate.
- **Objection to controllers (≈ s.19 `[unverified — secondary pinpoint]`):** prior BMA approval where a person becomes a **10% or majority shareholder controller** of a corporate trustee; the BMA may **object** (new or existing), **restrict the shares**, or seek a court-ordered **forced sale**.
- **Information-gathering & investigators:** the BMA may require information/reports/documents; **appoint investigators** (incl. where unlicensed trust business or breach of an exemption order is suspected); a magistrate may issue a **search warrant** where documents are being removed/destroyed.
- **Offences:** carrying on trust business without a required licence — **summary: $25,000 and/or 1 year**; **indictment: $100,000 and/or 5 years**. Obstructing an investigation is an offence. [verified]

## 2. Licence types — unlimited vs limited

- **Unlimited trust licence** — trust business with **no asset cap**; available to local/exempted companies (Companies Act 1981) and Overseas (Permit) Companies authorised by the Minister; **may act as sole trustee**. [verified]
- **Limited trust licence** — **cannot act as sole trustee**; **restricted to aggregate trust assets ≤ US$30 million** "unless the Authority agrees to a higher aggregate." [verified]
- **Raising the cap** — the BMA weighs: demonstrable **need** for limited licensing; integrity/skill appropriate to scale; **absolute amount** of assets; nature/range of the business; **number** of trust relationships; variety/complexity of responsibilities; available resources. [verified]

---

## 3. First Schedule — minimum licensing criteria (item by item)

The BMA cannot grant unless satisfied these are met and can continue to be met:

1. **Fit & proper controllers and officers** — every controller/officer of a corporate trustee.
2. **Direction by ≥2 individuals** — unless the BMA approves otherwise (four-eyes).
3. **Appropriate board composition** — as the BMA considers appropriate.
4. **Prudent conduct** — adequate accounting/records, adequate insurance, adequate systems & controls; compliance with the Act.
5. **Integrity & professional skill** — appropriate to the nature and scale of the business.
6. **Minimum net assets** — **US$250,000** (corporate trustee) / **US$25,000** (any other case); **or such larger amount as the BMA may require.** [verified]

## 4. Trust Business Code of Practice (final **18 December 2019**, s.7)

Consultation 26 Sep 2019; final 18 Dec 2019. Applies to **all s.11 licence holders** and is the lens through which the First-Schedule criteria are assessed.

- **Board must be solely individuals (~para 27):** "the board of directors should be solely comprised of individuals; **corporate directors are not considered appropriate**." [verified — direct quote]
- **Directors' collective compliance duty (~para 28):** directors must understand the board's **collective duty to ensure robust regulatory-compliance arrangements**; the board is **ultimately responsible for the compliance function** and must receive sufficient regular compliance information. [verified — direct quote]
- **Record protection:** documented policies protecting records from loss, theft, unauthorised access, alteration or destruction. [verified]
- **Custody / client assets:** licensees must ensure custodian/nominee requirements are met; client-asset handling/segregation expectations apply `[paragraph pinpoint unverified]`.
- **Outsourcing:** functions may be outsourced but the **licensee remains fully responsible** for compliance. [verified principle]
- **Risk management & systems and controls:** the Code elaborates "prudent conduct" into governance, risk-management, internal-control and procedure duties. [verified principle]

## 5. Statement of Principles (s.6) + enforcement posture

The **Statement of Principles** (issued with the Code) explains the BMA's approach to interpreting the minimum criteria, the grounds for revocation, and the exercise of its grant/revoke/restrict, emergency-intervention and information powers. The broader **Enforcement** framework is graduated (public censure → civil penalties → conditions/restrictions → revocation → court applications) — see §9 Meritus.

---

## 6. Exemption Order 2002 — in detail

Exempts specified categories from the s.9 licensing requirement.

**Private Trust Company (PTC) exemption (para 3):** a company exempt where it acts only as trustee of identified trusts. Conditions:
1. its **memorandum** (or, for an overseas company, its **permit**) authorises it to act as **trustee of the trusts identified therein** plus **other trusts approved by the Minister of Finance**; and
2. it **certifies to the BMA** (directors' certification) that it qualifies, **with particulars of the nature and scope** of its trust business.

**Annual declaration:** post-2018/2019, trustees must **apply** for exemption, and PTCs must **declare by 31 March each year** that they continue to qualify.

**2018 AML capture — exempt PTCs became regulated FIs:** the **Proceeds of Crime (Miscellaneous) Act 2018 (effective 21 March 2018)** extended "AML/ATF Regulated Financial Institution" to capture privately-exempt PTCs. They must **register with the BMA as a "non-licensed" AML/ATF regulated FI** and run a full AML/ATF programme (business risk assessment, client risk rating, CDD, compliance officer, reporting officer). **Carve-out:** PTCs that **use a Bermuda-licensed CSP or licensed trust business** are excluded. Non-licensed persons had to **register before 7 December 2018**. [verified] *(This is the canonical "a licensing exemption is not an AML exemption" case — see best-practice `bma-a-licensing-exemption-is-not-an-aml-exemption`.)*

## 7. AML/ATF overlay

Licensed trust businesses (and captured exempt PTCs) are **AML/ATF Regulated Financial Institutions** under the **AMLR 2008**, BMA-supervised (General Guidance Notes + a dedicated **Trust Business** sectoral set consulted 2021; CSP sectoral notes also cover trust customers). Trust-specific CDD:
- Treat **settlor(s) and trustee(s)** as trust customers, including any **"effective" settlor**.
- **Beneficial owners** include persons effectively owning/controlling **>10%** of the trust's funds/assets/voting rights, plus beneficiaries/controllers.
- Onboarding identifies/verifies **settlor, trustee, protector, beneficiaries (and classes), and controllers**; **EDD** for higher-risk (PEP, high-risk jurisdiction); periodic review (per the **15 Feb 2023** revisions).

See [`aml-atf.md`](aml-atf.md) for the full operational AML layer.

## 8. Interface with the Trustee Act 1975 (substantive law — NOT business regulation)

The **Trustee Act 1975** is the backdrop of trustees' **powers and duties** — not the licensing statute. Most-cited: **s.47**, under which the **Supreme Court may confer a power to effect any transaction it considers "expedient"** not otherwise authorised, **without** all-adult-beneficiary consent (incl. varying beneficial interests). Bermuda's **reserved-powers** regime and **firewall** protections sit in the **Trusts (Special Provisions) Act 1989** (settlor/third-party powers without invalidating the trust; protection against foreign forced-heirship, community-property, insolvency and divorce claims). These define what a licensed trustee *administers*; the TRTBA defines who may carry on that activity *for reward*. **Route substantive trust-law questions to counsel.**

## 9. Supervision, reporting, fees, enforcement

- **Reporting:** **audited financial statements + a Certificate of Compliance within 4 months of year-end**; net assets monitored against the First-Schedule minimums via a periodic **Minimum Liquidity & Net Assets Return** `[exact trust filing frequency unverified]`.
- **Fees:** set in the annual BMA Fees schedule; the **specific trust-licence annual fee figure is `[unverified]`** (a search trap: *private trusts* pay no government fee, but *trust-business licensees* do pay a BMA fee).
- **Enforcement — Meritus Trust Company Limited (notice 30 Dec 2024):** **US$600,000** civil penalties for failures under the **AMLR 2008, the Trusts Act and the CSPB Act**. Findings: internal controls that **failed to recognise an independent-audit third-party appointment as "outsourcing"**; **insufficient risk-rating** of products/services; **inadequate documentation** of policies/procedures; **over-reliance on manual processes** for international-sanctions screening. Originated in virtual on-site inspections Jul 2022; remediated by a Jul 2023 deadline. A rare overseas-territory **sanctions-related** trust fine.
