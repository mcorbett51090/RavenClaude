## 1. The CSPB Act 2012 — structure, definition, prohibition, exemptions, powers, offences

**Commencement:** in force **1 January 2013**; operative regime followed consequential Exchange Control Act 1972 amendments, completed by the **2014 Amendment Act**.

### s.2(2) — definition of "corporate service provider business"
"The provision of any of the following corporate services **for profit**":
- acting as a **company formation agent**, or agent for the establishment of a partnership;
- **providing nominee services**, incl. (without limitation) acting as or providing **nominee shareholders**;
- providing **administrative and secretarial services**, including: a **registered office**; an **accommodation, correspondence or administrative address**; **maintaining the books and records** of a company/partnership; filing statutory **forms, resolutions, returns and notices**; acting as/arranging a **person authorised to accept service**;
- acting as / fulfilling (or arranging another to fulfil) the function of a **director, officer, secretary, or alternate/assistant/deputy secretary** of a company, or an **officer of a partnership**;
- keeping or altering the **register of members under s.65 Companies Act 1981**;
- performing functions as **resident representative** under the Companies Act 1981, Exempted Partnerships Act 1992 and Overseas Partnerships Act 1995;
- providing **any additional corporate/administrative services specified in regulations**.

*(This fuller enumeration supersedes the four-head summary in earlier drafts — the "administrative/secretarial services" head expressly includes registered office, books-and-records, statutory filings, and director/secretary provision.)*

### Prohibition, exemptions, powers, offences
- **Prohibition:** no person may carry on CSP business **in or from within Bermuda** without being "a licensed undertaking under the Act." Carrying on without a licence is an offence `[exact s.# unverified — commonly s.3]`.
- **Exemptions:** entities already AML/ATF-regulated under sister regimes need not hold a separate CSP licence — principally **licensed trust businesses** (TRTBA 2001), **licensed/registered investment business** (IBA 2003; s.13 IBA is the IBA exemption-order power), and **exempt PTCs** (Trust Exemption Order 2002). A PTC that **uses a Bermuda-licensed CSP or licensed trustee** in its structure is outside the AML/ATF-FI net; otherwise it must register as a non-licensed AML/ATF institution. `[exact CSPB exemption section + full closed list unverified]`
- **BMA powers:** grant/restrict/revoke (grounds in the s.6 Statement of Principles); **objection to controllers** (notification of new/increased control; objection to new/increased control; objection to existing controller); **directions**; power by written notice to compel document production; routine **on-site reviews** incl. AML testing.

## 2. Licence types — unlimited vs limited

- **Unlimited licence** — provision of **any or all** s.2(2) corporate services.
- **Limited licence** — provision of the services in **s.2(2)(a) and (c)(vii)** only (a restricted subset, per the BMA's gloss). [verified — BMA CSP Licensing]

---

## 3. Schedule 1 — minimum licensing criteria

- **Fit & proper controllers and officers** (probity, competence, soundness of judgement, diligence).
- **Business conducted in a prudent manner** — the BMA takes into account any failure to comply with the Act, other law, the Code, and **international sanctions enforced in Bermuda**.
- **Appropriate corporate governance** — policies/processes proportionate to **nature, size, complexity and risk profile**.
- **Adequate accounting records, systems and controls.**
- **Integrity and skill** in conduct of the business.
- **Two-officer sign-off** — returns/declarations to the BMA signed by **two senior officers** `[unverified for the Act; explicit in the 2025 draft Rules]`.

## 4. CSP Business Code of Practice (2018, s.7)

Issued under **s.7** ("guidance on the duties, requirements, procedures, standards and **sound principles** to be observed"); read with the **s.6 Statement of Principles**; applied **proportionately** (a small but complex CSP still needs a sophisticated risk framework). Covered areas:
- **Corporate governance** — the board; board oversight responsibilities; chief/senior-executive responsibilities.
- **Risk-management framework.**
- **Outsourcing** — a CSP may outsource (asset management, custody, cyber, compliance, internal audit) but **retains full responsibility** "to the same level as if conducted in-house."
- **Accounting, record-keeping, and handling of client operations/money.**

## 6. The gatekeeper role — "Further Guidance: CSP Gatekeeper Role" (14 June 2017)

The CSP is Bermuda's **front-line gatekeeper** for entity formation. Required CSP policies/procedures:
- a **detailed vetting process for beneficial owners holding 10% or more**, and persons exercising significant control — a **fitness-and-propriety check in addition to AML/ATF CDD**;
- a **personal declaration** completed by such persons;
- **due-diligence checks using two independent search engines**, with an **escalation process** for adverse information;
- AML/ATF checks layered on top.

**⚠ Two thresholds — do not conflate:** the **10% gatekeeper fitness check** (a BMA supervisory expectation on the CSP) is distinct from the **25% statutory beneficial-owner definition** (§7) used for the central register. A 10–25% holder is gatekeeper-screened but not (on the percentage test alone) a registrable beneficial owner. *(Best-practice: `bma-keep-the-10-and-25-percent-bo-thresholds-distinct`.)*

## 7. Beneficial ownership — Beneficial Ownership Act 2025

In force **3 November 2025**; **ROC enforcement from 1 June 2026**:
- **25% definition** — an individual who ultimately owns/controls **25%+** of shares/voting/partnership interests (direct or indirect), or otherwise exercises **ultimate effective control**, or controls by other means.
- **Register moves BMA → Registrar of Companies (ROC)** — the ROC collects, verifies and maintains the central register.
- **CSPs as relevant persons / filing agents** — a legal person **must engage a CSP** to file BO information with the ROC central register; the entity must take **reasonable measures to identify, obtain and verify** the minimum required BO information and **keep records** of the verification used.
- **Captives** are brought into the register **for the first time.**

## 8. Proposed CSP Business Rules 2025 (consultation 29 Apr 2025) — NOT in force

- **Minimum net assets:** **US$12,000 (limited)** / **US$50,000 (unlimited)**.
- **Liquid-asset coverage:** liquid assets = **1 month** of annual expenditure (limited) / **3 months** (unlimited).
- **Client money:** segregate client funds; **regular reconciliations**; **annual independent reviews** of client accounts.
- **Reporting:** **annual + quarterly returns** with **signed senior-officer declarations** verifying reserves; **immediate BMA notification** on breach of liquidity/asset requirements or serious financial difficulty.
- **Status:** consultation only — **not in force**; never cite as binding.

## 9. AML/ATF — CSPs as regulated financial institutions

CSPs are **AML/ATF-regulated financial institutions**, brought in via **POCA s.42A(1)(fa)** `[exact paragraph unverified]`, subject to the **AMLR 2008**, POCA, the POCA (Supervision & Enforcement) Act, and ATFA 2004. Senior-management responsibilities sit primarily in **AMLR Regulations 16–19**. The BMA issues **CSP Sectoral Guidance Notes (Annex VI; editions 2018, 2021, 2022/2023)** under the General Guidance Notes:
- **CDD** at onboarding (general GN chapters 3–5; CSP annex paras VI.66–VI.148);
- **EDD** under **Reg 11** wherever customer/relationship/geography/product/channel is higher risk.

See [`aml-atf.md`](aml-atf.md) for the full operational AML layer.

## 10. Supervision, reporting, fees, enforcement

- **Reporting:** licensed undertakings file a **Certificate of Compliance within 4 months** of financial year-end; the BMA runs **routine on-site reviews** (incl. AML testing) and can compel document production.
- **Fees:** in the 2025 BMA Fees schedule; a per-entity-served fee (~**US$21/entity served** `[unverified — secondary]`) and a **proposed fee-band restructuring** are under consultation (proposed, not enacted).
- **Enforcement — Meritus Trust (30 Dec 2024):** **US$600,000** civil penalties on Meritus, licensed under **both** the Trusts Act 2001 **and the CSPB Act 2012**, for breaches of the **AMLR 2008, the Trusts Act and the CSPB Act** (outsourcing-control failures, insufficient product/service risk-rating, inadequate governance documentation, manual sanctions-screening over-reliance); on-site Jul 2022; remediated by Jul 2023.

## How the agent uses this file

Read this for any CSP question **and** any Bermuda beneficial-ownership question (the BO regime lives here). **Keep the 10% gatekeeper and 25% statutory thresholds distinct.** The proposed CSP Business Rules 2025 (net-asset/liquidity/client-money) are **not yet law.** The BO register moved **BMA → ROC** (2025); a CSP must be engaged to file.
