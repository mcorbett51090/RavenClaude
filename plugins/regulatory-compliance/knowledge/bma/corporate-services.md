## Definition of CSP business (s.2(2))

"Corporate service provider business" = the provision, **for profit**, of any of these corporate services:
- acting as a **company formation agent** (or agent for establishment of a partnership);
- providing **nominee services** (incl. acting as / providing **nominee shareholders**);
- providing **administrative and secretarial services** to companies or partnerships;
- performing functions as **resident representative** under the Companies Act 1981, Exempted Partnerships Act 1992 and Overseas Partnerships Act 1995;
- plus any additional corporate/administrative services specified in regulations.

*(Registered-office and director/secretary provision sit within the administrative-services / listed-functions categories — `[unverified]` as discrete enumerated heads.)*

## Who must be licensed; exemptions

- The Act **prohibits carrying on CSP business in or from within Bermuda unless licensed**.
- **Exemptions:** entities already AML/ATF-regulated under other regimes are carved out — exempted private trust companies (s.3 Trust Exemption Order 2002) and exempted investment business (s.13 Investment Business Act 2003). The precise CSP-Act exemption section number is `[unverified]` (commonly cited as s.4 — not literally confirmed).

### Licence types & criteria (Schedule 1)

- **Two licence types:** **unlimited** (any/all corporate services) and **limited** (specified services subject to limits).
- Minimum criteria: controllers and officers **fit and proper**; prudent conduct; appropriate direction/governance. Returns must be signed by **two officers**.
- **Net-asset thresholds:** the 2012 Act/Schedule set **no fixed figures**; the **proposed CSP Business Rules 2025** would introduce minimum net assets of **US$12,000 (limited) / US$50,000 (unlimited)** plus 1–3 months' liquid-asset coverage — **proposed, not yet in force.**

## AML/ATF & beneficial ownership (the gatekeeper role)

- CSPs are **AML/ATF-regulated financial institutions** under the Proceeds of Crime (AML/ATF) Regulations 2008, supervised by the BMA for AML/ATF compliance.
- **CSPs act as gatekeepers:** the Jun 2017 Further Guidance requires fitness/propriety assessment of **beneficial owners holding 10% or more** of a Bermuda entity, with personal declarations and AML/ATF checks.
- **Beneficial Ownership Act 2025** (in force 3 Nov 2025; ROC enforcement 1 Jun 2026) defines a beneficial owner as an individual ultimately owning/controlling **25% or more** of shares/voting/interests, or otherwise exercising ultimate effective control; it **transfers the central register from the BMA to the Registrar of Companies (ROC)**. CSPs are "relevant persons" who access the register for KYC and are front-line enforcers of BO collection/verification.
- **⚠ Two distinct thresholds — do not conflate:** **10%** = the CSP gatekeeper fitness-check trigger (Jun 2017 guidance); **25%** = the statutory beneficial-owner definition (BO Act 2025). A control interest can be below the 25% statutory definition yet still trigger the 10% gatekeeper check.
