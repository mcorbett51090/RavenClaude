## 1. IFA 2006 — definition, structure, BMA powers

**Definition of "investment fund"** (verbatim): *"any arrangements with respect to property of any description, including money, the purpose or effect of which is to enable persons taking part in the arrangements to participate in or to receive profits or income arising from the acquisition, holding, management or disposal of the property or sums paid out of such profits or income."* Narrowed by the **Investment Funds (Definition) Order 2019**, which excludes arrangements not operated by way of business, intra-group arrangements, pension funds, joint ventures, and securitisation SPVs.

**Part structure (triangulated):** Preliminary (definitions — "controller", "associate", "operator", "service provider", "investment fund"); the authorisation/registration/designation provisions; **Part III — Fund Administrators** (licensing; *much now migrated to FAPBA 2019*); **Part IV — Appeal Tribunals**; plus information-gathering, enforcement, confidentiality-gateway and offences. `[full Part numbering otherwise unverified]`

**BMA powers / offences:** demand production of documents/records + right of entry against the operator, its service providers, or a licensed administrator; issue directions, impose restrictions, revoke authorisation/registration/licences; prohibitions on operating **unauthorised/unregistered/undesignated** funds and on unlicensed fund administration. Confidentiality protected except via statutory **gateways** (BMA functions, criminal proceedings, DPP). **Part IV appeal rights** run against revocations/directions.

## 2. Authorised fund classes (BMA *authorises*; fuller supervision)

| Class | Core conditions |
|---|---|
| **Institutional Fund** | Open only to **qualified participants**, *or* requires each participant to invest **≥ US$100,000**. Appoint an officer/trustee/resident representative in Bermuda with book/record access. Custodian exemption available (and exempt from the requirement that the custodian be a Bermuda FI). **Prospectus must state it is "less regulated than a Standard fund."** |
| **Administered Fund** | Appoint a **Part III/FAPBA-licensed fund administrator** **and** *either* require **≥ US$50,000** per participant *or* be **listed on a BMA-recognised exchange.** |
| **Specified Jurisdiction Fund** | Recognised by **Minister of Finance by Order** for a named jurisdiction (e.g. the Investment Funds (Specified Jurisdiction Fund) (Japan) Order 2012 + Japan Rules 2012). |
| **Standard Fund** | **Residual** category; **retail-capable** (no sophistication/minimum gate) → **fullest BMA supervision.** |

## 3. Registered fund classes (registration-only; lighter touch)

- **Professional Class A Fund — s.6A(2).** Qualified participants only; the operator must appoint an **investment manager** that is **(a)** BMA-licensed under the **Investment Business Act 2003**, *or* **(b)** authorised/licensed by a **BMA-recognised foreign regulator** (e.g. SEC, FCA), *or* **(c)** has **gross AUM ≥ US$100 million** (itself or as part of a management group with consolidated AUM ≥ US$100m) and operates in/from Bermuda or a recognised jurisdiction. **This regulated-manager test is the defining feature vs Class B.** Can launch on **same-day self-certification.**
- **Professional Class B Fund — s.7(2).** Same qualified-participant gate + resident-officer/service-provider conditions, but the investment manager **need not be regulated** (the lighter manager test). BMA review precedes registration. Annual certification also reports director/service-provider changes.
- **Private Fund.** **≤ 20 participants**, **no public promotion**; appoint a **BMA-regulated local service provider** + a **custodian**; may file **management accounts** instead of audited financials.
- **Professional Closed Fund.** New **closed-ended** registered class (2019 reform; PE/closed-end vehicles); **audit waivers available** where the BMA considers appropriate.

**Common s.6A(2)/s.7(2) conditions:** qualified participants; appoint an **officer/trustee/representative resident in Bermuda** with book/record access; appoint required **service providers** (investment manager, fund administrator, registrar, auditor, custodian/prime broker); financials in **IFRS or Bermuda/Canada/UK/US GAAP** (or other GAAP the BMA accepts).

## 4. Qualified participant test — s.9(2)/(3)

- **High-income private investor:** personal income **> US$200,000** in *each* of the two preceding years (or **joint with spouse > US$300,000** each), with reasonable expectation of the same.
- **High-net-worth private investor:** net worth (or joint with spouse) **> US$1,000,000**, **excluding the value of the person's residence.**
- **Sophisticated private investor:** knowledge/experience sufficient to evaluate the merits and risks.
- **Bodies corporate / associations / partnerships / trusts** with **total assets ≥ US$5,000,000.**

## 5. The 2019/2020 reforms

**Investment Funds Amendment Act 2019 — operative 1 Jan 2020:**
- Brought **closed-ended funds** and **overseas funds** into scope for the first time.
- Created the **Professional Closed Fund** category.
- Introduced the **Overseas Fund** designation ("overseas investment fund" = one incorporated/established outside Bermuda — registered/designated, not authorised).
- **Six-month transition** for persons operating closed-ended/overseas funds before 1 Jan 2020.

**Incorporated Segregated Accounts Companies Act 2019 (ISAC Act) — in force 15 Jan 2020:** each **incorporated segregated account is a separate legal person** (unlike the older Segregated Accounts Companies Act, where cells are not bodies corporate). Each account has natural-person capacity, can contract with other accounts and the ISAC, ring-fences its assets from other cells' creditors, and can be wound up individually. Used for funds, (re)insurance, asset management, family offices, securitisation.

## 6. Key fund obligations

- **Offering document / prospectus** — describes the fund, terms, risks, service providers; an **Institutional prospectus must state it is "less regulated than a Standard fund."**
- **Required service providers** — **fund administrator; custodian or prime broker; auditor; officer/operator/resident representative** (Bermuda-resident, book/record access); + an **investment manager** for Professional classes. **Auditors must satisfy the "fit and proper" tests in ss.7 and 14.**
- **Valuation/NAV** — a NAV/valuation policy is required; financials in IFRS or recognised GAAP.
- **Annual audited financials** — for all classes **except Private funds** (management accounts permitted) and where a **Professional Closed Fund** audit waiver applies.
- **Annual certification** — registered funds **certify to the BMA within 6 months of financial year-end** that registration requirements still hold, filing audited financials + a certification form disclosing **material changes** to the prospectus, **NAV, amounts subscribed and redeemed** (Class B adds director/service-provider changes). Filings run through **Integra** (the portal that replaced Erica on 15 Mar 2022).
- **Periodic statistical filings** — Institutional/Administered/Specified-Jurisdiction funds file **quarterly, within 20 business days of quarter-end**; **Standard funds file monthly, within 20 business days of month-end.**

## 7. Fund Administration Provider Business Act 2019 (FAPBA)

- **Licensable activity:** FAPBA **prohibits carrying on (or purporting to carry on) "fund administration provider business" in or from within Bermuda unless licensed** — migrating administrator licensing out of IFA Part III into a standalone Act.
- **Regulatory status:** a licensee is a **"regulated financial institution"** for AML/ATF and a **"relevant financial institution"** for sanctions.
- **Minimum licensing criteria:** application must include **AML/ATF policies & procedures** compliant with the **AMLR 2008**; **corporate-governance policies** per the BMA's Corporate Governance Policy for IFA licensees; and **proof the minimum net-asset requirement of US$50,000 is secured.** Fit-and-proper of controllers/officers applies.
- **Code of Conduct for Fund Administrators** (29 Dec 2018) — the BMA may issue binding codes; **every licensed administrator must comply**; non-compliance is weighed in assessing prudent conduct. *(Clause-level provisions `[unverified]` — PDF 403.)*
- **IFA Part III interface:** an **Administered Fund still requires a FAPBA/Part III-licensed administrator.** Exact repeal mapping `[unverified]`.

## 8. Supervision, reporting, fees, enforcement

- **Supervisory model:** risk-based; information-gathering + entry powers; directions/restrictions/revocations subject to **Part IV appeal-tribunal** rights.
- **2025 fees (single-snippet — `[unverified]`, confirm against the 2025 PDF):** Professional Class A initial filing ≈ **US$1,870**; Class B ≈ **US$1,250**; Fund Administration Provider application ≈ **US$10,340**, annual ≈ **US$11,370**. **⚠ The older figures (Class A ~US$1,500, Class B ~US$1,000, FAPBA application ~US$8,270) are a prior-year schedule** — the 2025 figures are higher; confirm before budget reliance.
- **Enforcement themes:** prohibitions on unauthorised/unregistered/undesignated funds and unlicensed administration; information-gathering + on-site powers; confidentiality gateways.

## How the agent uses this file

Read this for any Bermuda funds or fund-administration question. **Resolve Authorised vs Registered and the specific class first** — each class's investor gate, service-provider set, audit and filing cadence differ. The **Class A vs Class B fork is the manager test** (s.6A(2) regulated manager vs s.7(2) lighter). A **fund is a Non-Registrable Person under the IBA**, but its **Class A manager needs IBA licensing** (see [`investment-business.md`](investment-business.md) and [`edge-cases.md`](edge-cases.md) A2).
