# United Kingdom — PRA & the "Twin Peaks" Framework

> **Last reviewed:** 2026-06-04. Primary-source map of the **UK Prudential Regulation Authority (PRA)** and the FCA boundary for the `uk-pra-specialist`. **Time-sensitive:** the **2027 implementation cliff** (Basel 3.1 + Strong-&-Simple SDDT both 1 Jan 2027) and the live SM&CR review (PS12/26 Phase 1, Apr 2026) need refresh before those dates. **Refresh when** those land, or any `[unverified]` section pin is confirmed.
>
> **Sourcing note:** consequential dates/thresholds were verified against legislation.gov.uk / Bank of England / FCA. Items marked `[unverified]` are exact statutory section pins (high-confidence from training, not re-fetched line-by-line).

The UK is **dual-peak**: the **PRA** (prudential, part of the Bank of England) and the **FCA** (conduct + prudential for non-PRA firms). This is the "home" regime that Bermuda/Cayman/Channel-Islands equivalence and Solvency-II-equivalence discussions reference.

---

## 1. Twin-peaks architecture

- **FSMA 2000** (c.8) — the foundational statute.
- **Financial Services Act 2012** (c.21) — **s.6** inserted **Schedules 1ZA (FCA)** and **1ZB (PRA)** into FSMA 2000; renamed the FSA → FCA and stood up the PRA. In force **1 Apr 2013**.
- **The PRA is part of the Bank of England**, exercised through the **Prudential Regulation Committee (PRC)** (the PRA-Ltd subsidiary was deauthorised by the Bank of England and Financial Services Act 2016).
- **FSMA 2023** (c.29) — the post-Brexit "smarter regulatory framework"; **s.1** revokes retained EU law (bulk from **1 Jan 2024**), moving firm-facing rules into regulator rulebooks; introduces the **Designated Activities Regime (DAR)** and the **SCGO** (§3).

---

## 2. What the PRA regulates

- **~1,500 firms** `[unverified — order of magnitude]`: **banks, building societies, credit unions, insurers/reinsurers, and PRA-designated investment firms** (the largest, designated under FSMA s.22A).
- **Dual regulation:** PRA-authorised firms are regulated by **both** the PRA (prudential) and the FCA (conduct). All other authorised firms are **solo-regulated by the FCA** (their prudential regulator too).
- **Threshold conditions** — the minimum conditions for authorisation; PRA-specific conditions in **FSMA 2000 Schedule 6** + the Threshold Conditions Order 2013 (SI 2013/555).

---

## 3. PRA's objectives (FSMA 2000 ss.2B–2H)

- **General objective — safety & soundness: s.2B.**
- **Insurance objective: s.2C** — appropriate policyholder protection (equal legal status for insurers).
- **Secondary Competitiveness & Growth Objective (SCGO):** inserted by **FSMA 2023 s.25**, in force **29 Aug 2023** — facilitate UK international competitiveness & medium-to-long-term growth, subject to alignment with international standards.
- **Secondary competition objective: s.2H(1).**

---

## 4. PRA Rulebook + supervisory framework

- **PRA Rulebook** — binding rules by Part (Capital Requirements, Liquidity, Senior Management Functions, Solvency Capital Requirement…). Supplemented by **Supervisory Statements (SS)** (expectations) and **Statements of Policy (SoP)**.
- **Proactive Intervention Framework (PIF):** five stages graded by proximity to failure; drives supervisory intensity.
- **Basel 3.1 (UK):** CP16/22 → near-final PS9/24 (Sept 2024) → **final rules PS1/26 (Jan 2026)**; **UK implementation 1 Jan 2027**; replaces the onshored UK CRR.
- **Strong & Simple regime** for **Small Domestic Deposit Takers (SDDTs):** simplified capital regime finalised in **PS4/26 (20 Jan 2026)**, effective **1 Jan 2027** (the earlier "Interim Capital Regime" is no longer to be implemented).

---

## 5. Capital & liquidity

- **ICAAP / ILAAP** — firm internal capital/liquidity adequacy assessments; the PRA's review (UK successor to the EU "SREP") sets **Pillar 2A/2B** add-ons and the PRA buffer.
- **MREL** — set by the Bank of England as resolution authority.
- **UK leverage ratio framework** — major UK banks/building societies `[exact threshold/scope unverified this session]`.
- **Ring-fencing: Financial Services (Banking Reform) Act 2013** (FSMA 2000 ss.142A–142Z). Applies to banking groups with **>£25bn core (retail/SME) deposits**; in force **1 Jan 2019**. HMT consulting on raising the threshold to **£35bn**.

---

## 6. Senior Managers & Certification Regime (SM&CR)

- **Joint PRA/FCA regime** (FSMA 2000 ss.59–64; PRA Rulebook + FCA Handbook SUP 10C, COCON, SYSC). Legislated Dec 2013 (Banking Reform Act); applied to banking Mar 2016; extended to all FSMA firms (insurers Dec 2018; solo-regulated Dec 2019).
- Three limbs: **Senior Managers Regime** (pre-approval + Statements of Responsibilities + Duty of Responsibility), **Certification Regime** (annual fitness certification), **Conduct Rules** (FCA COCON). A 2025–26 review is live (CP18/25, PS12/26 Phase 1, Apr 2026).

---

## 7. Insurance — "Solvency UK"

- The reformed, onshored Solvency II regime — delivered via **PS2/24** (Feb 2024) and **PS10/24** (Matching Adjustment reform, Jun 2024).
- **Risk margin reform:** ~**65%** reduction for long-term life, ~**30%** for general insurance; in force **31 Dec 2023**.
- **Matching adjustment (MA) reform** effective **30 Jun 2024**; remaining Solvency UK changes **31 Dec 2024**.
- *(Relevant to BMA work: Bermuda holds Solvency II equivalence; "Solvency UK" is the UK's divergence from the EU baseline — flag when comparing group capital regimes.)*

---

## 8. FCA boundary

- The FCA owns **conduct of business, market abuse (UK MAR), the Consumer Duty (FCA Handbook PRIN 2A, in force 31 Jul 2023), the FCA Handbook**, and **prudential supervision of FCA solo-regulated firms** — including **MIFIDPRU investment firms** under the **Investment Firms Prudential Regime (IFPR)**, in force **1 Jan 2022** (ICARA process).
- **AML/CFT:** the **FCA is the AML/CFT supervisor for the firms it regulates** under the **Money Laundering Regulations 2017** (SI 2017/692) — **NOT the PRA.** AML supervision is shared across the FCA, **HMRC**, and the professional-body supervisors overseen by **OPBAS** (housed in the FCA).
