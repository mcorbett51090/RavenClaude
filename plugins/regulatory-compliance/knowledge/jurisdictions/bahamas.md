# The Bahamas — Financial-Services Regulatory Framework

> **Last reviewed:** 2026-06-04. Primary-source map of The Bahamas' **multi-regulator** framework for the `bahamas-financial-services-specialist`. **Refresh when** (a) the Securities Industry Bill 2024 is enacted/commenced; (b) a FATF/CFATF evaluation changes status; (c) any `[unverified]` marker is resolved against the Act PDF.
>
> **Sourcing caveat:** the SCB acts page and some Central Bank / FIU pages returned **HTTP 403 to direct fetch**; titles/years are confirmed from search snippets against scb.gov.bs and laws.bahamas.gov.bs, but FIU-Act section pins were not fetched directly (`[unverified]`).

**Unlike the BMA/CIMA single-regulator model, The Bahamas has FOUR statutory regulators + an FIU.** Two structural traps catch outsiders: **trust companies are regulated by the Central Bank** (not the securities regulator); and **corporate/financial service providers are supervised by the Securities Commission** (not a registrar).

---

## 1. Central Bank of The Bahamas (CBOB)

- **Central Bank of The Bahamas Act 2020** (constitutes the Bank; resolution powers).
- **Banks and Trust Companies Regulation Act 2020** — licensing/supervision of **banks AND trust companies** (the distinctive Bahamian feature — trust supervision under the Central Bank).
- **Payment Systems Act 2012** + **Payment Instruments (Oversight) Regulations 2017** — non-bank PSPs, e-money, money transmission.
- **Licence classifications:** **Public** (deal with the general public) · **Restricted** (named persons only) · **Nominee trust** (nominee services only) · **Non-active** (voluntary liquidation / approved dormant).
- **Exchange-control designations** (separate from licence class): **Authorised Dealer** (gold/all FX within Exchange Control limits) and **Authorised Agent** (Bahamian/foreign-currency securities).
- **Prudential / Basel:** CBOB applies a **simplified Basel II & III** regime under Basel proportionality principles.
- **Crisis management:** CBOB Act 2020 + BTCRA 2020 give administrative resolution powers over failing banks.

---

## 2. Securities Commission of The Bahamas (SCB)

- **Securities Industry Act 2011** + **Securities Industry Regulations 2012** — securities markets, registrants, exchanges. **A Securities Industry Bill 2024 was introduced to repeal/replace the 2011 Act** — **confirm enactment/commencement before treating SIA 2024 as in force `[unverified as of mid-2026]`.**
- **Investment Funds Act 2019** (in force **1 Sep 2019**) — four classes: **Standard Fund** (catch-all, most strictly regulated), **Professional Fund** (sophisticated/accredited only), **SMART Fund** (Specific Mandate Alternative Regulatory Test — flexible, can forgo audits for semi-annual reports), **Recognised Foreign Fund**.
- **Financial and Corporate Service Providers Act 2020 (FCSP Act)** (assented 4 Nov 2020; Regs gazetted 30 Dec 2020) — the **Bahamian corporate-services / non-bank financial-services regime, supervised by SCB.** Activity-centric: corporate services, money lending/broking, payday/cash advance, credit, bill-paying, debt collection, financial leasing, financial intermediation, commodities trading, and **custody of digital assets / wallet service providers**.
- **Digital Assets and Registered Exchanges (DARE) Act 2024** — repealed/replaced DARE 2020; extends coverage to advisory/management, digital-asset derivatives and **staking**; defines **stablecoins** (registration + reserve-asset/custody/segregation/reporting/redemption requirements).
- **Carbon Credit Trading Act 2022.** SCB regulates the exchange (**BISX**).

---

## 3. Insurance Commission of The Bahamas (ICB)

- **Insurance Act 2005** (Ch. 347) — domestic insurance; **External Insurance Act 2009** — external/captive insurers underwriting non-Bahamian risk. ICB established **2 Jul 2009**; also enforces FTRA/AML across its insurers.

---

## 4. Compliance Commission of The Bahamas (CCB)

- **Financial Transactions Reporting Act 2018 (FTRA)** + **Regulations 2018**.
- **Independent statutory authority** — originally **s.39 FTRA 2000**, continued under **s.31 FTRA 2018**.
- **AML/CFT/CPF supervisor for DNFBPs** and **non-licensed FIs** specified in **s.4 FTRA 2018**: real-estate agents/brokers, land developers, dealers in precious metals/stones, pawnshops, lawyers, accountants, persons acting as trustee, designated government agencies. Issues **Codes of Practice under s.37 FTRA 2018**; maintains a registration regime.

---

## 5. Financial Intelligence Unit (FIU) & AML/CFT

- **Financial Intelligence Unit Act** (constitutes the FIU — STR receipt/analysis/dissemination). *(Original FIU Act 2000; confirm consolidation/amendment year — page 403'd, `[unverified current citation]`.)*
- **Proceeds of Crime Act 2018** (ML offences/confiscation); **Anti-Terrorism Act 2018** (TF offences); **FTRA 2018 + Regulations** (CDD/record-keeping/reporting).
- **FATF/CFATF history:** grey-listed (increased monitoring) **Oct 2018**, **delisted 18 Dec 2020**. Compliant/largely compliant on **38/40** Recommendations `[point-in-time]`.

---

## 6. Beneficial ownership & tax transparency

- **Register of Beneficial Ownership Act 2018** (in force 20 Dec 2018) — an **AG-operated secure search system** over registered-agent-held BO databases; **not a public register**.
- **Commercial Entities (Substance Requirements) Act 2018 (CESRA)** (in force 31 Dec 2018) — substance + annual reporting for entities carrying on relevant activities.
- **CRS:** automatic exchange under OECD/G20 CRS from **2018**.

---

## 7. Adjacent bodies

- **Registrar General's Department (RGD)** — company/IBC registries (Companies Act / International Business Companies Act); administers parts of the RBO framework.
- **Bahamas International Securities Exchange (BISX)** — registered as a Securities Exchange under SIA 2011 / SIR 2012, regulated by SCB.
