# Cayman Islands — CIMA Jurisdiction Reference

> **Last reviewed:** 2026-06-04. Primary-source map of the **Cayman Islands Monetary Authority (CIMA)** for the `cima-cayman-specialist`. **Refresh when** (a) a governing Act gets a newer Revision (several 2025/2026 Revisions now exist — flagged inline); (b) the FATF/EU/UK list status changes; (c) any `[unverified]` marker is resolved against the Act PDF.
>
> **Sourcing caveat:** cima.ky and legislation.gov.ky PDFs returned **HTTP 403 to direct fetch**; section-level claims rest on WebSearch's quoted snippets + canonical PDF URLs (which resolve in a browser). Section pins marked `[unverified]` need browser confirmation.

CIMA is an **integrated regulator** like the BMA — one authority across banking, trust/CSP, insurance, funds, securities and AML supervision. Useful BMA analogy for the user, but the licence classes and statutes differ; cite Cayman's own law.

---

## 1. CIMA — the regulator

- **Monetary Authority Act (2020 Revision)** (formerly the "Monetary Authority Law"); amended by the **Monetary Authority (Amendment) Act 2023** (Act 1 of 2023).
- **Sole integrated regulator** of Cayman financial services. Statutory **principal functions** in four families: **monetary** (issue/redeem Cayman currency), **regulatory** (licensing, supervision, enforcement), **cooperative** (assistance to overseas regulators), **advisory** (advising Government). Section pins `[unverified — likely ss.5–6]`.
- **Risk-based** model via specialist divisions: Banking Supervision, Fiduciary Services (trust/CSP), Insurance Supervision, Investments & Securities, AML/Compliance.
- Administrative sanctions: **Monetary Authority (Administrative Fines) Regulations (2022 Revision)**.

---

## 2. Banking

- **Banks and Trust Companies Act (2021 Revision)** (a 2025 Revision also exists).
- **Category "A" licence** — full domestic + international banking; may transact with residents. **Category "B" licence** — international/offshore, restricted from local business save limited exceptions; a **Restricted "B"** variant serves a named client list.
- **Minimum net worth:** locally incorporated banks/trust companies **CI$400,000** (or equivalent); **Restricted "B" / Restricted Trust CI$20,000**.
- **Basel:** CIMA applies **Basel II** to locally incorporated Cat A/B and home/host-regulated banks. CAR floor is Basel **8%**, but CIMA requires **12%** for subsidiaries and **15%** for privately-owned/affiliated banks.

---

## 3. Trust / fiduciary & corporate services (CSPs)

- **Trust licences (Banks and Trust Companies Act):** **Trust Licence (unrestricted)**; **Trust Licence (Restricted)** (named persons only); **Nominee (Trust) Licence** (act solely as nominee of a trust licensee).
- **Corporate services / company management (Companies Management Act (2021 Revision)):** **Company Manager Licence** (full corporate services per **s.3(1)** and any prescribed) and **Corporate Services Provider Licence** (only services in **s.3(1)(a)–(e)**).
- **Directors regime (Directors Registration and Licensing Act 2014, am. 2023):** natural-person directors of covered entities (regulated funds, SIBA registered persons) must **register**; those acting for ≥20 entities, and corporate directors, must be **licensed** `[≥20 threshold unverified]`.
- **Private Trust Companies Regulations** exempt qualifying PTCs from full trust-licensing while requiring registration `[reg numbers unverified]`.

---

## 4. Funds

- **Open-ended (Mutual Funds Act (2021 Revision)):** four routes — **Registered (s.4(3))**, **Administered (s.4(1)(b))** (CIMA-licensed administrator provides principal office), **Licensed (s.4(1)(a))**, **Master (s.4(3)(a)(iii))** (master with ≥1 CIMA-regulated feeder).
- **2020 reform:** the Mutual Funds (Amendment) Act removed the **≤15-investor "section 4(4)" exemption** `[s.4(4) cite unverified]`, an **EU-driven** reform bringing previously-unregulated funds into CIMA's net.
- **Closed-ended (Private Funds Act (2021 Revision)):** captures PE/real-estate/other closed-ended vehicles; **registration with CIMA** + annual audit/NAV/valuation, custody and cash-monitoring duties.
- **Fund administrators** separately **licensed** under the Mutual Funds Act.

---

## 5. Investments & securities (SIBA)

- **Securities Investment Business Act (SIBA) (2020 Revision):** two tiers — **Licensees (s.6 licence)** and **Registered Persons (RPs)**.
- **"Excluded persons" regime abolished:** following CFATF recommendations (Jun 2019), SIBA was amended (gazetted 16 Jan 2020); all Excluded Persons had to **re-register as Registered Persons by 15 Jan 2020** and now fall under CIMA's **full supervisory remit** (ownership/operations/control-structure reporting; on-site inspection).

---

## 6. Insurance (for completeness)

- **Insurance Act 2010** (in force 1 Nov 2012): **Class A** (domestic), **Class B** (captives/international, sub-divided **B(i)/B(ii)/B(iii)** by related-vs-unrelated business), **Class C** (ILS/special-purpose), **Class D** (reinsurers). Capital/solvency (MCR + PCR) under the **Insurance (Capital and Solvency) (Classes B, C and D Insurers) Regulations (2018 Revision)**.

---

## 7. AML / CFT

- **Proceeds of Crime Act (2020 Revision)** (enabling); **Anti-Money Laundering Regulations (2023 Revision)** (revised 31 Dec 2022) — CDD, risk assessment, record-keeping, and appointment of **AMLCO / MLRO / Deputy MLRO** on all "relevant financial business" `[reg numbers unverified]`.
- **CIMA** is the **AML supervisor** for its regulated sectors; the **Financial Reporting Authority (FRA / "CAYFIN")** is the **FIU** (SAR receipt); the **Anti-Money Laundering Unit (AMLU)** coordinates AML policy.
- **FATF history:** grey-listed (increased monitoring) **Feb 2021**; **FATF removed Cayman 27 Oct 2023**; **EU** delegated regulation to delist **Dec 2023**; **UK** removed Cayman from its high-risk-third-country list **5 Dec 2023**.

---

## 8. Beneficial ownership & economic substance

- **Beneficial Ownership Transparency Act 2023** (Act 13 of 2023, now **2026 Revision**) + **BO Transparency Regulations 2024** — the BO register regime (replacing the prior Companies-Act BO regime).
- **International Tax Co-operation (Economic Substance) Act** (2018, now **2024 Revision**): "relevant entities" carrying on "relevant activities" must meet the **economic-substance test** and file an annual ES return with the **DITC** (deadline **12 months after financial-year end**).
