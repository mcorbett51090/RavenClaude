# Global Regulator & Standard-Setter Directory — the bodies above every jurisdiction

> **Last reviewed:** 2026-06-04. The "other agencies we may need" map — the international standard-setters and cross-border bodies that sit **above** the BMA, CIMA, Bahamas, JFSC, GFSC, PRA and the US, and against which they are all evaluated. Every jurisdiction specialist consults this for the supranational layer. **Time-sensitive:** the **FATF grey/black lists change every plenary (Feb/Jun/Oct)** — re-pull each cycle. **Refresh when** a plenary updates the lists, the EU AMLA timeline advances, or a standard is revised.
>
> **Why this file exists:** the user asked us to "discover other agencies we may need information on." The sector and jurisdiction files cover the *direct* regulators; this file covers the **standard-setters, FATF-style regional bodies, FIU network, and tax-transparency bodies** whose rules every one of those regulators implements. Treat it as the cross-jurisdiction index.

## 1. AML/CFT standard-setters & regional bodies

| Body | Role | Why it matters to the jurisdictions in this plugin | URL |
|---|---|---|---|
| **FATF** | Global AML/CFT/CPF standard-setter; **40 Recommendations**; mutual evaluations; grey/black lists | The master standard BMA/CIMA/Bahamas/Jersey/Guernsey are evaluated against; grey-listing drives correspondent-banking de-risking + EU-list knock-on | fatf-gafi.org |
| **CFATF** (FSRB) | Caribbean FATF-style regional body; runs the mutual evaluations | **Bermuda, Cayman, Bahamas, BVI, TCI** are all CFATF members | cfatf-gafic.org |
| **MONEYVAL** (FSRB) | Council of Europe FATF-style body | **Jersey, Guernsey, Isle of Man** (Crown Dependencies) + Gibraltar are MONEYVAL members | coe.int/moneyval |
| **Egmont Group** | Network of ~170 FIUs; secure FIU-to-FIU info sharing | An offshore FIU's Egmont membership is a baseline credibility/exchange marker (Bermuda FIA, CAYFIN, Bahamas FIU, Jersey/Guernsey FIUs) | egmontgroup.org |
| **Wolfsberg Group** | Industry body (13 global banks); correspondent-banking AML standards; the **CBDDQ** | Offshore banks must complete the Wolfsberg CBDDQ to retain correspondent relationships — a de-risking pressure point | wolfsberg-principles.com |

**Current FATF list status (Oct 2025 plenary — re-verify each cycle):**
- **Black list (call for action):** **Iran, DPRK** (countermeasures), **Myanmar** (EDD).
- **Grey list (increased monitoring):** **British Virgin Islands added Jun 2025** (still listed at Oct 2025); Bolivia also added Jun 2025; Croatia, Mali, Tanzania removed Jun 2025.
- **Core offshore centres in this plugin — NOT grey/black-listed** as of Oct 2025: **Bermuda, Cayman, Bahamas, Jersey, Guernsey**. (Cayman grey-listed 2021, **delisted Oct 2023**; Bahamas grey-listed 2018, **delisted Dec 2020**.)

## 2. Prudential & sectoral standard-setters

| Body | Role | Why it matters | URL |
|---|---|---|---|
| **BCBS** (at BIS) | Bank prudential standards — Basel III / "Basel 3.1 endgame"; **Core Principles for Effective Banking Supervision** | Every banking regulator here maps capital/liquidity to Basel; Core Principles drive IMF/FSAP assessments | bis.org/bcbs |
| **IAIS** | Insurance supervisory standards — **Insurance Core Principles (ICPs)**; **ICS adopted Dec 2024** (group capital standard for IAIGs / ComFrame) | Bermuda (BMA) is a major IAIG/reinsurance domicile — ICS/ComFrame directly shape its regime | iais.org |
| **IOSCO** | Securities standard-setter; **MMoU** (129 signatories) + enhanced **EMMoU** (23) for cross-border enforcement cooperation | Offshore securities regulators must be MMoU signatories to be credible / exchange evidence | iosco.org |
| **FSB** | Financial Stability Board — coordinates G20 financial-stability policy; NBFI, crypto, resolution | Sets the macro agenda the other bodies implement | fsb.org |

## 3. Tax-transparency & EU layer

| Body / regime | Role | Why it matters | URL |
|---|---|---|---|
| **OECD — CRS / AEOI** | Automatic exchange of financial-account info; the Global Forum on Transparency | The core annual compliance burden for offshore funds/entities (**116 jurisdictions** exchanging; ~EUR 13T / 171M+ accounts in 2024) | oecd.org |
| **OECD — BEPS / Economic Substance** | Base-erosion measures; substance requirements | Drives the Cayman/Bermuda/Bahamas/Channel-Islands economic-substance regimes | oecd.org |
| **OECD — Pillar Two** | **15%** global minimum effective tax (GloBE) | Reshapes zero-tax offshore models (**55 jurisdictions** enacting/introduced as of Jan 2025; 140+ committed) — Bermuda enacted a corporate income tax in response | oecd.org |
| **EU — AMLR/AMLD + AMLA** | The EU AML single rulebook + new supervisor | **AMLA** seated in **Frankfurt**, operational **1 Jul 2025**, direct supervision of highest-risk cross-border FIs from **2028**; **AMLR (Reg (EU) 2024/1624)** directly applicable from **1 Jul 2027** | amla.europa.eu |
| **EU — AML high-risk third-country list** | EU's own AML blacklist | Triggers EU-firm enhanced due diligence on listed jurisdictions | europa.eu |
| **EU — non-cooperative tax list (Annex I)** | EU tax blacklist | Reputational/market-access cost; offshore centres actively manage against it. **Oct 2025: no changes** — 11 jurisdictions incl. Anguilla, Trinidad & Tobago, US Virgin Islands, Vanuatu, Russia, Panama (next update ~Feb 2026) | consilium.europa.eu |

## How the agents use this file

Every jurisdiction specialist consults this for the **supranational layer** — the standard the direct regulator implements. **The FATF list status of a counterparty's jurisdiction is a live AML input** (it can force enhanced due diligence) and **changes every plenary — never quote a list from memory; re-pull it.** When a question crosses jurisdictions (e.g. "compare Bermuda and Cayman fund regimes"), anchor on each jurisdiction's file and use this directory for the FATF/CFATF/MONEYVAL/OECD frame that's common to both.
