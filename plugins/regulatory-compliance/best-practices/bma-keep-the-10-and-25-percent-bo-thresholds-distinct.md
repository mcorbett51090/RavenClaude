# BMA — keep the 10% gatekeeper and 25% statutory beneficial-ownership thresholds distinct

**Status:** Absolute rule — Bermuda runs **two different** beneficial-ownership thresholds for two different purposes; collapsing them into one number produces wrong advice in both directions.

**Domain:** Bermuda / BMA beneficial ownership & corporate services

**Applies to:** `regulatory-compliance`

---

## Why this exists

Bermuda's beneficial-ownership framework uses **two distinct percentage thresholds** that are easy to conflate:

- **10% — the CSP gatekeeper fitness check.** The BMA's June 2017 "Further Guidance — CSP Gatekeeper Role" requires corporate service providers to assess the fitness and propriety of **beneficial owners holding 10% or more** of a Bermuda entity, with personal declarations and AML/ATF checks. This is a *front-line gatekeeping* threshold.
- **25% — the statutory beneficial-owner definition.** Under the beneficial-ownership regime (Companies Act 1981 as amended, now the Beneficial Ownership Act 2025), a beneficial owner is an individual who ultimately owns or controls **more than 25%** of shares/voting rights/interests, or otherwise exercises ultimate effective control. This is the *register* threshold.

A control interest can sit **below 25%** (so it is not a statutory beneficial owner for the register) yet **at or above 10%** (so it still triggers the CSP gatekeeper fitness check). Using one number for both purposes either under-screens (applying 25% to the gatekeeper check) or over-reports (pushing 10%-holders onto the statutory register).

## How to apply

**Name the purpose, then pick the threshold:**

1. **CSP / gatekeeper fitness and propriety assessment → 10%.**
2. **Statutory beneficial-owner identification for the central register → 25%** (or control by other means; failing that, the senior manager).
3. **State both** when the question spans gatekeeping and registration, and note that a 10–25% holder is screened but not (on the percentage test alone) a registrable beneficial owner.
4. **Use the current register holder** — the Beneficial Ownership Act 2025 moved the central register from the **BMA to the Registrar of Companies (RoC)** (ROC enforcement from 1 June 2026). Don't advise that the BMA still holds the register.

**Do:** name the purpose (gatekeeping vs registration) before quoting a percentage; keep 10% and 25% separate; flag the BMA→RoC register move.

**Don't:** apply 25% to a CSP gatekeeper screen; push every 10% holder onto the statutory register; describe the BMA as the current register holder.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

"Control by other means" and the senior-managing-official fallback can make someone a beneficial owner **regardless of percentage** — the 25% test is the first, not the only, route. Effective dates and exact definitions for the BO Act 2025 are recent; confirm the current position (ROC enforcement timing) at time of use.
