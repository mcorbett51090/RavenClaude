# Cite the source and date — never fabricate a number

Search volumes, keyword difficulty, rankings, deliverability thresholds, and channel benchmarks move fast and are easy to misremember. Every quoted figure names its source and freshness (the tool + the date it was pulled), and any number you can't verify this session is marked `[verify-at-build]` rather than stated as current fact. A fabricated or stale volume/rank/benchmark presented as truth is worse than no number — it sends the strategy at a phantom. This is the marketing-specific face of the core anti-hallucination protocol: a confident wrong number is as damaging as an invented one. When a keyword tool or analytics figure isn't available, give a tool-neutral estimate flagged `[verify-at-build]`, name how to confirm it, and never launder a guess into a citation.
