# Measure outcomes, not vanity

Pageviews, list size, and open rate can all rise while the business doesn't — open rate especially is a privacy-inflated proxy, not a truth. Pair every throughput metric with an outcome: conversion and funnel-stage progression, engaged-list health, revenue per recipient, organic-traffic-to-pipeline. Name the source and freshness of every number, and mark any figure you can't verify this session as `[verify-at-build]` rather than quoting it as current fact. A metric that can rise while developer/customer experience falls is the wrong metric.
