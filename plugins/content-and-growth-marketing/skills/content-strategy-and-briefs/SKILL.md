# Content Strategy & Briefs

## Start from the audience job, not the keyword
List the reader's jobs-to-be-done and the decisions they're trying to make, by funnel stage. The content's job is to serve those jobs; the keyword comes *after*. Content that targets a query but not a job ranks and bounces.

## Topic clusters over a flat archive
Design 2-3 pillar themes, each a deep pillar page surrounded by interlinked cluster content. Interlinked depth compounds; scattered one-offs decay. Hand the internal-link topology to `seo-program-lead` to validate against search intent.

## A brief is the unit of quality
The leverage point is the brief, not the draft. A complete brief makes a good draft the default and makes the work delegable. Every brief names: audience + job, search intent + target query, the differentiated angle/POV, the outline, internal links in and out, the CTA, and the success metric.

## A sustainable cadence beats a heroic sprint
Right-size the calendar to real capacity; protect depth over frequency. A calendar the team can't keep produces gaps and guilt. Kill a low-value slot before shipping filler that dilutes the cluster.

## Distribution and repurposing are half the work
Plan the atomization (one pillar → social, email, video, snippets) and the channel-fit map *before* publish. The cheapest high-quality asset is usually the one you already made, repurposed for another surface.

## Output
A topic-cluster strategy (pillars + clusters + cadence + explicit non-goals) and/or a content brief per the [`content-brief`](../../templates/content-brief.md) template. Route SEO validation to `seo-program-lead`, lifecycle wiring to `lifecycle-marketing-engineer`, and the site build to `web-design`.
