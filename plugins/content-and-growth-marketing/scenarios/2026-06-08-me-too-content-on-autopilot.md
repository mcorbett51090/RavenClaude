## Problem

A scaling startup pointed an AI writing pipeline at its keyword list and published 200+ articles in a quarter to "win SEO at scale." Every post was a competent paraphrase of the existing first page of the SERP — no original data, no point of view, no first-hand expertise. After a brief bump, organic growth stalled and then *declined*: a core-update-style ranking shift hit the thin, undifferentiated pages hardest, and several invented statistics (the model had fabricated plausible-looking numbers with no source) surfaced in a customer-facing post and embarrassed the brand.

## Constraints context

- The goal had been framed as output volume ("publish more, faster") rather than any defensible reason for each asset to exist.
- The pipeline had no fact-checking or source-citation step, so fabricated numbers and stale claims shipped as confident fact.
- The pages competed with each other for the same intents (cannibalization) and offered nothing the SERP didn't already have — no experience, expertise, or original angle.

## Attempts

- Tried: generating *even more* AI articles to outpace the decline. Failed — it deepened the thin-content footprint and accelerated the ranking loss; volume of me-too content is a liability, not an asset.
- Tried: lightly editing the existing AI drafts for tone. Failed — polishing prose doesn't add a point of view, original data, or expertise; the pages were still interchangeable with the SERP.
- Tried: stopping the firehose, pruning/consolidating the worst thin pages, and reorienting around a "differentiated POV or don't ship" bar — each new piece had to carry original data (their own product/usage data, a survey, a practitioner interview), a real angle, and a cited source on every figure. AI was kept as a *drafting assistant* under a human expert + fact-check gate, not an autopilot. This worked.

## Resolution

A much smaller volume of genuinely differentiated pieces — backed by proprietary data and a clear POV — recovered and then grew organic traffic, earned links the paraphrase content never could, and rebuilt topical authority. The fabricated-number incident drove a hard rule: every figure carries a source and date or it doesn't ship. Throughput dropped by an order of magnitude; pipeline contribution rose.

## Lesson

A differentiated POV or don't ship — me-too content that restates the SERP (whether a human or a model wrote it) adds nothing and is the first to fall in a quality-focused ranking shift. AI is a drafting assistant under a human-expertise and fact-check gate, never an autopilot. And never fabricate a number: cite the source and date on every figure, or mark it unknown — a confident invented statistic is worse than a gap.
