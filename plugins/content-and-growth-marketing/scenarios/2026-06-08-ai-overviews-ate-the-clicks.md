## Problem

A mid-market software company held stable page-one rankings for its top informational head terms, yet organic sessions to those pages fell ~30% over two quarters while rank trackers showed *no* position loss. The content team was baffled: the rank-tracking dashboard was green, but the analytics warehouse showed the click-through collapsing. The keywords most affected were exactly the "what is / how does X work" questions the brand had built its top-of-funnel cluster around.

## Constraints context

- The decline was concentrated on informational queries; transactional and comparison queries held their CTR.
- Leadership's SEO scorecard was *average position* — a metric that looked healthy while the underlying clicks bled out, so the problem was invisible at the exec level for months.
- The content was well-written prose but buried its answers in long narrative intros, with no structured data, no concise answer block near the top, and no schema markup.

## Attempts

- Tried: pushing for higher rank on the same terms (more backlinks, more on-page tuning). Failed — the pages were already #1-#3; the clicks weren't lost to a competitor's blue link, they were absorbed by the AI Overview / answer box sitting above all of them. Ranking higher than #1 isn't possible.
- Tried: blaming seasonality and waiting it out. Failed — the next quarter confirmed a structural shift, not a dip.
- Tried: reframing AEO/GEO as a first-class surface. Restructured the high-value informational pages to lead with a concise, citable answer block, added FAQ/HowTo structured data, tightened the prose so the key claim was extractable, and split measurement into two tracks: classic blue-link rank *and* answer-engine citation/inclusion. This recovered a meaningful share of the lost engagement and, more importantly, made the trade-off visible.

## Resolution

The brand accepted that some informational queries now resolve in the answer engine without a click, and shifted those pages' job from "win the click" to "be the cited source and capture the higher-intent follow-on." Blue-link CTR on restructured pages partially recovered (concise answer blocks won featured placement), citation in AI Overviews rose, and the team retired *average position* as the headline KPI in favor of organic-to-pipeline plus an answer-engine inclusion track. The honest read: total informational clicks did not fully return — the surface itself had changed — but the pages that did get clicked converted better because intent matched.

## Lesson

Search now includes answer engines, and a green rank tracker can hide a collapsing click-through when an AI Overview sits above #1. Treat AEO/GEO as a first-class surface — concise, citable, structured, extractable answers — and measure it *distinctly* from blue-link rank. Above all, measure outcomes (organic-to-pipeline), not the vanity of average position, or you'll optimize a number that's already decoupled from the business.
