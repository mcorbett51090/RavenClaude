# Client Fit Screen Is a Required Intake Step, Not a Courtesy

**Status:** Absolute rule
**Domain:** Small-firm legal practice
**Applies to:** `legal-small-firm`

---

## Why this exists

The most expensive client in a small firm is the one who should have been declined at intake. Uncooperative clients, clients who dispute every invoice, clients whose matter has no legal merit, and clients whose economics don't match the fee required — all are predictable at the intake stage with a short screen. Without a documented fit screen, attorneys take matters they shouldn't because they are in the room and the engagement feels within reach. A structured fit check makes the decline decision explicit, defensible, and routine.

## How to apply

Run a five-point fit screen on every new matter before the engagement letter is sent:

```
Client Fit Screen
───────────────────
1. Legal merit / viability
   [ ] Does the matter have a plausible legal basis or defense?
   [ ] If litigation: is the recovery realistic relative to the likely fee?
   Notes: ___________________

2. Client ability to pay
   [ ] Can the client pay the estimated fee + initial retainer?
   [ ] Are there red flags (prior attorney, unpaid bills elsewhere, evasive on cost)?
   Notes: ___________________

3. Client cooperation score
   [ ] Does the client have the documents, access, and responsiveness required?
   [ ] Is the client willing to follow legal strategy or will they direct the matter against advice?
   Notes: ___________________

4. Scope/complexity match
   [ ] Does this matter fall within the firm's competence and current capacity?
   [ ] Is the timeline compatible with existing docket?
   Notes: ___________________

5. Relationship dynamics
   [ ] Is there a reason to expect a billing dispute (unusually price-sensitive, prior attorney change)?
   [ ] Are there red flags on communication style in the intake call?
   Notes: ___________________

Decision:
  [ ] Accept  [ ] Decline  [ ] Accept with conditions: ___________________
```

**Do:**
- Complete the screen before the conflict check is resolved — a favorable conflict result on an unfit client is still a decline.
- Document the decline reason in the matter file, even when the reason is purely a fit call (not a conflict). A documented decline is protection if the prospective client later claims representation.
- Send a declination letter on every declined intake, even informally — it starts the period for any future conflict analysis.

**Don't:**
- Skip the fit screen on referrals or existing-relationship prospective clients — fit problems are not correlated with how the prospect found the firm.
- Use the fit screen to discriminate on any protected basis.
- Treat a "difficult client" on an otherwise strong matter as an automatic decline — the screen is a structured weighing, not a veto checklist.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

Court appointments and pro bono assignments through a legal aid program may not allow a fit screen at the attorney's discretion; the referral source provides a preliminary fit determination.
