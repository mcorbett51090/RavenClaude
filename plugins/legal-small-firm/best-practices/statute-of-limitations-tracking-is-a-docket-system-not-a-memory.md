# Statute of Limitations Tracking Is a Docket System, Not a Memory

**Status:** Absolute rule
**Domain:** Small-firm legal practice
**Applies to:** `legal-small-firm`

---

## Why this exists

Missing a statute of limitations is among the most common malpractice claims against solo and small-firm practitioners — and the most preventable. The failure mode is almost never "the attorney didn't know the limitations period." It is "the attorney trusted memory or a single-entry calendar item to track the deadline on a matter with multiple open questions about the applicable period." A docket-system rule means: every limitations-sensitive date is calculated, documented, and entered into an independent system at intake — not when the complaint is being drafted.

## How to apply

Every matter with a potential limitations-period constraint must be docketed at intake:

```
Limitations Docket Entry Checklist
─────────────────────────────────────
Matter:              [name / number]
Claim(s):            [each cause of action or filing type]

For each claim:
  Jurisdiction:      ____________
  Limitations period (cite the statute):  ___ years / months
  Accrual date:      ____________
  Tolling applicable?  [ ] Yes (document basis) [ ] No [ ] Unknown (research needed)
  Calculated deadline: ____________
  Safety-margin reminder date: ____________ (recommend: 60 days before)
  System entered:    [ ] Case management / docket system  [ ] Calendar (attorney + assistant)

Research note:
  If the accrual date or tolling basis is uncertain, record the date as of the
  earliest plausible accrual and flag for research before the safety-margin date.
```

**Do:**
- Enter the deadline into the firm's case-management system AND a secondary calendar (belt-and-suspenders) on the same day the matter is opened.
- Set the safety-margin reminder to at minimum 60 days before the statute runs — 90 days on complex or multi-claim matters.
- When the accrual date or the applicable period is uncertain, calculate from the most conservative (earliest/shortest) assumption until research clarifies.

**Don't:**
- Defer docketing until "after we review the facts" — the conservative deadline enters the system on day one, and is revised as facts clarify.
- Rely on a single calendar entry with no reminder or backup.
- Treat a tolling question as resolved until it is documented and attributed to an authority.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

Transactional matters with no litigation exposure do not require a statute of limitations entry. However, many transactional matters carry their own critical-date obligations (option periods, notice deadlines, closing conditions) that should follow the same docket-entry discipline.
