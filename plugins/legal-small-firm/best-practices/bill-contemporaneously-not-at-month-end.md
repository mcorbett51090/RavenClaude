# Bill Contemporaneously, Not at Month-End in Batches

**Status:** Pattern
**Domain:** Small-firm legal practice
**Applies to:** `legal-small-firm`

---

## Why this exists

Attorneys who record time days or weeks after the work occurred consistently under-record billable time — research in practice management consistently estimates 10–20% of billable time is lost to delayed entry. Beyond time leakage, batched month-end billing introduces a delay in the A/R clock: an invoice sent on the last day of the month is 30 days older to the client by the time they see it, which compresses the effective collection window and inflates A/R aging. Contemporaneous entry also surfaces scope-creep moments in real time, when an additional-services conversation is still possible.

## How to apply

Establish a same-day or next-morning time-entry discipline:

```
Target cadence:
  Billable time entry   →  same day (strong default) or ≤24 hrs
  Expense entry         →  same day receipts captured
  Invoice review        →  within 3 business days of matter milestone or month-end
  Invoice delivery      →  within 5 business days of the period end
```

For client-facing billing cadence, set it explicitly in the engagement letter:
- Monthly for ongoing matters (hourly).
- At-phase-completion for transactional flat-fee work.
- At milestone (filing, settlement, closing) for litigation and deal work.

**Do:**
- Use a time-tracking app with mobile capture so entries happen in the field, not at the desk.
- Flag a matter for budget review if the time-entered total crosses 80% of the budgeted estimate — the billing-cadence discipline makes this check automatic.
- Send invoices promptly after the close of each billing period, not on a convenience schedule.

**Don't:**
- Reconstruct time from calendar entries or emails at month-end — it is both inaccurate and unpleasant, creating incentive to skip it.
- Let A/R age past 60 days before an outreach step (see A/R follow-through rule).
- Hold invoices because the matter "isn't finished" — interim invoices on active hourly matters are standard and maintain the cash-flow clock.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

Fixed-fee matters where a single invoice is issued at completion do not require monthly billing. The time-entry discipline still applies internally (for budget vs. actual tracking), even if no invoice is sent until the milestone.
