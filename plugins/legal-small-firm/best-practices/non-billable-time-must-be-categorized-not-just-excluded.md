# Non-Billable Time Must Be Categorized, Not Just Excluded

**Status:** Pattern
**Domain:** Small-firm legal practice
**Applies to:** `legal-small-firm`

---

## Why this exists

When an attorney logs non-billable time as a single bucket, the capacity picture is invisible. Business development, administration, continuing legal education, pro bono, and firm management are each a distinct investment with different expected returns. Collapsing them hides whether the attorney is spending 15 hours a week on administration (a process problem) or 15 hours on BD (a pipeline investment), both of which look identical as "non-billable hours."

## How to apply

Use a minimum of four non-billable categories in the time-tracking system. Map every non-billable entry to one:

| Category | Includes | Metric to watch |
|---|---|---|
| **Administration** | billing, file management, intake paperwork, trust accounting | Hours/week; target < 5 hrs for a solo |
| **Business development** | networking, proposals, CLE presentations, social content | Hours/week; track pipeline generated |
| **Professional development** | CLE (required + elective), mentoring, bar association | Hours/year; track CLE credits |
| **Pro bono / reduced-fee** | pro bono matters, sliding-scale matters | Hours/year; track separately from write-offs |

Compute total capacity weekly:

```
Total available hours                    = ___
  Less: non-billable (by category)       = ___
Targeted billable hours                  = ___
Actual billable hours                    = ___
Utilization rate                         = actual ÷ targeted × 100%
```

**Do:**
- Require attorneys to categorize non-billable entries the same day — broad reclassification at month-end is inaccurate.
- Review the administration category monthly; hours above 5/week in a solo are a signal that a process or technology fix is available.
- Treat BD hours as an investment; correlate them to intake volume on a 90-day lag.

**Don't:**
- Allow "misc." or "office" as a non-billable category — it becomes a black hole.
- Count required CLE as wasted capacity; it is a compliance cost and tracks separately.
- Conflate pro bono with write-offs — they have different ethics obligations and different reporting requirements.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

A firm with a single attorney and under $200 k gross annual revenue may track at only two categories (billable / non-billable) as a starting point, but should migrate to four categories as the practice grows.
