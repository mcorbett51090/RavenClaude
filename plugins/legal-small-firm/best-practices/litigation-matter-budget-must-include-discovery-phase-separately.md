# Litigation Matter Budget Must Include Discovery as a Separate Phase

**Status:** Pattern
**Domain:** Small-firm legal practice
**Applies to:** `legal-small-firm`

---

## Why this exists

Discovery is the most unpredictable cost center in litigation — it can double or triple the expected fee on a case that was budgeted as routine. When an attorney quotes a litigation matter with a single total estimate, discovery variance swallows the entire margin. Budgeting discovery as its own phase, with its own assumptions and re-scope trigger, gives the attorney a documented basis for a mid-matter fee conversation and gives the client a clear picture of where uncertainty lives before they commit.

## How to apply

Structure every litigation budget with at minimum four phases, each with its own estimate:

```
Litigation Phase Budget
────────────────────────
Phase 1 — Pleadings + pre-answer:    $[low]–$[high]
  Assumptions: complaint/answer only; no PI motion

Phase 2 — Discovery:                 $[low]–$[high]
  Assumptions: [# depositions], [# document requests], [page volume estimate]
  Discovery re-scope trigger: "If depositions exceed [X] or document volume exceeds
    [Y] pages, discovery estimate will be revised before proceeding."

Phase 3 — Motions (if any):          $[low]–$[high]
  Assumptions: MSJ only; no Daubert / expert motions

Phase 4 — Trial preparation + trial: $[low]–$[high]
  Assumptions: [# trial days], [# witnesses]

Total estimated range:               $[sum low]–$[sum high]
Contingency margin:                  +[X%] for unforeseen complexity
```

**Do:**
- Make the discovery re-scope trigger specific and observable — not "if discovery gets complicated" but "if depositions exceed four."
- Revisit the budget at each phase transition and document whether the estimate held or why it changed.
- Flag early discovery signals (counterparty is uncooperative, volume is larger than expected) to the client in writing at the moment they appear, not at billing.

**Don't:**
- Bundle discovery into a single "litigation budget" line — it hides the variance driver.
- Quote a firm flat fee for contested discovery without a re-scope clause.
- Wait until the discovery budget is exhausted to have the re-scope conversation — the conversation must happen before the trigger fires, not after.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

Summary proceedings with no discovery (small claims, certain administrative hearings) do not require a separate discovery phase. Uncontested matters (default judgment, consent orders) similarly do not.
