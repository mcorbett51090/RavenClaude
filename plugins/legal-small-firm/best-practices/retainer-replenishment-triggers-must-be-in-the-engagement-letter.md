# Retainer Replenishment Triggers Must Be in the Engagement Letter

**Status:** Absolute rule
**Domain:** Small-firm legal practice
**Applies to:** `legal-small-firm`

---

## Why this exists

A retainer collected at intake and never replenished is consumed by the second billing cycle, after which the attorney is extending unsecured credit to the client. This is how A/R ages. The engagement letter is the only place to lock in the replenishment obligation before work begins — a verbal conversation, a mid-matter request, or a re-invoice does not carry the same enforcement weight as a signed agreement clause. Trust accounting rules in most jurisdictions also require that the replenishment obligation, and the handling of any unearned balance, be documented in the engagement agreement.

## How to apply

Every engagement letter with a retainer must include three provisions:

```
Retainer Provisions Checklist
───────────────────────────────
1. Initial amount:          Client shall pay $[___] as an advance fee/cost retainer
                            upon execution of this agreement.

2. Replenishment trigger:   When the retainer balance falls below $[threshold, e.g., $500],
                            Client shall replenish to $[full amount] within [X] business days
                            of written notice. Work may be suspended if not replenished.

3. Unearned balance:        Any unearned retainer balance will be refunded within [X] days
                            of matter conclusion or termination.
                            [If IOLTA: "Funds are held in a trust account per [state] Rules of
                             Professional Conduct Rule X.X and are not firm funds until earned."]
```

**Do:**
- State the replenishment threshold in a dollar amount, not a percentage — percentages require the client to do math they won't do.
- Include a work-suspension clause tied to non-replenishment — without it, the trigger is advisory, not contractual.
- Confirm that the retainer handling language complies with your jurisdiction's Rules of Professional Conduct (IOLTA, earned-on-receipt, or hybrid) before the engagement letter goes out.

**Don't:**
- Accept an initial retainer that covers only one billing cycle without a replenishment clause — a one-cycle retainer without replenishment is a down payment, not a security deposit.
- Commingle retainer funds in the operating account; the retainer must go to the trust account until earned (in most jurisdictions) [unverified — confirm with your jurisdiction's rules].
- Use template engagement letters with blank replenishment-threshold fields; fill them before the letter is sent.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

Flat-fee matters where the full fee is paid in advance and earned on receipt do not require a replenishment clause — the retainer is fully earned at the outset. Contingency matters have no retainer in the traditional sense.
