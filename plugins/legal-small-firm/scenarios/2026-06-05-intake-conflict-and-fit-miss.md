## Problem

A solo expanding into a second practice area took on a matter that turned into the year's biggest realization sinkhole and a near-miss conflict. The client had been signed the day they walked in — no documented conflict check beyond "the name didn't ring a bell," no fit/viability screen, no scope conversation. Three months in, an adverse party surfaced who was a **former client**, and the matter's scope had ballooned with a client who couldn't pay. The risk: intake treated as a sales step instead of risk management is where the matters that destroy realization *and* create ethics/malpractice exposure are born (§3 #2).

## Context

- Segment: solo, independent, expanding from estate planning into small-business disputes (a less-familiar area).
- Constraint: this is an **intake-discipline** gap, not a competence gap. The conflict check was run only against the *prospective client*, not against **all parties** (adverse parties, related entities, former clients) — the most common way a real conflict slips through. The plugin does not resolve the ethics question; it standardizes the intake *process* as decision-support and routes the conflict call to the responsible attorney (§2, §3 #6).
- The owner conflated "I don't recognize the name" with "I ran a conflict check."

## Attempts

- Tried: rebuilt intake as a **gated process** — conflict check on **all parties** first (client, adverse parties, related entities, witnesses, former clients), then a fit/viability screen, then a deliberate scope + fee-structure conversation before any engagement letter. Outcome: a repeatable checklist that fails *before* the engagement, not after.
- Tried: ran the new conflict step against the firm's full former-client list (not just active clients) and documented the result for every new matter. Outcome: caught two adjacent near-conflicts in the next quarter that the old "does the name ring a bell" approach would have missed. The conflict call still routes to the attorney — the plugin produces the check, not the waiver decision.
- Tried: added a fit/viability screen (ability to pay, realistic scope, whether the matter is the kind of work the firm should take) so the worst client is **declined**, not signed. Outcome: a documented decline rationale replaced gut-feel intake.

## Resolution

The miss was **process, not knowledge** — intake was a courtesy, not a required risk-management step. A conflict check on **all parties** (not just the client) plus a fit/viability screen and a scope/fee conversation before the engagement letter closed both the realization leak and the ethics exposure. Conflict and fit before the engagement (§3 #2); the conflict resolution itself routes to the attorney and the applicable rules of professional conduct.

**Action for the next consultant hitting this pattern:** make the conflict check run on **every party**, not just the prospective client — that is the single most common real-conflict miss. Gate the engagement on conflict → fit/viability → scope+fee, in that order, and document the decline rationale when you turn a matter away. Route the conflict-resolution / waiver decision to the attorney (this plugin is not a licensing authority and gives no legal advice, §2).
