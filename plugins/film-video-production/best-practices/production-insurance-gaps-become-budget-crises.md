# Production insurance gaps become budget crises — confirm coverage before the shoot

**Status:** Absolute rule
**Domain:** Film & video production / risk / budgeting
**Applies to:** `film-video-production`

---

## Why this exists

A production that begins shooting without confirming the full scope of its insurance coverage is building an unquantified contingency liability. The gaps that actually cost money — a crew injury not covered by workers' comp because a crew member was misclassified, a location damage claim that exceeds the general liability limit, a camera package loss with a deductible that exceeds the equipment rental budget for the rest of the shoot — are not discovered until the claim is filed. By then they are budget crises, not budget line items. Insurance is not a cost to minimize; it is the mechanism that converts catastrophic variance into a known cost.

## How to apply

Confirm coverage against this checklist before the first shoot day:

```
Insurance confirmation checklist — [Project] — [Date]

General Liability
  Carrier / policy #:              ____
  Per-occurrence limit:            $____
  Aggregate limit:                 $____
  Location-specific riders added:  Y/N → list: ____
  Certificate of insurance issued to locations: Y/N

Workers' Compensation
  Carrier / policy #:              ____
  Coverage includes: Employees [ ]  Loan-outs [ ]  1099 workers [as required]
  Statutory limits met in shooting state: Y/N (state: ____)

Equipment / Camera Package
  Floater policy:                  Y/N
  Covered value (must match replacement value, not rental value): $____
  Deductible:                      $____
  Confirm: deductible is in the budget as a contingency line: Y/N

Errors & Omissions (if distribution is in scope)
  Required by distributor:         Y/N
  Effective date:                  ____
  Coverage includes music / clearances: Y/N

Cast Insurance (if applicable)
  Lead cast insured:               Y/N
  Replacement cost coverage:       Y/N
  Medical certificate obtained:    Y/N
```

**Do:**
- Request certificates of insurance from every production services company and vendor — and confirm the certificates name the production company as additional insured.
- Confirm the deductibles are budgeted as a contingency sub-line; an unbudgeted deductible in a damage scenario is a direct cost overrun.
- Verify that any loan-out company arrangements for key talent are reflected in the workers' comp endorsement — loan-outs are a common coverage gap.

**Don't:**
- Assume a production services company's equipment coverage protects the production — the vendor's policy covers the vendor; the production needs its own floater.
- Wait until the distributor asks for the E&O certificate to obtain it — the chain-of-title review required for E&O issuance takes weeks.
- Accept a certificate of insurance without reading the policy limits; a $1M GL policy is not adequate for a large-location shoot with public access.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

- Productions shooting entirely on a studio lot where the studio provides blanket coverage may have modified requirements — confirm in writing with the studio risk management, and keep your own evidence of their coverage.
- Student productions at accredited institutions may operate under the institution's umbrella policy; verify scope limitations, as institutional policies typically exclude commercial distribution.
