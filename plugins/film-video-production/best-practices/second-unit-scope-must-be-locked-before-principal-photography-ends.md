# Second Unit Scope Must Be Locked Before Principal Photography Ends

**Status:** Absolute rule
**Domain:** Film & video production — scheduling and budgeting
**Applies to:** `film-video-production`

---

## Why this exists

Second unit — inserts, pickup shots, establishing exteriors, stunts, or additional coverage — is almost universally under-estimated and under-documented in the original budget. When second unit scope is left open at the start of principal photography, it silently expands as the director and editor identify coverage gaps. By the time second unit is locked, key crew members may be on other projects, locations may be unavailable or more expensive, and the line producer's leverage to manage cost has evaporated. The result is a budget hit that arrives after the contingency has already been drawn. The rule: define second unit deliverables as a shot list before the first day of principal, and lock any scope changes by the end of principal photography's last week.

## How to apply

**Pre-production checklist for second unit:**

```
[ ] Second unit shot list drafted and approved by director
[ ] Each shot tagged: stunt / insert / establishing / pickup / aerial / other
[ ] Second unit director (or 1st AD handling second unit) named
[ ] Crew confirmed: DP, 1st AC, sound (if sync), grip/electric, PA
[ ] Gear package quoted and in the budget
[ ] Locations confirmed — if same as principal, shoot days and access blocked
[ ] Insurance certificate covers second unit dates and activities (stunts especially)
[ ] Second unit cost line in the budget with its own account number (not buried in BTL misc)
[ ] Picture-lock assumption — confirm second unit must wrap before editorial assembly or
    can run concurrently (affects post start date)
```

**During principal photography — scope change protocol:**

| Trigger | Action | Owner |
|---|---|---|
| Director requests a new second unit shot | Cost-estimate the shot before it is added to the list | Line producer / UPM |
| Shot count increases > 20% from the approved list | Formal scope-change approval from producer | Producer |
| Location becomes unavailable | Identify replacement before second unit begins, not during | Location manager |
| Second unit date slips past principal wrap | Reassess crew availability and re-quote gear; don't assume same rates | Line producer |

**Budget discipline:**
- Second unit has its own line in the top sheet, not folded into principal photography.
- Day rate, equipment, and location cost are built up as if it were a separate shoot day.
- If second unit is handled by the principal crew after wrap, the overtime and turnaround cost is modeled, not assumed to be zero.

**Do:**
- Lock the shot list and date range before the production insurance certificate is issued.
- Confirm with the editor that the second unit shot list fills actual coverage gaps, not director wish-list shots.
- Shoot second unit before principal wrap when possible — it simplifies crew contracting and insurance.

**Don't:**
- Leave second unit as a TBD line — even a rough estimate plus contingency is better than no number.
- Assume second unit is "just a day" — a stunt day or aerial day can easily be 3–5× a standard shoot day.
- Let the second unit shot list grow after the first assembly edit without a formal cost impact conversation.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

On documentary productions, second unit as a formal concept often doesn't apply — pickup and b-roll shooting is integrated into the production calendar as a flexible schedule element, not a distinct unit with a formal shot list. Apply the underlying discipline (define scope, cost it up, track it) to the b-roll budget instead.
