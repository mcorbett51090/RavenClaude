# Issue deal memos before crew starts — not at wrap

**Status:** Absolute rule
**Domain:** Film & video production / crew / legal / labor
**Applies to:** `film-video-production`

---

## Why this exists

A crew member who begins work without a signed deal memo has an undocumented rate, an undocumented overtime structure, and no written record of the work-for-hire provision that vests copyright in the production company. The production learns this is a problem at one of two moments: when the crew member disputes their pay at wrap (and neither party has the agreed rate in writing), or when the production's E&O insurer or distributor asks for chain-of-title documentation and there is no work-for-hire agreement for a key crew member. Both moments are expensive. The first adds a payroll dispute to an already-closed production; the second can delay or block a distribution deal.

## How to apply

Issue deal memos using a checklist gate:

```
Deal memo gate — [Production] — required before Day 1 on any crew member

Required fields (minimum):
  [ ] Crew member legal name and address
  [ ] Position / title
  [ ] Start date / end date (or "through completion of services")
  [ ] Daily / weekly rate (state explicitly: flat or scale)
  [ ] Overtime basis (straight 8s / 10-hour days / union schedule — specify)
  [ ] Meal penalty provisions (if applicable)
  [ ] Per diem (if applicable) — amount per day, taxable status
  [ ] Work-for-hire provision (copyright vests in production company)
  [ ] Credit block (if negotiated)
  [ ] Morals / conduct clause
  [ ] Non-disclosure
  [ ] Production company signatory name / title
  [ ] Crew member signature

Deal memo received and filed:  Y/N — Date: ____
Copy to production accountant:  Y/N
```

**Do:**
- Send deal memos before the crew member's start date — "we'll sort the paperwork later" is how disputes start.
- For union productions, confirm the deal memo is consistent with the applicable agreement (SAG-AFTRA, IATSE, Teamsters, WGA — as applicable); a memo that conflicts with the CBA does not override the CBA but creates confusion.
- File executed deal memos with the production accountant in the same week they are signed; they are needed for the final cost report and the E&O chain-of-title review.

**Don't:**
- Issue verbal deal confirmations and assume the written memo will follow; verbal offers that go unpapered invite rate disputes.
- Use a generic contractor agreement for crew who are employees; misclassification creates payroll tax exposure and breaks the work-for-hire chain on projects where the crew member is an employee under applicable labor law.
- Allow a crew member to begin prep work (tech scouts, pre-production planning) before the deal memo is signed; prep days are compensable work.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

- Some union agreements (e.g., certain low-budget SAG-AFTRA agreements) have their own deal memo forms that override the production's standard template; use the union form for covered performers.
- Day players hired on the day from a vendor roster may require an abbreviated same-day deal confirmation; have a template that can be signed in the field.
