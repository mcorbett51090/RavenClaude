# Production budget benchmarks & context (2025)

Dated budget-structure and union-rate benchmarks for a production engagement. Figures carry a source + date; route union/legal specifics to the specialist.

## Budget structure

A film budget typically splits ~25–35% above-the-line (story/producer/director/principal cast), ~40–50% below-the-line production (crew/equipment/locations), ~10–20% post (editorial/VFX/sound/music), and ~5–10% other (insurance/bond/contingency) — build to a top-sheet (§3 #1).

## Contingency

Industry-standard contingency is ~10% of below-the-line + post (range ~5–15%, higher for VFX-heavy or remote shoots); financiers and bond companies expect to see it. Contingency is managed against real risk, not hoped away (§3 #4).

## Union rates & fringes

IATSE combined fringe runs ~28–32% of gross wages (e.g. ~$280k–$320k on $1M of crew wages); Local 600 camera-operator minimums were ~$750–$900/day, with a 4% wage increase effective Aug/Sep 2025. Build below-the-line to current, local rates and route union/legal specifics to the specialist (§3 #7).
