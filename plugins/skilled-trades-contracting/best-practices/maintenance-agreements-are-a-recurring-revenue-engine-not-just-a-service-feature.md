# Maintenance Agreements Are a Recurring Revenue Engine, Not Just a Service Feature

**Status:** Pattern
**Domain:** Service revenue / customer economics
**Applies to:** `skilled-trades-contracting`

---

## Why this exists

HVAC, plumbing, and electrical contractors who offer maintenance agreements treat them as a customer convenience or a retention tool. They are also one of the most capital-efficient revenue streams a service business can build: low labor intensity per visit, contracted in advance, with a predictable renewal rate and a built-in lead source for repair and replacement revenue. A maintenance agreement customer who has a relationship with the contractor converts to replacement work at 2–4x the rate of a one-time service customer [unverified — training knowledge]. Contractors who don't track maintenance agreement count, renewal rate, and revenue per agreement are leaving their most stable revenue stream unmanaged.

## How to apply

Build the maintenance agreement economics model:

```
Maintenance agreement metrics (track monthly):
  Active agreements:                        ______
  New agreements (this month):              ______
  Cancelled agreements (this month):        ______
  Annual renewal rate:                      ______%  (target: ≥ 85%)

  Revenue per agreement (annualized):
    Agreement price:                        $______/year
    Visits included:                        ______ visits/year
    Labor cost per visit:                   $______
    Parts / consumables per visit:          $______
    Total visit cost:                       $______
    Agreement margin:                       agreement price − total visit cost = $______
    Agreement margin %:                     ______%  (target: 40–60% [unverified])

  Lead conversion value:
    % of agreement customers who convert to repair/replace in the year: ______%
    Average replacement ticket for converted customers:                 $______
    Annual replacement revenue attributable to agreement base:          $______

  Total agreement program value:
    Recurring revenue:     active agreements × price = $______/year
    Replacement pipeline:  ______/year
    Combined:              $______/year
```

**Do:**
- Track the renewal rate as the primary health metric of the agreement program — a renewal rate below 80% signals pricing, service quality, or communication failure.
- Train technicians to offer an agreement at every service call; the close rate on a satisfied service customer is the highest close-rate opportunity in the business.
- Model the lifetime value of an agreement customer vs. a one-time customer when justifying the agreement pricing and any acquisition investment.

**Don't:**
- Price agreements to "break even" on the visits — the visit margin matters, but the lead-conversion and retention value is where the program ROI lives.
- Let the renewal process be manual or passive; an automated renewal reminder and payment system is the single highest-ROI process investment in the agreement program.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

New-construction contractors who do not do service work do not have a maintenance agreement business to build; the rule is specific to service, replacement, and residential/light-commercial contractors. Subcontractors who do not have direct customer relationships cannot offer maintenance agreements independently.
