# Good-Better-Best Option Presentation Raises Average Ticket Without More Calls

**Status:** Pattern
**Domain:** Sales / pricing
**Applies to:** `skilled-trades-contracting`

---

## Why this exists

Most service technicians present a single repair or replacement option and wait for a yes/no answer. This is the minimum-revenue approach: the customer either buys or they don't, and the ticket is capped at the one option presented. Good-better-best (or good/better/best/premium) option presentation gives the customer three distinct solutions at three price points, with the middle option positioned as the recommended value. Industry data consistently shows that multi-option presentation raises average ticket 20–40% [unverified — training knowledge] compared to single-option presentation, without increasing the number of calls, leads, or marketing spend. The close rate on the "good" option also absorbs customers who would have declined a single mid-price option.

## How to apply

Build the good-better-best framework into the flat-rate book and technician training:

```
Option presentation structure (per service category):
  GOOD (entry level):
    Scope: repair the immediate failure; minimum warranty
    Price: $______
    Margin: $______   Margin %: ______%

  BETTER (recommended):
    Scope: repair + preventive maintenance; standard warranty
    Price: $______
    Margin: $______   Margin %: ______%

  BEST (premium):
    Scope: repair + PM + extended warranty + priority service agreement
    Price: $______
    Margin: $______   Margin %: ______%

  Presentation script (per option):
    "For [option X], we would [brief scope], and that includes [warranty/guarantee].
    The investment for that is [price]. Most customers in your situation choose [BETTER],
    because it [key benefit]. Here's what that looks like..."

  Track per technician (weekly):
    Multi-option close rate (% of calls where options were presented): ______%
    Average ticket:                                                      $______
    % who chose BETTER or BEST:                                         ______% (target: >50%)
```

**Do:**
- Make the BETTER option the most clearly described and most actively recommended — "anchoring" the customer on the middle option is the technique that drives the ticket lift.
- Train technicians to present options with confidence; hesitant presentation signals that the technician doesn't believe in the value.
- Review the BETTER/BEST selection rate weekly — if it drops below 50%, the pricing gap between options is too wide or the options aren't meaningfully differentiated.

**Don't:**
- Present the BEST option first at its full price without context — it anchors the customer on a high number without a reference point.
- Build options where the GOOD option has a negative or zero margin — every option must stand alone as a profitable transaction.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

Emergency-only calls where the customer's only concern is "get the system running tonight" often collapse the option window — a single clear solution with a clear price is appropriate. New-construction bids are typically competitive sealed bids where option presentation is inappropriate and potentially disqualifying.
