# Trade-contracting KPI glossary

The metrics a trade contractor is judged on — formulas, the misreads, and where a figure needs a date or a `[unverified]` mark.

## Job & pricing metrics

Loaded labor rate, flat-rate price, job gross margin, material markup, average ticket. The loaded rate is the master estimating number (§3 #1, #2).

- **Loaded labor rate** = base wage × (1 + labor burden) + overhead ÷ sellable hours. The billable rate that absorbs wage, burden, vehicle, tools, insurance, and overhead before profit. Misread: stopping at wage + burden (overhead never recovered).
- **Gross margin** = (price − direct cost) ÷ **price**. **Markup** = (price − cost) ÷ **cost**. They are NOT equal — see the markup-vs-margin decision tree. Misread: applying a markup equal to the desired margin (underprices every job).
- **Material markup** = real purchase cost + waste, then the markup the market bears — name all three (§3 #5).

## Field metrics

Billable-hour ratio, first-time-fix/callback rate, revenue-per-truck-per-day, jobs-per-day (§3 #3, #4, #6).

- **Billable-hour ratio** = billable hours ÷ paid hours. The field's master number; non-billable drive/restock/rework is the silent margin killer.
- **First-time-fix / callback rate** — a callback is a free truck roll that doubles a job's labor cost (§3 #4); first-time-fix is a financial metric, not just quality.
- **Revenue per truck per day** — how a fleet of service vans is judged; an under-utilized or under-stocked truck is lost margin (§3 #6).

## Sales metrics

Quote close rate, average ticket, option-presentation take-rate, lead-to-sale (§3 #7).

- **Bid-hit ratio** = bids submitted ÷ jobs awarded — the estimating-efficiency number; see the bid/no-bid decision tree.
- **Close rate / average ticket** — move revenue more than lead volume; good/better/best option presentation lifts ticket without more marketing spend.

## Benchmark reference (cited, dated — `[verify-at-use]`)

> These are **public, segment-dependent rules-of-thumb**, retrieved 2026-06-05. They move with trade, region, and work type and must be validated against the contractor's actual data before any deliverable (§3 #8). Gross-margin figures are at **different scopes** (whole-project GC vs specialty trade vs service/install) — read each against the scope you're pricing, never interchangeably.

### Margin & profitability

| Metric | Rule-of-thumb (2025–2026) | Source |
|---|---|---|
| GC gross margin (whole project) | ~12–16% | Siana 2026 benchmarks |
| Specialty-trade gross margin (electrical/HVAC, GC comparison) | ~15–25% | Siana 2026 benchmarks |
| HVAC **install** gross margin | ~35–45% (avg ~40%) | FieldEdge |
| HVAC service business **blended** gross target | ~50–55% | FieldEdge |
| Construction **net** profit margin | ~5–6% typical; ~8–10% healthy | Aladdin Bookkeeping 2025 |
| HVAC net margin | ~5–10% (median ~5.8%, top quartile ~13.2%) | Profitability Partners |

### Pricing mechanics

| Metric | Rule-of-thumb | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Total contractor markup (overhead + profit) | ~20–40% of cost | Angi / Siana |
| "10-and-10" baseline | ~10% overhead + ~10% profit (≈20% markup) | NEXT Insurance / Procore |
| Overhead as % of revenue (top performers) | ~8–12% | Siana 2026 benchmarks |
| Markup ≠ margin (the trap) | 20% markup → 16.7% margin; 35% markup → 25.9% margin | Buildern / Procore |

### Change orders & bidding

| Metric | Rule-of-thumb | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Change orders as % of contract value (commercial) | ~8–14% (benchmark ~10%; distressed up to ~25%) | Rhumbix / AIA |
| Change-order markup | commonly ~15% | Case Western Facilities |
| Bid-hit ratio (hard-bid) | ~5:1 healthy; public ceiling ~10:1 | Sunflower Bank |
| Bid-hit ratio (negotiated/relationship) | ~3:1 or better | DowntoBid |
