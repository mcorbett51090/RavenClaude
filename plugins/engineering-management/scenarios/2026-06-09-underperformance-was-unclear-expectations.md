## Problem

A new EM was ready to label a mid-level engineer (fictional) an underperformer after two quarters of "slow" delivery and start a PIP. The risk: a claim about a person was being treated as a verdict, not a hypothesis, and the system hadn't been checked — most "performance problems" are expectation/clarity problems in costume (§3 #1 #5).

## Context

- 6-person team; the engineer had transferred in and picked up ambiguous, cross-team work.
- Constraint: a person conclusion is the *last* hypothesis, not the first; the deliverable about a person is a draft for a human to own, not an autonomous verdict (§2 #5).
- The manager had no dated behavioral evidence — just a feeling and a recency-heavy impression (§3 #4).

## Attempts

- Tried: **wrote the evidence first** — specific events, dates, impact — before any judgment (§3 #1 #4). Outcome: the "slow" work turned out to be repeatedly blocked on an undocumented cross-team dependency.
- Tried: **checked the system** (expectations, context, altitude, blockers) before the person, traversing Tree 1 (§3 #5). Outcome: expectations for the role had never been made explicit; the engineer was guessing at priorities.
- Tried: **clarified expectations and unblocked the dependency** in the next 1:1, then watched a measured window (§3 #2 #5). Outcome: delivery recovered without any PIP.

## Resolution

The fix was a **system fix — explicit expectations plus an unblocked dependency**, not a performance instrument. The output was the dated evidence, the system diagnosis, and a fair expectations-and-support plan as a draft the manager owned.

**Action for the next manager hitting this pattern:** **write dated behavioral evidence before the judgment, and check the system before the person.** A genuine gap may still exist — but it's the last hypothesis. If it does turn out to be HR/legal territory, route it to `people-operations-hr` and counsel (§2). See Tree 1 and the `run-one-on-one` skill.

Benchmark and threshold figures are org-/level-/date-dependent — treat as `[unverified — training knowledge]` and validate against the real situation before any deliverable (§3 #8).
