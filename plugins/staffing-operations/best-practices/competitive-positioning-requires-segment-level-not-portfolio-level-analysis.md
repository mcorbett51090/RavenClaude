# Competitive Positioning Requires Segment-Level, Not Portfolio-Level, Analysis

**Status:** Absolute rule
**Domain:** Staffing operations
**Applies to:** `staffing-operations`

---

## Why this exists

A staffing firm's competitive position in travel nursing is determined by a completely different set of competitors, pricing dynamics, and client expectations than its position in school-based speech therapy. Presenting a blended "we are a leading healthcare and education staffing firm" competitive statement without segment decomposition is not a competitive analysis. The competitors in travel nursing (Aya Healthcare, AMN Healthcare, TravelNurse Source) have essentially no overlap with the competitors in school-based teletherapy (Presence, eLuma, Soliant's therapy division, Illuminate Education). Mixing them produces a false sense of relative position in each.

## How to apply

Structure competitive analysis as a segment matrix, not a single-firm narrative:

```
Competitive Position Matrix
────────────────────────────
Firm being analyzed:  ________________
Date:  ________________  Source:  ________________

Segment | Primary competitors | Firm's relative position | Win rate evidence | Differentiation lever
────────|---------------------|--------------------------|-------------------|─────────────────────
Travel nursing | Aya, AMN, TravelNurse | ________________ | ________________ | ________________
Allied health | CHG, Cross Country, Medstaff | ________________ | ________________ | ________________
Locum tenens | CHG, Jackson, Weatherby | ________________ | ________________ | ________________
Per-diem | Local agencies, ShiftMed, Clipboard | ________________ | ________________ | ________________
School therapy (in-person) | Soliant, Pediatric TH, TSSG | ________________ | ________________ | ________________
School teletherapy | Presence, eLuma, Talkspace Health | ________________ | ________________ | ________________
School subs/paras | ESS, Kelly Education, local | ________________ | ________________ | ________________
```

**Do:**
- Run the segment matrix before any "competitive" claim in a deliverable — a claim about competitive position that isn't segment-specific is unsubstantiated.
- Note the source and date for each competitor entry (SIA, press release, LinkedIn headcount, public filings) — training knowledge on competitor size and position is marked `[unverified — training knowledge]`.
- Identify the segments where the firm's dual-segment depth (healthcare + education) is a genuine advantage vs. where single-segment specialists dominate.

**Don't:**
- Quote "total healthcare staffing market" as the market size denominator for a firm that operates only in travel nursing and school therapy — the addressable market is the segment, not the whole.
- Use a 2021–2022 competitive landscape for current competitive analysis without a refresh note; the travel nurse spike and its collapse significantly reshaped market shares.
- Conflate competitor revenue or headcount with market share in a segment — size in one segment does not imply presence in another.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

When the client asks for a single integrated competitive brief (e.g., for a board presentation), provide the segment matrix and a one-page synthesis. The synthesis does not replace the segment analysis; it summarizes it.
