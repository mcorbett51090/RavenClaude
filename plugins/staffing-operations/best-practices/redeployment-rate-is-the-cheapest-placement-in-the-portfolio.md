# Redeployment Rate Is the Cheapest Placement in the Portfolio

**Status:** Pattern
**Domain:** Staffing operations
**Applies to:** `staffing-operations`

---

## Why this exists

Every traveler or per-diem clinician who rolls off an assignment and is immediately placed on another without returning to the candidate pool is a placement with near-zero sourcing cost, zero advertising spend, and a known credential and compliance record. In a healthcare or school-based staffing business, redeployment rate is a margin lever that competes directly with an expensive sourcing pipeline — but it is almost never reported as a KPI. Firms that ignore redeployment optimize for new placements at the cost of the cheapest placement in their portfolio.

## How to apply

Track and report redeployment rate as a separate KPI alongside fill rate and sourcing channel mix:

```
Redeployment Rate KPI
──────────────────────
Definition:  Clinicians who accepted a new assignment within [X] days of ending a prior
             assignment with the same agency ÷ total clinicians who completed an assignment
             in the period.

Typical redeployment window:  14–21 days (adjust by segment)

Period:  ________________
Segment:  [ ] Travel nursing  [ ] Allied  [ ] Per-diem  [ ] School-based  [ ] Locum

Total clinicians who completed an assignment:  ___
Redeployed within [X] days:                    ___
Redeployment rate:                             ___%

Benchmark:  25–40% is a common industry range for travel nursing [ESTIMATE — confirm against SIA data]

Cost comparison:
  Average sourcing cost per new placement:     $______
  Estimated sourcing cost per redeployment:    $______  (recruiter time only)
  Margin difference per redeploy vs. new:      $______
```

**Do:**
- Measure redeployment rate by segment — travel nursing redeployment economics differ from per-diem.
- Analyze the reasons for non-redeployment: did the clinician go to a competitor, leave the market, or simply not be contacted? Each has a different fix.
- Calculate the margin improvement from a 5-point redeployment-rate increase before proposing a sourcing-investment increase — the redeployment lever is usually cheaper.

**Don't:**
- Report redeployment rate without the window definition — a 7-day window and a 30-day window produce very different numbers from the same data.
- Treat redeployment as the responsibility of a single team; it requires recruiter, clinical-liaison, and operations coordination between assignment end and next assignment start.
- Omit redeployment from the margin bridge when explaining period-over-period margin changes.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

Per-diem pools with very short assignments (single-day or shift-by-shift fills) may define redeployment differently — as repeat usage of the same clinician within the period rather than a cross-assignment track. Adapt the definition to the segment's assignment structure.
