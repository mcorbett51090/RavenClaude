# Decompose margin into bill − pay − burden before calling it a pricing problem

**Status:** Absolute rule. **Constitution:** §3 #3, §4.

## Use when
Gross margin or spread is moving and someone proposes to reprice.

## The rule
A "margin is down" finding gets decomposed into **bill − pay − burden**, with the burden stack itemized, before anyone touches the price.

## Why
Margin compression *looks* like a pricing problem because the headline is a percentage. But it hides in the **burden line** far more often than in the markup: a rise in malpractice premiums (locums), per-diem/housing stipends (travel), workers' comp, or unbilled **bench/idle time** all compress margin without the bill rate changing. Reprice in those cases and you lose orders without fixing anything.

The four real drivers — bill-rate down, pay-rate up, a burden line up, or a portfolio **mix shift** to lower-margin segments — each have a different lever. Only one of them is "reprice."

## How to apply
- Write the identity and compute the **dollar bridge**, not just the %: a margin-% move can be a bill-rate (denominator) effect.
- Itemize every burden line ([`../knowledge/healthcare-staffing-economics.md`](../knowledge/healthcare-staffing-economics.md) §1).
- Attribute the move across the four drivers; usually one or two dominate.
- Anchor any "rate is low" claim to the dated SIA/NATHO series — an operator anchored to 2021 peak rates misreads a normalized rate as a crisis.
- Surface stipend/classification **compliance** levers but route the treatment to client tax/legal counsel — don't advise.
