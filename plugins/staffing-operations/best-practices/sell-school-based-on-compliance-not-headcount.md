# Sell school-based services on compliance, not headcount

**Status:** Pattern. **Constitution:** §3 #8.

## Use when
Positioning K-12 school-based staffing (special-ed, SLP/OT/PT, psych, BCBA, nursing) to a district.

## The rule
Frame the value as **IEP service-delivery compliance and Medicaid-revenue capture**, not bodies filled. Missed-session rate and IEP-minutes-delivered are the district's liability and revenue gates, not back-office metrics.

## Why
Under IDEA, related services are **legally mandated** per the IEP regardless of budget or staffing — demand is non-discretionary. A **pattern of missed sessions can constitute a denial of FAPE**, obligating the district to provide compensatory (make-up) services: a direct legal and financial exposure. Meanwhile expanded **Medicaid school-based billing** (BSCA 2022 + CMS 2023 guide; ~25 states expanded) means properly-documented contracted clinicians become *partially self-funding* for the district. A headcount pitch ("we'll fill your SLP vacancies") competes on price; a compliance pitch ("we'll close your FAPE exposure and protect your Medicaid claims") competes on risk and revenue — a far stronger position, especially under ESSER-cliff budget pressure that pushes districts toward variable-cost contract staffing anyway.

## How to apply
- Lead with **service-delivery compliance**: missed-session rate, make-up rate, IEP minutes delivered vs. contracted, documentation completeness (also the Medicaid gate).
- Position contract staffing as the **budget-flexible** answer to the ESSER cliff, not an added cost.
- Use the **rural-equity** gap as the teletherapy case (days-to-launch vs. months for onsite).
- Treat clearance/credentialing turnaround as a competitive differentiator. Keep all examples free of student PII (§3 #10).
