# Split supply from order-quality before blaming the recruiters

**Status:** Primary diagnostic. **Constitution:** §3 #4, §3 #6, §4.

## Use when
Fills or placements are down and the recruiting team is the suspected cause.

## The rule
Before attributing a fill miss to recruiter performance, split the cause into **supply** (no qualified candidates) vs. **order-quality / demand** (uncompetitive bill rates, aged or un-workable orders). They are identical in the fill-rate number and have nothing in common as fixes.

## Why
The most expensive wrong-first-pick in staffing is "fixing" recruiters who are actually **under-fed**. A recruiter working aged, on-hold, or below-market-bill orders produces low placements — but the cause is upstream (intake, pricing, sourcing), and reorganizing or PIP-ing the desk wastes the engagement while the real problem persists. Revenue-per-recruiter without a **reqs-per-recruiter** denominator is a vanity metric that manufactures false underperformers.

## How to apply
- Clean the denominator first: separate **nominal** orders from **fillable** orders (strip dead/on-hold/uncompetitive).
- Pull **submittals-per-workable-order** and the **order-aging distribution** — low submittals on healthy orders is supply; healthy submittals on uncompetitive orders is order-quality.
- Normalize any recruiter comparison for reqs carried and order difficulty (credentialing-heavy, rural).
- Only after supply, order-quality, seasonality, and credentialing are ruled out is "execution" on the table — and even then, lead with the specific funnel stage that's off.
