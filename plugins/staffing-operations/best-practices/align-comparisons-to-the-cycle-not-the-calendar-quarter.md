# Align comparisons to the cycle, not the calendar quarter

**Status:** Pattern. **Constitution:** §3 #5, §4.

## Use when
A fill-rate, time-to-fill, or volume comparison crosses a healthcare surge or the education spring-recruit/fall-start boundary.

## The rule
Compare **like-cycle-to-like-cycle** (YoY same-period or cycle-indexed), never calendar-quarter-to-calendar-quarter across a seasonal boundary.

## Why
Both segments are strongly seasonal, and a month/quarter comparison that straddles the boundary produces a confident false finding. Education is the sharp case: districts run a July 1 fiscal year and bulk-fill roles in late spring/summer for a fall start, so a **Q4 fill rate is *expected* to be low** — the hiring already happened in Q2–Q3. Read Q4-vs-Q3 and you'll "discover" a collapse that is just the calendar. Healthcare has its own surge/peak structure. A seasonal artifact dressed as a performance change sends the engagement chasing a problem that doesn't exist.

## How to apply
- Identify the governing cycle first (academic calendar / healthcare surge).
- Re-cut the comparison **YoY same-period**; if only one year exists, index each period to its share of the annual cycle and compare to that expected share.
- State explicitly which part of a move is **seasonal (expected)** and which is the **residual signal**: "fill fell 9 points QoQ, but 7 are the normal fall-to-winter school cycle; the 2-point YoY residual is the real story."
- Recommend the client set KPI windows against the recruiting cycle going forward (front-load spring/summer for education). See [`../knowledge/education-staffing-fundamentals.md`](../knowledge/education-staffing-fundamentals.md) §3.
