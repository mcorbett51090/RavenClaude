# Anchor market numbers to SIA, and date every figure

**Status:** Absolute rule. **Constitution:** §3 #9, §4.

## Use when
Any external market, competitor, or benchmark number enters a deliverable.

## The rule
Anchor staffing market sizing to **SIA's segment definitions** (industry-canonical), footnote broad-TAM research houses separately, and put a **source URL + retrieval date** on every external figure — marking advisory-blog numbers `[ESTIMATE]` and anything not read first-hand `[unverified]`.

## Why
Staffing benchmarks move fast and definitions vary wildly. The **2021–22 travel-nurse spike and its collapse** is the cautionary tale: travel-nurse bill rates ran ~$133/hr (2022) and ~$90/hr (2025) — a figure with no date can be off 30%+ and will be challenged by an operator who lives the market. Worse, broad-TAM houses (Grand View, Precedence) publish "healthcare staffing" TAMs several-fold larger than SIA's by sweeping in direct-hire and adjacencies; mix them into one chart and the analysis is incoherent. Most public operational "benchmarks" (fill 60–85%, margin 20–25%, submittal 3:1) are advisory-blog numbers, not audited statistics — presenting them as fact is a credibility risk.

## How to apply
- Lead sizing with SIA segment figures (travel ~$14.2B, allied ~$9.8B, locum ~$9.6B, per-diem ~$4.5B for 2025) and a date; footnote broad-TAM houses, never blend.
- **Triangulate** trend claims — a press release is not a trend; corroborate with a SEC filing where possible.
- Mark `[ESTIMATE]` / `[unverified]` and name the primary source that would close each gap (SIA editorial/BLS/Becker's often 403 to automated fetch — extracted figures stay `[unverified]` until re-opened).
- Name the **inflection**, not just the level ("−9% YoY but +6% sequential"). See [`../knowledge/staffing-market-trends-2026.md`](../knowledge/staffing-market-trends-2026.md).
