# Pair fill rate with time-to-fill — always

**Status:** Absolute rule. **Constitution:** §3 #2, §4.

## Use when
Any time fill rate is reported, in a scorecard, readout, or verbal finding.

## The rule
Never quote fill rate without time-to-fill, or time-to-fill without fill rate. They describe two different diseases that look like one number.

## Why
In staffing, **speed wins the placement** — when two agencies work the same order, the fast qualified submittal usually takes it. So a **95% fill rate at 45 days** can be *losing* to a competitor running **80% at 9 days**. The high-fill/slow-speed firm thinks it's winning (great fill!) while its placements quietly migrate to the faster shop. Fill rate alone hides this entirely.

The two failure modes have opposite fixes:
- **High fill, slow speed** → the problem is latency (intake, submittal turnaround, credentialing). Adding candidates won't help.
- **Low fill, fast speed** → the problem is supply or order-quality. Speeding up won't help.

Report them apart and you'll prescribe the wrong cure half the time.

## How to apply
- On a scorecard, place the two tiles **adjacent** so neither can be read alone.
- Measure time-to-fill to **start** (post-credentialing), not offer-accept (§3 #7).
- When stating a fill finding aloud, append the speed in the same sentence: "fill held at 78%, but time-to-fill slipped to 38 days."
- The plugin hook flags a fill-rate file with no time-to-fill mention.
