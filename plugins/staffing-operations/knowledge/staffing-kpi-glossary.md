# Staffing KPI glossary — definitions, formulas, pitfalls, benchmarks

> **Last reviewed:** 2026-06-04. The glossary the operations-analyst and funnel-strategist point to when a metric definition is contested or someone asks "which metric do I lead with?" Covers ~30 staffing KPIs across funnel, financial, quality/retention, productivity, and MSP/VMS. **Refresh when:** SIA/NATHO, Bullhorn GRID, ClearlyRated, or NSI publish a new annual benchmark, or a client's own data establishes a firmer internal baseline than the public ranges here.

**Confidence notation:** **High** = primary source / audited survey; **Medium** = single credible source or practitioner consensus; **`[ESTIMATE]`** = advisory-blog or vendor figure, directional only — *mark these `[ESTIMATE]` in any client deliverable; most public staffing "benchmarks" are advisory-blog numbers, not audited statistics.*

**The standing rule (constitution §3 #1):** every KPI ships with a **definition, a window, and a baseline**, or it doesn't ship. Many staffing arguments are really arguments about *which denominator* — name it first.

---

## A. Recruiting funnel

### 1. Fill rate
- **Definition.** Share of demand that gets filled. **At least four common denominators** — name which one: (a) orders filled ÷ orders *received*; (b) ÷ orders *workable* (excludes on-hold/dead/uncompetitive); (c) placements ÷ submittals-accepted; (d) ÷ orders open in the period.
- **Pitfall.** Comparing two periods (or two divisions) on different denominators. A "fill rate fell" finding is often a denominator shift (more dead orders inflating the base).
- **Benchmark.** Healthy ~**60–85%**; below ~70% signals sourcing gaps or weak intake; top MSP programs cite ~**98%**. `[ESTIMATE]`
- **Always pair with time-to-fill** (§3 #2).

### 2. Time-to-fill
- **Definition.** Calendar days from req opening to candidate acceptance. **In healthcare/school-based work, measure to *start* (post-credentialing), not just accept** (§3 #7) — see time-to-start.
- **Benchmark.** ~**30–60 days** typical; ~45 days "competitive" for perm; ~4–5 days median light-industrial. AI-enabled firms ~90% more likely to place within 20 days. `[ESTIMATE]`

### 3. Time-to-submit / time-to-present
- **Definition.** Days (often hours) from req receipt to first qualified candidate presented. The MSP/VMS scorecard metric; high-velocity healthcare desks run <24–48 hrs. No clean public benchmark — treat as internal SLA. `[ESTIMATE]`

### 4. Submittal-to-interview ratio
- **Definition.** Submittals required per interview granted. Lower = higher submission quality / better client fit. Benchmark ~**3:1**. `[ESTIMATE]`

### 5. Submittal-to-hire ratio
- **Definition.** Submittals per placement. "Sweet spot" ~**3–4:1** (≈40% of NA firms); high performers **1–2:1** (≈14%). `[ESTIMATE]`

### 6. Interview-to-offer & offer-acceptance rate
- **Definition.** Offers per interview; % of offers accepted. No single hard benchmark; low acceptance signals process friction or an uncompetitive package. Track as trend, pair with offer-to-accept latency (speed loses placements).

### 7. Requisition aging
- **Definition.** Days an order stays open/unfilled; the distribution matters more than the mean. A leading indicator of fill risk and a denominator-cleaner: aged, on-hold, uncompetitive orders should be split out of "workable" demand before any fill-rate or capacity read.

## B. Financial

### 8. Gross margin / spread
- **Definition.** (Revenue − direct labor cost) ÷ revenue; the gap between bill and pay rate. **Decompose into bill − pay − burden** before calling a margin problem a pricing problem (§3 #3).
- **Benchmark.** Temp staffing typically **~20–25%**; healthcare travel margins compressed 2023–25. `[ESTIMATE]`

### 9. Bill rate / 10. Pay rate / 11. Markup
- **Bill rate** = pay rate + markup (the hourly rate billed to the client). **Pay rate** = rate paid to the worker (for travelers: taxable wage + non-taxable stipends). **Markup** = bill ÷ pay as a % uplift; temp/contract **20–75%**, perm placement fee **10–20%**, profitable multiplier typically **1.45–1.75×** pay. `[ESTIMATE]` Travel-nurse aggregate bill ~**$90/hr** in 2024–25 (SIA/NATHO).

### 12. Conversion ratio
- **Definition.** Often gross profit ÷ revenue, or temp-to-perm conversions. Gauges desk efficiency; no universal figure — define which you mean.

### 13. Revenue per recruiter / 14. Revenue per placement
- **Definition.** Total revenue ÷ recruiter headcount; revenue ÷ placements. **Revenue-per-recruiter without a reqs-per-recruiter denominator is a vanity metric** (§3 #4) — an under-fed recruiter looks like a low performer.

### 15. DSO (days sales outstanding)
- **Definition.** Average days to collect after billing. "Good" is **<45 days**; cutting 10 days of DSO frees roughly $1.37M working capital on $50M revenue. `[ESTIMATE]` A cash-flow lever distinct from margin.

## C. Quality / retention

### 16. Redeployment rate
- **Definition.** % of contractors placed on a new assignment after one ends. **The cheapest placement you'll make** — lowers re-source cost. Strong NPS correlates with higher redeployment (ClearlyRated). Internal targets often ~50%+. `[ESTIMATE]`

### 17. Contractor / traveler retention & 18. Assignment completion rate
- **Definition.** % retained across assignments; % of assignments completed without early termination. Under-tracked, high-value; pair with extension rate.

### 19. Fall-off rate
- **Definition.** % of placements that cancel before or just after start. In healthcare/school-based work, **credentialing/clearance fallout is the dominant cause** (accept→start stage). Collected in the SIA/NATHO travel-nurse benchmarking survey alongside margin and hours billed.

### 20. Extension rate
- **Definition.** % of assignments extended rather than ended. A leading indicator of client satisfaction *and* margin (no re-source cost).

### 21. Candidate NPS (talent) & 22. Client NPS
- **Definition.** 0–10 likelihood-to-recommend, talent-side and client-side. Industry benchmark **~30% (talent)** and **~36% (client)**; ClearlyRated Best-of-Staffing winners average **~73%** and **~77%**. (Global rule of thumb: 50% excellent, 70%+ world-class.) **Confidence: Medium** (ClearlyRated). A NPS without the follow-up "what would make it a 10?" is wasted.

## D. Productivity

### 23. Recruiter productivity (leading vs. lagging)
- **Leading:** job orders worked, candidates sourced/screened, submittals, client meetings, contractor check-ins (effort, not output). **Lagging:** placements, billings, placement rate, revenue/working-day. Forecast with leading, judge with lagging.

### 24. Hires per recruiter
- **Benchmark.** Bottomed at **~4.5/quarter** (early 2023), recovered to **~7.3/quarter** (Q1 2026) per Recruiterflow. `[ESTIMATE]`

### 25. Billable FTE / utilization
- **Definition.** Average field contract personnel (FTE) on assignment — the public-company proxy for volume. E.g., Cross Country averaged **7,035 FTE in 2025** vs. 8,415 prior year, showing volume (not just rate) drove the contraction. **Confidence: High** (SEC filings).

### 26. AI productivity uplift (context, not a KPI)
- Bullhorn GRID 2026: AI/automation can save up to **~17 hrs/recruiter/week** (4.5 search/match + 3.6 screening/admin) against ~14.6 hrs/week spent sourcing; top firms 4× more likely to use AI, 2× more likely to have grown revenue. Relevant when "hire more recruiters" might be "unblock the ones you have." `[ESTIMATE]`

## E. MSP / VMS

### 27. Tier-1 fill rate / vendor tiering
- Vendor tiers (T1/T2/T3) are driven by fill rate, time-to-fill, and candidate quality; tier-1 fill rate measures how much volume top-tier vendors absorb first.

### 28. Time-to-present vs. 29. Time-to-start
- **Time-to-present** = vendor submits a qualified candidate. **Time-to-start** = candidate begins work *after* credentialing/onboarding. Tracked separately from time-to-fill; the gap between them is the credentialing clock (§3 #7).

### 30. Candidate quality / vendor scorecard
- Composite of submittal-to-interview, fall-off, completion, and client satisfaction per vendor. Reference MSP outcome (one program): 98% fill, 20% faster time-to-fill, 12% lower cost, 27% time savings. `[ESTIMATE]`
