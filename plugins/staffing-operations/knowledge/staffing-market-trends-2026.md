## 2. The trends (2023–2026)

1. **Healthcare normalization & bottoming.** Travel-nurse bill rates fell **~$133→$90/hr (2022→2025)**; total healthcare staffing declined ~24% (2023) and kept falling through 2024–25; SIA calls **2025 the bottom**, modest 2026 improvement. Public confirmation: **AMN FY2025 revenue $2.730B (−8%)**, Nurse & Allied $1.647B (−9%), with travel-nurse YoY declines narrowing each quarter (−36% Q1 → −9% Q4, +6% sequential in Q4 — an inflection signal). **Cross Country FY2025 Nurse & Allied $862.8M (−25%)**, Physician $191.5M (−4%). *(SEC filings — High confidence.)*
2. **Locum tenens is the consistent growth segment.** ~+6% in 2025; physician shortages + APP reliance; ~80–81% of organizations maintained or increased locum use; utilization ran ~25% above plan; locum APP revenue +23.8% YoY in H1 2024.
3. **MSP/VMS consolidation, internal float pools, direct sourcing.** Hospitals built internal/float pools and direct-sourcing to cut agency reliance, compressing agency margins; vendor-neutral VMS is a stated 2025–26 trend. In locums, MSPs = ~38% of AP staffing revenue, standalone VMS ~9%. `[unverified]`
4. **AI in recruiting is the differentiator.** Bullhorn GRID 2026: AI-using firms **2× more likely to grow revenue**, 90% more likely to place <20 days, up to **~17 hrs/recruiter/week** saved.
5. **Gig / per-diem platforms.** ShiftKey, Clipboard Health, CareRev, ShiftMed, IntelyCare raised cumulatively >$850M–$1.4B; platforms now **>20%** of US temp-staffing revenue. Rising regulatory friction — **NY in 2025 reclassified gig nursing platforms as staffing agencies**, prompting several to exit the state.
6. **Demand drivers / regulatory.** Aging population; clinician burnout; ~33% of nurses nearing retirement (AMN 2025 RN survey); HRSA physician-shortage forecasts through 2037. Headwinds: agency-spend scrutiny / rate caps, gig-classification laws, credentialing mandates.
7. **Education: the ESSER cliff** (obligated Sept 30 2024; spend extensions to Mar 30 2026) pushes districts from FTEs toward **variable-cost contract staffing**; structural SpEd/SLP/OT/psych/BCBA shortages; teletherapy goes structural; **Medicaid school-based billing expansion** (BSCA 2022 + CMS 2023 guide, ~25 states expanded) is a tailwind. Detail in [`education-staffing-fundamentals.md`](education-staffing-fundamentals.md).

## 3. How to turn a trend into a client-defensible claim

1. **Name the segment** — "healthcare staffing is down" is false for locums. Always specify.
2. **Anchor to SIA + a date**, mark advisory-blog numbers `[ESTIMATE]`.
3. **Triangulate** — a single press release is not a trend (§4). Confirm with a second primary source (a SEC filing beats a vendor blog).
4. **State the inflection, not just the level** — "−9% YoY but +6% sequential" tells the operator the bottom is in; "−9%" alone doesn't.
