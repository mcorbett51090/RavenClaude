# Credentialing & compliance — the part of time-to-fill nobody puts on the scorecard

> **Last reviewed:** 2026-06-04. Reference for the `healthcare-staffing-specialist` and `education-staffing-specialist`. Treats credentialing/clearance as a **time-to-fill component** (§3 #7) and compliance as **the product** in these two segments (§3 #8), not back-office hygiene. **Refresh when:** Joint Commission updates Health Care Staffing Services standards, or CMS changes school-based Medicaid billing rules.

---

## Why this is an operations metric, not back-office

A 9-day submit-to-offer that then sits 21 days in credentialing is a **30-day fill**. If the scorecard's time-to-fill stops at offer-accept, it is hiding a third of the clock and mis-attributing the delay to sales when it lives in compliance. **Measure time-to-start, and track the credentialing/clearance interval as its own KPI** (§3 #7). The accept→start stage is also where most **fall-off** happens — a candidate who accepts but can't clear is a failed placement and a re-source cost.

---

## 1. Healthcare credentialing

- **End-to-end provider credentialing commonly runs 90–120 days** (range 60–180). Best-practice targets: document/compliance completion **>98%**, urgent primary-source-verification (PSV) response **<1 business day**, electronic-application adoption **>80%**, automated-verification completion **>60%**. Credentialing teams spend **60–70%** of their time on manual follow-up; delays create ~90-day revenue gaps. `[ESTIMATE — Verisys/Medwave/Atlas advisory sources]`
- **Joint Commission "Health Care Staffing Services" certification** is both a client-facing trust signal and a documentation regime (the agency must show a defined process for verifying competence, licensure, and ongoing performance). **Soliant has held it since 2011.** A Joint-Commission-certified firm carries audit obligations that themselves consume credentialing time — relevant when benchmarking turnaround.
- **Document set (typical healthcare):** active state license per practice state, certifications (e.g., ARRT for imaging), current BCLS/CPR, physical + immunization/titer records, background check, recent-experience verification, professional references (often automated, e.g., RefAssured).
- **Per-traveler compliance/document-completion %** is a hard gate before assignment start; it drives time-to-start directly.

## 2. School-based clearance

- Background checks (often state-specific fingerprint clearance), district onboarding, and license/certification verification per state are a real time-to-fill component for school placements — treat like the healthcare credentialing clock. No clean public benchmark for turnaround exists `[unverified — internal-metric-to-define]`; track it as a competitive differentiator.

## 3. IDEA / IEP service-delivery compliance — the compliance-as-product lever

This is the education-segment equivalent of credentialing as a first-class metric, and it has **legal teeth**:

- **IEP minutes delivered / service-delivery compliance.** Under IDEA, related services (SLP, OT, PT, counseling, psych) must be delivered per the IEP. A **pattern of missed sessions causing lost progress can constitute a denial of FAPE**, and districts are generally required to provide **compensatory (make-up) services** for therapist/school-caused misses (IEP Says; Wrightslaw). This converts **missed-session rate** and **make-up rate** from ops metrics into **direct district liability** — which is the sales lever: sell on compliance, not headcount.
- **Service documentation / logs.** Districts are advised to require service logs and provider session notes for every IEP service (WI DPI) — the substrate for both compliance and Medicaid billing.
- **Medicaid school-based billing (a tailwind).** The 2022 **Bipartisan Safer Communities Act** + the May 2023 CMS school-based-services guide eased school Medicaid claiming; BSCA funded ~$50M in grants. As of late 2023, **~25 states** expanded school Medicaid beyond IEP/IFSP (the "free care" reversal). **Documentation completeness gates reimbursement**, so contracted clinicians become *partially self-funding* for districts — a strong consulting angle. **Watch:** a later proposed simplification rule was **withdrawn** (K-12 Dive), so the regime is in flux.

### KPIs to track here (mostly internal-to-define — no public benchmark)
- Missed-session rate; make-up/compensatory-services rate
- IEP minutes delivered vs. contracted
- Service-documentation completeness % (also the Medicaid-billing gate)
- Days-of-coverage delivered vs. contracted
- Clearance/credentialing turnaround

Flag each as **internal-metric-to-define** rather than inventing a benchmark (§3 #9).
