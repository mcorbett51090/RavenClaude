# Every flag has a kill switch and a lifecycle

Build every risky change behind an ops flag that can disable it instantly without a deploy, and give every temporary flag an owner and a removal date. An unmanaged flag estate becomes a combinatorial configuration space nobody can reason about and a recurring source of incidents, while a missing kill switch turns a bad release into an emergency deploy. Flags are powerful precisely because they are reversible — keep them that way and clean them up.
