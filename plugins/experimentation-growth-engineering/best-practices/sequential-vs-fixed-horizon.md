# Choose sequential or fixed-horizon up front — and don't mix them

There are two valid ways to run a test: fix the sample size and duration in advance and look only once at the end, or use a sequential method (group-sequential, always-valid p-values) explicitly designed to let you monitor continuously and stop early. What you cannot do is run a fixed-horizon test and peek at it daily, because checking a fixed-horizon test repeatedly is exactly the false-positive inflation that peeking causes. The apparatus enforces the choice you make with applied-statistics: a fixed-horizon test hides the readout until the pre-registered end; a sequential test exposes a valid stopping boundary. Pick the method by whether early stopping is worth its lower power, then build the tooling to make the wrong way impossible rather than merely discouraged.
