# Analyze on exposure, not assignment — and log it at the point of effect

A user assigned to a variant who never reached the changed surface dilutes your effect with pure noise, so the population you analyze is those actually exposed to the treatment, and exposure is logged at the moment the variant's behavior could have differed — not at page load, not at session start. Get this wrong and you measure a real effect spread thin across people it never touched, hiding wins and wasting power. The logging point is a design decision, not an afterthought: it must fire deterministically, once, at the divergence point, and carry the assignment so the analysis can intersect them. 'Did we assign them?' is the wrong question for the readout; 'were they exposed to the thing that changed?' is the right one.
