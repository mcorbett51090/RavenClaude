## Funder & grant types (know what you're actually pursuing)

- **By funder:** **federal** (heaviest compliance; direct or pass-through), **state/local** (often pass-through federal — the compliance flows down), **foundation** (funder-defined rules, LOI-first common), **corporate/sponsorship** (lightest, alignment + visibility).
- **By restriction:** **project** (restricted to a defined program), **general operating** (flexible, rarer, prized), **capacity-building** (invest in the org itself).
- **By mechanism:** **competitive/discretionary** (win on a rubric) vs **formula/block** (eligibility-based allocation — a compliance contest more than a writing one).

The type sets the compliance weight *before* you write a word — federal project money and a corporate sponsorship are not the same commitment.

## The program-design spine (the narrative must chain)

A fundable proposal chains, each link justifying the next:

1. **Needs statement** — evidence of the problem for *this* population in *this* place (data, not adjectives). The reviewer must believe the need is real and that you understand it.
2. **Logic model / theory of change** — **inputs → activities → outputs → outcomes → impact**. The theory of change adds the *why*: the causal assumptions linking activities to outcomes. This is the spine everything else hangs on.
3. **Goals & SMART objectives** — Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound. Objectives are the measurable promises; vague objectives ("improve outcomes") are unscoreable and unaccountable.
4. **Methods / activities** — how the objectives get met; each activity traces back to the logic model and forward to a budget line.
5. **Evaluation plan** — how you'll *know* it worked: the indicators, data sources, collection cadence, and who analyzes it. Formative (improve as you go) + summative (did it work). A proposal that promises outcomes with no evaluation plan is not credible — and the plan becomes a binding post-award obligation.
6. **Budget & budget narrative** — every line justified in prose and tied to an activity; every activity funded. **The single most common reviewer red flag is a budget that doesn't mirror the narrative.**

> **The rule:** if a reviewer can't trace need → activity → objective → evaluation → budget line as one chain, the proposal reads as a wish list. Chain it or lose points.

## US-federal mechanics (the plumbing every federal applicant must run)

- **SAM.gov registration + UEI.** You cannot receive federal funds without an active **SAM.gov** registration and a **Unique Entity ID (UEI)** (which replaced the DUNS number). Registration renews annually — a lapsed registration blocks a submission at the worst moment. _(Retrieved 2026-07-09 — verify current renewal/UEI process at SAM.gov.)_
- **Grants.gov + Workspace.** Federal discretionary opportunities post on **Grants.gov**; applications are built and submitted through **Workspace** (multi-user, form-by-form). Find the NOFO/Assistance Listing, download the package, complete the forms, submit early (portal congestion at deadline is real). _(Retrieved 2026-07-09.)_
- **The SF-424 family.** The standard federal application forms: **SF-424** (Application for Federal Assistance — the cover), plus program-specific variants (SF-424A budget for non-construction, SF-424B assurances, etc.). The NOFO lists which forms its package requires. _(Retrieved 2026-07-09 — form versions change; use the package's forms.)_
- **The NOFO/RFP is the rubric.** Read the review criteria and their point weights; answer each in the funder's order and language; build a crosswalk so nothing scored is unaddressed.

## 2 CFR 200 Uniform Guidance — cost principles & indirect rates

The **Uniform Guidance** (2 CFR Part 200) governs federal awards. The core post-award tests:

### The three-part cost test (Subpart E)
Every federal cost must be **all three**:
- **Allowable** — permitted by the cost principles *and* the award's terms (some costs are expressly unallowable — e.g. certain entertainment, lobbying).
- **Allocable** — benefits the award in proportion to the amount charged; you can't dump an unrelated cost on a convenient grant.
- **Reasonable** — a prudent person would incur it under the circumstances; the price and the necessity both hold up.

"We had budget for it" is **not** the test. Document the rationale per charge — that documentation is what survives an audit.

### Indirect cost rates
Indirect (F&A) costs — the shared costs (admin, facilities) not directly attributable to one award — are recovered via an **indirect rate**:
- **NICRA (Negotiated Indirect Cost Rate Agreement)** — a rate negotiated with your **cognizant federal agency**. If you have one, you use it.
- **De minimis rate** — if you've never had a NICRA, you may elect the **de minimis** rate applied to **Modified Total Direct Costs (MTDC)**. This rate was historically **10%**; recent Uniform Guidance revisions **raised the de minimis ceiling (widely reported as up to 15%)** — **verify the current rate and effective date against eCFR before quoting.** _(Retrieved 2026-07-09 — de minimis figure is volatile.)_
- **MTDC base** — direct costs *minus* certain exclusions (e.g. equipment, capital expenditures, the portion of each subaward over a threshold). Apply the rate to the *right* base or you over/under-recover.

### Other Subpart D obligations
- **Time & effort / personnel activity reporting** — payroll charged to a federal award must be supported by records reflecting **actual** work, certified after the fact. **The most-cited audit finding area** — reconstructing effort from memory is a finding.
- **Procurement standards** (2 CFR 200.317–.327) — competition, documentation, and cost/price analysis for purchases (contractor relationships).
- **Period of performance** — costs must be incurred within the award's start/end dates; watch pre-award costs and no-cost extensions.
- **Drawdowns** — draw funds per the award's method (advance vs reimbursement), track against the period of performance, and don't draw ahead of need.

> **Volatile:** the de minimis rate, MTDC exclusion thresholds, specific section numbers, and unallowable-cost lists have moved across Uniform Guidance revisions. Treat the figures here as a 2026-07 snapshot and **re-verify against eCFR.** **Not accounting advice — a CPA/auditor owns the final call.**

## Reporting — financial + performance

| Report | What | Cadence |
|---|---|---|
| **SF-425 (FFR)** | Federal Financial Report — expenditures, obligations, indirect, cost share vs the award | Per award terms (quarterly / semi-annual / annual + final) |
| **RPPR** | Research Performance Progress Report — the standard performance report for research awards (accomplishments, participants, products, plans) | Typically annual + final |
| **SF-PPR** | Performance Progress Report — the general (non-research) performance report | Per award terms |
| **Program-specific** | Some programs have their own reporting portals/forms | Per NOFO |

Report **from the compliance tracker**, on the calendar, figures traced to the ledger. **A missed report jeopardizes the current draw and the next award.**

## Subrecipient monitoring (once you've made the 200.331 call)

If a partner is a **subrecipient** (carries out the program, subject to compliance — see the decision-tree doc for the five-characteristic test), you **must monitor** (2 CFR 200.332):
- **Risk assessment** of the subrecipient before and during the subaward.
- **Include required terms** in the subaward (the flow-down of federal requirements).
- **Review their financial + performance reports**; verify they had the required **Single Audit** if applicable; follow up on **findings**.
- **Document** the monitoring — an unmonitored subaward is a pass-through-entity finding, and the finding lands on *you*.

A **contractor** instead triggers **procurement standards**, not monitoring. Substance decides which — not the title on the agreement.

## Single Audit (2 CFR 200 Subpart F)

- A non-federal entity that **expends ≥ the threshold** in federal awards in its fiscal year must have a **Single Audit** (an org-wide audit of federal awards). The threshold was long **$750,000** and a recent Uniform Guidance revision **raised it (widely reported to $1,000,000)** — **verify the current threshold and effective date against eCFR before relying on it.** _(Retrieved 2026-07-09 — threshold is volatile.)_
- Below the threshold → no Single Audit, but keep the documentation trail regardless.
- Readiness is **continuous**: the trail (allowability rationale, time & effort certs, procurement docs, subrecipient monitoring) is built as you go, not reconstructed at audit time.

---

## Closeout (the last gate)

As the period of performance ends, run the **closeout checklist**: **final FFR**, **final performance report**, **equipment/property disposition** per the award, **records retention** (commonly 3 years from final report — **verify per award**), and reconciliation of drawdowns to expenditures. A clean closeout protects the relationship and the next award.
