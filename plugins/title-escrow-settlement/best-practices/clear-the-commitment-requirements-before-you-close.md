# Clear the commitment requirements before you close

**Status:** Absolute rule
**Domain:** Title / commitment / curative
**Applies to:** `title-escrow-settlement`

> Advisory operations rule, not legal or title-underwriting advice. Underwriter guidelines are `[verify-at-use]`. No PII.

---

## Why this exists

A title commitment promises coverage **only if** the Schedule B-I requirements are satisfied. Closing on open requirements — an unreleased mortgage, an unresolved judgment, a chain gap — means disbursing and recording against a title that is not yet insurable. That is how a settlement operation turns a clerical shortcut into a title claim, a rescinded closing, or a loss the underwriter will not cover.

## How to apply

- **Drive open requirements to zero before setting the closing.** Every B-I requirement is cured, released, or has documented underwriter approval to insure over.
- **Separate requirements from exceptions.** B-I requirements *must* be satisfied; B-II exceptions are carved out of coverage and disclosed — don't confuse "excepted" (fine) with "ignored" (not fine).
- **Get insure-over approval in writing, first.** Off-guideline coverage without documented underwriter approval is unauthorized risk (see the exception decision tree).
- **Confirm insurability with the examiner** before the closer sets the table — the clear-to-close is a title event, not just a lender event.

**Do:** track requirement clearance as a hard gate; escalate anything off-guideline to the underwriter.
**Don't:** close "subject to" an open requirement you plan to clean up after; assume a prior policy cleared it.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

Some requirements are satisfied *at* closing (payoff of a lien from closing proceeds, recording the release) — those are sequenced into the disbursement, not skipped. The rule is that nothing is left *unaddressed*, not that everything records before the signing.
