## 1. IIA Global Internal Audit Standards (2024) — the conformance frame

> **Last reviewed: 2026-07-14 · verify at use.** The IIA's **Global Internal Audit Standards** were released in **2024** and became **effective January 2025**, superseding the prior International Professional Practices Framework (IPPF) *International Standards for the Professional Practice of Internal Auditing*. They are organized into **5 domains** containing **15 principles** (each principle carries requirements, considerations for implementation, and examples of evidence of conformance). Confirm the current version, effective date, and any amendments at use.

The **5 domains** (high level):

| # | Domain | What it governs (high level) |
|---|---|---|
| I | **Purpose of Internal Auditing** | Why internal audit exists — enhancing the organization's value, governance, risk management, and control through independent, objective assurance and advisory services. |
| II | **Ethics & Professionalism** | The professional foundation — integrity, objectivity, competency, due professional care, confidentiality (the ethics principles the profession is built on). |
| III | **Governing the Internal Audit Function** | The **board / audit committee's** role — authorizing the mandate, the internal audit charter, independence, and the CAE's positioning and reporting line. |
| IV | **Managing the Internal Audit Function** | The **CAE's** role — strategy, the risk-based **plan**, resources, communicating results, and the **QAIP**. |
| V | **Performing Internal Audit Services** | The **engagement** work — planning, gathering evidence, evaluating, communicating, and monitoring the resolution of issues. |

> The **15 principles** distribute across these domains (roughly: II →1–4 ethics principles; III → governing/mandate/charter/independence principles; IV → CAE strategy/planning/resourcing/QAIP principles; V → engagement-execution principles). **Verify the exact principle numbering and text at use — do not quote a specific principle from memory in a deliverable.**

**Independence & objectivity (from the Standards):** the CAE reports **functionally to the board / audit committee** and **administratively to senior management** — the dual line that protects independence. Internal audit provides assurance and advisory services but **does not assume management responsibility, own controls, or make management decisions.** Where independence or objectivity is impaired, it must be disclosed.

**Conformance statement:** engagements and the function may claim to be "conducted in conformance with the Global Internal Audit Standards" **only when substantiated by the QAIP** (below). Non-conformance that affects the overall scope or operation is disclosed to the board and senior management.

## 2. COSO Internal Control – Integrated Framework — "is this a control, and is it effective?"

> **Last reviewed: 2026-07-14 · verify at use.** The COSO **Internal Control – Integrated Framework** defines internal control across **5 components** and **17 principles**. It's the reference the risk & control matrix leans on for classifying and evaluating controls (heavily used for SOX ICFR).

The **5 components**:

1. **Control Environment** — the tone, integrity, ethical values, governance, and structure that set the foundation (entity-level).
2. **Risk Assessment** — identifying and analyzing risks to objectives, including fraud risk and change.
3. **Control Activities** — the policies and procedures (preventive/detective, manual/automated) that mitigate risk.
4. **Information & Communication** — capturing and sharing the information needed to run controls, up/down/across.
5. **Monitoring Activities** — ongoing and separate evaluations that confirm the components are present and functioning.

An effective system of internal control requires **all five components present and functioning, operating together.**

## 3. COSO ERM — enterprise-risk framing

> **Last reviewed: 2026-07-14 · verify at use.** COSO's **Enterprise Risk Management – Integrating with Strategy and Performance** frames risk in the context of strategy and performance (its components span governance & culture; strategy & objective-setting; performance; review & revision; and information, communication & reporting). Internal audit uses it to frame the **audit-universe risk assessment** — the org's risk appetite and profile that drive what's material to audit.

## 4. The Three Lines Model (2020) — where internal audit sits

> **Last reviewed: 2026-07-14 · verify at use.** The IIA's **Three Lines Model** (2020 revision of the older "Three Lines of Defense") clarifies roles:

| Role | Who | Responsibility |
|---|---|---|
| **First line** | Management / operational roles | **Owns and manages** risk — runs the controls in day-to-day operations. |
| **Second line** | Risk, compliance, quality, control functions | **Oversight & expertise** — helps build and monitor first-line controls; does *not* provide independent assurance. |
| **Third line** | **Internal audit** | **Independent, objective assurance** to the governing body on the effectiveness of governance, risk management, and control. |
| **Governing body** | Board / audit committee | Accountability to stakeholders; oversees all three lines. |

**The line internal audit must not cross:** collapsing into first- or second-line **control ownership** forfeits the independence that is IA's entire value. Advisory work is allowed; owning the remediation is not.

## 5. The engagement lifecycle (the durable methodology — High confidence)

```
Audit universe → risk-rank (residual) → ANNUAL PLAN (board-approved)
   → ENGAGEMENT: planning memo (objectives · scope · criteria · approach · timing)
      → risk & control matrix (risk → control → type → tests)
      → walkthrough (confirm design)
      → test of DESIGN + test of OPERATING EFFECTIVENESS (sampling)
      → workpapers (sufficient · relevant · reliable evidence + review trail)
      → FINDINGS (5 C's) + rating (impact × likelihood)
      → management action plans (owner · date)  ← IA facilitates, does NOT own
      → REPORT + audit-committee summary (state of control, residual risk)
   → FOLLOW-UP: re-test remediation before close
→ QAIP (internal ongoing + periodic; external quality assessment every 5 years)
```

**The 5 C's of a finding** (durable):

| C | Question | Common failure |
|---|---|---|
| **Criteria** | What *should* be (policy/reg/framework/standard)? | Missing → it's an observation, not a finding |
| **Condition** | What *is* (the tested result)? | Stated without the sample evidence |
| **Cause** | *Why* did it happen (root, not symptom)? | Symptom mistaken for root cause → fix doesn't stick |
| **Consequence** | *So what* — the risk/exposure? | Not quantified → rating and urgency don't land |
| **Corrective action** | Management's agreed fix (owner + date) | IA authors it → independence lost |

**Evidence bar:** sufficient (enough), relevant (bears on the objective), and reliable (source quality). Reliability ranking (strongest → weakest): **re-performance / physical inspection · third-party externally-sourced · system-generated with reliable ITGCs · internally-generated documents · inquiry / observation alone.** Corroborate inquiry; never conclude on inquiry alone.

**Sampling for control tests (attribute sampling):** the sample size is driven by the **population size**, the **control frequency** (annual → many-per-day), and the **tolerable vs expected deviation** rate. Illustrative frequency-based baselines many shops use for a **key** control with low tolerable deviation: annual → 1, quarterly → 2, monthly → 2–5, weekly → 5–15, daily → 20–40, many-per-day → 25–60. **These are illustrative, not authoritative — verify against your firm's sampling methodology / a current sampling table at use.** A reliable **automated** control (with sound ITGCs) can be concluded on a **single well-tested instance** plus a small confirmatory sample.

## 6. Common audit programs (starting points — tailor to the entity)

| Program | Key risks | Representative key controls to test |
|---|---|---|
| **Procure-to-pay (P2P)** | Unauthorized/duplicate/inaccurate disbursement; SoD | Three-way match; PO approval / delegation of authority; vendor master change control; SoD between requisition, approval, and payment |
| **Order-to-cash / revenue** | Revenue recognition; unauthorized credits; cutoff | Credit approval; revenue-recognition review; billing accuracy; cutoff controls; AR reconciliation |
| **Payroll** | Ghost employees; unauthorized rate/pay changes | New-hire/termination controls; pay-change approval; payroll reconciliation; SoD between HR and payroll |
| **IT general controls (ITGC)** | Unauthorized access; unauthorized change; availability | Access provisioning/de-provisioning + periodic review; change management; SDLC; backup/recovery (these *gate* reliance on automated app controls) |
| **Financial close** | Misstatement; unsupported entries | Account reconciliations; journal-entry review/approval; management review controls |
| **Third-party / vendor risk** | Concentration; SoC-report gaps; contract non-compliance | Due-diligence; SOC 1/2 review; contract-compliance monitoring |
| **Entity-level / fraud** | Tone at the top; override of controls | Code of conduct; whistleblower program; fraud-risk assessment; management-override monitoring |

> Deep **security-control** testing (ISO 27001, NIST 800-53) is `cybersecurity-grc`; **AML/financial-regulatory** testing is `regulatory-compliance` — IA coordinates with and may rely on them rather than duplicating.

## 7. QAIP & external quality assessment

> **Last reviewed: 2026-07-14 · verify at use.** The **Quality Assurance & Improvement Program (QAIP)** substantiates the conformance claim and covers:
> - **Internal assessments** — *ongoing* monitoring (embedded in supervision, engagement review, metrics) **and** *periodic* self-assessments.
> - **External assessments** — an **external quality assessment (EQA)** by a qualified, independent assessor **at least once every five years** (a full external assessment, or a self-assessment with independent external validation). **Confirm the exact cadence and options at use.**

The QAIP is what lets the CAE report the function's conformance with the Global Internal Audit Standards to the board and senior management.

## 8. Function KPIs / health metrics (illustrative — tie to your mandate)

| Metric | What it signals |
|---|---|
| **Plan completion %** | Did the risk-based plan get delivered? |
| **Risk coverage %** | Share of high-residual-risk universe audited within its cycle |
| **Findings by rating (High/Med/Low) + trend** | The state of control and its direction |
| **% findings agreed by management** | Quality/credibility of findings |
| **Remediation on-time % / overdue past-due count** | Whether issues actually close |
| **Repeat findings** | Root-cause quality; whether fixes stick |
| **Time-to-report (fieldwork end → report)** | Engagement efficiency |
| **Stakeholder satisfaction; QAIP conformance** | Value and standards conformance |

> KPIs are a **management-information** aid, not a substitute for the residual-risk narrative the audit committee needs.

## 9. Seams (what leaves the general internal-audit function)

- **Security-control assurance** (ISO 27001, NIST, SOC 2, technical control testing) → `cybersecurity-grc`.
- **AML / sanctions / financial-regulatory** obligations → `regulatory-compliance`. **SOX ICFR** overlaps external audit and finance — coordinate scoping, don't duplicate.
- **ESG / sustainability assurance** → `esg-sustainability-reporting`.
- **Process redesign** (improving the audited process, not assuring it) → `process-improvement`.
- **Program/RAID management** for a multi-week audit or QAIP remediation → `ravenclaude-core/project-manager`.
