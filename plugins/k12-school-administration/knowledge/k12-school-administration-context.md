## Where defensible benchmarks come from

Per-pupil funding rates, chronic-absence thresholds, student:teacher ratio targets, and proficiency benchmarks are **state-, year-, and definition-specific** and vary by funding model (ADA vs enrollment-based). Common reference sources practitioners cite require a dated cut: state education agency reports, the district's own SIS/finance data (the most defensible source), and federal/state assessment results. **Name the source and date, or mark the figure `[unverified — training knowledge]` (§3 #8).**

## Operating rhythm

- **Enrollment / attendance review** of the pipeline, ADA, and chronic-absence flags (§3 #1 #2 #5).
- **Budget / staffing review** fitting ratios and per-pupil allocation to the envelope (§3 #3 #4).
- **Outcomes review** read segmented with the attendance link (§3 #2 #6).
- **Retention review** of teacher turnover as a budget + outcome lever (§3 #7).

## The standing caution

Special-education and IEP determinations, FERPA student-privacy compliance, student-discipline rights, and employment/bargaining-unit law are **counsel's** call — the team frames the operating decision and routes it. Student PII / education records (names, IDs, grades, IEP, attendance) are FERPA-protected and stay out of deliverables (§2).
