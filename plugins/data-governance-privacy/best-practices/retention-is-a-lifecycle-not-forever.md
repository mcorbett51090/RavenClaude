# Data has a lifespan; automate its end

Every category of personal data should have a defined retention period tied to its lawful basis and purpose, with an automated deletion job that enforces it — because indefinite retention is unbounded, compounding risk that no one chose, just accumulated. "We keep everything in case it's useful" means every record is permanent DSR scope, permanent breach exposure, and permanent storage cost, and the data you no longer have a basis to hold becomes a liability the moment a regulator or an attacker asks. Don't rely on someone remembering to purge; the deletion is a scheduled job that runs, logs, and is verified, with the narrow set of records you're legally required to retain (a financial record, say) explicitly carved out and justified. Retention is a decision you make on purpose for each data category, not the default that happens when no one deletes anything.
