# Govern the highest-risk data first, not the whole estate at once

Don't try to boil the ocean — start governance where the risk and obligation are real, with the restricted and special-category PII, and expand outward in stages. A program that promises full governance over every asset on day one delivers a binder nobody follows, because the effort is spread so thin nothing is actually controlled; meanwhile the health records or payment data that genuinely needed protection wait in the queue behind the marketing email list. Sequence it as a roadmap — inventory, then classify, then control, then monitor — applied to the highest-risk domains first, so each stage produces real protection on the data that matters before moving on. Governance maturity is a path you walk deliberately, prioritized by risk; treating it as a switch you flip everywhere at once is how it becomes ceremony instead of protection.
