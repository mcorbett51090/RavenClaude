# A playbook needs a bright-line escalation trigger

A contract playbook lets business teams close the easy majority on a pre-approved template without a lawyer in the loop — its most important line is the escalation trigger: the bright line that tells them exactly when to stop and get a lawyer. Write it as a testable boundary (value threshold, beyond-fallback deviation, regulated-data touch), not a vibe. A playbook that still routes every contract through a lawyer is a queue, not self-serve; a playbook with no escalation trigger leaves business teams guessing where the cliff is.
