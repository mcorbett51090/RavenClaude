# Cycle time is measured in business days, against an SLA

A legal-ops cycle-time metric that counts calendar days punishes the team for weekends and holidays and makes every Friday intake look slow. Measure intake-to-signed in business days, segmented by request class (a standard NDA and a negotiated MSA have different clocks), and compare each against a per-class SLA so the number triggers a decision: a class breaching its SLA means add capacity, simplify the playbook, or widen self-serve — not just a redder dashboard cell. Report the breach rate, not only the average, because a healthy mean can hide a tail of contracts stuck for weeks. Pair the speed signal with a risk signal so faster never quietly means looser. Not legal advice: this measures the process, not the merits.
