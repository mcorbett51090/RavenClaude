# Fee Schedule Review Is an Annual Margin Protection Event

**Status:** Pattern
**Domain:** Practice economics / fee management
**Applies to:** `dental-practice`

---

## Why this exists

A dental fee schedule that is not reviewed and updated annually erodes in real terms as costs rise. Dental lab fees, supply costs, and staff wages increase each year; a fee schedule anchored to prior-year (or multi-year-ago) pricing silently shrinks margin even when production volume is flat. For fee-for-service and out-of-network patients, the fee schedule is the direct revenue lever. For PPO patients, the usual-and-customary (UCR) fee sets the ceiling above which PPO allowables are calculated — a UCR that is too low gives away bargaining position when PPO contracts are renegotiated.

## How to apply

Treat fee schedule review as a fixed annual operational event, not a reaction to a profit problem.

```
Annual fee review checklist:
1. Benchmark current fees:
   - Pull UCR data for the practice's zip code (source: ADA Survey of Dental Fees,
     FAIR Health, or dental fee analytics services)
   - Compare practice fees to 75th–90th percentile for the region [unverified — source varies]
   - Flag procedures where the practice fee is below the 50th percentile

2. Cost-side check:
   - Lab fee increase since last fee review? Adjust crown, bridge, denture fees accordingly.
   - Supply cost index change? Apply to composite and other material-intensive procedures.
   - Staff wage increase? Verify that high-time-intensity procedures (longer appointments) 
     still carry adequate fee-to-time ratios.

3. PPO contract review:
   - Which PPO allowables are below the practice's cost floor for that procedure?
   - Which plans are scheduled for renegotiation? Raise UCR before renegotiating — PPO 
     allowables are capped at UCR.
   - Consider terminating plans where the allowable is more than 30–35% below UCR
     [unverified — threshold varies by market; assess patient volume impact].

4. Fee update execution:
   - Update practice management system fee schedule on a fixed date each year
   - Communicate fee changes to patients via the financial policy, not case-by-case
   - Document the benchmark source and date in the practice's administrative records
```

**Do:**
- Update the fee schedule before PPO contract renewals, not after — the PPO allowable negotiation starts from the UCR.
- Apply increases selectively: crown-and-bridge, implant, and perio procedures typically carry more elasticity than preventive services.
- Benchmark against the correct geographic market — using national averages in a high-cost metro understates the market.

**Don't:**
- Apply a blanket percentage increase to all procedures without procedure-level cost analysis.
- Assume PPO patients don't notice fee changes — the patient portion (after allowable) does increase, and financial conversations must keep pace.
- Raise UCR fees while simultaneously accepting new PPO plans at lower allowables — the strategies conflict.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

Medicaid-only and CHIP practices operate under state-set fee schedules and cannot set their own UCR for covered services — the review still applies to any out-of-scope or fee-for-service services the practice offers.
