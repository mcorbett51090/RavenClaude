# Recall Reappointment Rate Is the Hygiene Department's Retention Score

**Status:** Pattern
**Domain:** Hygiene department / patient retention
**Applies to:** `dental-practice`

---

## Why this exists

Recall reappointment rate — the percentage of hygiene patients who leave each appointment with their next appointment already scheduled — is the single most important leading indicator of hygiene department health and patient retention. A practice where patients leave without a next appointment must rely on recall calls, cards, and texts to bring them back, and recall response rates are well below pre-schedule reappointment rates. [unverified — training knowledge] A low reappointment rate compounds: each missed recall cycle reduces the probability of the patient's next visit and quietly erodes the active-patient count that future production depends on.

## How to apply

Track reappointment rate as a daily and monthly hygiene metric and set a floor standard.

```
Recall reappointment metrics:
- Pre-schedule reappointment rate: 
  % of hygiene patients who schedule their next appointment before leaving today
  Target: ≥85% [unverified — training knowledge; verify against current dental benchmarks]
  Concern: <70% — requires immediate workflow intervention

- Reappointment at checkout vs. at the chair:
  Reappointment at the chair (by the hygienist) converts at a higher rate than at checkout
  Best practice: hygienist schedules the next appointment during the treatment conversation,
  front desk confirms and adds to the book

- Recall fill rate (secondary metric):
  % of hygiene appointment slots filled at 2 weeks before the appointment date
  Target: ≥90% [unverified — training knowledge]

Tracking workflow:
  [ ] Daily: hygienist records patients seen vs. patients pre-scheduled at end of day
  [ ] Monthly: pre-schedule rate reported on the hygiene dashboard
  [ ] Root cause if <85%: appointment template availability? Patient refusing? Scheduling friction?
```

**Do:**
- Make next-appointment scheduling the hygienist's standard closing action before the patient leaves the chair — not a handoff to the front desk.
- Train the hygiene team on the language: "Let's get your 6-month appointment on the books right now while we're together" is more effective than "stop at the front on your way out."
- Review reappointment rate per hygienist to identify individual coaching opportunities.

**Don't:**
- Use the recall list length as a success metric — a long recall list of patients who haven't been in for 6+ months is a symptom of low reappointment rates, not a healthy pipeline.
- Confuse scheduled recall appointments with kept appointments — track no-show and cancellation rates separately.
- Rely solely on automated recall systems (text/email) to compensate for a low reappointment rate — automation is a back-stop, not a substitute.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

Periodontal maintenance patients on 3–4 month intervals have a different reappointment cadence and may have insurance limitations on how far in advance they can schedule — track them separately from standard recall.
