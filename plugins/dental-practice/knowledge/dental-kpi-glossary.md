# Dental practice KPI glossary

The metrics a dental practice is judged on — formulas, the misreads, and where a benchmark needs a date. Benchmark tables below were web-verified **2026-06-05**; every figure carries a source. They are **trade-source rules-of-thumb**, not hard rules — mark `[verify-at-use]` and calibrate to the practice's segment, location, and case mix before any deliverable (§3 #8).

## Revenue & collection metrics

| Metric | Formula | Benchmark | Misread to avoid |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Production** | Σ fees for work performed (at full UCR) | n/a — a gross number | Production is not income; collections are (§3 #2). |
| **Collections** | Dollars actually banked | — | — |
| **Collection ratio** | collections ÷ net production | **target 96–99%** sustained; ~95% is "average" [verify-at-use] | A single good month isn't the ratio; read it sustained. |
| **Effective fee (by payer)** | collected ÷ full UCR fee = (1 − write-off%) | varies per plan | A blended average hides the worst PPO contracts (§3 #6). |
| **A/R aging** | receivables by 30/60/90+ buckets | <90-day balance kept low | Aged A/R is collections you've already produced but not banked. |

## Production & capacity metrics

| Metric | Benchmark (2025–26) | Read |
|---|---|---|
| **Dentist production / hour** | ~**$475–$575/hr**; high performers **$700+** [verify-at-use] | Per-hour, not per-day, exposes yield (§3 #4). |
| **Hygiene production / hour** | ~**$145–$175/hr**; healthy = **3–3.5× the hygienist's wage** [verify-at-use] | Under-yield = unbooked margin (§3 #5). |
| **Hygiene share of total production** | ADA baseline ~**25%**; high performers **30–33%** [verify-at-use] | A low share signals an under-run hygiene engine. |
| **Hygiene reappointment rate** | typical ~**50%**; top performer **70%+**; KPI target **85–90%+** [verify-at-use] | The cheapest schedule fill is pre-booking from the chair. |
| **Schedule utilization** | — | A full-but-low-yield book looks identical to a maxed one per day. |
| **Case acceptance** | national avg ~**50–60%**; healthy **65–85%**; by dollars closer to **35–45%** [verify-at-use] | Preventive accepts ~80–90%, large restorative ~60–70% — a low rate is presentation, not price (§3 #3). |

## Margin metrics

| Metric | Benchmark (2025–26) | Note |
|---|---|---|
| **Overhead %** | national median ~**62%** of collections; high performers **55–60%**; rising ~**5%/yr** in 2024–25 [verify-at-use] | The master margin number — read before cutting any single cost (§3 #1). |
| **Staff cost %** | ~**25–30%** of collections — the largest single expense [verify-at-use] | A unit-economics lever, not just a cost line. |
| **Net profit margin** | most profitable practices keep **30–40%** [verify-at-use] | — |
| **PPO write-off %** | participating PPOs commonly **~30–45%**; heavily-contracted toward **50%** of gross production [verify-at-use] | A strategy decision, not an accident (§3 #6). |
| **DSO/group margin context** | group locations often run **~28–35%** on shared overhead [verify-at-use] | Position an independent to this reality, not against it (§3 #7). |

## Practice-scale context (2025–26)

- **Average general-practice revenue:** ~**$750k–$1M/yr** (some sources $700k–$941.6k); monthly collections ~$65k–$120k depending on flow and geography [verify-at-use].
- **Per $1,000 produced**, some practices collect only ~**$550–$580** after PPO adjustments [verify-at-use].

## Practice-management-system (PMS) landscape context

This plugin is deliberately **PMS-neutral** (CLAUDE.md §2 — it is not a practice-management system and stores no PHI). For orientation only, the metrics above are typically pulled from a PMS/analytics layer: **Dentrix**, **Eaglesoft**, **Open Dental**, **Curve Dental**, **Denticon** (DSO-oriented), with **Dental Intelligence (Dental Intel)** and **Jarvis Analytics** as common KPI/reporting overlays. Treat any vendor-published benchmark (e.g. a "performance board" figure) as a trade source — cite + date it like any other (§3 #8).
