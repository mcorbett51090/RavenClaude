# Price the membership model, not just the number

**Status:** Pattern
**Domain:** Membership / pricing
**Applies to:** `fitness-studio-operations`

---

## Why this exists

Two studios can charge the same monthly dollar and run completely different businesses depending on the *model* behind it. Drop-in, class packs, unlimited recurring, and founding-member pricing have different cash-flow timing and different retention shapes — picking the price before the model means you've chosen a business shape by accident.

## How to apply

- Choose the **model shape first**: drop-in (volatile high margin, no retention), packs (cash pulled forward, expiry churn cliff), unlimited recurring (predictable MRR, lives on churn), founding-member (funds launch, caps lifetime value).
- Only then set the number, sized to the unit economics.
- For founding-member rates, **always cap the cohort and sunset the terms** — cheap-forever pricing anchors your best fans at your lowest rate permanently.
- Prefer limited-time offers or value-adds over cutting the headline rate; a discount is near-permanent and trains expectations.

**Do:** name the cash-flow and retention shape the model creates.
**Don't:** copy a competitor's price without modeling whether your churn supports the model under it.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

A pure walk-in/tourist location may legitimately run drop-in only — but that's a deliberate model choice, made with eyes open to its zero-retention shape.
