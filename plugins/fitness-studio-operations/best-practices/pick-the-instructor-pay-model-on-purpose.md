# Pick the instructor pay model on purpose

**Status:** Absolute rule
**Domain:** Staff ops / pay
**Applies to:** `fitness-studio-operations`

---

## Why this exists

How you pay instructors decides who carries attendance risk and what behavior you reward — and it's the line item most likely to break the studio in a slow month or quietly demotivate your best instructor. Hourly, per-head, and rev-share each shift risk and incentive differently, and a model tuned only at a typical attendance level can fail badly at the extremes.

## How to apply

- Choose on **who carries attendance risk and who controls the draw**: hourly (studio carries it), per-head (instructor carries it, rewards their own draw), rev-share (shared — usually needs a per-class floor).
- **Work an example at both low and high attendance.** The model must survive a slow week *and* a packed one without bankrupting the studio or starving the instructor.
- Don't put attendance risk on the instructor for a studio-branded class whose marketing is the studio's job — that's what the rev-share floor protects against.

**Do:** model the pay at the extremes, and match it to who drives the draw.
**Don't:** copy another studio's split, or test the model at one attendance level.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

A named-talent instructor who brings their own following can fairly carry more attendance risk (per-head or higher rev-share) — the rule is to *choose* it, not to inherit it.

## The classification companion

Whatever the pay model, **flag the 1099-vs-W2 question** (control + relationship lean it toward W2) and defer the binding call to `people-operations-hr` and counsel. Misclassification is expensive.
