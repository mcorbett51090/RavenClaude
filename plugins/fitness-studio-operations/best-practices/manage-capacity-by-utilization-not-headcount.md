# Manage capacity by utilization, not headcount

**Status:** Absolute rule
**Domain:** Schedule / capacity
**Applies to:** `fitness-studio-operations`

---

## Why this exists

"The studio was busy" and "we had 200 visits" tell you nothing about whether the *schedule* is healthy. A class is healthy at a target fill rate — full enough to feel alive and pay for itself, with room to grow — not at "full" or "empty." Managing on totals hides a strong evening subsidizing a dead mid-morning that's quietly burning instructor pay and rent.

## How to apply

- Measure **fill rate = average attendance ÷ capacity per slot** (day × time × format), not daily or weekly totals.
- Read each slot against a **target fill band** (often ~60-85%, `[verify-at-use]` for your room/format): chronically below → move/merge/change format/cut; chronically waitlisted → add capacity.
- Put **margin per class** (revenue minus instructor cost minus marginal room cost) on the table before defending a slot — an empty class still pays the instructor and the rent.
- Before cutting a low-fill slot, check it isn't an **anchor class** with `member-retention-analyst`.

**Do:** decide prune/hold/grow on per-slot utilization and margin.
**Don't:** defend or cut a class on sentiment, or on a single thin month.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

A deliberately small, premium, low-ratio format (e.g. 1:3 PT) has a different "healthy" band — set the target band per format, then manage to it.
