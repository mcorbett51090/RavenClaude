# CRE market trends (2025–2026)

Dated market context for underwriting assumptions. Cap rates, vacancy, and investment volume move quarterly — every figure here carries a source and a retrieval date.

## Investment & cap rates

CRE investment activity was expected to grow ~10% in 2025 to ~$437B (still ~18% below the 2015–19 average); transaction volume up ~19%. CBRE expected cap rates to ease slowly from their cyclical peak, with the all-property estimate holding steady. The cap-rate-vs-10-yr-Treasury spread stood at ~172 bps in Q3 2025 — the 24th percentile since 1965 (§3 #3).

## Office vacancy bifurcation

U.S. office vacancy hit a record 19.6% in Q1 2025; CBRE expected the prime vacancy rate to fall to ~13.6% by year-end and toward an ~8% pre-pandemic level by late 2027. The prime-vs-commodity gap is widening — quote the tier, not the blend (§3 #4).

## 2026 outlook

Sources point to a slow recovery and a bifurcated market into 2026; treat any single forecast as a data point and triangulate (§3 #8).
