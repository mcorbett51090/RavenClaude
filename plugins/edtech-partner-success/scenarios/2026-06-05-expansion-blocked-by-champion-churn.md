## Problem

A top-quartile partner had earned an expansion conversation (additional schools + a tier upgrade), and the PSM was ready to pitch — when the single named champion left the district. The expansion motion was about to be sent into an empty seat. The risk: pitching to a departed buyer (the ghost-sponsor pattern) wastes the whole motion, and worse, an expansion pitch arriving as the org is in flux reads as tone-deaf.

## Context

- Segment: K-12, single-threaded relationship — one curriculum-director champion, no mapped economic buyer or executive sponsor behind them.
- Constraint: K-12 leadership churn is structurally high — **superintendent turnover ran ~23% in 2024-25** — so a single-threaded champion is a known fragility, not bad luck. The expansion 3-gate model (top-quartile health AND demonstrable adoption AND organizational readiness) had its first two gates met but the third — organizational readiness — just failed.
- The partner profile hadn't been refreshed against the org chart in >90 days, so the departure surfaced late.

## Attempts

- Tried: **stop the expansion motion the moment the champion left** and re-map the buying committee before any commercial pitch — confirm a live decision-maker exists (the renewal/escalation trees both gate everything on "named decision-maker confirmed alive in role THIS quarter"). Outcome: no live sponsor yet existed; the correct first move was sponsor-mapping, not pitching.
- Tried: the **6-role sponsor taxonomy** (economic buyer / champion / technical buyer / user-champion / blocker / executive sponsor) to find a successor or a parallel thread among the user-champions who were already getting value. Outcome: identified a building-level user-champion with board credibility as the re-thread path, and flagged the multi-thread coverage gap for permanent fixing.
- Tried: **re-gate organizational readiness before re-opening expansion** — readiness is gate 3 of the expansion model, and a leadership transition fails it until a new sponsor is established and bought-in. Outcome: held expansion; ran a stabilize-and-re-thread motion first, so the expansion pitch could later land on an engaged successor rather than a cold one.

## Resolution

The expansion was *deferred, not lost* **because the PSM treated champion churn as a sponsor-mapping problem before a commercial one** — re-threading to a credible successor and re-establishing organizational readiness before re-opening the pitch. The standing fix logged: **multi-thread the buying committee and track champion redundancy** so the next departure isn't single-point-of-failure. Expansion to a thriving partner is a *when-readiness-returns* motion, never a *push-through-the-transition* one.

**Action for the next PSM hitting this pattern:** **champion churn is a sponsor-mapping problem, not an expansion-timing problem.** Stop the commercial motion, confirm a live decision-maker, re-map the 6-role committee for a successor / parallel thread, and re-gate organizational readiness before re-opening expansion. Then fix the root cause: multi-thread so no partner is single-championed. Never pitch expansion into a leadership transition — it reads as tone-deaf and lands in a dead inbox.
