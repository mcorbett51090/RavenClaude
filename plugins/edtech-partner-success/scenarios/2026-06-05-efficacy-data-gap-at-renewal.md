## Problem

A partner was 90 days from renewal, adoption was healthy, the relationship was warm — but the PSM had **no outcomes story**. Usage was high; the question the board would ask ("did it work?") had no answer tied to the district's own success criteria. The risk: in a 2025-26 budget climate where leaders prioritize *impact over volume* and **~71% of K-12 buyers cite cost as the #1 keep/cut driver**, "they use it a lot" loses to "we can't prove it worked, cut if uncertain."

## Context

- Segment: K-12, district renewal, academic-side champion who needs board-ready language, not vendor metrics.
- Constraint: the outcomes gap was structural, not lazy — **no baseline was captured at onboarding**, so a clean pre/post efficacy claim wasn't available at T-90. The honest move under that constraint is not to fabricate an outcome but to assemble the strongest *defensible* evidence stack and tie it to the district's stated criteria.
- ESSA evidence tiering (Strong / Moderate / Promising / Demonstrates-a-Rationale) has crossed from vendor marketing into the district *renewal rubric* — the academic champion translates it to the board.

## Attempts

- Tried: **a proxy-evidence stack instead of a single missing efficacy number** (the Capability Grounding alternate-methods discipline) — within-district comparison (X of Y schools hit the usage-quality bar), within-history (vs. last year's baseline where any existed), and the **product's published ESSA evidence tier** as the third leg when the partner-specific causal number was unavailable. Outcome: a defensible three-legged stack that spoke the board's rubric language, even without a clean pre/post.
- Tried: **tie measured outcomes to the district's stated success criteria** (the ones set at onboarding), not the vendor's preferred metrics. Outcome: the renewal narrative was the district's own goals met, which the academic champion could carry to the board directly.
- Tried: the **bottom-quartile honesty check** — confirm the partner is NOT actually in the unused-43% before building an impact report (a report papering over real non-use is *worse* than none; it resurfaces as "vendor claimed X, usage showed Y"). Outcome: this partner cleared the check (usage was genuinely healthy), so the impact report was the right artifact — had it failed, the route was a recovery-restart plan, not a renewal-defense report.

## Resolution

The renewal survived the "did it work?" question **because the PSM built a defensible proxy-evidence stack tied to the district's own criteria and the product's ESSA tier** — rather than either fabricating an outcome or shipping a usage-only deck. The durable lesson was logged for *next* onboarding: **capture the baseline at onboarding** so the next renewal has a clean pre/post instead of a reconstructed proxy stack.

**Action for the next PSM hitting this pattern:** when the efficacy number is missing at renewal, **don't fabricate and don't ship usage-only** — assemble a three-legged proxy stack (within-district / within-history / published ESSA tier), tie it to the district's stated success criteria, and run the bottom-quartile honesty check first (impact report vs. recovery-restart). Then fix the root cause for next time: **baseline at onboarding.** The efficacy claim's strength is `[verify-at-use]` — it depends on the district's data, not the vendor's preferred framing (CLAUDE.md §3 #12 provenance).
