# ESSA evidence tier is now a renewal asset, not just marketing

**Status:** Pattern
**Domain:** EdTech partner renewal
**Applies to:** `edtech-partner-success`

---

## Why this exists

ESSA evidence tiering (Strong / Moderate / Promising / Demonstrates a Rationale) has crossed from vendor marketing into district renewal rubrics. District curriculum directors and school board members are increasingly using the What Works Clearinghouse and Evidence for ESSA ratings as a filter for renewal decisions — not because every district administrator reads them, but because one person on the budget or curriculum committee often does, and a vendor without any ESSA evidence enters the renewal conversation at a disadvantage when a competitor has a Promising or above rating. PSMs who have not located their product's ESSA standing before the renewal conversation are navigating a dimension of the decision they don't control.

## How to apply

Before each renewal cycle, verify the product's ESSA evidence standing and prepare a one-paragraph positioning statement that a district decision-maker can use in an internal budget conversation.

```
ESSA renewal preparation checklist:
  1. Look up the product at:
     — What Works Clearinghouse (ies.ed.gov/ncee/wwc/)
     — Evidence for ESSA (evidenceforessa.org)
     — Vendor's own evidence page (verify claims match the clearinghouse)

  2. Classify the standing:
     Strong / Moderate / Promising: include in the renewal materials verbatim
     Demonstrates a Rationale: use carefully — it is the lowest bar
     No listing: do NOT claim ESSA compliance; instead prepare: "We are currently [running a study / in the clearinghouse review process / preparing our submission]"

  3. Positioning paragraph (for renewal materials):
     "[Product] holds a [Tier] rating from the What Works Clearinghouse (verified [date]),
     meeting your district's evidence standard for [goal area]. This supports your use of
     ESSER carryover / Title I / curriculum-adoption funds."

  4. Refresh the lookup annually — ratings can change as new studies are added.
```

**Do:**
- Position the ESSA rating before the renewal conversation, not in response to a challenge.
- Link to the clearinghouse listing in renewal materials so the decision-maker can verify independently.
- Coordinate with the vendor's sales or marketing team on the current official evidence statement.

**Don't:**
- Claim a tier not listed in the clearinghouse or Evidence for ESSA database.
- Use "ESSA-aligned" as a proxy for a clearinghouse rating — the phrase has no defined standard.
- Ignore a competitor's higher ESSA rating in the RFP response — address it proactively.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

Corporate L&D and higher-ed segments do not use the ESSA framework; this rule applies specifically to K-12 district renewal conversations. For products in category areas where no studies exist (niche subjects, very new edtech categories), the correct posture is documenting the "rationale" tier evidence and being honest about the research pipeline.
