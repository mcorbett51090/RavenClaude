# Open the QBR with the partner's stated outcomes, not your product's features

**Status:** Pattern

**Domain:** QBR composition

**Applies to:** `edtech-partner-success`

---

## Why this exists

A QBR that opens with PSM-internal KPIs — renewal probability, expansion ARR, total logins — tells the partner the meeting is about the vendor's goals, not theirs. The buyer's renewal criterion in K-12 is the *outcome* the product was supposed to move (assessment gains, time-on-task, family engagement), not the engagement telemetry the PSM finds convenient. The QBR is the operational Realize phase of a Value Realization motion: named business outcomes, measured baselines, current-state-vs-baseline, and what changes next. A usage report dressed as a QBR loses the room — and worse, the CFO who reads the renewal reads the line-item delta against the outcome, not the activity chart. Leading with the partner's own words (captured verbatim by the profile curator) earns the right to everything that follows.

## How to apply

Structure the narrative arc from the partner's goals outward, and prove every headline with the chart and the baseline.

```
QBR narrative arc (outcomes-first):
  OPEN  — quote the partner's stated goals VERBATIM from the success plan / profile.
          Not paraphrased, not the internal KPI restated.
  MIDDLE— where we are vs THOSE goals, each claim with a cited signal:
          "engagement up 18%" → up vs WHAT (last quarter / cohort / onboarding target)
          + source query + date range. One headline per slide; show the chart that proves it.
  CLOSE — what we'll do next, with named owners + dates (see the commitments rule).
  LENGTH— 12-18 slides. A 30-slide deck reads as "we don't know what's important."
  RENEWAL VARIANT — confirm the decision-maker is alive in the role BEFORE the meeting;
          lead with value delivered, price second (Recurly: 71% cite price as #1 churn driver).
```

**Do:**
- Schedule the K-12 EBR ahead of the Jan-Mar budget-build window so the success story enters the budget conversation, not after it's set.
- Pair the outcome lens with usage evidence — usage supports the outcome story; it doesn't replace it.

**Don't:**
- Pitch expansion in the same meeting as a recovery conversation — different motions; mixing them reads as tone-deaf.
- Put an expansion slide in a QBR for a bottom-quartile-adoption partner (a §4 anti-pattern and a credibility burn).

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

- **Pure recovery QBR** — open with listening, not an outcome scorecard the partner already knows is red; the outcome framing comes after the cause is understood.
- **Outcome data not yet available** (early stage-1 partner) — open with the leading-signal trajectory toward the stated outcome, explicitly labeled as leading, rather than inventing an outcome number.
- **Higher-ed re-accreditation cycle** — the "outcome" the institution cares about is accreditation evidence; align the EBR to that window and frame outcomes as evidence the product produces.
