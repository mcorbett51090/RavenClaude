# Provenance on every QBR claim — source, date, and baseline

**Status:** Absolute rule
**Domain:** EdTech QBR quality
**Applies to:** `edtech-partner-success`

---

## Why this exists

A QBR slide that says "engagement is up 18%" gets challenged with two questions the PSM must answer in the room: "compared to what?" and "where did you pull this?" If the answer to either is "I'll follow up," the PSM has lost credibility precisely when they need it most — in a renewal-adjacent conversation. Unprovenanced claims invite the district to question every other number in the deck. The provenance discipline — source query, date range, and comparison baseline on every quantitative claim — is what transforms a QBR from a slide show into a document the district will actually act on.

## How to apply

For every quantitative claim in a QBR deck, document the source, the date range, and the comparison baseline. Use a footnote-style citation in the slide or a speaker-notes-level documentation that the PSM can state verbally.

```
QBR claim provenance standard:

  Format: "[Metric] was [value] in [date range], compared to [baseline] ([source], pulled [date])."

  Examples:
    Good: "Active users per week averaged 142 in Q2 2026 (Jan–Mar), up 18% from Q2 2025 (121) —
          pulled from Planhat product-usage dashboard on 2026-06-02."

    Bad: "Engagement is up 18%." — no date range, no comparison baseline, no source

  What counts as a baseline:
    — Prior period (same quarter last year / prior quarter)
    — Contract benchmark (what the partner's success plan said they would hit)
    — Cohort comparison (how this district compares to others at similar adoption stage)
    — The SAME type of baseline used consistently throughout the deck (don't cherry-pick)

  Minimum provenance per slide:
    — At least one quantitative claim per slide must have a visible or speaker-note source
    — Claims sourced from the partner's own data must note the extraction date
    — Claims sourced from benchmark databases must note the benchmark source and date
```

**Do:**
- Write the source, date range, and baseline into the slide or speaker notes during QBR prep, not after.
- If a claim can't be sourced before the QBR, drop it or label it explicitly as "director's estimate."
- Use consistent baselines across the deck — don't compare to last year for some metrics and to contract targets for others without labeling the difference.

**Don't:**
- Use phrases like "significantly improved" or "meaningfully higher" without a quantified comparison.
- Pull data the morning of the QBR and cite today's date — pull several days before and note the pull date.
- Present benchmark comparisons without naming the benchmark source.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

Executive-level QBRs for senior district leadership may have fewer data slides; the provenance standard applies to every quantitative claim regardless of audience seniority. For partners who request a simplified one-page summary rather than a full deck, provenance footnotes go in the appendix.
