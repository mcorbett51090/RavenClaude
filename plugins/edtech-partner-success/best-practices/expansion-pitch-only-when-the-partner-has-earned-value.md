# Pitch expansion only when the partner has earned value — three gates, all required

**Status:** Absolute rule

**Domain:** Expansion motion / Signal interpretation

**Applies to:** `edtech-partner-success`

---

## Why this exists

The PSM is not the AE (house opinion §3 #10): the job is to make the partner succeed at what they bought, not to close more. Expansion plays exist, but they fire when the partner has *earned value* — not when a quarterly quota needs them to. An expansion pitch to a bottom-quartile-adoption partner is the named §4 anti-pattern, and it does two kinds of damage: it burns the PSM's credibility (the partner hears "they don't even know we're struggling"), and it misallocates the motion away from partners who'd actually buy. The eligibility test is a three-gate model — top-quartile health AND demonstrable adoption AND organizational readiness — and all three must be true, because a partner can be healthy but not ready (mid-leadership-transition), or adopting deeply but not yet at the outcome that justifies a bigger spend.

## How to apply

Run the three gates before any expansion motion; if any gate fails, the answer is not-yet, and the right move is to keep driving value.

```
Expansion eligibility (ALL THREE gates required — any failure = not yet):
  GATE 1  HEALTH      — top-quartile on a sound (non-drifting) score.
                        A score in drift can't gate expansion — audit it first.
  GATE 2  ADOPTION    — demonstrable DEPTH (the earned-value trigger fired), not breadth.
                        a value-trigger taxonomy event, tied to the partner's stated outcome.
  GATE 3  ORG READY   — named decision-maker alive in the role, budget cycle aligned,
                        no active recovery / leadership transition in flight.
  IF ALL THREE → step up gradually (seat / module / department / tier / multi-year);
                 arm the internal champion with the ROI story in their language.
  IF ANY FAIL  → NO pitch. Keep driving value; re-evaluate next quarter.
  TIMING       — never in the same meeting as a recovery conversation; never during a
                 testing window / dead zone; align to the budget-build window.
```

**Do:**
- Hand the expansion ROI story to the internal champion to carry — expansion is won inside the partner org, not pitched at it.
- Withdraw any in-flight expansion (and advocacy) ask the moment a partner enters recovery — bottom-quartile partners are off-limits.

**Don't:**
- Fire expansion on usage breadth or a vanity-metric high — breadth without depth isn't earned value.
- Mix an expansion pitch into a recovery QBR; the partner reads it as tone-deaf and trust takes the hit.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

- **Partner-initiated expansion** — when the partner asks to buy more, the gates become a *readiness sanity-check* (can they actually absorb it?) rather than a go/no-go on whether to raise it.
- **Contractual seat-true-up** — a true-up for seats already in use is a renewal-hygiene step, not an expansion pitch; it isn't gated by earned-value.
- **Corporate L&D enterprise rollout** — a phased rollout contracted up front may expand on a schedule independent of the per-phase health gate; gate each *new* phase, not the contracted ones.
