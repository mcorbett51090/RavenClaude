# Confirm the named decision-maker is alive in the role before running any renewal-adjacent motion

**Status:** Absolute rule

**Domain:** Churn risk / Sponsor health

**Applies to:** `edtech-partner-success`

---

## Why this exists

A renewal play, an expansion play, or a recovery play threaded through a named buyer who left the org 60 days ago arrives in an inbox no one reads — the "play-to-ghost-sponsor" wrong-first-pick. In K-12 this is not an edge case: superintendent turnover hit 23% in the 500 largest districts in 2024-25 `[verify-at-build — superintendent turnover figure]`, and curriculum-director / C&I-director churn is higher still — and that role often sets what kind of solution wins before procurement even opens. The §4 anti-pattern is named directly: a renewal play executed without the partner's named decision-maker confirmed alive in the role. A missing or silent named champion is among the most predictive single churn signals in many books; both renewal and expansion motions require a live sponsor, and running them without one is talking to an empty room.

## How to apply

Make decision-maker confirmation a standing pre-flight step on every renewal-adjacent motion, and re-confirm at least quarterly.

```
Decision-maker liveness check (run BEFORE any renewal / expansion / recovery play):
  1. Is the named economic buyer / champion still in the role? (confirm THIS quarter,
     not "the profile says so" — the profile may be stale)
  2. If changed → SPONSOR re-engagement FIRST (identify successor, re-introduce value),
     before any commercial motion. A new sponsor inherits no context; rebuild it.
  3. If silent 30+ days → treat as a sponsor signal, not a usage signal; re-engage.
  4. Champion-redundancy status: 1 = single point of failure, 2 = thin, 3+ = robust.
     A partner with one champion is one departure from churn — surface early, design for it.
  5. Record the confirmation (name, role, date) in the durable profile so the next
     PSM doesn't re-derive it.
```

**Do:**
- Confirm the named decision-maker every quarter in K-12 — turnover is the rule, not the exception.
- Treat QBR no-shows by the sponsor as a sponsor-health signal, not a scheduling nuisance.
- Map the buying committee to the 6-role taxonomy (economic buyer / champion / technical buyer / user-champion / blocker / executive sponsor) so a single departure doesn't blind the whole motion.

**Don't:**
- Open a renewal conversation on the strength of a profile the curator hasn't touched in 6 months.
- Mistake a ghost-sponsor's silence for partner disengagement and fire a recovery play at the wrong cause.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

- **Robust 3+ champion coverage with a recent QBR** — confirmation can ride the QBR rather than a separate touchpoint; you still record it.
- **Corporate L&D under M&A disruption** — the whole buying committee may reshuffle at once; pause the motion and re-map before re-engaging, rather than confirming one seat.
- **Higher-ed** — provost / dean / chair turnover is the dominant decline trigger; the confirmation cadence may need to be tighter than quarterly around leadership-transition seasons.
