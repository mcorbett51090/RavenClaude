# Start the K-12 renewal clock at 120-180 days, not the SaaS-default 90

**Status:** Pattern

**Domain:** Renewal motion / Timing

**Applies to:** `edtech-partner-success`

---

## Why this exists

The horizontal-SaaS 90-day renewal default misses the one window that decides a K-12 renewal: the January-March budget-build for July-1 fiscal-year districts. By the time a generic motion is 90 days out, the budget that funds the renewal is already set, and the PSM is negotiating against a number that's locked. The renewal in K-12 is not a single signature — it threads a budget-build window (Jan-Mar), a board-approval window (Apr-Jun), and frequently a consent-agenda mechanic, each on the school calendar. The closing synthesis from the field is that the most-common mistake is running a generic SaaS motion and discovering too late that the curriculum director was never the buyer. Starting at T-180 buys the runway to get the success story into the budget conversation, confirm the decision-maker, and assemble board-readable evidence before the window closes.

## How to apply

Anchor the renewal motion to the partner's fiscal/budget calendar, not a fixed day-count from the contract date.

```
K-12 renewal clock (July-1 fiscal-year district):
  T-180 (≈Jan)  — open the motion. Success story enters the BUDGET-BUILD window.
                  Confirm the named decision-maker is alive in the role (turnover ~23%).
  T-120 (≈Feb-Mar) — value-evidence pack assembled; multi-thread the buying committee.
  T-90  (≈Apr)  — board-approval-ready artifacts; renewal QBR / decision memo lands.
  T-0   (Jul 1) — new budget cycle; spend authorizations land.
  PRICING — lead with value delivered; "we held price for existing" second.
            Recurly: 71% cite price increases as #1 churn driver. `[verify-at-build]`
  MULTI-YEAR — in K-12, multi-year is the EXCEPTION: "subject to annual appropriation"
            makes 3-year contracts price HOLDS, not commitments. Frame accordingly.
  RFP DEFENSE — if competitive procurement opens, incumbent win rates run 60-90%
            `[verify-at-build]` IF you get on the RFP-writing team and supply reference
            districts before the RFP drops.
```

**Do:**
- Suppress major renewal/expansion asks during state testing windows (≈Mar-May) — admin attention is on the test.
- Author the renewal decision memo 120-180 days out, leading with a clear recommendation (RENEW-FLAT / RENEW-INCREASE-X% / EXPAND / RECOVERY-AND-RENEW / DO-NOT-RENEW).

**Don't:**
- Run the SaaS 90-day clock on a July-1 district — you'll miss the budget-build window entirely.
- Open a renewal conversation in the end-of-year wrap-up (late May-June); if you're starting it there, you're already too late.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

- **Non-July-1 fiscal years** — some districts and most higher-ed/corporate partners run other calendars; anchor to *their* budget-build window, not a generic 180.
- **Higher-ed** — align the renewal to the academic-year and any re-accreditation cycle, not the K-12 fiscal calendar.
- **Corporate L&D** — fiscal-year-end varies by partner (calendar / April-end / October-end); confirm per partner and back-time the clock from there — and watch the "L&D first to cut" budget-squeeze pattern.
