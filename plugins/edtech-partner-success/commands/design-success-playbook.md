# Design a success playbook

You are running `/edtech-partner-success:design-success-playbook`. Build the success plan the user described (`$ARGUMENTS`) as a measurable, time-phased motion — the work the `success-playbook-designer` and `edtech-partner-success-manager` agents own. A plan whose only success criterion is "they'll be happier" can't actually manage a partnership.

## When to use this

You are designing the onboarding-through-renewal motion for a partner (or a repeatable playbook for a segment). NOT for a single QBR (that is `/edtech-partner-success:compose-qbr`) or a churn intervention (that is `/edtech-partner-success:run-save-play`).

## Steps

1. **Define and instrument first-value before go-live** (`onboarding-instrument-first-value-from-day-one.md`): decide what "first value" means for *this* partner at contract close, instrument the signal on day one, and watch it weekly — time-to-first-value is the most predictive early renewal signal, and the K-12 settling window (weeks 4-8) is where adoption patterns set.
2. **Sequence depth before breadth in stage one** (`onboarding-sequence-depth-before-breadth-in-stage-one.md`): get one use case deeply adopted before spreading thin across features.
3. **Compress time-to-value through the train-the-trainer cascade** (`onboarding-compress-time-to-first-value-through-the-train-the-trainer-cascade.md`): plan the cascade so internal champions carry adoption rather than relying on PSM-led training alone.
4. **Build the health score on leading signals, with decay** (`health-design-leading-not-lagging-signals.md`, `health-give-every-signal-decay-and-audit-the-score-quarterly.md`): weight leading signals (depth-of-adoption trend, champion engagement, train-the-trainer completion) to dominate; give every component a half-life so the score reflects *current* state; and schedule the quarterly correlation audit against real renewal outcomes.
5. **Map the buying committee and track champion redundancy** (`sponsor-map-the-buying-committee-and-track-champion-redundancy.md`): record the 6-role committee in the durable profile with a redundancy status — a partner with one champion is one departure from churn, and in K-12 the C&I director often decides what wins before procurement opens.
6. **Start the K-12 renewal clock at 180 days** (`renewal-start-the-k12-clock-at-180-days-not-90.md`): phase the plan back from a 180-day renewal runway, not 90, and confirm the decision-maker is alive in the role every quarter (`risk-confirm-the-decision-maker-is-alive-in-the-role-every-quarter.md`). Use generic placeholders (`<district>`) throughout.

## Guardrails

- "They'll be happier" is not a success criterion — every milestone is named, dated, and measurable.
- A health score with no decay keeps a now-disengaged partner green on nine-month-old history; an un-audited score stops predicting within a few quarters — build in both.
- Single-threading is invisible until the champion leaves and the renewal lands in a dead inbox — track redundancy as a durable signal.
