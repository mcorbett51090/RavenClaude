# ESSA Evidence Tier as Renewal Asset

> ESSA evidence tiering (Strong / Moderate / Promising / Demonstrates a Rationale) has crossed from a vendor marketing detail to a **district renewal rubric** in 2025-2026. Districts under tool-rationalization pressure (71% cite cost as primary keep/cut driver per EdWeek Market Brief 2026) lead with "show me the ESSA evidence" — a PSM who can't quote the tier is exposed.

## 1. The four ESSA evidence tiers (what each means)

Per **ESEA Title VIII, Section 8101(21)(A)** and the IES What Works Clearinghouse:

| Tier | Name | What it requires |
|---|---|---|
| **1** | **Strong evidence** | At least one well-designed, well-implemented **experimental study** (randomized control trial) showing a statistically significant favorable effect |
| **2** | **Moderate evidence** | At least one well-designed, well-implemented **quasi-experimental study** showing a statistically significant favorable effect |
| **3** | **Promising evidence** | At least one well-designed, well-implemented **correlational study with statistical controls** showing a statistically significant favorable effect |
| **4** | **Demonstrates a Rationale** | A well-defined logic model, citing prior research, that demonstrates a *rationale* for the intervention — no efficacy study required |

`[verify-at-use — 2026-06-04 — ESSA statute citation + WWC criteria]`

**The 2026 rubric districts apply:** Strong + Moderate is the table-stakes threshold for renewal in a contracting district budget; Promising is "yellow"; "Demonstrates a Rationale" alone is increasingly treated as marketing, not evidence.

## 2. Where to look up a vendor's ESSA tier

| Source | Coverage | When to use |
|---|---|---|
| **IES What Works Clearinghouse** ([ies.ed.gov/ncee/wwc](https://ies.ed.gov/ncee/wwc)) | Federal authoritative; rigorous but narrower catalog | Always check first; if listed, the WWC tier supersedes vendor self-reporting |
| **Evidence for ESSA** ([evidenceforessa.org](https://www.evidenceforessa.org/)) | Johns Hopkins-curated; broader catalog of K-12 programs | When WWC has no entry — Evidence for ESSA's tiering aligns with ESSA criteria |
| **Vendor's own research/evidence page** | Self-reported; varies in rigor | Last; cross-verify with the underlying study and either WWC or Evidence for ESSA |
| **State approved-products lists** (e.g., Delaware AI Assurance Lab, TX SBOE) | State-specific; carries procurement weight | When the partner district is in a state with an approved-products list |

## 3. Worked example — Lexia Core5 portfolio

Per Lexia's vendor research page (verified 2026-06-04):
- **20 peer-reviewed studies** meeting ESSA standards
- **8 with Strong evidence (Tier 1)** — the highest ESSA tier
- Used by 3.7M pre-K–5 students in 18,400+ schools

**PSM positioning:** "Of 20 peer-reviewed studies, 8 meet ESSA Strong — Tier 1 — and 7 more meet Moderate or Promising. The remaining 5 demonstrate a rationale through the WWC logic-model framework."

That sentence is a 90-day-renewal-conversation opener with a board member or curriculum director. It's not marketing — it's the rubric the district is already applying.

---

## 4. How to position ESSA evidence in 90/60/30 conversations

### 90-day window (renewal motion start)
- **Lead with the ESSA tier portfolio.** "Here's where our product sits in the WWC/Evidence-for-ESSA frame."
- **Pre-empt the "cost vs evidence" conversation** — district budget officer is going to ask this; PSM should bring the answer first.
- **Map the vendor's strongest studies to the district's stated outcomes** (e.g., if district is focused on K-5 literacy, surface the Lexia Strong-tier early-literacy studies first).

### 60-day window (decision-narrative crystallization)
- **Convert the ESSA portfolio into the renewal-defense narrative** the curriculum director can carry into a school-board consent-agenda conversation.
- **The Garland HS pattern check** — if the district shows low-usage signals despite Strong evidence, the ESSA tier is *not enough* on its own; need usage-recovery story too.

### 30-day window (final commitments)
- **Embed the ESSA tier in the renewal decision memo** ([`templates/renewal-decision-memo.md`](../templates/renewal-decision-memo.md)) explicitly.
- **Anchor multi-year discount conversations** on ESSA tier — Strong evidence justifies premium pricing in a contracting market; "Demonstrates a Rationale" only does not.

## 5. What to do if your vendor's product has no ESSA evidence

This is increasingly common for newer / niche products. Three plays:

### 5.1 Build "Demonstrates a Rationale" rapidly
- Document a clear logic model citing prior research literature.
- Frame the product's mechanism through established learning-science findings.
- This gets you to Tier 4 — not Strong, but not "no evidence at all."

### 5.2 Sponsor a quasi-experimental study with a willing district partner
- 6-12 month design + implementation cycle.
- Cohort comparison with statistical controls = Tier 3 (Promising).
- A randomized-classroom design = Tier 2 (Moderate).
- Use the district as co-author for publication; gives them an evidence portfolio asset too.

### 5.3 Lean on alternative evidence frames
- **Usage-effectiveness studies** — even non-ESSA-tier, usage data tied to outcomes still moves the renewal conversation forward.
- **Teacher-testimonial validation** from named district partners.
- **District-internal pilot data** — co-build with partner district, present joint findings.

**The honest read:** in 2026's tool-rationalization environment, "no ESSA evidence + no logic model + no usage-outcome data" is a credibility wall at renewal. PSMs at vendors in this position should escalate to product leadership before the renewal motion starts.

---

## 6. Anti-patterns

- **Treating "Demonstrates a Rationale" as equivalent to Strong/Moderate/Promising.** Districts now read this as "marketing label" not "evidence."
- **Citing studies without the ESSA tier.** A "peer-reviewed study" with no ESSA classification is one rung lower in the rubric than the vendor expects.
- **Leading with feature roadmap when district is in tool-rationalization mode.** ESSA tier + cost-of-cutover are the right openers.
- **Quoting an outdated tier** (vendor's old study superseded by WWC review). Always verify the IES WWC entry before quoting.
