# K-12 renewal motion — the 180/120/90/60/30 model in practice

> **Last reviewed:** 2026-06-04. Source: Planhat — *The Complete Guide to B2B Customer Success Renewals* (primary, `[high]`); ChurnZero / Matik / Realm convergence on the 60-90-day decisive QBR (`[high — multiple]`); LearnPlatform / Instructure on districts "demanding evidence of effectiveness and long-term value" (`[high]`); EdWeek + AASA on 23% YoY top-500 superintendent turnover (`[high]`); NationGraph on K-12 procurement cycles 6-11 months for 37% / 12-17 months for 22% (`[high]`). Refresh when: (a) Planhat publishes a 2027 update, (b) K-12 procurement-cycle medians shift materially, (c) the underlying [`renewal-pricing-conversations-edtech.md`](renewal-pricing-conversations-edtech.md) 120-180-day clock is re-derived, or (d) a district fiscal-calendar convention shifts.
>
> **Scope.** The renewal *motion* — the operational sequence of checkpoints. The renewal *pricing conversation* (multi-year math, board mechanics, RFP defense, state-law re-trigger) lives in [`renewal-pricing-conversations-edtech.md`](renewal-pricing-conversations-edtech.md). Read both before drafting a renewal play.

## 1. Role-clarity rule — pin this to the team wall

Planhat's load-bearing claim, quoted verbatim:

> "Customer Success owns Value Realization, Adoption, and Health. Sales or Account Management owns the Commercial Contract, Procurement, and Negotiation." — Planhat, *The Complete Guide to B2B Customer Success Renewals* `[high — primary]`

**Operational consequence for the PSM.** The PSM never freelances on pricing, discounting, or contract terms. The PSM owns the *value* conversation that earns the renewal; the RM (Account Manager in Planhat's language) owns the *paper* that closes it. Crossing this line either burns the partner's trust (PSM caught negotiating without authority) or burns the PSM-RM relationship (RM walks into the deal blind on value evidence). Both are common; the rule prevents both.

**The corollary.** When a partner asks the PSM "what's the price for next year?" — the answer is "let me get the RM in this conversation" *and a same-day intro*. Not "I'll find out and circle back."

---

## 2. The counter-pattern warning — the model is the execution surface, not the mechanism

Planhat, again verbatim:

> "You don't win a 12-month B2B renewal on day 330; you win it during onboarding." `[high — Planhat]`

**Read this as:** the 180/120/90/60/30 model is the **execution surface**, not the **mechanism**. The mechanism is value delivered consistently across the school year. A PSM running the cadence perfectly on a partner whose teachers stopped logging in three months ago is running a beautiful sequence to a foregone conclusion.

**If the partner has not had a Success Metrics Strategy Meeting by day 60 of the school year, the 180-day checkpoint is already a recovery play, not a renewal play.** Route through [`partner-health-decline-which-play.md`](partner-health-decline-which-play.md) before deciding whether to run renewal or recovery first.

## 3. The full 180/120/90/60/30 model — K-12 translation

For a district on a **July 1 fiscal year** (the dominant K-12 pattern; NY mandates it, TX allows it `[high — NationGraph]`). Calendar dates assume a July 1 renewal date.

| Days out | Calendar | Action | Owner | Output |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **180** | **early January** | First renewal-prep internal review. Refresh champion map (assume superintendent turnover until verified — 23% YoY in top-500 districts `[high — EdWeek/AASA]`); pull YTD usage + outcome data; identify expansion candidates; confirm fiscal-year posture and any in-flight RFP risk. | PSM + RM | Renewal opportunity created in CRM; internal go/no-go on expansion; champion map refreshed |
| **120** | **early March** | **Year-end impact deck drafting.** Districts on July fiscal year are entering spring budget-finalization. The deck must speak SETDA's 5 Quality Indicators vocabulary (Safe / Evidence-Based / Inclusive / Usable / Interoperable — see ``setda-quality-indicators-procurement-language.md`` (deferred)). | PSM | Outcome story aligned to district's stated success criteria + SETDA-aligned evidence pack |
| **90** | **early April** | **EBR / spring strategy meeting** with executive sponsor. This is the value-realization conversation. ChurnZero / Matik / Realm all converge on 60-90 days pre-renewal as the decisive QBR `[high — multiple]`. Lexia calls this the "Success Metrics Strategy Meeting" `[high]`. | PSM + Exec Sponsor | Verbal commit; expansion scoped |
| **60** | **early May** | Proposal delivered. **Procurement starts in parallel** — K-12 procurement averages 6-11 months for 37% of districts, 12-17 months for another 22% `[high — NationGraph]`. Anything net-new from this point is a *next-year* conversation, not this renewal. | RM | Quote / order form into district procurement |
| **30** | **early June** | Commercial execution only. POs flowing through district procurement; PSM in support mode for the RM. | RM | Signed renewal |
| **0** | **July 1** | Renewal closes. PSM transitions to next-year success plan **immediately** — there is no post-signature lull in K-12 because **summer = setup window for fall go-live.** | PSM | Year-2 success plan kickoff scheduled for July |

**Two dead zones to plan around** (per [`k12-psm-operating-cadence.md`](k12-psm-operating-cadence.md)):

- **Mid-December → early January.** Do not schedule the 180-day checkpoint *during* this window — schedule it for the first week back. A January 8 internal review beats a December 22 one that no one reads.
- **Late June → mid-July.** The PSM owes the year-end impact report *before* the district scatters (see [`k12-renewal-calendar-jan-may.md`](k12-renewal-calendar-jan-may.md)).

## 4. Per-checkpoint operating notes

### 180-day checkpoint (early January) — internal posture

The 180-day checkpoint is **PSM-internal**. The partner is not in this meeting. Output is a renewal go/no-go memo to the RM.

- **Champion map diff.** Compare today's named decision-maker / curriculum lead / CTO / cabinet anchor against the names on file. Any role with new tenure < 90 days is a recovery flag, not a renewal flag. Cite the Pedagogue advisory: "map the cabinet, not the chair" `[high — Pedagogue]`.
- **YTD usage pull.** Teacher MAU/WAU per school, student session minutes, outcome-against-success-criteria status. Compare to onboarding-stated criteria; if the district hasn't restated criteria, that's a finding.
- **Expansion candidate scoping.** Is the partner top-quartile on adoption + health AND organizational readiness? If not, expansion is off the table — running an expansion pitch into a struggling adoption is the #1 credibility-burn anti-pattern (see [`expansion-play-design`](../skills/expansion-play-design/SKILL.md)).
- **Procurement-route check.** Is there an open RFP / cooperative-purchasing change / consortium shift in the district's posture? Incumbent RFP win rates are 60-90% `[high]` but only if you know it's open.

### 120-day checkpoint (early March) — deck-finalization window

The deck the PSM is drafting at 120 days is the one the executive sponsor will see at the 90-day EBR. It must:

- Tie measured outcomes to the **district's stated success criteria** (not the vendor's preferred metrics).
- Use SETDA's 5 Quality Indicators vocabulary (Safe / Evidence-Based / Inclusive / Usable / Interoperable) — the language the academic-side champion will translate to the board. See ``setda-quality-indicators-procurement-language.md`` (deferred).
- Include comparative data — "47 of 52 schools hit ≥X minutes/student/week" — the cohort framing the superintendent uses to defend a line item.
- Surface the LearnPlatform 43%-unused finding without being smug — the district is *not* asking the PSM to be told they wasted money; they are asking for evidence they didn't. See [`k12-spend-utilization-43pct.md`](k12-spend-utilization-43pct.md).

### 90-day checkpoint (early April) — the decisive EBR

Use the EBR template at ``../templates/k12-ebr-template.md`` (deferred). The objective is *verbal commit* — Planhat's wording:

> "The Day 60 Quarterly Business Review (QBR) should be focused entirely on business outcomes, realized ROI, and future strategic goals. The objective here is to secure a verbal 'yes' to the renewal by proving undeniable value." `[high — Planhat]`

The K-12 equivalent runs at day 90 (not 60) because procurement is slower — but the *posture* is identical.

### 60-day checkpoint (early May) — RM takes the wheel

> "With value proven at Day 60, Day 30 is purely about commercial execution. This is when Account Management navigates B2B procurement, handles discount requests, and finalizes the contract extension paperwork." `[high — Planhat]`

In K-12, this is the moment the PSM hands the active conversation to the RM and shifts to procurement-support mode (data the procurement team asks for, references, prior PO numbers, NASPO / cooperative-purchasing alignment).

### 30-day checkpoint (early June) — procurement support

The PSM is no longer the active driver. The PSM's job at 30 days is to *not lose the deal* in district procurement workflow friction. Have prior PO numbers, district financial-system vendor IDs, COI / W-9 / data-privacy-agreement renewals queued.

### 0-day (July 1) — immediate transition to next-year success plan

Renewal closes; the year-2 success plan must already be drafted (deliver it before the district scatters in late June per [`k12-renewal-calendar-jan-may.md`](k12-renewal-calendar-jan-may.md)). This is the **durability mechanism against superintendent turnover** — the plan is owned by the *position*, not the *person*, so a July superintendent change doesn't reset to zero.

## 5. What an agent should do differently

- The `partner-success-manager` agent, when asked "draft the renewal motion for partner X," must surface the **calendar dates** (not just day-counts) and identify which dead zones the motion bumps into. A 90-day EBR landing on a state-testing window is a re-schedule, not a meeting.
- The `success-playbook-designer` agent, when refreshing the renewal play, anchors against the 90/60/30 segment Planhat owns and adds the K-12-specific 180/120 leading edges.
- The `qbr-composer` agent, when composing the 90-day EBR, must align the deck structure to SETDA's 5 indicators and the district's stated success criteria — see ``../templates/k12-ebr-template.md`` (deferred).
- The `partner-profile-curator` agent must carry the renewal date *and the four checkpoint dates derived from it* in the partner profile.
