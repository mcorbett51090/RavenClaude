# K-12 renewal calendar — Jan-May runs the motion, not Jun-Jul

> **Last reviewed:** 2026-06-04. ## 1. The frame: K-12 renewal motion runs in spring

The dominant K-12 fiscal pattern is **July 1 fiscal year start**. NY mandates it; TX allows it; most other states follow it `[high — NationGraph]`. For a district on a July-1 fiscal year:

- Budget-build window opens in **January-February** of the prior calendar year (preliminary scoping)
- Budget proposals draft in **February-March**
- **April is the peak purchasing window** `[high — NationGraph]` — boards review budgets; principals signal next-year intent
- **May is final approval + PO drafting**
- June is closeout; renewals must be signed (or in procurement workflow) by mid-June or they're a fall-quarter risk

**The operating rule.** Anything not closed by mid-June is a fall risk; **districts are not making decisions in July about contracts they let expire in June.** The signed-renewal target is "before the district scatters" — late May / early June.

## 2. The month-by-month operational map (PSM motion)

For a July-1 renewal, walked through the calendar year leading up to it:

| Month | District state | PSM motion | Renewal-clock |

| **Jul** | New fiscal year starts; big POs cleared; "districts issue large strategic POs (avg order size 13.8× a typical March order)" `[high — NationGraph]` | Year-2 kickoff for renewing districts; deployment for new districts; rostering/integration setup | -360 days |
| **Aug** | POs convert to tactical spend; back-to-school PD weeks | Train-the-trainer; teacher PD sessions; principal walk-throughs | -330 days |
| **Sep** | First weeks of student use | BOI activation monitoring; daily/weekly health checks; first-30-day activation review | -300 days |
| **Oct** | Implementation settling; conference season | First QBR / Success Metrics Meeting; usage diagnostic per school | -270 days |
| **Nov** | Pre-Thanksgiving; testing windows | Mid-implementation review; capture early outcome signals | -240 days |
| **Dec** | **DEAD ZONE — district operations effectively closed last 2 weeks** | Internal account planning ONLY; no district-facing pushes; no decisions expected | -210 days |
| **Jan** | Spring semester start; **budget-planning conversations begin** | **180-day renewal-prep checkpoint** for Jul renewals; champion map refresh (assume superintendent turnover until verified); mid-year check-in QBR | **-180 days** |
| **Feb** | Budget proposals being drafted | Year-end impact deck drafting; SETDA-aligned evidence assembly; mid-cycle health audit | -150 days |
| **Mar** | **Budget-finalization window**; spring testing season ramps | **EBR / value-realization meeting** (per Planhat 90-day model the EBR may land late-Mar or early-Apr depending on testing-window collision); expansion scoping | -120 to -90 days |
| **Apr** | School boards reviewing budgets; **peak purchasing window begins** `[high — NationGraph]` | **Proposal delivered**; commercial conversation; procurement-team intro; PSM transitions toward RM | -90 to -60 days |

| **Jun** | School year ends; admin focus on closeout | Year-End Impact Report delivered; reflection meeting; **next-year success plan ratified before district scatters** | -30 to 0 days |

## 3. The two dead zones

### Dead zone 1: mid-December → early January

- **Window:** ~Dec 22 → first full week of January (state-variable; some districts return Jan 2, others Jan 6-8)
- **Why:** Districts closed for winter break; admin offices partially staffed at most; curriculum directors are not reading vendor email
- **What it suppresses:** All proactive PSM signals. No-response in this window is **not** a yellow signal. Do not schedule the 180-day checkpoint *inside* this window — schedule it for the **first full week back**, typically Jan 6-13.
- **What still works:** Reactive support escalations only.

### Dead zone 2: late June → mid-July

- **Window:** ~last 2 weeks of school year (state-variable) through ~mid-July
- **Why:** Grades closing, last-day events, summer planning, then district staff on summer leave; admin returns piecemeal in July
- **What it suppresses:** All renewal-clock conversations. **If the renewal isn't already in procurement workflow by mid-June, it's a fall risk — the July-1 fiscal year does not give you a recovery window.**
- **What still works:** Year-end impact report delivery (must land *before* June 15-20 to be read); next-year success plan delivery; PO follow-up by RM with district business office where one is still staffed

## 4. Calendar collisions to watch

Even within the Jan-May renewal motion, three sub-windows collide with the motion's checkpoints and must be planned around:

- **State testing windows** (~March-May, varies by grade and assessment). The 90-day EBR cannot land during the district's primary state-testing window — the curriculum director is in "do not disturb" for test administration. Push the EBR ±2 weeks.
- **Spring break** (state-variable, March or April, ~1-2 weeks). Confirm the partner's spring-break dates before scheduling the 90-day EBR; don't lose 1-2 weeks of the proposal-to-close window to a calendar collision.
- **Board-meeting cadence.** Most districts board-meet 1-2× monthly. The April / May renewal proposal needs to align to a board meeting in the budget-approval cycle. If the partner board meets the second Tuesday and your proposal lands May 15, you've missed the May board cycle and are betting on the June meeting — which often runs short.

## 5. Off-cycle fiscal years (the exceptions)

Not every K-12 district runs July-1 fiscal. The most common exceptions:

- **California (some districts):** July-1 fiscal but with LCAP (Local Control Accountability Plan) requirements that pull the budget-build conversation earlier — typically December-January draft → June board adoption
- **Texas (some districts):** September-1 fiscal year (Texas allows either); shift the entire calendar 2 months forward
- **State agencies / DOE customers:** Often calendar-year fiscal (Jan 1 start); shift the motion to a Jul-Nov build window
- **Charter networks / private K-12:** Variable; often calendar-year; confirm per partner

**The rule.** The Jan-May motion in this document is the **dominant case**, not the universal case. The `partner-profile-curator` agent must carry the partner's **fiscal-year start date** and the PSM derives the renewal-motion calendar from that, not from the default. A Texas Sep-1 fiscal district running on this document's Jan-May calendar will miss the entire procurement window.

---

## 6. What an agent should do differently

- The `edtech-partner-success-manager` agent, when drafting any renewal-motion or 180-day-checkpoint artifact, must **calendar-date the checkpoints** (not just day-counts) and flag any collision with a dead zone, state-testing window, spring break, or board-meeting cycle. "T-180" without a date is not actionable.
- The `success-playbook-designer` agent, when designing a renewal play, anchors checkpoint dates to the **partner's** fiscal year (read from the profile), not to a default July-1 calendar.
- The `qbr-composer` agent, when composing the 90-day EBR, **must** check the partner's spring-break and state-testing-window dates before locking the EBR date.
- The `partner-profile-curator` agent carries `fiscal_year_start`, `board_meeting_cadence`, `spring_break_window`, `state_testing_window` as four mandatory fields on the durable profile.
