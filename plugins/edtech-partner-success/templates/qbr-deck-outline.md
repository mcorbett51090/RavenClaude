## Slide outline

> Target: 12–18 slides. One headline per slide. Charts > body copy.

### 1. Opener
- **Headline:** The partner's stated goals, in their words
- **Content:** Verbatim quote(s) from the success plan, with attribution + date
- **Talk track:** Why we're here — to assess progress against *their* goals, not ours

### 2. Quarter at a glance
- **Headline:** `<one-line summary of the quarter — honest, not glossy>`
- **Content:** 3–4 sparklines or quick visuals of the top outcomes
- **Talk track:** Set the stage for the next 10 slides

### 3–7. Outcomes against goals (one slide per partner-stated goal)
- **Headline:** `<the goal, in partner's words>` — current state
- **Content:** The measurable outcome with chart, date range, baseline comparison (all named)
- **Talk track:** What this number means in partner's context; where they're likely to push back; how to respond

### 8–9. What's working
- **Headline:** Specific win (named user, named feature, named outcome)
- **Content:** Case-evidence — quote, number, screenshot, behavioral signal
- **Talk track:** Recognize the partner's contribution explicitly

### 10–11. What's at risk
- **Headline:** Honest concern, not buried, not catastrophized
- **Content:** Signal + likely cause + what we're proposing
- **Talk track:** Acknowledge before they raise it; show we have a plan

### 12–14. What we recommend next
- **Headline:** One specific action per slide
- **Content:** What, who, by when, why
- **Talk track:** Get partner buy-in slide-by-slide; capture pushback

### 15. Followups (the most important slide)

| Action | Owner | Date | Cadence to review |
|---|---|---|---|
| `<specific action, partner's framing where possible>` | `<named person>` | `<YYYY-MM-DD>` | `<biweekly / monthly>` |

### 16. Renewal posture (when within 90 days of renewal)
- **Headline:** Recommendation (renew / multi-year / address-specific-risks-first)
- **Content:** Reasoning grounded in the prior slides
- **Talk track:** Explicit decision-maker confirmation; objection pre-handling

### 17–18. Appendix (optional; deeper data for the audience that wants it)
- Anything that would slow the main flow
- Sources, methodology, queries
