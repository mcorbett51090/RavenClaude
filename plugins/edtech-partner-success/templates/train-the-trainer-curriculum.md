# Train-the-trainer curriculum — `<partner_name>`

> **What this is.** A curriculum template for the PSM-led session that equips 2-3 partner-side internal champions to run subsequent training waves for their own staff. Train-the-trainer is the *only* training model that scales in K-12 — direct teacher-by-teacher training by the vendor doesn't.
>
> **Pair with:** [`../skills/partner-training-program-design.md`](../skills/partner-training-program-design.md) (the playbook), [`../knowledge/k12-pd-norms-and-constraints.md`](../knowledge/k12-pd-norms-and-constraints.md) (PD hours, in-service days, modality norms), [`implementation-90-day-plan.md`](implementation-90-day-plan.md) (where in the arc this happens — weeks 5-6).
>
> **Audience.** The 2-3 internal champions — usually a curriculum coach, an instructional coach, or a teacher-leader. They are NOT the end-user teachers; they are the people who will train end-users. Curriculum framing should reflect that.

## Curriculum

### Session 1 — Product depth (2-3 hours)

**Goal:** the champion can answer any frontline-teacher question about the product without escalating.

- **Module 1:** Product overview — what it does, what it doesn't do, where it fits in the district stack
- **Module 2:** Core workflows (3-5 most-used flows) — walk through each end-to-end
- **Module 3:** Admin console — the champion's view (different from a teacher's)
- **Module 4:** Troubleshooting decision tree — "user says X, you check Y, you escalate Z"
- **Module 5:** FERPA and data-handling for the champion's downstream training audience — what to teach teachers about consent, multilingual comms, parent-facing surfaces (per [`../knowledge/parent-comms-jurisdictional-bear-traps.md`](../knowledge/parent-comms-jurisdictional-bear-traps.md))

### Session 2 — Facilitation craft (2-3 hours)

**Goal:** the champion can RUN a training session, not just attend one.

- **Module 6:** The teacher's learning objectives — what does success look like for a frontline teacher after 90 minutes of training?
- **Module 7:** Discussion-driven training vs lecture training — what works in K-12 PD specifically (per [`../knowledge/k12-pd-norms-and-constraints.md`](../knowledge/k12-pd-norms-and-constraints.md))
- **Module 8:** Modality planning — when to do live-in-person, when async-with-followup, when hybrid (depends on district PD-day calendar and teacher availability)
- **Module 9:** Mock training — the champion runs a 20-minute segment, PSM gives feedback
- **Module 10:** Continuous-improvement loop — how the champion gathers teacher feedback after each wave + adjusts subsequent waves

### Followup — first independent wave + retrospective

- **Champion runs first independent training wave** (without PSM in the room): typically a school-level group of 10-20 teachers, 60-90 minutes
- **Within 1 week:** PSM debrief with champion(s) — what worked, what didn't, what to refine
- **Within 2 weeks:** Aggregate teacher feedback collected by the champion + reviewed with the PSM
- **Outcome:** champion is now self-sufficient; PSM shifts to a quarterly check-in cadence on training (not weekly)

## Materials checklist (the PSM hands these over)

- [ ] Slide deck (editable — the champion will adapt for their context)
- [ ] Recorded module walkthroughs (asynchronous reference; respects district PD modality norms)
- [ ] Troubleshooting decision-tree quick-reference (printable 1-pager)
- [ ] Sample agenda template for the champion's first 3 training waves
- [ ] Teacher feedback form (paper + digital versions)
- [ ] District-PD-credit documentation if applicable (sign-in sheets, learning-objective alignment)

## Failure-mode flags

These should be visible in the train-the-trainer process; surface them to the PSM if seen:

- **"Trained-trainer never trains anyone."** Champion attends sessions but never runs their own wave. Causes: too-busy, didn't feel ready, district didn't formally assign training to them. Mitigation: confirm formal assignment via the district's PD office in week 5 of [`implementation-90-day-plan.md`](implementation-90-day-plan.md).
- **"Champion becomes a bottleneck."** Champion is the only one who can answer questions, gets overloaded. Mitigation: train 2-3 from the start (the template assumes this); document a tier-2-escalation path (champion → district IT → vendor support).
- **"Training cascade dies after wave 1."** First wave happens, subsequent waves don't. Causes: champion lost momentum, district PD schedule didn't allow for follow-up sessions, no measurement of cascade progress. Mitigation: PSM tracks training-wave count quarterly; flags if waves stop.
