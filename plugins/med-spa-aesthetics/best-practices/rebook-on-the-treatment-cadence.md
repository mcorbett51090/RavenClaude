# Rebook on the treatment cadence

**Status:** Absolute rule
**Domain:** Retention / patient flow
**Applies to:** `med-spa-aesthetics`

> Operations rule. No patient PHI/PII. The **provider** sets the clinical interval; the coordinator operationalizes booking it. Rebook benchmarks are `[verify-at-use]`.

---

## Why this exists

Aesthetic results fade on a clinical schedule — neuromodulators, filler, and device series have natural return intervals. A patient who loves the result but drifts instead of rebooking is retention you paid marketing money to earn once. Rebooking at the provider-set cadence, before the patient leaves, is the cheapest growth in the practice.

## How to apply

- The **provider** sets the clinically recommended return interval — the coordinator never prescribes it.
- The front desk offers a **specific** next slot at that interval, not "we'll call you."
- For the hesitant patient, offer a deposit-to-hold or tentative booking plus a reminder.
- Track rebook-on-cadence rate **per provider** and coach to it.

**Do:** book the next visit at checkout at the provider-set interval; measure per provider.
**Don't:** let the coordinator invent an interval; end with "we'll call you."

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

A one-time service with no clinical cadence (a single event-driven treatment) has nothing to rebook — capture the patient for skincare retail and referral instead.
