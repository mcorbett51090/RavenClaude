# Invest at the weakest funnel stage, not the loudest channel

**Status:** Primary diagnostic
**Domain:** DevRel strategy
**Applies to:** `developer-relations`

---

## Why this exists

The most common DevRel misallocation is pouring effort into *more awareness* — more posts, more
talks, more social — when the funnel's actual leak is *activation*: developers arrive but never
get to first success. Adding awareness spend on top of a broken activation step just fills a
leaky bucket faster. The discipline is to locate the weakest stage by its leading indicator and
invest there, even when it's the less glamorous work (fixing a quickstart rarely feels as good
as landing a keynote).

## How to apply

Walk the funnel — awareness → activation → habit → advocacy — and read the leading indicator at
each stage (content reach, quickstart completion / TTFS, retention, contributor count). The
lowest-converting step is the leak. Then pick the lowest-effort intervention at that stage
before proposing any net-new program.

**Do:**
- Diagnose the weakest stage with data before recommending work.
- Prefer the cheapest fix at the leak (e.g. audit the quickstart) over a new content initiative.
- Re-locate the leak each planning cycle — it moves as you fix it.

**Don't:**
- Default to "do more content" as the answer to every funnel problem.
- Fund the stage that's easiest to produce output for rather than the one that's actually broken.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

- A genuinely brand-new product with zero awareness *does* need awareness investment first — but
  confirm activation works on the developers you do get before scaling reach.
