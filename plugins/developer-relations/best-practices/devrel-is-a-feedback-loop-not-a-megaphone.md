# DevRel is a feedback loop, not a megaphone

**Status:** Pattern
**Domain:** Developer advocacy
**Applies to:** `developer-relations`

---

## Why this exists

The fastest way for a DevRel program to lose developer trust — and its reason to exist — is to
become a one-way broadcast channel for marketing messages. An advocate who only publishes is
doing marketing with an engineer's job title. The half of the role that creates durable value is
**carrying developer pain back to product and engineering** in a form they can act on. That loop
is what makes the product better, which is what actually moves the funnel.

## How to apply

Treat listening as a first-class deliverable. Synthesize community signal into themed,
evidenced feedback reports and route them to `product-management`.

- "Developers are frustrated" is noise.
- "12 forum threads + 8 GitHub issues: the auth quickstart's token step fails on Windows" is a
  prioritizable, evidenced theme.

**Do:**
- Rank feedback themes by frequency × severity, each with verbatim evidence and a suggested owner.
- Close the loop publicly when product ships a fix ("you asked, we shipped") — it proves listening.
- Spend real time *in* the community, not just posting to it.

**Don't:**
- Forward raw complaint volume with no synthesis (product can't act on it).
- Treat advocacy as a distribution channel for pre-written marketing copy.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

- Pure awareness content (a launch announcement) is legitimately broadcast — just don't let the
  whole program collapse into it.
