# Recognize answerers, or the community stalls

**Status:** Pattern.

**Rule:** The asker→answerer transition is powered by recognition. People answer when answering is
seen and rewarded. Surface top answerers, give them status, and thank them publicly — or the
community never develops the self-answering capacity that lets it scale.

## Why

The defining property of a healthy developer community is that it answers itself: members help
members, and staff capacity stops being the ceiling. That only happens if answering carries social
reward. Absent recognition, answering is unpaid labor with no return, so it doesn't happen, and the
community stays dependent on staff — which caps its growth at whatever the team can personally
sustain. Recognition is the cheapest, highest-leverage community investment.

## What it looks like in practice

- Top answerers are surfaced (leaderboards, badges, roles) and thanked publicly and specifically.
- The self-answer ratio (community answers ÷ total answers) is a tracked health metric.
- Consistent answerers are the natural recruitment pool for the ambassador/champion tier.

## Anti-pattern

A community where staff answer everything quickly and member answers go unacknowledged. It looks
healthy (fast responses) but is brittle — it cannot grow past the support team, and the answerer
flywheel never starts.
