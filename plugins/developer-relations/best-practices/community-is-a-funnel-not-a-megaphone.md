# Community is a funnel, not a megaphone

**Status:** Pattern.

**Rule:** A healthy community converts lurkers → askers → answerers → contributors → champions.
Broadcasting at a community is not community building. Model the funnel and the conversion between
stages, then invest in the stage that's stuck.

## Why

Member counts and message volume hide whether the community is actually healthy. What matters is
movement: do lurkers become askers, do askers get answered, do answerers become contributors? A
community that answers itself scales; one that depends on staff to answer everything does not. The
growth lever is always the stage with the worst conversion, not the top of the funnel.

## What it looks like in practice

- Each funnel stage is sized and its conversion measured (active ratio, answer rate, contributor
  conversion).
- The stuck stage gets the intervention: recognition loops for answerers, good-first-issues for
  contributors, an ambassador tier with a real value exchange for champions.
- Unanswered questions are read as a docs-gap signal and routed to onboarding.

## Anti-pattern

Announcing releases and events into a channel and calling it "community." The hook flags community
claims that cite no funnel stage or conversion.
