# Developer experience is the product

**Status:** Absolute rule.

**Rule:** DevRel does not market a product that lives somewhere else — the friction a developer hits
in the first hour *is* the product they judge. Optimize for the developer's lived experience, never
for the company's internal convenience or funnel.

## Why

A developer forms their verdict on a product before they ever talk to a human: at the signup page,
the quickstart, the first error message. No talk, post, or campaign recovers a developer who failed
to get a working result in the first hour. The experience and the product are the same thing from
where the developer sits.

## What it looks like in practice

- A confusing required step is a product defect, not a "docs nit" — file it as one.
- Internal-org boundaries ("that's the SDK team's problem") are invisible to the developer; own the
  end-to-end experience anyway and route the fix.
- When a talk and a quickstart compete for time, the quickstart wins — it's the surface more
  developers meet.

## Anti-pattern

Pouring advocacy effort into awareness while activation leaks, because events are more visible than
onboarding fixes. The hook flags content/program work that names no journey stage.
