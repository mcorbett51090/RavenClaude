# Attribution is honest or it's absent

**Status:** Absolute rule
**Domain:** DevRel metrics / integrity
**Applies to:** `developer-relations`

---

## Why this exists

Developer journeys are mostly invisible: a developer reads a post, lurks in the community for
months, and then signs up "directly." This **dark funnel** tempts DevRel into inventing
attribution — assigning signups to a talk or a post because the data is silent and a number is
politically useful. A fabricated attribution figure that drives a roadmap or budget decision is
the exact failure the inherited Grounding Protocol exists to prevent: a confident claim with no
source. Honest "we can't fully attribute this" beats a made-up percentage every time.

## How to apply

Use **self-reported attribution** ("how did you hear about us?") as the honest primary signal,
and name the dark funnel explicitly when reporting.

- "32% of new developers self-report content/community as their source; 41% are
  direct/unattributable — likely content-and-community-influenced but not individually traceable."

**Do:**
- Cite the source and date of any quantitative claim, or mark it `[unverified]`.
- Report self-reported attribution and label its limits.
- Name the unattributable share rather than reallocating it to whatever looks good.

**Don't:**
- Assign a signup to a channel the data can't support.
- Present a single clean attribution number for an inherently fuzzy journey.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

- Where real tracking exists (a unique signup link from a specific campaign), use it — the rule
  bans *inventing* attribution, not measuring it where it's genuinely measurable.
