# Attribution is honest or it's absent

**Stance:** Report DevRel impact with **honest, self-reported attribution** and an
explicit name for the unattributable share — never a fabricated number for the dark
funnel.

## Why

Developer journeys are mostly invisible: a developer reads a post, lurks in the
community for months, then signs up "directly." This dark funnel tempts DevRel into
inventing attribution — assigning signups to a talk or a post because the data is
silent and a clean number is politically useful. A fabricated attribution figure that
drives a roadmap or budget decision is exactly the failure the inherited Capability
Grounding & Claim-Grounding protocols ([`../CLAUDE.md`](../CLAUDE.md)) exist to
prevent: a confident claim with no source. Honest "we can't fully attribute this"
beats a made-up percentage every time, because the made-up percentage is the one that
gets quoted back in the room where the budget is set.

## In practice

- Use **self-reported attribution** ("how did you hear about us?") as the honest
  primary signal, and label its limits.
- **Name the dark funnel** explicitly: "41% direct/unattributable — likely
  content-and-community-influenced but not individually traceable."
- Cite the source/date of any quantitative claim, or mark it `[unverified]`; never
  reallocate the unattributable share to whatever channel looks good this quarter.
- Where real tracking genuinely exists (a unique campaign signup link), use it — the
  rule bans *inventing* attribution, not *measuring* it where it's measurable.

## Smell

A single clean attribution pie chart for an inherently fuzzy journey, a deck that
credits last quarter's signups entirely to one webinar, or any funnel number in a
board slide with no source behind it.
