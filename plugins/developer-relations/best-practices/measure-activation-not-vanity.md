# Measure activation, not vanity

**Status:** Absolute rule.

**Rule:** Stars, followers, impressions, and event headcount are inputs at best and noise at worst.
The metrics that matter are developers reaching value: activation rate, time-to-first-value, and
production adoption. Never report a vanity number without the activation number behind it.

## Why

Vanity metrics rise without the product getting any better and fall without it getting any worse.
Optimizing them produces motion without outcomes — and trains the exec team to distrust DevRel when
the vanity line goes up but revenue doesn't follow. Activation and adoption are the numbers that
move because the developer experience moved.

## What it looks like in practice

- Every input metric is reported paired with the outcome it's supposed to drive (event attendees →
  post-event activations; blog impressions → quickstart starts).
- The scorecard leads with activation and adoption; inputs go below the fold.
- A metric that doesn't change a decision is cut.

## Anti-pattern

A DevRel report headlined by GitHub stars or follower count. The hook flags reports that cite
vanity metrics with no activation/adoption metric present.
