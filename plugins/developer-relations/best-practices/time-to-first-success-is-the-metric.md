# Time-to-first-success is the metric

**Stance:** the north-star of developer experience is how long it takes a brand-new
developer to get a real result. Every artifact is judged by whether it shrinks it.

## Why

A developer evaluating your product makes a go/no-go call in the first ten minutes.
If they hit a wall before their first real result, they leave — and they tell their
team. Every downstream metric (activation, retention, expansion) is gated on that
first success. Optimizing reach, content volume, or community size while
time-to-first-success (TTFS) is bad is pouring water into a leaking bucket.

## In practice

- **Measure TTFS from a clean state** — new account, no insider knowledge — and
  treat anything you "already knew" as friction a real developer hits.
- Anchor every getting-started path on one explicit, early **first-success
  milestone**.
- Rank friction by **funnel position**: friction at "signup → first call" loses
  more developers than friction deep in an advanced flow.
- Fix the getting-started path **before** investing in reach.

## Smell

A DevRel program reporting follower growth and blog count while no one can say how
long it takes a new developer to make a successful first call.
