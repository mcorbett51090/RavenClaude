# DevRel earns its seat with a feedback loop

**Status:** Pattern.

**Rule:** The unique asset DevRel owns is structured developer feedback flowing back to product and
engineering. A DevRel team that only pushes outward and never routes signal inward is under-using
its position and will struggle to justify its budget.

## Why

Advocacy, content, and community all point outward — and every other function (marketing, sales,
support) also points outward. What DevRel uniquely has is direct, unfiltered contact with developers
at the moment they struggle: in the quickstart, at the conference Q&A, in the Discord thread. Turning
that contact into structured signal the roadmap can act on is the function no other team performs,
and it's the strongest argument for DevRel's seat at the table.

## What it looks like in practice

- A defined capture format for field feedback (what's logged, how it's tagged) and a triage cadence.
- A routing path into product: recurring friction becomes prioritized roadmap input, not anecdotes.
- Recurring questions surface as docs gaps and feature requests with frequency attached.

## Anti-pattern

A DevRel team measured only on outbound output (posts shipped, talks given) with no inbound signal
loop. When budgets tighten, the outbound-only team is the first cut; the feedback-loop team is load-bearing.
