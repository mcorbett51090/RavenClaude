# Positioning precedes content

**Status:** Pattern.

**Rule:** Decide the positioning — the developer's job-to-be-done, the alternative they'd otherwise
use, and the differentiated path — before producing content. Content authored without positioning is
a pile of topics that doesn't compound.

## Why

Positioning is the thesis every piece of content argues. Without it, the content team produces
individually-fine pieces that pull in different directions, confuse the audience about what the
product is *for*, and never build a cumulative narrative. With it, each post, talk, and demo
reinforces the same job-to-be-done and the audience forms a clear, durable understanding. Positioning
is also what makes content trustworthy to a technical audience: specificity about the job and the
tradeoffs, not superlatives.

## What it looks like in practice

- A positioning statement (job / alternative / differentiated path / proof) exists before the content
  calendar is filled.
- Messaging is segmented by adopting role (IC builder, tech lead, platform owner).
- Superlatives developers distrust ("seamless", "blazing-fast") are replaced with numbers and honest
  tradeoffs.

## Anti-pattern

A content calendar full of topics with no shared thesis, where "what are we actually telling
developers this product is for?" has no crisp answer. The hook flags content with no journey stage;
this rule adds that content with no positioning underneath it won't compound.
