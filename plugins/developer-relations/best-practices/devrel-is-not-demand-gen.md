# DevRel is not demand gen

**Stance:** DevRel success is developer **activation and retention**, measured on
the activation funnel — not MQLs, leads, followers, or impressions.

## Why

When DevRel is measured like marketing, it behaves like marketing: it optimizes for
reach and lead volume, starts speaking in superlatives, and loses the one thing a
developer audience won't give back — trust. Developers can smell marketing speak
instantly, and a single "10x your productivity!" post undoes a year of credible,
engineer-to-engineer content. The funnel DevRel owns is signup → first call → first
app → retained; demand gen owns the top of a different funnel.

## In practice

- Measure the **activation funnel** and **time-to-first-success**, not MQLs or
  follower count.
- Speak **engineer-to-engineer** — show the code, skip the superlatives.
- When the ask is genuinely a campaign, brand play, or lead-gen motion, route it to
  `marketing-operations` instead of contorting DevRel metrics to fit.
- Community health is **answered questions + returning contributors**, not members.

## Smell

A DevRel team with an MQL target, a "thought-leadership" content calendar with no
activation goals, and a community dashboard that leads with member count.
