# Design the replay strategy before you need it

Decide up front how you will reprocess history — to backfill a new consumer, recover from a processing bug, or rebuild downstream state — because replay capability is a design constraint, not a runtime convenience. Replay demands retention long enough to cover your recovery window (or a compacted changelog topic for current-state rebuilds), consumers that are idempotent so reprocessing doesn't double-count, and an offset-reset plan that won't corrupt live state. A pipeline whose only knob is "process from now" can never fix a bug retroactively or onboard a new sink without a manual data migration. The teams that recover gracefully from a bad deploy are the ones who built replay in; the ones who can't discover the gap mid-incident.
