# Exactly-once is end-to-end or it's a marketing claim

Exactly-once is a property of the whole path — source, processor, and sink participating in one coordinated transaction — not a flag you flip on the broker. Kafka's transactional EOS guarantees exactly-once within Kafka, but the moment your sink is an external database, cache, or API that doesn't enlist in that transaction, you are back to at-least-once at the boundary and must make the sink idempotent to get the effect you wanted. Teams routinely enable EOS, see the broker-side guarantee, and assume their downstream writes are deduplicated — then duplicate rows appear on the first retry. Trace the guarantee hop by hop and name where it actually holds; an exactly-once claim that stops at the broker is a claim that doesn't cover the part the business cares about.
