# Decide what happens to late data, explicitly

Events arrive after their window's watermark has passed, and you must choose their fate on purpose: allowed-lateness to update a closed window, a side-output to capture stragglers for reconciliation, or an accepted drop — but never an unexamined default. The watermark trades completeness for timeliness, and the events beyond it are exactly the ones a silent pipeline loses without a trace; the gap only surfaces when someone reconciles the stream's total against the source of record. Set the allowed lateness from the real tail of your arrival-delay distribution, route the truly-late to a dead-letter or correction path, and document the policy. Late data isn't an edge case in streaming — it's the normal case you must design for.
