# Pitch Deck Outline (Template)

> The canonical **10-12 slide** seed/Series A deck. One idea per slide. The deck's job is to **survive the partner meeting you're not in** — make it skimmable and repeatable.
>
> Co-owned by the [`pitch-and-narrative-coach`](../agents/pitch-and-narrative-coach.md) (narrative + craft) and the [`fundraising-strategist`](../agents/fundraising-strategist.md) (the ask + use-of-funds math). Stage shifts the headline: **pre-seed/seed lead with team + why-now; Series A leads with traction** — see [`fundraising-stages-decision-tree.md`](../knowledge/fundraising-stages-decision-tree.md).

Fill each `<…>`. Delete the guidance italics before sharing.

---

## 1. Cover / one-liner
**`<Company>` — `<one sentence: what you do, for whom>`**
*The single line an investor repeats to their partners. If it needs a second sentence, it's not tight enough.*

## 2. Problem
`<The pain, who has it, how big/frequent/expensive it is today.>`
*Make the tension visceral. The reader should feel it before slide 3.*

## 3. Solution
`<What you built and why it resolves the problem — in one idea.>`
*The product what/why behind this routes to product-management; here it's the story, not the spec sheet.*

## 4. Why now
`<The shift — tech, regulatory, behavioral, market — that makes this possible/urgent now.>`
*Why didn't this work 5 years ago, and why will someone else win it if not you?*

## 5. Market (TAM / SAM / SOM)
`<Bottom-up sizing: # of buyers × price, not "1% of a $X billion market".>`
*Top-down TAM is a tell. Build it from your actual buyer.*

## 6. Product
`<Screens / demo flow / the "aha" moment.>`
*Show, don't describe. One key insight per visual.*

## 7. Traction
`<The metric that matters + its trend + a cohort. Revenue/usage/retention/pipeline.>`
*The headline slide if you have it. No traction yet → keep it honest and let team + why-now carry the deck.*

## 8. Business model
`<How you make money: pricing, unit economics at a high level.>`
*Detailed model lives with finance and in the data room; this is the shape.*

## 9. Competition
`<Honest landscape + your durable wedge/moat.>`
*"We have no competitors" reads as "we don't understand the market."*

## 10. Team
`<Founders + key hires; why THIS team wins THIS problem.>`
*Underrated pre-traction (it's the bet), don't over-explain post-traction.*

## 11. The ask + use of funds
**Raising `<amount>` `<instrument: SAFE / priced>` to reach `<the specific next milestone>`.**
Use of funds: `<rough split — e.g., 50% engineering, 30% GTM, 20% ops>`.
*The ask is "this much buys us to THAT milestone." Tie the amount + dilution to the [stages tree](../knowledge/fundraising-stages-decision-tree.md) and model it with the [cap-table skill](../skills/model-cap-table-and-dilution/SKILL.md). Instrument framing must be accurate — see [term-sheet-and-safe-essentials.md](../knowledge/term-sheet-and-safe-essentials.md) (NOT legal advice).*

## 12. (Optional) Vision / appendix
`<Where this goes if it works; backup slides for diligence questions.>`

---

### Pre-send checklist
- [ ] One idea per slide; no slide needs a paragraph.
- [ ] Strongest evidence is the lede (traction or team, per stage); nothing important buried.
- [ ] Every claim carries its evidence (bottom-up market, cohort for traction).
- [ ] The ask ties to a milestone, and the amount/dilution is modeled.
- [ ] Deck claims reconcile with the [data room](../skills/prepare-data-room/SKILL.md) — no diligence surprises.
