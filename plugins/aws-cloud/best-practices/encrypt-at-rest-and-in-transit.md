# Encrypt at rest and in transit, with keys you control

Turn on encryption-at-rest everywhere it's an option — S3, EBS, RDS, EFS, DynamoDB — and prefer customer-managed KMS keys over AWS-managed ones where you need key-level access control, rotation policy, and an audit trail of who decrypted what. Enforce in-transit encryption with TLS, and use bucket policies / VPC endpoint policies that deny unencrypted (`aws:SecureTransport: false`) requests so plaintext is impossible, not merely discouraged. Encryption is nearly free, default-on for most services, and the cheapest control to satisfy compliance and limit the blast radius of a leaked snapshot or misconfigured bucket. The only encryption that helps in an incident is the encryption that was already on — retrofitting after exposure is too late.
