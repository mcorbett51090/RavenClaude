# Power is a budget you spend, mostly in sleep

A battery device lives or dies on its sleep current and duty cycle, not its active-mode efficiency. Design the sleep modes and wake sources first, then the active path: pick the deepest sleep state that retains what you need (RAM-retention vs. cold boot), enumerate every wake source (timer, GPIO, radio, sensor interrupt), and account for the leakage of every peripheral left powered. Compute the average current from the active/sleep duty cycle and divide the battery capacity by it to get a life estimate — then hold margin for self-discharge, temperature, and the OTA path. A µA of avoidable sleep current dwarfs a wasted mA in a 0.1%-duty-cycle active burst; the radio dominates active draw, so duty-cycle it hard. "We'll measure power later" on a coin-cell device is a dead product shipped.
