## Problem

A ~200-life employer ran terminations and new-hire enrollment by ad-hoc email between HR and the carrier — no written eligibility rules, no standing compliance calendar. A terminated employee's COBRA election notice went out weeks late because nobody owned the clock, and a separate new hire blew past the 30-day enrollment window with no documented QLE trail. When the late COBRA notice surfaced, the instinct in the room was to backdate the paperwork and "fix it quietly."

## Constraints context

- Eligibility lived in tribal knowledge, not a written rule set — classes, waiting periods, and notice deadlines were all "whatever HR remembered."
- The carrier and the TPA each assumed the *other* was tracking COBRA notice timing; nobody actually owned it.
- A real exposure existed: a missed/late COBRA notice carries statutory penalty risk, and papering it over compounds it.

## Attempts

- Tried: backdate the COBRA notice to look timely. Rejected immediately — papering over a missed notice is worse than the miss; it converts an administrative lapse into a credibility/legal problem and is exactly the anti-pattern the team flags.
- Tried: write down the eligibility rules and a standing filing calendar (classes, waiting periods, QLE/special-enrollment windows, COBRA/1095-C/5500/SPD-SBC deadlines) with a single named owner per deadline. This held — but didn't resolve the notice already late.
- Tried: escalate the actual late-notice exposure to ERISA counsel rather than self-adjudicate. This was the correct disposition — agents coordinate the filing, they don't sign or interpret the legal exposure.

## Resolution

The team stood up a written eligibility rule set and a dated compliance calendar with an accountable owner per deadline, so notice timing stopped depending on memory. The already-late COBRA notice was sent correctly-dated (not backdated) and the exposure was handed to ERISA counsel to assess penalty risk and remediation. The next quarter's terminations and QLEs ran on the calendar with a clean documented trail.

## Lesson

Eligibility and compliance are rules and a calendar, not vibes — written classes, waiting periods, and a standing filing calendar with a named owner prevent the scramble that produces a missed notice. Never paper over a missed COBRA notice; escalate the exposure to counsel. (Educational scaffolding; ERISA counsel signs off the legal interpretation and any remediation.)
