## Problem

A ~70-life employer got a +24% fully-insured medical renewal and a broker pitched "go self-funded, you'll save 24% and keep the surplus." Leadership was ready to sign. The renewal had not been decomposed, the group's credibility was low, and nobody had modeled a bad claims year or the stop-loss attachment points. Self-funding was being used as a one-year escape hatch from a renewal, not chosen as a risk posture.

## Constraints context

- 70 lives — low credibility; a single large claim was driving much of the "experience."
- Thin cash reserves; the CFO confirmed the company could not comfortably absorb a worst-case claims year.
- The +24% had never been broken into trend vs experience vs pooling vs one large claimant.

## Attempts

- Tried: take the self-funded pitch at face value and project the 24% "savings." Failed — the savings assumed an average claims year and ignored the downside; at this group size variance was large.
- Tried: decompose the renewal first. Found roughly: trend ~+8, one large pooled claimant ~+10, demographic ~+3, plan-buy-up ~+3 — most of the spike was a single, largely non-credible claim that pooling exists to smooth.
- Tried: level-funded as the middle path — fixed monthly cost with stop-loss and a potential surplus return, far less downside exposure than full self-funding for a 70-life group. This fit.

## Resolution

The group stayed off full self-funding (it couldn't absorb the downside and wasn't credible enough to bank on its own experience), re-marketed the fully-insured renewal armed with the decomposition (which pulled the increase down), and moved to level-funded as a deliberate stepping stone — not as a reaction to one bad year. The large claimant regressed the following year, exactly as pooling predicted.

## Lesson

Funding is a multi-year risk decision sized to the group, not a one-year escape hatch from a renewal. Decompose the renewal before reacting — a single large claim in a low-credibility group is mostly noise — and for groups that can't absorb full self-funded variance, level-funded is the honest middle path. (Educational scaffolding; an actuary and broker sign off the actual funding decision.)
