# Stop-loss is where the self-funded decision is actually made

For a self-funded or level-funded group, the stop-loss contract — not the claims fund — is where the real risk transfer lives, and it is where the fine print hides. Read both layers: the **specific** attachment (per-member catastrophic cap) and the **aggregate** attachment (the whole-group ceiling, usually ~125% of expected). Then read the *contract basis* — incurred vs paid, the 12/12 vs 12/15 run-in/run-out window, lasering of known high-cost claimants, and renewal-rate caps — because a low stop-loss premium with a bad basis can leave the group exposed exactly when a big claim lands. A self-funded plan is only as safe as its stop-loss terms; quote the attachment points *and* the basis together, never the premium alone. Educational scaffolding; a stop-loss broker and the plan's counsel review the actual contract.
