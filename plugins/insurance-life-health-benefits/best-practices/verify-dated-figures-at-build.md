# Verify every dated figure at build, never from memory

ACA affordability percentages, MLR thresholds, COBRA timing, HSA/HDHP limits, ALE counts, and Form 5500 / 1095-C deadlines are indexed or revised year to year by the IRS, DOL, and CMS — a figure that was right last year is a liability this year. Tag every quantitative or dated claim `[verify-at-build]` and re-check it against the authoritative current-year source before it gets relied on or written into a deliverable. A confidently-stated stale threshold is as dangerous as a hallucination and harder to catch: it can blow an affordability safe harbor, mis-time a filing, or misstate a rebate. Quote no ACA/COBRA/ERISA/MLR number from memory; cite the source and date, or mark it unverified and offer to verify. Educational scaffolding; a broker or ERISA counsel confirms the current-year figures and filing dates.
