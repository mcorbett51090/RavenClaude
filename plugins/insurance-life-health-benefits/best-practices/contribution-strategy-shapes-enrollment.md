# Contribution strategy shapes enrollment, not just cost

The employer/employee premium split is a behavioral lever, not only a budget line. How much the employer contributes — and *how* (flat dollar vs percentage, tiered by coverage level, defined-contribution) — drives who enrolls, which plan they pick, and whether the risk pool stays healthy. Under-contribute on dependent or family tiers and you push families off the plan, leaving an adversely-selected pool; over-contribute on the richest plan and you subsidize buy-up no one needed. Model the *take-up* and the *tier mix* the contribution structure produces, not just the per-employee-per-month cost — and check ACA affordability on the lowest-cost self-only plan before locking the split. Educational scaffolding; a broker confirms affordability and the contribution design.
