# Check network adequacy before plan-design elegance

A clever plan on a thin network is a worse member experience than a plain plan on a broad one. Before tuning deductibles, coinsurance tiers, or HSA mechanics, confirm the carrier's network actually covers the *workforce's* providers, specialists, hospitals, and geography — including remote and multi-state employees who silently fall out of network. A narrow-network or EPO/HMO design can cut premium meaningfully, but only enroll a population whose care is genuinely in-network; otherwise members hit balance bills and out-of-network costs the plan summary never showed. Verify network fit against the real employee/dependent provider mix first, then design the cost-share. Educational scaffolding; the carrier confirms current network composition before relying on it.
