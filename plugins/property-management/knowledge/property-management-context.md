## Where defensible benchmarks come from

Cap rates, market rents, turn-cost, concession, and renewal-rate benchmarks are **market-, asset-class-, and date-specific** and move with the cycle. Common reference sources practitioners cite require a dated cut: third-party market surveys, the asset's own trailing operating statements (the most defensible source), and submarket comps. **Name the source and date, or mark the figure `[unverified — training knowledge]` (§3 #8).**

## Directional frames (illustrative only — `[unverified — training knowledge]`)

| Area | Directional frame | Must-verify |
|---|---|---|
| Renewal vs new-lease cost | Renewals widely cited as materially cheaper than turns | Derive turn + vacancy + leasing cost from the asset's own books |
| Unit-turn time | Days for a light turn to weeks for a heavy one | Median by turn type from work-order history |
| Cap rate | Varies sharply by market and asset class | Use recent submarket trades, dated |
| Economic vs physical occupancy | Gap widens with concessions and delinquency | Read both; the gap is the give-back |

## Operating rhythm

- **Weekly leasing/occupancy review** of the funnel, exposure, and renewals (§3 #1 #6).
- **Delinquency aging review** to direct collections along the curve (§3 #2).
- **Make-ready / turn board** to attack turn days and the work-order backlog (§3 #3).
- **Monthly NOI/financial review** against budget, with capex/opex discipline (§3 #4 #7).

## The standing caution

Fair-housing, landlord-tenant law, eviction, lease enforcement, and security-deposit determinations are **the qualified legal authority's** call — the team frames the operating decision and routes it. Keep tenant/applicant PII (names, SSNs, payment and credit data) out of deliverables (§2).
