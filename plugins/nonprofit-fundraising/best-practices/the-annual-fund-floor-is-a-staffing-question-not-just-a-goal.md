# The annual fund floor is a staffing question, not just a goal

**Status:** Pattern
**Domain:** Nonprofit development planning
**Applies to:** `nonprofit-fundraising`

---

## Why this exists

A development plan that sets an annual fund goal without asking "how many touchpoints can this staff realistically execute?" is a plan that will fail silently. Every meaningful touchpoint — a personal call, a handwritten note, a site visit, a cultivation event follow-up — takes staff time. The gap between the plan's ambition and the staff's capacity is where dollars are left on the table and where team burnout accumulates. The annual fund floor — the minimum revenue the org needs from individual giving — is not just a goal-setting exercise; it is a staffing and time-allocation problem. The development director must work backward from the goal to the number of touchpoints required and confirm the team can execute them before the goal is set.

## How to apply

Calculate the touchpoint load implied by the development goal and compare it to available staff capacity. Flag any goal that requires more touches than the team can execute as a resource constraint, not just a stretch target.

```
Annual fund capacity check:
  Goal: $400,000 from individual donors
  Average gift (current donors): $175
  Upgrade rate assumption: 20% give 10%+ more if personally cultivated
  → donors to cultivate with personal touch: $400k / ($175 × 1.1 upgrade × 20%) = ~1,040 personal touches

  Available staff time for personal outreach per year:
    Development director: 25% of 240 working days = 60 days
    Development associate: 40% of 240 working days = 96 days
    Total: 156 days × ~6 meaningful touches per day = ~936 touches

  Gap: 1,040 required / 936 available = resource constraint
  → either reduce the goal, hire, or use bulk channels for a portion of the touchpoints

Note: "touch" = a personal call, note, visit, or curated email — not a mass email blast.
```

**Do:**
- Build a touchpoint-load model before finalizing the annual fund goal.
- Flag resource constraints to the ED/board as a staffing question, not a strategy failure.
- Tier the donor list so the highest-capacity touches go to the highest-LTV donors.

**Don't:**
- Set an annual fund goal without modeling the staff capacity required to achieve it.
- Assume a mass email campaign replaces the personal touch for mid-level and major donors.
- Present a goal to the board without the staffing implication attached.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

For very large donor files (10,000+), the annual fund is primarily driven by direct mail and digital channels where the per-touch cost is low and the staffing math is different. This rule is most binding for mid-sized shops ($500k–$5M development budgets) where personal cultivation drives a material share of the annual fund.
