# Mid-level donors are the underfunded segment in most shops

**Status:** Pattern
**Domain:** Nonprofit donor segmentation and cultivation
**Applies to:** `nonprofit-fundraising`

---

## Why this exists

Most development shops invest heavily at two ends of the giving pyramid: mass cultivation programs for annual-fund donors ($1–$999) and personal cultivation for major-gift donors ($10k+). The mid-level band ($1,000–$9,999 in many shops, though the threshold varies) receives neither the personal attention of a major-gift relationship nor the systematic touchpoint cadence of the annual fund — and quietly laps over. Mid-level donors who are systematically cultivated with personal but scalable touchpoints upgrade to major-gift level at 2–4× the rate of un-cultivated mid-level donors [ESTIMATE]. The segment is also the primary feeder into the major-gift pipeline; ignoring it closes the funnel above.

## How to apply

Define the mid-level band explicitly in the development plan. Assign it distinct cultivation and stewardship activities that differ meaningfully from the annual fund.

```
Mid-level program design (illustrative — adjust thresholds to your shop):
  Band definition: $1,000–$9,999 cumulative in last 12 months (or per-gift threshold)
  Size of segment: typically 1–5% of active donor file [ESTIMATE]
  Goal: convert 5–10% to major-gift level per year through cultivation

  Distinct touchpoints for mid-level (vs. annual fund):
    — Bi-annual personal phone call from development director or ED (not staff or volunteer)
    — Handwritten thank-you note from ED within 48 hours of gift
    — Personalized impact report sent annually (not the mass annual report)
    — Invitation to site visit or behind-the-scenes program experience annually

  Distinct touchpoints for mid-level (vs. major gift):
    — No portfolio-assigned gift officer (scalable, not fully personalized)
    — May use a mid-level society or recognition group to provide community without cost of full major-gift cultivation

Annual review: identify top-10 mid-level donors showing capacity signals for major-gift upgrade and transition to full major-gift portfolio.
```

**Do:**
- Define the mid-level band in the development plan with explicit dollar thresholds.
- Assign distinct cultivation activities that are more personal than the annual fund but scalable without a full portfolio.
- Review the mid-level pool quarterly for major-gift upgrade candidates.

**Don't:**
- Treat mid-level donors the same as annual-fund donors — the ask strategy and cultivation cadence should differ.
- Create a mid-level society without substantive benefits or touchpoints — a label without service doesn't convert.
- Ignore the mid-level segment because "we don't have enough staff for a mid-level program" — the phone calls are 15-minute investments.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

Very small shops with fewer than 100 total donors may not have a meaningful mid-level tier distinct from major gifts. In that case, treat every donor above the median gift as a major-gift cultivation priority and apply the cycle accordingly.

## Provenance

Codifies the mid-level-segment gap observed consistently in development-office audits. The two-tier program (annual fund + major gifts, nothing in between) is the dominant error in shops of $500k–$3M in fundraising budget; the mid-level program is the standard fix.
