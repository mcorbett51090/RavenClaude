# Spend Only on Allowable, Allocable, Reasonable Costs

**Status:** Absolute rule
**Domain:** Nonprofit grants — post-award compliance
**Applies to:** `nonprofit-fundraising`

---

## Why this exists

A grant award is restricted money with a rulebook attached. The single most common — and most expensive — post-award error is charging a cost to a grant that the grant does not actually cover. For federal and federal pass-through awards, the test is the Uniform Guidance (2 CFR Part 200) three-part standard: a cost must be **allowable** (permitted by the cost principles *and* by this award's terms), **allocable** (it actually benefits this award, in proportion to the benefit), and **reasonable** (a prudent person would incur it, at this amount). A cost that fails any one of the three is a disallowed cost — and a disallowed cost discovered at audit is money the organization has to pay back, often with a finding attached that raises scrutiny on every future award. Private-foundation and corporate grants aren't bound by 2 CFR 200, but the same discipline (read the agreement, charge only what it covers) applies. `[verify-at-use 2026-06-22 — confirm against current 2 CFR 200 text + the specific award terms]`

## How to apply

Run every cost through the gate **before** charging it, then classify direct vs indirect:

```
Allowable?  → permitted by the cost principles AND by THIS award (no excluded category)
   no  → do NOT charge the grant; pay from unrestricted funds if still wanted
Allocable?  → does it benefit THIS award, proportionally?
   shared across awards → ALLOCATE on a documented, consistent basis (e.g. time-and-effort)
   benefits no grant activity → not a grant cost
Reasonable? → would a prudent person incur it, at this amount?
   no  → do NOT charge; a high variance needs a documented justification
Direct or indirect?
   direct   → charge the correct direct budget line; keep the source document
   indirect → apply the negotiated (NICRA) or de-minimis rate; never double-charge as both
```

**Do:**
- Make the determination *before* the charge posts, while you can still redirect it.
- Keep the source document (invoice, timesheet, time-and-effort, contract) with every charge.
- Treat a cost type consistently — a cost recovered as indirect cannot also be charged direct.

**Don't:**
- Charge a cost to the grant because "there's budget left" — budget room doesn't make a cost allowable.
- Recover the same cost twice (once direct, once in the indirect pool).
- Apply an indirect rate without confirming the award permits it and the current de-minimis percentage `[verify-at-use]`.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

Private-foundation and corporate grants are governed by their individual agreement, not 2 CFR 200 — but read that agreement and charge only what it covers. Unrestricted operating support has no cost restriction. When award terms and the Uniform Guidance disagree, **the award terms govern for that award**.
