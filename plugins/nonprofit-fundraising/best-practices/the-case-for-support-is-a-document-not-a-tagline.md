# The case for support is a document, not a tagline

**Status:** Pattern
**Domain:** Nonprofit major gifts and grants
**Applies to:** `nonprofit-fundraising`

---

## Why this exists

"Changing lives one student at a time" is a tagline. A case for support is a written document that answers four specific questions a prospective donor or funder must be able to answer before committing a significant gift: What is the problem, and why is it urgent? What is the organization's specific and differentiated approach? What evidence exists that the approach works? And what will this donor's gift specifically fund, and what will it achieve? A development team that pitches major gifts or submits grant proposals without a written case for support is improvising answers to these questions differently in every meeting — generating inconsistency, weak follow-up, and the impression that the organization hasn't thought through its own argument.

## How to apply

Build a case for support document before launching a major-gifts cultivation cycle or a significant grant campaign. Keep it as a living internal document, updated annually or when programs change materially.

```
Case for support structure:
  Section 1: The problem and why now (urgency framing)
    — 2–3 paragraphs: the specific need, its scope, why the moment calls for action
    — Avoid generic "there are still people who need X" — name the specific gap you're filling

  Section 2: Our approach and why it works
    — 1 page: the intervention model, the theory of change, the 2–3 differentiators
    — Include outcome evidence: data or a specific story that shows the approach works

  Section 3: The ask and what it funds
    — Tiered gift impact ($5k funds X; $25k funds Y; $100k funds Z)
    — Concrete and specific — "will train 40 teachers" not "will support our programs"

  Section 4: Why this donor / funder specifically
    — Customized for each major donor or funder category based on their stated interests
    — Not boilerplate — this section is what distinguishes cultivation from mass appeal

Review cycle: update annually + after any major program or leadership change.
```

**Do:**
- Write the full case before the first major-gift ask — not after.
- Test the document with a board member or friendly donor for clarity before using it externally.
- Use the tiered gift-impact section as the backbone of the ask conversation.

**Don't:**
- Substitute a one-page annual report summary for a case for support.
- Use the same case document for individual donors, corporate sponsors, and foundation grants without customizing Section 4.
- Leave the case document un-updated for more than 18 months.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

For a single-program organization with a simple, well-established mission, the case for support may be short and relatively stable. The document-vs-improvisation discipline is most critical for multi-program organizations, campaigns seeking $100k+ gifts, and any grant where multiple reviewers will independently evaluate the proposal.
