# Lapsed donor reactivation window closes at 18 months

**Status:** Pattern
**Domain:** Nonprofit donor retention
**Applies to:** `nonprofit-fundraising`

---

## Why this exists

A donor who gave last year and hasn't given this year is lapsed but recoverable; a donor who hasn't given in two years or more requires acquisition-level effort to reactivate and rarely pays back above the cost-to-reacquire. The reactivation window — when a tailored outreach can pull a lapsed donor back at a meaningful rate — closes around 18 months of inactivity for most annual-fund programs. Beyond that point, the reactivation rate drops below 5–8% [ESTIMATE], and the cost-per-reactivated-dollar approaches or exceeds the cost-per-acquired-dollar for a new donor. Acting at 13–18 months with a specific, personalized reactivation touchpoint is materially cheaper and more effective than acting at 24 months.

## How to apply

Segment the donor file into four lapse-status tiers based on months since last gift. Build distinct communication treatments for each tier.

```
Lapse-status tiers:
  Current (gave in the last 12 months): standard stewardship cadence
  Lapsing (13–18 months): 2–3 touch reactivation sequence with personal ask
  Lapsed (19–36 months): single last-ditch appeal with strongest offer / impact story
  Long-lapsed (36+ months): suppress from standard appeals; treat as prospecting

Reactivation sequence for "Lapsing" tier (months 13–18):
  Touch 1: Impact report — "Here's what your last gift made possible"
  Touch 2: Personal phone call or handwritten note (for donors > $250 threshold)
  Touch 3: Specific ask at their last gift level + upgrade option

After 36 months: move to lapsed-prospect stream; do not send the same messaging as current donors.
```

**Do:**
- Run lapse-tier segmentation at least twice per year and update it before any major appeal.
- Personalize the reactivation ask to the donor's last gift amount and stated interest area when available.
- Track reactivation rate by tier to calibrate the 18-month threshold against your specific donor file.

**Don't:**
- Send the same annual appeal to a current donor and an 18-month lapsed donor without differentiation.
- Wait for a donor to reach 36 months of lapse before attempting reactivation.
- Suppress lapsed donors from all communication — they need a distinct reactivation stream, not silence.

## Edge cases / when the rule does NOT apply

Major-gift donors (typically $10k+) have longer cultivation cycles and longer reactivation windows — a major donor who hasn't given in two years is still a cultivation priority, not a suppression candidate. Event-driven donors (gala, golf tournament) may naturally gap between events; apply lapse tiers relative to the event cycle, not the calendar year.
